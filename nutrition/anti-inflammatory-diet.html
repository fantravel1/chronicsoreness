Anti-Inflammatory Diet

Eating to reduce inflammation and pain

Key Takeaways

  • Chronic inflammation contributes to many pain conditions
  • Certain foods promote inflammation; others help reduce it
  • The Mediterranean diet has the strongest evidence for anti-inflammatory effects
  • Diet works best as part of a comprehensive pain management plan

The Inflammation-Pain Connection

Chronic low-grade inflammation plays a role in many pain conditions, including arthritis, fibromyalgia, and cardiovascular disease. While diet alone may not eliminate chronic pain, reducing inflammatory foods and increasing anti-inflammatory foods can support overall pain management.1

How Diet Affects Inflammation

  • Foods affect inflammatory markers (CRP, IL-6, TNF-α)
  • Gut microbiome influences systemic inflammation
  • Excess body fat produces inflammatory chemicals
  • Nutritional deficiencies can worsen inflammation
Mediterranean Diet
has the strongest evidence for reducing inflammation and chronic disease2

Anti-Inflammatory Foods

Foods that help reduce inflammation:3

Fatty Fish

  • Salmon, mackerel, sardines, herring
  • Rich in omega-3 fatty acids (EPA and DHA)
  • Aim for 2-3 servings per week
  • Fish oil supplements if not eating enough fish

Colorful Fruits and Vegetables

  • Berries: Blueberries, strawberries, cherries
  • Leafy greens: Spinach, kale, collards
  • Cruciferous vegetables: Broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts
  • Orange/red vegetables: Sweet potatoes, carrots, tomatoes
  • Aim for 5-9 servings per day

Healthy Fats

  • Extra virgin olive oil: Contains oleocanthal (anti-inflammatory compound)
  • Avocados: Healthy monounsaturated fats
  • Nuts: Walnuts, almonds (in moderation)
  • Seeds: Flaxseed, chia seeds (omega-3s)

Other Anti-Inflammatory Foods

  • Turmeric: Contains curcumin (add black pepper for absorption)
  • Ginger: Anti-inflammatory properties
  • Green tea: Rich in polyphenols
  • Whole grains: Oats, brown rice, quinoa
  • Legumes: Beans, lentils
  • Dark chocolate: In moderation (70%+ cocoa)

Pro-Inflammatory Foods to Limit

Foods that may increase inflammation:4

Refined Carbohydrates

  • White bread, white rice, pastries
  • Sugary cereals
  • Cause rapid blood sugar spikes

Added Sugars

  • Sodas and sweetened beverages
  • Candy and desserts
  • Many processed foods contain hidden sugars

Processed Meats

  • Hot dogs, sausages, bacon
  • Deli meats
  • Contain advanced glycation end products (AGEs)

Trans Fats and Excessive Omega-6

  • Partially hydrogenated oils
  • Some fried foods
  • Many packaged snacks
  • Excessive vegetable oils (soybean, corn)

Alcohol

  • Excessive alcohol promotes inflammation
  • If drinking, limit to moderate amounts
  • Red wine in moderation may be least harmful

The Mediterranean Diet

The most studied anti-inflammatory eating pattern:2

Key Components

  • Abundant vegetables and fruits
  • Olive oil as primary fat source
  • Fish and seafood several times per week
  • Moderate poultry, eggs, cheese, yogurt
  • Limited red meat
  • Whole grains, legumes, nuts, seeds
  • Herbs and spices instead of salt
  • Red wine in moderation (optional)

Evidence for Pain Conditions

  • Reduces inflammatory markers
  • May help with rheumatoid arthritis symptoms
  • Associated with less chronic pain in population studies
  • Supports healthy weight

Specific Nutrients

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

  • EPA and DHA from fish; ALA from plants
  • Reduce inflammatory prostaglandins
  • Fish oil supplements: 1-3g EPA+DHA daily
  • May help with RA and other inflammatory conditions5

Vitamin D

  • Deficiency common and linked to chronic pain
  • Get tested if low energy or pain
  • Sources: sunlight, fatty fish, fortified foods
  • Supplementation if deficient

Magnesium

  • Many people don't get enough
  • Important for muscle and nerve function
  • Sources: leafy greens, nuts, seeds, whole grains

Antioxidants

  • Vitamins C, E, selenium
  • Combat oxidative stress
  • Best obtained from whole foods

Practical Tips

Getting Started

  • Make gradual changes rather than overhaul overnight
  • Add anti-inflammatory foods before removing others
  • Focus on whole foods over supplements
  • Read labels—avoid added sugars and trans fats

Meal Ideas

  • Breakfast: Oatmeal with berries and walnuts
  • Lunch: Large salad with olive oil, salmon, vegetables
  • Dinner: Grilled fish with roasted vegetables
  • Snacks: Nuts, fresh fruit, hummus with vegetables

Managing Expectations

  • Diet changes take weeks to months to show effects
  • Diet alone may not eliminate pain
  • Part of a comprehensive approach
  • Helps overall health regardless of pain impact

Weight Management

Maintaining healthy weight reduces inflammation and joint stress:

  • Excess fat tissue produces inflammatory chemicals
  • Weight loss reduces arthritis pain
  • Anti-inflammatory eating naturally supports weight management
  • Even modest weight loss (5-10%) can help
