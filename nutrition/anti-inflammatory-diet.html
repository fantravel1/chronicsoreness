Anti-Inflammatory Diet

Eating to reduce chronic inflammation

What Is an Anti-Inflammatory Diet?

An anti-inflammatory diet emphasizes foods that help reduce chronic inflammation, a process linked to many pain conditions. It's similar to the Mediterranean diet and focuses on whole, unprocessed foods.1

Foods to Emphasize2

Fruits and Vegetables

  • Leafy greens (spinach, kale)
  • Berries (blueberries, strawberries)
  • Tomatoes
  • Cherries
  • Colorful vegetables

Healthy Fats

  • Olive oil
  • Fatty fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel)
  • Nuts (walnuts, almonds)
  • Avocados

Other Beneficial Foods

  • Whole grains
  • Legumes
  • Herbs and spices (turmeric, ginger)
  • Green tea
  • Dark chocolate (in moderation)

Foods to Limit3

  • Refined carbohydrates (white bread, pastries)
  • Sugary beverages
  • Fried foods
  • Processed meats
  • Excessive red meat
  • Trans fats
  • Highly processed foods

Key Principles

  • Eat mostly plants
  • Choose whole foods
  • Include omega-3 fatty acids
  • Use herbs and spices liberally
  • Limit processed foods
  • Maintain healthy weight

Evidence for Pain

  • May help inflammatory conditions (RA)
  • Associated with reduced pain in some studies
  • Supports overall health
  • Not a quick fix, but sustainable approach
