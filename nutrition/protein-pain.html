Protein and Pain

Building blocks for muscle and healing

Why Protein Matters

Adequate protein intake is essential for maintaining muscle mass, supporting tissue repair, and overall health. People with chronic pain may be at risk for muscle loss due to reduced activity, making protein even more important.1

Protein's Role2

  • Builds and repairs tissues
  • Maintains muscle mass
  • Supports immune function
  • Creates enzymes and hormones
  • Helps with satiety

How Much Do You Need?

  • General: 0.8g per kg body weight daily
  • Older adults: 1.0-1.2g per kg
  • Active/recovering: 1.2-1.6g per kg
  • Spread intake throughout the day
  • Aim for protein at each meal

Good Protein Sources3

Animal-Based

  • Lean meat and poultry
  • Fish and seafood
  • Eggs
  • Dairy (Greek yogurt, cottage cheese)

Plant-Based

  • Legumes (beans, lentils)
  • Tofu and tempeh
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Whole grains

Protein and Muscle Loss

  • Inactivity leads to muscle loss
  • Adequate protein helps preserve muscle
  • Combine with resistance exercise if possible
  • Especially important in older adults

Practical Tips

  • Include protein at breakfast
  • Keep convenient options available
  • Use protein supplements if needed
  • Balance with vegetables and whole grains
