Anti-Inflammatory Foods

Eating to reduce inflammation

Food and Inflammation

What you eat can influence inflammation in your body. While no single food is a cure, a diet rich in anti-inflammatory foods may help reduce chronic inflammation associated with many pain conditions.1

Top Anti-Inflammatory Foods2

Fatty Fish

  • Salmon, mackerel, sardines, herring
  • Rich in omega-3 fatty acids
  • Aim for 2-3 servings per week

Berries

  • Blueberries, strawberries, raspberries
  • High in antioxidants (anthocyanins)
  • Fresh or frozen

Leafy Greens

  • Spinach, kale, collards, Swiss chard
  • Rich in vitamins and antioxidants
  • Include daily if possible

Nuts

  • Walnuts (highest omega-3)
  • Almonds, pistachios
  • Handful daily

Olive Oil

  • Extra virgin olive oil best
  • Contains oleocanthal (NSAID-like effect)
  • Use for cooking and dressings

Other Anti-Inflammatory Foods

  • Tomatoes
  • Broccoli and other cruciferous vegetables
  • Cherries
  • Avocados
  • Green tea
  • Turmeric and ginger
  • Dark chocolate (in moderation)

Foods That May Increase Inflammation3

  • Refined sugars and carbohydrates
  • Processed foods
  • Fried foods
  • Red and processed meats (in excess)
  • Excessive alcohol
  • Trans fats

Practical Tips

  • Focus on adding good foods, not just avoiding bad
  • Aim for variety and color
  • Cook at home more often
  • Read labels for hidden sugars
  • Changes take timeâ€”be patient
  • Small changes add up

Sample Anti-Inflammatory Day

  • Breakfast: Oatmeal with berries and walnuts
  • Lunch: Salad with leafy greens, salmon, olive oil dressing
  • Snack: Apple with almond butter
  • Dinner: Grilled chicken, roasted vegetables, quinoa
