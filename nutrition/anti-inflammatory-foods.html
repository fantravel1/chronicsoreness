Anti-Inflammatory Foods

Eating to reduce inflammation

Food and Inflammation

Chronic inflammation contributes to many pain conditions. While diet alone won't cure chronic pain, an anti-inflammatory eating pattern may help reduce inflammation levels and complement other treatments. Research suggests certain foods have anti-inflammatory properties while others may promote inflammation.1

Anti-Inflammatory Foods2

Fatty Fish

  • Salmon, mackerel, sardines
  • Rich in omega-3 fatty acids
  • Aim for 2-3 servings per week

Colorful Vegetables

  • Leafy greens (spinach, kale)
  • Broccoli, cauliflower
  • Bell peppers, tomatoes
  • Beets, carrots

Fruits

  • Berries (blueberries, strawberries, cherries)
  • Oranges, grapes
  • Apples, pomegranates

Healthy Fats

  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Avocados
  • Nuts (walnuts, almonds)

Other Beneficial Foods

  • Turmeric and ginger
  • Green tea
  • Dark chocolate (in moderation)
  • Legumes
  • Whole grains

Pro-Inflammatory Foods to Limit3

  • Processed and fried foods
  • Refined carbohydrates (white bread, pastries)
  • Sugar-sweetened beverages
  • Excessive red meat
  • Processed meats (hot dogs, sausage)
  • Trans fats
  • Excessive alcohol

Anti-Inflammatory Eating Patterns

  • Mediterranean diet
  • DASH diet
  • Whole foods plant-based
  • Focus on variety

Practical Tips

  • Add more vegetables to every meal
  • Swap refined grains for whole grains
  • Use olive oil instead of butter
  • Snack on nuts and fruits
  • Cook at home more often
  • Make gradual changes

Realistic Expectations

  • Diet is one piece of the puzzle
  • Changes take weeks to months to notice
  • Won't replace medical treatment
  • Individual responses vary
  • Consistency matters more than perfection
