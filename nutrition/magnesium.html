Magnesium

Essential mineral for muscle and nerve function

Why Magnesium Matters

Magnesium is involved in over 300 enzymatic reactions in the body, including muscle and nerve function, blood pressure regulation, and protein synthesis. Many people with chronic pain are deficient.1

Benefits for Pain Conditions2

  • Migraines - Can reduce frequency and severity
  • Muscle cramps - May help relaxation
  • Fibromyalgia - Low levels common
  • Neuropathic pain - May help nerve function
  • Sleep - Can improve sleep quality

Signs of Deficiency

  • Muscle cramps and twitches
  • Fatigue
  • Poor sleep
  • Headaches
  • Anxiety
  • Constipation

Forms of Magnesium3

  • Glycinate - Well absorbed, calming, less GI upset
  • Citrate - Good absorption, may cause loose stools
  • Oxide - Lower absorption, may cause GI issues
  • Malate - May help energy and fibromyalgia
  • Threonate - May cross blood-brain barrier

Food Sources

  • Dark leafy greens
  • Nuts (almonds, cashews)
  • Seeds (pumpkin, chia)
  • Legumes
  • Whole grains
  • Dark chocolate

Dosing

  • RDA: 310-420 mg/day
  • For migraines: 400-600 mg/day
  • Start low, increase gradually
  • Take in divided doses
