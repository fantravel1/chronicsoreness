Magnesium

An essential mineral for pain and muscle function

Why Magnesium Matters

Magnesium is involved in over 300 enzymatic reactions in the body, including muscle and nerve function, energy production, and protein synthesis. Low magnesium levels have been associated with various pain conditions, and supplementation may help some people with chronic pain.1

Magnesium and Pain2

  • Blocks NMDA receptors (involved in pain processing)
  • Muscle relaxant effects
  • Anti-inflammatory properties
  • Affects nerve transmission
  • Important for sleep (which affects pain)

Conditions Where Magnesium May Help

  • Migraines (prevention)
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Muscle cramps and spasms
  • Tension headaches
  • General chronic pain
  • Neuropathic pain (some evidence)

Signs of Deficiency

  • Muscle cramps and twitches
  • Fatigue
  • Weakness
  • Sleep problems
  • Anxiety
  • Many people have low intake

Food Sources3

  • Dark leafy greens (spinach, kale)
  • Nuts (almonds, cashews)
  • Seeds (pumpkin, sunflower)
  • Legumes (black beans, lentils)
  • Whole grains
  • Dark chocolate
  • Avocados
  • Bananas

Supplement Forms

  • Magnesium glycinate (well absorbed, gentle)
  • Magnesium citrate (good absorption)
  • Magnesium oxide (common but less absorbed)
  • Magnesium threonate (may cross blood-brain barrier)
  • Magnesium malate (often for fibromyalgia)

Dosing

  • RDA: 400-420 mg/day (men), 310-320 mg/day (women)
  • Supplement doses vary (200-400 mg common)
  • Split doses if GI upset occurs
  • Take with food
  • Discuss with healthcare provider

Side Effects

  • Diarrhea (most common)
  • Nausea
  • Stomach cramps
  • Usually dose-dependent
  • Try different forms if problematic

Precautions

  • Kidney diseaseâ€”use caution
  • May interact with some medications
  • Antibiotics (take separately)
  • Bisphosphonates (take separately)
  • Very high doses can be dangerous

What to Know

  • Blood tests may not reflect tissue levels
  • Food first approach is best
  • Not a magic cure for pain
  • May take weeks to see benefit
  • Part of overall approach
