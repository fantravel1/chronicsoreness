Magnesium

Essential mineral for muscles and nerves

Why Magnesium Matters

Magnesium is involved in over 300 enzymatic reactions in the body, including muscle contraction, nerve function, and energy production. Deficiency is common and may contribute to various pain conditions. About 50% of Americans don't get enough magnesium.1

Roles in Pain2

  • Muscle relaxation
  • Nerve transmission
  • NMDA receptor modulation (pain processing)
  • Inflammation regulation
  • Sleep quality (affects pain perception)

Conditions That May Benefit

  • Muscle cramps and spasms
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Migraines (prevention)
  • Tension headaches
  • Restless leg syndrome
  • Chronic fatigue

Signs of Deficiency

  • Muscle cramps or twitches
  • Fatigue
  • Weakness
  • Poor sleep
  • Anxiety
  • Numbness or tingling

Food Sources

  • Dark leafy greens (spinach, Swiss chard)
  • Nuts and seeds (almonds, pumpkin seeds)
  • Legumes
  • Whole grains
  • Dark chocolate
  • Avocados
  • Fatty fish

Supplement Forms3

  • Magnesium citrate - Well absorbed, may cause loose stools
  • Magnesium glycinate - Well absorbed, gentle on stomach
  • Magnesium oxide - Less absorbed, often used for constipation
  • Magnesium malate - Sometimes preferred for fibromyalgia
  • Magnesium threonate - Crosses blood-brain barrier

Dosage

  • RDA: 310-420mg daily (varies by age/sex)
  • Supplements often 200-400mg
  • Divide doses if taking higher amounts
  • Take with food to reduce GI effects

Safety

  • Generally safe at recommended doses
  • Excess can cause diarrhea
  • Caution with kidney disease
  • May interact with some medications
