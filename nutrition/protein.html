Protein and Chronic Pain

Supporting tissue repair and muscle health

Why Protein Matters

Adequate protein intake is essential for tissue repair, muscle maintenance, and overall health. People with chronic pain may have increased protein needs, especially if managing conditions that cause muscle wasting or if participating in rehabilitation exercise.1

Functions Relevant to Pain

  • Tissue repair and wound healing
  • Muscle maintenance and building
  • Immune function
  • Enzyme and hormone production
  • Collagen synthesis (joints, tendons)

How Much Protein?

  • General: 0.8g per kg body weight (minimum)
  • Active/exercising: 1.2-1.6g per kg
  • Older adults: 1.0-1.2g per kg (higher needs)
  • Healing/rehab: May need more

Protein Sources2

Animal Sources

  • Lean meats (chicken, turkey)
  • Fish and seafood
  • Eggs
  • Dairy (Greek yogurt, cottage cheese)

Plant Sources

  • Legumes (beans, lentils)
  • Tofu and tempeh
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Whole grains (quinoa, etc.)

Timing

  • Distribute throughout the day
  • Include protein at each meal
  • After exercise for recovery

Special Considerations

  • Kidney disease: may need to limit protein
  • Inflammatory conditions: focus on fish, plant proteins
  • Sarcopenia: higher protein + resistance exercise
