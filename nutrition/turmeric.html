Turmeric & Curcumin

The golden spice with anti-inflammatory properties

What Is Curcumin?

Curcumin is the active compound in turmeric that gives it anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. While turmeric contains only 2-8% curcumin, concentrated supplements provide higher doses.1

How It May Help Pain

  • Inhibits inflammatory pathways (COX-2, NF-ÎºB)
  • Antioxidant effects
  • May reduce inflammatory markers
  • Similar mechanism to some NSAIDs

Research Evidence2

  • Osteoarthritis - Some positive trials for knee OA
  • Rheumatoid arthritis - May reduce symptoms
  • Post-exercise pain - May reduce soreness
  • More research still needed

Absorption Challenge

Curcumin is poorly absorbed. To improve absorption:

  • Take with black pepper (piperine)
  • Take with fat/meals
  • Use enhanced formulations (phytosomes, micelles)

Typical Dosing3

  • Curcumin extracts: 500-2000 mg/day
  • Usually divided into 2-3 doses
  • May take 4-8 weeks for benefits

Precautions

  • May interact with blood thinners
  • May affect blood sugar medications
  • Gallbladder issues - use caution
  • Stop before surgery
  • Can cause GI upset in some
