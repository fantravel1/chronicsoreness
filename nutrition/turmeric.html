Turmeric and Curcumin

The golden anti-inflammatory spice

What Is Turmeric?

Turmeric is a yellow spice used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its active compound, curcumin, has anti-inflammatory properties that have generated interest for chronic pain conditions. Research suggests potential benefits, though absorption remains a challenge.1

How It May Help

  • Reduces inflammatory markers
  • Antioxidant effects
  • May modulate pain pathways
  • Affects multiple inflammatory mechanisms

Research Evidence2

Osteoarthritis

  • Most studied condition
  • Some studies show modest pain reduction
  • May reduce stiffness and improve function
  • Comparable to NSAIDs in some trials

Other Conditions

  • Rheumatoid arthritis - limited evidence
  • Muscle soreness - some benefit
  • General inflammation - promising
  • More research needed for most conditions

Absorption Challenge3

  • Curcumin is poorly absorbed
  • Rapidly metabolized and eliminated
  • Enhanced formulations available
  • Black pepper (piperine) increases absorption
  • Fat improves absorption

Forms Available

  • Turmeric spice (1-3% curcumin)
  • Standard curcumin supplements
  • Enhanced absorption formulas
  • Curcumin with piperine
  • Liposomal curcumin

Dosage

  • Studies typically use 500-2000mg curcumin daily
  • Often divided into 2-3 doses
  • Take with food containing fat
  • Allow 4-8 weeks to assess effect

Safety and Precautions

  • Generally well tolerated
  • May cause GI upset at high doses
  • Caution with blood thinners
  • May affect some medications
  • Avoid before surgery
  • Caution with gallbladder problems
