Turmeric and Curcumin

A natural anti-inflammatory spice

What Is Turmeric?

Turmeric is a yellow spice from the root of Curcuma longa, commonly used in Indian cuisine. Curcumin is its main active compound, which has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Turmeric and curcumin supplements have become popular for managing chronic pain and inflammation.1

How It May Help2

  • Anti-inflammatory effects
  • Inhibits inflammatory pathways (COX-2, NF-kB)
  • Antioxidant properties
  • May reduce pain signals
  • Generally well-tolerated

Research Evidence3

  • Most studied for osteoarthritis
  • Some evidence of modest benefit
  • May reduce pain and improve function
  • Effects similar to NSAIDs in some studies
  • More research needed

Conditions Studied

  • Osteoarthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • General chronic pain
  • Post-surgery inflammation
  • Various inflammatory conditions

Absorption Challenge

  • Curcumin is poorly absorbed
  • Black pepper (piperine) increases absorption 2000%
  • Fat improves absorption
  • Many supplements include piperine
  • Some use specialized formulations

How to Use

In Food

  • Add to curries, soups, rice
  • Golden milk (turmeric latte)
  • Add black pepper
  • Cook with fat

Supplements

  • Curcumin extract (more concentrated)
  • Typical dose: 500-2000 mg curcuminoids/day
  • Look for enhanced absorption formulas
  • Take with meals

Side Effects

  • Generally well-tolerated
  • May cause GI upset in some
  • Diarrhea at high doses
  • Yellow stool (harmless)

Cautions and Interactions

  • Blood thinners (may increase bleeding risk)
  • Gallbladder problems

  • Not a replacement for medical treatment
  • Effects may be modest
  • Takes weeks to notice benefit
