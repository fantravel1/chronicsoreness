Gluten and Pain

Understanding the connection

The Gluten Question

Many people with chronic pain wonder if gluten is contributing to their symptoms. While there's clear evidence that gluten causes problems for people with celiac disease and possibly non-celiac gluten sensitivity, the relationship between gluten and general chronic pain is less clear. Here's what the evidence shows.1

What Is Gluten?

  • Protein found in wheat, barley, rye
  • Gives bread its elasticity
  • Found in many processed foods
  • Not inherently harmful for most people

Celiac Disease2

  • Autoimmune reaction to gluten
  • Damages small intestine
  • CAN cause joint pain
  • May cause neuropathy
  • Other symptoms: GI, fatigue, skin
  • Blood test and biopsy diagnose
  • Lifelong gluten-free diet required

Non-Celiac Gluten Sensitivity

  • Symptoms from gluten without celiac
  • Controversial diagnosis
  • No specific test
  • May include pain, fatigue
  • Diagnosed by elimination

Gluten and Chronic Pain3

Evidence For

  • Celiac can cause joint pain
  • Some fibromyalgia patients report improvement
  • Individual responses vary

Evidence Against/Unclear

  • No strong evidence for widespread benefit
  • Placebo effect significant
  • Nocebo effect also exists
  • May be other components of wheat

Should You Try Gluten-Free?

  • Get tested for celiac FIRST (before eliminating)
  • If celiac negative, could try elimination
  • Give it 4-6 weeks
  • Be strict if trialing
  • Reintroduce to test response
  • Keep food diary

Considerations

  • Gluten-free diet can be nutritionally limited
  • Processed gluten-free foods often less healthy
  • Can be expensive
  • Socially challenging
  • Don't assume it will help

When It Makes Sense

  • Diagnosed celiac disease (essential)
  • GI symptoms with gluten
  • Family history of celiac
  • Interested in trial after celiac ruled out

The Bottom Line

  • No evidence everyone should go gluten-free
  • Test for celiac before elimination
  • Trial may be reasonable if curious
  • Don't expect miracles
  • Focus on overall healthy diet
