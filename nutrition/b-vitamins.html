B Vitamins

Essential nutrients for nerve health and energy

B Vitamins and Pain

B vitamins play crucial roles in nerve function, energy production, and cellular health. Several B vitamins are particularly relevant for chronic pain conditions, especially neuropathies.1

Key B Vitamins for Pain

Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin)2

  • Essential for nerve health
  • Deficiency causes neuropathy
  • Common in older adults, vegetarians
  • May help diabetic neuropathy
  • Metformin depletes B12

Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine)

  • Important for nerve function
  • May help carpal tunnel syndrome
  • Too much can cause neuropathy (>200mg/day)

Vitamin B1 (Thiamine)3

  • Critical for nerve function
  • Benfotiamine form may help neuropathy
  • Deficiency common in diabetes, alcoholism

Folate (B9)

  • Works with B12
  • Important for nervous system

Signs of B12 Deficiency

  • Tingling in hands and feet
  • Numbness
  • Balance problems
  • Fatigue
  • Memory issues
  • Depression

Food Sources

  • B12: Meat, fish, eggs, dairy
  • B6: Poultry, fish, potatoes, bananas
  • B1: Whole grains, pork, legumes
  • Folate: Leafy greens, legumes

Supplementation

  • Check levels before supplementing (especially B12)
  • B12 injections for severe deficiency
  • Methylcobalamin form may be preferred
  • B complex covers all B vitamins
