Magnesium and Pain

Essential mineral for nerve and muscle function

Why Magnesium Matters

Magnesium is an essential mineral involved in hundreds of bodily processes, including nerve transmission, muscle contraction, and pain modulation. Many people don't get enough magnesium, and deficiency may contribute to certain pain conditions. Supplementation shows promise for some types of chronic pain.1

Magnesium's Role in Pain2

  • Blocks NMDA receptors (pain receptors)
  • Reduces nerve excitability
  • Relaxes muscles
  • Anti-inflammatory effects
  • Involved in serotonin function
  • Modulates pain sensitization

Conditions Where Magnesium May Help

Migraine Prevention

  • Best evidence for magnesium and pain
  • Recommended in guidelines
  • May reduce frequency and severity
  • Often deficient in migraine patients

Muscle Cramps

  • Traditional use
  • Mixed research evidence
  • May help if deficient

Fibromyalgia

  • Often low levels found
  • Some studies show benefit
  • Part of some treatment protocols

Signs of Deficiency3

  • Muscle cramps and twitches
  • Fatigue
  • Weakness
  • Numbness and tingling
  • Mood changes
  • Irregular heartbeat
  • Blood tests often miss it

Food Sources

  • Dark leafy greens
  • Nuts (almonds, cashews)
  • Seeds (pumpkin, chia)
  • Legumes
  • Whole grains
  • Dark chocolate
  • Avocado
  • Fatty fish

Supplement Forms

  • Magnesium citrate (well absorbed)
  • Magnesium glycinate (gentle on stomach)
  • Magnesium oxide (more likely to cause GI upset)
  • Magnesium threonate (may cross blood-brain barrier)
  • Epsom salt baths (topical)

Dosing

  • RDA: 310-420 mg/day (adults)
  • Migraine prevention: often 400-600 mg
  • Start lower to assess tolerance
  • Divide doses through day
  • Take with food

Side Effects

  • Loose stools/diarrhea (most common)
  • Nausea
  • Abdominal cramping
  • Lower dose if GI issues

Cautions

  • Kidney disease (impairs excretion)
  • Can interact with some medications
  • Don't take with certain antibiotics
  • Very high doses dangerous
  • Discuss with healthcare provider
