Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Natural anti-inflammatory nutrients

What Are Omega-3s?

Omega-3 fatty acids are essential fats that your body cannot make on its own. They have anti-inflammatory properties and play important roles in brain and heart health. For chronic pain, omega-3s may help reduce inflammation that contributes to pain.1

Types of Omega-3s

  • EPA (eicosapentaenoic acid): Most anti-inflammatory
  • DHA (docosahexaenoic acid): Important for brain health
  • ALA (alpha-linolenic acid): Plant-based, converts poorly to EPA/DHA

Anti-Inflammatory Effects2

  • Reduce inflammatory cytokines
  • Compete with pro-inflammatory omega-6 fats
  • Produce anti-inflammatory molecules (resolvins)
  • May reduce need for NSAIDs in some people

Food Sources

Best Sources (EPA/DHA)

  • Fatty fish: salmon, mackerel, sardines, herring
  • Anchovies
  • Fish roe
  • Algae (vegan DHA source)

Plant Sources (ALA)

  • Flaxseeds and flaxseed oil
  • Chia seeds
  • Walnuts
  • Hemp seeds

Evidence for Pain3

  • Rheumatoid arthritis: Moderate evidence for benefit
  • Inflammatory conditions: May reduce inflammation markers
  • Neck and back pain: Some studies show benefit
  • Neuropathic pain: Limited evidence
  • Results vary between studies

Supplements

  • Fish oil capsules or liquid
  • Krill oil
  • Algae-based (vegan option)
  • Look for high EPA content for inflammation
  • Typical dose: 1-3 grams EPA+DHA daily

Quality Considerations

  • Choose purified, tested products
  • Look for third-party testing
  • Store properly to prevent rancidity
  • Fishy burps may indicate poor quality

Cautions

  • High doses may increase bleeding risk
  • Discuss with doctor if on blood thinners
  • Fish allergy concerns
  • May interact with some medications
