Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Anti-inflammatory fats for pain

What Are Omega-3s?

Omega-3 fatty acids are essential fats with anti-inflammatory properties. The main types are EPA and DHA (from fish) and ALA (from plants). They may help reduce inflammation associated with various pain conditions.1

Types of Omega-3s

  • EPA (eicosapentaenoic acid) - Strong anti-inflammatory
  • DHA (docosahexaenoic acid) - Brain and nerve health
  • ALA (alpha-linolenic acid) - Plant-based, less potent

How They Help Pain2

  • Reduce inflammatory markers
  • Compete with pro-inflammatory fats
  • Support nerve function
  • May reduce joint stiffness

Food Sources

Best Sources (EPA/DHA)

  • Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines)
  • Fish oil supplements
  • Krill oil
  • Algae-based supplements (vegan)

Plant Sources (ALA)

  • Flaxseed and flaxseed oil
  • Chia seeds
  • Walnuts
  • Hemp seeds

Evidence for Pain3

  • Rheumatoid arthritis - good evidence
  • Inflammatory back pain - some evidence
  • Neuropathic pain - emerging evidence
  • General chronic pain - modest benefits

Supplementation

  • Combined EPA+DHA: 1-3 grams daily typical
  • Choose quality brands
  • Look for third-party testing
  • Take with food
  • Effects may take 6-12 weeks

Cautions

  • May increase bleeding risk
  • Discuss with doctor if on blood thinners
  • Fish oil can cause fishy aftertaste
