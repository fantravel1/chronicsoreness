Sugar & Inflammation

How sugar may worsen chronic pain

The Sugar-Pain Connection

Excessive sugar consumption promotes inflammation throughout the body, which can worsen chronic pain conditions. Reducing sugar is one of the most impactful dietary changes for managing pain.1

How Sugar Causes Inflammation2

  • Triggers release of inflammatory cytokines
  • Increases oxidative stress
  • Promotes advanced glycation end products (AGEs)
  • Contributes to insulin resistance
  • May affect gut bacteria

Sources of Added Sugar

  • Sweetened beverages (soda, juice, sports drinks)
  • Desserts and sweets
  • Breakfast cereals
  • Flavored yogurt
  • Condiments (ketchup, BBQ sauce)
  • Processed foods
  • Many "healthy" foods (granola bars)

Names for Sugar

  • High fructose corn syrup
  • Sucrose, glucose, fructose
  • Maltose, dextrose
  • Cane sugar, cane juice
  • Anything ending in "-ose"

Reducing Sugar3

Start Gradually

  • Don't go cold turkey (cravings will be intense)
  • Reduce added sugar week by week
  • Replace sugary drinks with water first

Strategies

  • Read labels
  • Choose whole foods
  • Use fruit for sweetness
  • Cook at home more
  • Plan for cravings

Recommended Limits

  • AHA: Women <25g/day, Men <36g/day added sugar
  • WHO: <10% of calories from added sugar
