Weight Management

Healthy weight with chronic pain

Weight and Pain Connection

Excess weight can contribute to chronic pain through mechanical stress on joints and spine, increased inflammation, and other mechanisms. Losing even a modest amount of weight can improve pain and function, but managing weight with chronic pain presents unique challenges.1

How Weight Affects Pain2

  • Extra stress on weight-bearing joints
  • Increased inflammation from fat tissue
  • More pressure on spine and discs
  • Worse outcomes after surgery
  • Higher risk of developing chronic pain

The Challenge

  • Pain limits physical activity
  • Some medications cause weight gain
  • Depression and stress can affect eating
  • Comfort eating is common
  • Energy for meal prep may be limited

Strategies That Help3

Nutrition Focus

  • Focus on quality, not just quantity
  • Anti-inflammatory eating patterns
  • Mindful eating
  • Regular meal times
  • Meal planning and prep on better days

Movement Within Limits

  • Water exercise
  • Chair exercises
  • Short walks
  • Gentle yoga or stretching
  • Movement snacks throughout day

Behavioral Approaches

  • Set realistic goals
  • Focus on health, not just weight
  • Track patterns, not just numbers
  • Address emotional eating
  • Celebrate non-scale victories

Realistic Expectations

  • Slow, steady progress is sustainable
  • 5-10% weight loss can improve pain
  • Health gains happen before scale changes
  • Progress may be slower than typical

  • Behavioral weight management programs
  • Medical weight management
  • Integrated pain and weight programs
