Weight Management

The relationship between weight and chronic pain

Weight and Pain Connection

Excess weight is strongly associated with chronic pain conditions. The relationship is bidirectional - obesity increases pain risk, and chronic pain can make weight management more difficult. Even modest weight loss can significantly improve pain.1

How Excess Weight Affects Pain2

Mechanical Stress

  • Increased load on joints (especially knees, hips, spine)
  • 1 pound of weight = 4 pounds of knee force
  • Altered biomechanics

Inflammation

  • Fat tissue produces inflammatory chemicals
  • Systemic low-grade inflammation
  • Affects even non-weight-bearing joints

Other Factors

  • Sleep apnea (disrupts sleep)
  • Reduced physical activity
  • Psychological impact

Conditions Linked to Obesity

  • Osteoarthritis
  • Low back pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Gout
  • Plantar fasciitis

Benefits of Weight Loss3

  • Reduced joint pain
  • Improved function
  • Decreased inflammation
  • Better sleep
  • Improved mobility
  • May delay/avoid joint replacement

Challenges with Chronic Pain

  • Pain limits exercise
  • Some medications cause weight gain
  • Depression affects motivation
  • Emotional eating

Strategies

  • Low-impact exercise (swimming, cycling)
  • Focus on diet changes initially
  • Gradual, sustainable changes
  • Work with healthcare team
  • Address emotional eating
  • Consider medical/surgical options if appropriate
