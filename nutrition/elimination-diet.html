Elimination Diet

Identifying food triggers for pain

What Is an Elimination Diet?

An elimination diet is a systematic way to identify foods that may trigger or worsen your symptoms. You remove potential trigger foods for a period, then reintroduce them one at a time while monitoring for reactions. It's used to identify food sensitivities that may contribute to pain or inflammation.1

Why It May Help Pain2

  • Some foods may trigger inflammation
  • Food sensitivities differ from allergies
  • Reactions may be delayed (hours to days)
  • Individual responses vary widely
  • Standard allergy tests don't detect all sensitivities

Common Trigger Foods

  • Gluten (wheat, barley, rye)
  • Dairy products
  • Eggs
  • Soy
  • Corn
  • Sugar and artificial sweeteners
  • Processed foods
  • Nightshades (tomatoes, peppers, potatoes)
  • Alcohol
  • Caffeine

How to Do It3

Phase 1: Elimination (2-4 weeks)

  • Remove all suspected trigger foods
  • Eat simple, whole foods
  • Keep a food and symptom diary
  • Note any changes in pain or symptoms

Phase 2: Reintroduction

  • Add back one food at a time
  • Eat the food 2-3 times daily for 3 days
  • Wait 3 days, monitoring for reactions
  • If no reaction, food is likely safe
  • If symptoms return, remove and note as trigger

Conditions That May Respond

  • Migraine headaches
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • IBS with chronic pain
  • Chronic fatigue

Important Cautions

  • Consult healthcare provider before starting
  • Work with dietitian for complex diets
  • Ensure adequate nutrition
  • Not appropriate for everyone
  • Don't eliminate too many foods long-term
  • Not a substitute for medical treatment

What It Won't Do

  • Cure chronic pain conditions
  • Work for everyone
  • Replace other treatments
  • Identify true food allergies (need proper testing)

When to See a Professional

  • History of eating disorders
  • Multiple medical conditions
  • Taking medications affected by diet
  • Difficulty maintaining adequate nutrition
  • Unclear results
