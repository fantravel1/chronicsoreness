Elimination Diet

Identifying foods that may worsen your pain

What Is an Elimination Diet?

An elimination diet involves removing common trigger foods for a period, then systematically reintroducing them to identify which foods may worsen symptoms. Some people with chronic pain find certain foods trigger flares.1

Why It May Help

  • Some foods promote inflammation
  • Food sensitivities can affect pain
  • Gut health influences whole-body health
  • Personalized approach to identify YOUR triggers

Common Trigger Foods2

  • Gluten
  • Dairy
  • Sugar and artificial sweeteners
  • Processed foods
  • Alcohol
  • Nightshades (for some)
  • Eggs
  • Soy
  • Corn

The Process

Phase 1: Elimination (2-4 weeks)

  • Remove suspected trigger foods
  • Eat whole, unprocessed foods
  • Keep a symptom diary

Phase 2: Reintroduction

  • Add one food back at a time
  • Wait 2-3 days between new foods
  • Watch for symptom changes
  • Record reactions

Phase 3: Personalized Diet

  • Avoid confirmed triggers
  • May retry triggers later

Conditions That May Benefit3

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Migraines
  • IBS
  • Inflammatory arthritis
  • Chronic fatigue

Important Notes

  • Work with dietitian if possible
  • Ensure adequate nutrition
  • Not everyone has food triggers
  • Don't make it overly restrictive long-term
