Supplements for Chronic Pain

Evidence-based supplements with research-backed dosing—what works and what doesn't

Important Notes

Supplements are not substitutes for proven treatments. They work best alongside—not instead of—evidence-based approaches like exercise, physical therapy, and appropriate medications. Always discuss supplements with your healthcare provider, especially if you take medications or have health conditions.

Magnesium

Daily Dose: 200-400mg (up to 800mg for fibromyalgia)

Evidence

  • Systematic review shows reduced muscle soreness in active individuals
  • For fibromyalgia: 400-800mg/day studied
  • Essential for over 300 enzymatic reactions including muscle function

Best Forms

  • Magnesium glycinate: Best absorbed, calming, less GI upset
  • Magnesium citrate: Good absorption, may cause loose stools
  • Magnesium malate: May specifically help fibromyalgia fatigue
  • Avoid: Magnesium oxide (poorly absorbed)

Tips

  • Split into 2-3 doses if taking >400mg
  • Take with food to reduce GI effects
  • Evening dose may help sleep

Vitamin D

Daily Dose: 1,000-5,000 IU (based on blood levels)

Evidence

  • Meta-analysis of 19 RCTs showed significant pain reduction in deficient individuals
  • 76% of Americans have insufficiency
  • Women at higher risk of deficiency than men

Key Points

  • Get tested first: Target blood level 30-50 ng/mL
  • Choose D3: Cholecalciferol is more effective than D2
  • Take with fat: Absorption requires dietary fat
  • Deficiency treatment: 50,000 IU weekly for 8 weeks, then maintenance

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Daily Dose: 2,000-3,000mg EPA+DHA combined

Evidence

  • Meta-analysis (17 RCTs): Reduces joint pain intensity, morning stiffness, NSAID use
  • Anti-inflammatory through prostaglandin pathway modulation
  • Benefits cardiovascular and brain health alongside pain

What to Look For

  • High EPA:DHA ratio: EPA is more anti-inflammatory
  • Third-party tested: For purity and potency
  • Triglyceride form: Better absorbed than ethyl ester
  • Check total EPA+DHA: Not just "fish oil" amount

Timeline

8-12 weeks for full anti-inflammatory benefit—don't expect immediate results

Curcumin (Turmeric)

Daily Dose: 500-1,500mg curcumin (not turmeric powder)

Evidence

  • 2021 review of 15 RCTs: Relieved osteoarthritis pain as well as NSAIDs without GI side effects
  • Anti-inflammatory through multiple pathways
  • Also antioxidant effects

Absorption Challenge

Curcumin is poorly absorbed. You need enhanced formulations:

  • With piperine (black pepper): Increases absorption 2,000%
  • Phytosome formulations: Meriva, Longvida
  • Nano/liposomal: Enhanced delivery
  • BCM-95: Enhanced bioavailability

Stop 2 weeks before surgery due to blood thinning effect. Use caution with blood thinners.

CoQ10 (Coenzyme Q10)

Daily Dose: 200-400mg

Evidence for Fibromyalgia

  • RCT: 300mg/day for 40 days reduced pain and fatigue by >50%
  • Fibromyalgia patients have 40-50% depleted CoQ10 levels
  • Essential for cellular energy production

Which Form?

  • Ubiquinol: Active form, better absorbed, especially for older adults
  • Ubiquinone: Conventional form, less expensive

Tips

  • Take with food containing fat
  • Split into 2 doses if taking >200mg
  • May take 4-8 weeks to notice benefit

Summary Table

Supplement Dose Best For Timeline
Magnesium 200-800mg Muscle pain, cramps, sleep 2-4 weeks
Vitamin D 1,000-5,000 IU Deficient patients, widespread pain 8-12 weeks
Omega-3s 2,000-3,000mg EPA+DHA Joint pain, inflammation 8-12 weeks
Curcumin 500-1,500mg Osteoarthritis, inflammation 4-8 weeks
CoQ10 200-400mg Fibromyalgia, fatigue 4-8 weeks

Quality Matters

Supplements aren't strictly regulated. Look for:

  • Third-party testing: USP, NSF, ConsumerLab verification
  • GMP certification: Good Manufacturing Practices
  • Transparent labeling: Exact amounts of active ingredients
  • Reputable brands: With track record and quality standards
