Ginger

A natural anti-inflammatory root

Ginger and Pain

Ginger (Zingiber officinale) has been used for thousands of years for its medicinal properties. Research suggests it has anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects that may help with certain types of pain.1

Active Compounds

  • Gingerols (main bioactive)
  • Shogaols
  • Paradols
  • Zingerone

How It May Work

  • Inhibits prostaglandin synthesis (like NSAIDs)
  • Reduces inflammatory cytokines
  • Antioxidant effects
  • May affect pain receptors

Evidence2

Promising Evidence

  • Osteoarthritis pain - multiple positive studies
  • Muscle soreness after exercise
  • Menstrual pain

Less Clear

  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • General chronic pain
  • Fibromyalgia

How to Use3

  • Fresh ginger - In cooking, tea
  • Dried ginger powder - 250mg-1g 2-4 times daily
  • Ginger extract supplements
  • Ginger tea - 1-2 cups daily
  • Topical ginger - Compresses, oils

Safety

  • Generally safe in food amounts
  • May cause mild GI upset in some
  • Caution with blood thinners
  • Caution before surgery
  • Consult provider if pregnant

Practical Tips

  • Add fresh ginger to smoothies, stir-fries
  • Make ginger tea with sliced fresh root
  • Try crystallized ginger as snack
  • Consistent use may be needed for effects
