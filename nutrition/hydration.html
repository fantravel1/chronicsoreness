Hydration

The role of water in pain management

Why Hydration Matters for Pain

Adequate hydration is essential for overall health and may play a role in pain management. Dehydration can contribute to headaches, muscle cramps, and joint discomfort. While not a cure for chronic pain, proper hydration supports the body's healing processes.1

Dehydration and Pain2

  • Can trigger or worsen headaches
  • May increase muscle tension and cramps
  • Affects joint lubrication
  • Reduces blood flow to tissues
  • Can increase pain sensitivity
  • Affects medication absorption

Signs of Dehydration

  • Dark urine
  • Thirst (late sign)
  • Fatigue
  • Headache
  • Dizziness
  • Dry mouth and skin
  • Muscle cramps

How Much Water?3

  • General guideline: 8 cups (64 oz) daily
  • Individual needs vary
  • More needed with exercise, heat, illness
  • Urine color is a good indicator
  • Pale yellow = well hydrated

Tips for Staying Hydrated

  • Keep water bottle with you
  • Drink before you're thirsty
  • Set reminders if needed
  • Drink water with medications
  • Eat water-rich foods
  • Limit caffeine and alcohol

Water-Rich Foods

  • Watermelon
  • Cucumbers
  • Oranges
  • Strawberries
  • Lettuce
  • Celery
  • Soups and broths

Special Considerations

  • Some medications increase fluid needs
  • Certain conditions require fluid limits
  • Check with doctor if you have kidney or heart issues
  • Balance hydration with bladder conditions

Beyond Water

  • Herbal tea counts
  • Milk and juice contribute
  • Coffee/tea have mild diuretic effect
  • Limit sugary drinks
