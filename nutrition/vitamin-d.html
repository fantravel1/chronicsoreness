Vitamin D and Pain

The sunshine vitamin's role in chronic pain

Vitamin D and Pain Connection

Vitamin D deficiency is common in people with chronic pain, and research suggests a connection between low vitamin D levels and various pain conditions. While the relationship is complex and not fully understood, ensuring adequate vitamin D may be one component of pain management.1

Why It Matters

  • Vitamin D receptors in nervous system and muscles
  • Involved in inflammation regulation
  • Important for bone health
  • Affects muscle function
  • Deficiency very common worldwide

Pain Conditions Linked to Deficiency2

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic widespread pain
  • Chronic back pain
  • Musculoskeletal pain
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Diabetic neuropathy

Risk Factors for Deficiency

  • Limited sun exposure
  • Dark skin
  • Older age
  • Obesity
  • Malabsorption conditions
  • Northern latitudes
  • Chronic illness

Testing3

  • 25-hydroxyvitamin D blood test
  • Deficiency: Below 20 ng/mL
  • Insufficiency: 20-29 ng/mL
  • Sufficient: 30+ ng/mL
  • Some experts suggest higher optimal levels

Sources of Vitamin D

Sunlight

  • Primary natural source
  • 10-30 minutes midday sun (varies)
  • Skin produces vitamin D from UVB rays
  • Difficult in northern latitudes in winter

Food Sources

  • Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel)
  • Egg yolks
  • Fortified foods (milk, cereal)
  • Mushrooms (UV-exposed)

Supplements

  • Vitamin D3 (cholecalciferol) preferred
  • Typical doses: 1000-4000 IU daily
  • Higher doses may be needed for deficiency
  • Follow provider recommendations

Evidence for Pain3

  • Correcting deficiency may help pain
  • Results mixed in studies
  • Not a cure-all for pain
  • Best evidence when deficiency present
  • Worth testing if risk factors present

Cautions

  • Very high doses can be toxic
  • Can affect calcium levels
  • May interact with some medications
  • Work with healthcare provider
