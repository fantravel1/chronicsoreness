Vitamin D

The sunshine vitamin and its role in pain

Vitamin D and Pain

Vitamin D deficiency is common and has been associated with chronic pain conditions. While the relationship isn't fully understood, maintaining adequate vitamin D levels may be important for musculoskeletal health and pain management.1

Functions Relevant to Pain

  • Bone health and calcium absorption
  • Muscle function
  • Immune system regulation
  • Anti-inflammatory effects
  • Nervous system function

Deficiency Symptoms2

  • Bone pain
  • Muscle weakness and aches
  • Fatigue
  • Generalized pain
  • Mood changes

Risk Factors for Deficiency

  • Limited sun exposure
  • Darker skin
  • Older age
  • Obesity
  • Malabsorption conditions
  • Living at higher latitudes

Pain Conditions Linked to Low Vitamin D3

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic low back pain
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Chronic widespread pain

Testing and Levels

  • Blood test: 25-hydroxyvitamin D
  • Deficient: <20 ng/mL
  • Insufficient: 20-29 ng/mL
  • Sufficient: 30-100 ng/mL

Supplementation

  • RDA: 600-800 IU/day (adults)
  • Higher doses may be needed if deficient
  • Vitamin D3 (cholecalciferol) preferred
  • Take with fat-containing meal
  • Recheck levels after supplementing

Food Sources

  • Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel)
  • Fortified milk and orange juice
  • Egg yolks
  • Fortified cereals
  • Mushrooms (UV-exposed)
