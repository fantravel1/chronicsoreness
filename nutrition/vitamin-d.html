Vitamin D

The sunshine vitamin and chronic pain

Vitamin D and Pain

Vitamin D deficiency is common and has been associated with various chronic pain conditions. While it's essential for bone health, research suggests it may also play roles in muscle function, inflammation, and nerve health.1

Who's at Risk for Deficiency

  • Limited sun exposure
  • Darker skin
  • Older adults
  • Obesity
  • Malabsorption conditions
  • Living at high latitudes
  • Always wearing sunscreen

Conditions Linked to Low Vitamin D2

  • Osteomalacia (bone softening)
  • Muscle pain and weakness
  • Chronic widespread pain
  • Fibromyalgia (association unclear)
  • Inflammatory conditions

Testing

  • Blood test: 25-hydroxyvitamin D
  • Deficient: <20 ng/mL
  • Insufficient: 20-29 ng/mL
  • Sufficient: 30+ ng/mL
  • Many experts suggest 40-60 ng/mL

Sources

Sun Exposure

  • 10-30 minutes midday sun
  • Depends on skin tone, location

Food Sources3

  • Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel)
  • Fortified milk and orange juice
  • Egg yolks
  • Mushrooms exposed to UV light

Supplements

  • Vitamin D3 (cholecalciferol) preferred
  • Typical dose: 1,000-4,000 IU daily
  • Higher doses may be needed if deficient
  • Take with fatty food for absorption

Cautions

  • Too much can be toxic
  • Test before high-dose supplementation
  • May interact with certain medications
