Glucosamine and Chondroitin

Popular joint supplements examined

What Are These Supplements?

Glucosamine and chondroitin are substances naturally found in cartilage. They're commonly taken as supplements for osteoarthritis, with the theory that they help maintain or rebuild cartilage. They're often sold together.1

Types of Glucosamine

  • Glucosamine sulfate - Most studied
  • Glucosamine hydrochloride - More concentrated
  • N-acetyl glucosamine - Different form
  • Sulfate form generally preferred

What the Evidence Shows2

  • Mixed results in studies
  • Some positive trials (mostly glucosamine sulfate)
  • Large NIH study showed modest benefit for subset
  • May help some people modestly
  • Unlikely to rebuild cartilage
  • Generally safe to try

Dosing

  • Glucosamine: 1500 mg/day (can divide doses)
  • Chondroitin: 800-1200 mg/day
  • Often sold in combination products
  • May take 2-4 months to see effects

Safety3

  • Generally well tolerated
  • Mild GI upset possible
  • Usually derived from shellfish (allergies)
  • May interact with blood thinners
  • May affect blood sugar (diabetics monitor)

Who Might Consider

  • Mild to moderate osteoarthritis
  • Want to try before other treatments
  • Looking for lower-risk options
  • Willing to give 3+ months trial

Bottom Line

Evidence is mixed but they're generally safe. Some people report benefit. Consider a 3-month trial if interested.
