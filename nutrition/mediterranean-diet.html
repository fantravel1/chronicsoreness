Mediterranean Diet

An evidence-based anti-inflammatory eating pattern

What Is the Mediterranean Diet?

The Mediterranean diet is an eating pattern inspired by the traditional foods of countries bordering the Mediterranean Sea. It emphasizes plant foods, healthy fats, and moderate amounts of fish and poultry. It's one of the most studied diets for health benefits.1

Key Components2

Eat Daily

  • Vegetables (at every meal)
  • Fruits
  • Whole grains
  • Olive oil (main fat source)
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Herbs and spices

Eat Often (Weekly)

  • Fish and seafood (2-3 times)
  • Legumes
  • Poultry
  • Eggs
  • Cheese and yogurt

Limit

  • Red meat
  • Sweets
  • Processed foods
  • Refined grains

Evidence for Pain3

  • Associated with lower inflammation
  • May reduce arthritis symptoms
  • Supports healthy weight
  • Benefits cardiovascular health
  • High in anti-inflammatory compounds

Getting Started

  • Switch to olive oil for cooking
  • Add more vegetables to meals
  • Choose fish over red meat
  • Snack on nuts and fruits
  • Use herbs instead of salt
  • Gradual changes are fine

Sample Day

  • Breakfast: Greek yogurt with nuts and berries
  • Lunch: Salad with olive oil, chickpeas, vegetables
  • Dinner: Grilled fish, roasted vegetables, whole grain
  • Snacks: Fresh fruit, handful of almonds
