Gut Health and Pain

The gut-pain connection

The Gut-Brain-Pain Axis

Research increasingly shows connections between gut health and chronic pain. The gut microbiome (trillions of bacteria in your intestines) communicates with the brain and immune system, potentially influencing pain and inflammation. While research is still emerging, supporting gut health may be one component of pain management.1

How Gut Health May Affect Pain2

  • Gut bacteria influence inflammation
  • Microbiome affects immune function
  • Gut produces neurotransmitters (like serotonin)
  • Vagus nerve connects gut and brain
  • Leaky gut may increase systemic inflammation

Conditions with Gut Links

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • IBS with chronic pain
  • Chronic fatigue syndrome
  • Some neuropathic conditions

Supporting Gut Health3

Dietary Approaches

  • Eat variety of plant foods
  • Include fiber-rich foods
  • Fermented foods (yogurt, kefir, sauerkraut)
  • Limit processed foods
  • Reduce added sugars

Prebiotic Foods

  • Fiber that feeds good bacteria
  • Onions, garlic, leeks
  • Bananas, asparagus
  • Whole grains
  • Legumes

Probiotic Foods

  • Contain live beneficial bacteria
  • Yogurt with live cultures
  • Kefir
  • Sauerkraut, kimchi
  • Miso, tempeh

What Can Harm Gut Health

  • Prolonged antibiotic use
  • Chronic stress
  • Low-fiber diet
  • Excessive alcohol
  • Some medications (long-term)

Realistic Expectations

  • Research is still emerging
  • Not a cure for chronic pain
  • One piece of the puzzle
  • Healthy gut habits benefit overall health
  • Changes take time

When to See a Doctor

  • Significant GI symptoms
  • Suspected food intolerances
  • Before major dietary changes
  • If considering supplements
