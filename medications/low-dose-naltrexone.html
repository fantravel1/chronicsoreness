Low-Dose Naltrexone (LDN)

An emerging off-label treatment

What Is Low-Dose Naltrexone?

Naltrexone is an opioid antagonist FDA-approved for addiction treatment at 50mg. At much lower doses (1-5mg), it may have immunomodulatory and anti-inflammatory effects. LDN is used off-label for various chronic pain conditions.1

How It May Work2

  • Brief opioid receptor blockade
  • Rebound increase in endorphins
  • May reduce microglial activation
  • Anti-inflammatory effects
  • Exact mechanism unclear

Conditions Being Studied

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic pain syndromes
  • Autoimmune conditions (Crohn's, MS)
  • Complex regional pain syndrome

Dosing

  • Usually 1.5-4.5mg at bedtime
  • Start low (0.5-1mg)
  • Increase gradually
  • Compounded from pharmacies

Current Evidence3

  • Promising but limited studies
  • Best evidence for fibromyalgia
  • Small trials show benefit
  • Larger studies needed
  • Generally well tolerated

Side Effects

  • Vivid dreams (common initially)
  • Sleep disturbance
  • Headache
  • Nausea
  • Usually mild and temporary

Important Considerations

  • Cannot use with opioid medications
  • Not FDA-approved for pain
  • May not be covered by insurance
  • Requires compounding pharmacy
