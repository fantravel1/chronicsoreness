CGRP Medications

New targeted treatments for migraines

What Is CGRP?

Calcitonin gene-related peptide (CGRP) is a protein that plays a key role in migraine. During migraine attacks, CGRP is released and causes blood vessel dilation and inflammation. Blocking CGRP can prevent and treat migraines.1

Types of CGRP Medications

Monoclonal Antibodies (Prevention)2

  • Erenumab (Aimovig) - Monthly injection
  • Fremanezumab (Ajovy) - Monthly or quarterly
  • Galcanezumab (Emgality) - Monthly injection
  • Eptinezumab (Vyepti) - IV infusion quarterly

Gepants (Acute and Prevention)

  • Ubrogepant (Ubrelvy) - Acute treatment
  • Rimegepant (Nurtec) - Acute and prevention
  • Atogepant (Qulipta) - Prevention

Benefits3

  • Designed specifically for migraine
  • Good efficacy in clinical trials
  • Fewer side effects than many older preventives
  • No cardiovascular contraindications
  • Option for patients who failed other treatments

Side Effects

Monoclonal Antibodies

  • Injection site reactions
  • Constipation (most common)
  • Generally well tolerated

Gepants

  • Nausea
  • Drowsiness
  • Generally mild

Who May Benefit

  • 4+ migraine days per month
  • Failed or can't tolerate other preventives
  • Contraindications to other options
  • Chronic migraine
