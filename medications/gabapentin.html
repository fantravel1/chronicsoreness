Gabapentin (Neurontin)

A first-line treatment for nerve pain

What Is Gabapentin?

Gabapentin is an anticonvulsant medication widely used for chronic pain, especially neuropathic pain. While originally developed for epilepsy, it's now one of the most commonly prescribed medications for various pain conditions.1

FDA-Approved Uses

  • Postherpetic neuralgia (after shingles)
  • Epilepsy (adjunct)
  • Restless legs syndrome (Horizant)

Common Off-Label Uses2

  • Diabetic neuropathy
  • Other neuropathic pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic back pain
  • Migraine prevention

Dosing

  • Start low: 100-300 mg at bedtime
  • Titrate slowly: Increase every 3-7 days
  • Typical range: 900-3600 mg/day in divided doses
  • Max: 3600 mg/day
  • Reduce dose if kidney problems

Side Effects3

  • Dizziness
  • Drowsiness/fatigue
  • Peripheral edema (swelling)
  • Weight gain
  • Cognitive effects ("foggy")
  • Coordination problems

Important Information

  • Takes weeks to reach full effect
  • Must taper off slowly
  • Some potential for misuse
  • Interacts with some medications
  • May worsen respiratory depression with opioids

Tips for Use

  • Take at consistent times
  • Start at bedtime (helps with drowsiness)
  • Be patient with titration
  • Report significant side effects
