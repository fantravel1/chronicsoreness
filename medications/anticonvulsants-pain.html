Anticonvulsants for Pain

Anti-seizure medications that help nerve pain

How They Work

Anticonvulsants reduce nerve pain by stabilizing overactive nerve signals. They modulate calcium and sodium channels in nerves, reducing abnormal electrical activity that causes neuropathic pain.1

Common Medications

Gabapentin (Neurontin)

  • First-line for many neuropathic conditions
  • FDA approved for postherpetic neuralgia
  • Start low, increase gradually
  • Typical dose: 900-3600 mg/day in divided doses

Pregabalin (Lyrica)

  • Similar to gabapentin but more predictable absorption
  • FDA approved for fibromyalgia, diabetic neuropathy
  • Twice daily dosing
  • Schedule V controlled substance

Carbamazepine (Tegretol)2

  • First-line for trigeminal neuralgia
  • Requires blood monitoring
  • Many drug interactions

Oxcarbazepine (Trileptal)

  • Similar to carbamazepine, fewer interactions
  • Used for trigeminal neuralgia

Common Side Effects3

  • Drowsiness, dizziness
  • Weight gain
  • Peripheral edema
  • Cognitive effects ("brain fog")
  • Blurred vision
