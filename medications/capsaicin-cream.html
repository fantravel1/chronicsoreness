Capsaicin Cream

Pain relief from chili peppers

What Is Capsaicin?

Capsaicin is the compound that makes chili peppers hot. When applied topically, it can help reduce pain by affecting pain-signaling nerve fibers in the skin. Available in creams, gels, and patches, capsaicin is used for various chronic pain conditions. It works differently than other pain relievers.1

How It Works2

  • Binds to TRPV1 receptors on nerve fibers
  • Initially causes burning sensation
  • With repeated use, depletes substance P
  • Desensitizes pain fibers over time
  • Reduces pain signal transmission

Available Forms

OTC Creams (Low Concentration)

  • 0.025% - 0.1% capsaicin
  • Applied 3-4 times daily
  • Several weeks for full effect

Prescription Patch (High Concentration)

  • 8% capsaicin (Qutenza)
  • Applied by healthcare provider
  • Single application lasts weeks/months
  • For postherpetic neuralgia

Conditions Treated

  • Postherpetic neuralgia (shingles pain)
  • Diabetic neuropathy
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Muscle pain
  • Back pain

How to Use3

  • Apply thin layer to affected area
  • Wash hands thoroughly after
  • Keep away from eyes and mucous membranes
  • Use consistently for best results
  • May take 2-4 weeks for full effect

The Burning Sensation

  • Burning, stinging is expected initially
  • Usually decreases with continued use
  • Part of how it works
  • Start with lower concentration
  • Most people tolerate it

Tips for Tolerating

  • Start with less frequent application
  • Use small amount
  • Apply to smaller area first
  • Expect 1-2 weeks of burning
  • Don't apply to broken skin

Side Effects

  • Burning, stinging (most common)
  • Redness at application site
  • Usually local effects only
  • Rarely causes allergic reaction

Precautions

  • Avoid eyes, nose, mouth
  • Don't bandage tightly over it
  • Don't use with heating pad
  • Wash hands well (or use gloves)
  • Don't use on broken skin
