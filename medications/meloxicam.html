Meloxicam (Mobic)

Prescription NSAID for arthritis and pain

What Is Meloxicam?

Meloxicam is a prescription nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) commonly used for arthritis and chronic pain conditions. It works by reducing inflammation and is taken once daily, making it convenient for long-term use. Brand names include Mobic and Vivlodex.1

How It Works2

  • Inhibits COX-2 enzyme (preferentially)
  • Reduces prostaglandin production
  • Decreases inflammation
  • Relieves pain and swelling
  • Long half-life allows once-daily dosing

Uses

  • Osteoarthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Juvenile rheumatoid arthritis
  • Chronic pain conditions
  • Ankylosing spondylitis

Dosing

  • Typical dose: 7.5-15 mg once daily
  • Start with lowest effective dose
  • Can take with or without food
  • Maximum: 15 mg/day
  • Lower doses for elderly

Common Side Effects3

  • Stomach upset, nausea
  • Diarrhea or constipation
  • Dizziness
  • Headache
  • Fluid retention
  • Elevated blood pressure

Serious Risks

  • GI bleeding and ulcers
  • Cardiovascular events (heart attack, stroke)
  • Kidney problems
  • Liver damage (rare)
  • Allergic reactions

Who Should Not Take It

  • Allergy to NSAIDs or aspirin
  • History of GI bleeding
  • Severe kidney or liver disease
  • Before or after heart surgery
  • Third trimester of pregnancy

Drug Interactions

  • Blood thinners (increased bleeding)
  • ACE inhibitors (reduced effectiveness)
  • Diuretics (kidney risk)
  • Lithium (increased levels)
  • Methotrexate (increased toxicity)
  • Other NSAIDs (avoid combining)

Safety Tips

  • Use lowest dose for shortest time
  • Take with food if stomach upset
  • Stay hydrated
  • Report black stools or stomach pain
  • Regular monitoring if long-term use
