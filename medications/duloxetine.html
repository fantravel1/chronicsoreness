Duloxetine (Cymbalta)

An SNRI for pain and depression

What Is Duloxetine?

Duloxetine is a serotonin-norepinephrine reuptake inhibitor (SNRI) that's FDA-approved for several chronic pain conditions as well as depression and anxiety. It affects neurotransmitters involved in both mood and pain perception.1

FDA-Approved Pain Uses

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Diabetic peripheral neuropathy
  • Chronic musculoskeletal pain
  • Also: depression, anxiety

How It Works2

  • Increases serotonin and norepinephrine
  • Enhances descending pain inhibition
  • Pain relief independent of mood effects
  • Takes 2-4 weeks to see benefit

Dosing

  • Start: 30 mg once daily
  • Target: 60 mg once daily
  • Maximum: 120 mg daily (some conditions)
  • Take at same time each day
  • Can take with or without food

Common Side Effects3

  • Nausea (often improves)
  • Dry mouth
  • Constipation
  • Fatigue or drowsiness
  • Dizziness
  • Decreased appetite
  • Sweating
  • Sexual dysfunction

Important Warnings

  • Must taper off slowly (discontinuation syndrome)
  • Black box warning for suicidal thoughts in young adults
  • Avoid with MAOIs
  • Caution with liver disease
  • May increase blood pressure
