Carbamazepine

First-line treatment for trigeminal neuralgia

What Is Carbamazepine?

Carbamazepine is an anticonvulsant medication that's the first-line treatment for trigeminal neuralgia (TN), a condition causing severe facial pain. It works by stabilizing nerve membranes and reducing abnormal electrical activity. Brand names include Tegretol and Carbatrol.1

How It Works2

  • Blocks sodium channels
  • Stabilizes nerve cell membranes
  • Reduces nerve firing
  • Prevents pain signal transmission
  • Most effective for lancinating pain

Primary Pain Uses

  • Trigeminal neuralgia (gold standard)
  • Glossopharyngeal neuralgia
  • Other paroxysmal nerve pain

Other Uses

  • Epilepsy
  • Bipolar disorder
  • Some other neuropathic pain

Dosing3

  • Start low: 100-200 mg twice daily
  • Increase slowly over weeks
  • Typical maintenance: 400-1200 mg/day
  • Extended-release available
  • Blood level monitoring recommended

Common Side Effects

  • Dizziness
  • Drowsiness
  • Nausea
  • Unsteadiness
  • Double vision
  • Headache
  • Often improve with time

Serious Risks

  • Severe skin reactions (SJS/TEN)
  • Bone marrow suppression
  • Liver problems
  • Low sodium (especially elderly)
  • Heart rhythm changes

Important Precautions

  • Genetic testing recommended for some
  • HLA-B*1502 increases skin reaction risk
  • Regular blood monitoring needed
  • Many drug interactions
  • Induces its own metabolism
  • Affects hormonal birth control

Monitoring Requirements

  • Complete blood count
  • Liver function tests
  • Sodium levels
  • Drug levels (sometimes)
  • More frequent initially

Effectiveness for TN

  • 70-80% get significant relief
  • Response helps confirm diagnosis
  • May need dose adjustments over time
  • Some develop tolerance

Alternatives

  • Oxcarbazepine (often better tolerated)
  • Baclofen (can combine)
  • Lamotrigine
  • Surgical options for refractory cases
