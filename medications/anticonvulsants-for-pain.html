Gabapentin & Pregabalin

Anticonvulsant medications used for fibromyalgia, neuropathic pain, and nerve-related pain conditions

Medical Disclaimer: This information is for educational purposes only. Always consult your healthcare provider before starting, stopping, or changing medications. Never adjust doses without medical guidance.

Overview

Gabapentin (Neurontin) and pregabalin (Lyrica) are anticonvulsant medications originally developed for epilepsy but now widely used for chronic pain. They work by calming overactive nerve signals.

Pregabalin (Lyrica)

FDA-Approved for:

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Diabetic neuropathy
  • Post-herpetic neuralgia (shingles pain)
  • Spinal cord injury pain

Gabapentin (Neurontin)

FDA-Approved for:

  • Post-herpetic neuralgia
  • Epilepsy (adjunctive)
  • Often used off-label for fibromyalgia, various pain conditions

How They Work

  • Bind to calcium channels in the nervous system
  • Reduce release of excitatory neurotransmitters
  • Calm "overactive" nerves sending pain signals
  • May also improve sleep (helps pain indirectly)

Typical Dosing

Titration is Critical

Both medications require slow titration (gradual dose increase) to minimize side effects. Starting at full dose causes significant side effects. Your provider should start low and increase slowly over weeks.

Pregabalin (Lyrica)

  • Starting dose: 75 mg twice daily (or 50 mg three times daily)
  • Titration: Increase every 1-2 weeks as tolerated
  • Fibromyalgia dose: 150-225 mg twice daily (300-450 mg/day)
  • Maximum: 600 mg/day
  • Dosing: Twice or three times daily

Gabapentin (Neurontin)

  • Starting dose: 100-300 mg at bedtime (or 100 mg three times daily)
  • Titration: Increase by 100-300 mg every 3-7 days
  • Typical effective dose: 1800-3600 mg/day
  • Maximum: 3600 mg/day
  • Dosing: Three times daily (uneven absorption at high doses)

Common Side Effects

Most side effects improve as your body adjusts (usually within 2-4 weeks):

Very Common

  • Drowsiness/sedation
  • Dizziness
  • Fatigue
  • Brain fog/"drugged" feeling

Common

  • Weight gain
  • Peripheral edema (swelling)
  • Dry mouth
  • Constipation
  • Blurred vision

Less Common

  • Mood changes
  • Coordination problems
  • Memory issues
  • Tremor

Managing Side Effects

  • Drowsiness: Take larger dose at bedtime; consider slow titration
  • Weight gain: Monitor diet and activity; discuss alternatives if significant
  • Dizziness: Rise slowly from sitting/lying; usually improves with time
  • Brain fog: Often improves after 2-4 weeks; may improve with slower titration
  • Swelling: Elevate legs; discuss with provider if significant

Important Considerations

Kidney Function

Both medications are excreted by the kidneys. Doses must be reduced in kidney disease. Your provider should check kidney function.

Pregnancy and Breastfeeding

Important for Women of Childbearing Age

  • Pregabalin: Category C—animal studies show risk; limited human data
  • Gabapentin: Category C—similar concerns
  • Breastfeeding: Both pass into breast milk
  • Planning pregnancy: Discuss with provider well in advance

Interactions

  • CNS depressants: Increased sedation with alcohol, opioids, benzodiazepines
  • Antacids: May reduce gabapentin absorption
  • Opioids: FDA warning about increased risk of respiratory depression

Discontinuation

Do Not Stop Suddenly

Both medications require gradual tapering over at least 1 week (longer if high doses). Abrupt discontinuation can cause withdrawal symptoms including anxiety, insomnia, nausea, pain, and sweating. Pregabalin may also cause seizures if stopped abruptly in some patients.

What to Expect

Timeline

  • Week 1-2: Starting dose; mainly adjusting to side effects
  • Week 2-4: Gradual dose increases; some may notice benefit
  • Week 4-8: Reaching therapeutic dose; pain relief should become clearer
  • Week 8-12: Full effect assessment

Realistic Expectations

  • Not everyone responds—about 30-50% get significant benefit
  • Usually partial relief (30-50% pain reduction), not complete
  • Benefits include improved sleep and reduced anxiety for many
  • May take 6-12 weeks to fully assess effectiveness
  • If no benefit after adequate trial, discuss alternatives with provider

Pregabalin vs Gabapentin

Feature Pregabalin Gabapentin
FDA approval for fibromyalgia Yes No (off-label)
Dosing frequency 2x daily 3x daily
Absorption Linear, predictable Non-linear (decreases at high doses)
Generic available Yes Yes (longer)
Cost Higher Lower
Controlled substance Schedule V Varies by state

  • How long should I try this before deciding if it works?
  • What side effects should prompt me to call you?
  • How does this interact with my other medications?
