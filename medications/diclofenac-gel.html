Diclofenac Gel

Topical anti-inflammatory for joint pain

What Is Diclofenac Gel?

Diclofenac gel is a topical nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) applied directly to the skin over painful joints or muscles. Brand names include Voltaren and Pennsaid. Now available over-the-counter in some formulations, it provides local pain relief with fewer systemic side effects than oral NSAIDs.1

How It Works2

  • Inhibits COX enzymes
  • Reduces prostaglandins locally
  • Decreases inflammation at site
  • Penetrates through skin to underlying tissue
  • Lower blood levels than oral form

Approved Uses

  • Osteoarthritis of hands, knees, feet
  • Acute pain from strains/sprains
  • Superficial joint pain

How to Use3

  • Apply to clean, dry skin
  • Use measured dose (varies by product)
  • Gently rub in
  • Wash hands after (unless treating hands)
  • Don't cover with bandages
  • Apply 2-4 times daily

Benefits Over Oral NSAIDs

  • Lower systemic absorption
  • Less GI side effects
  • Targeted to affected area
  • May be safer for some patients
  • Can use with oral NSAIDs (caution)

Effectiveness

  • Works best for superficial joints
  • Hands, knees respond well
  • May not reach deep joints (hip)
  • Evidence supports use for OA
  • May take 1-2 weeks for full effect

Side Effects

Local

  • Skin irritation
  • Dryness or redness
  • Itching at application site

Systemic (Less Common)

  • GI upset possible
  • Blood pressure effects
  • Lower risk than oral NSAIDs

Precautions

  • Don't apply to broken skin
  • Avoid sun exposure on treated area
  • Don't use with other topical products
  • NSAID allergy caution
  • Kidney or heart disease (discuss with doctor)
  • Avoid near eyes and mucous membranes

Drug Interactions

  • Other NSAIDs (additive effects)
  • Blood thinners
  • Some blood pressure medications
  • Lithium
  • Review with pharmacist

OTC vs. Prescription

  • 1% gel now OTC
  • Higher strengths by prescription
  • Solution formulation prescription only
  • Same active ingredient
