Methocarbamol

Muscle relaxant for spasms and strains

What Is Methocarbamol?

Methocarbamol is a muscle relaxant used to treat muscle spasms and associated pain. Brand names include Robaxin. It's available both over-the-counter and by prescription (higher doses). It works centrally in the nervous system and is considered one of the safer muscle relaxants with less sedation and abuse potential.1

How It Works2

  • Central nervous system depressant
  • Exact mechanism not fully known
  • May act on brain stem
  • Reduces muscle hyperactivity
  • General CNS sedation contributes

Uses

  • Acute muscle spasms
  • Low back pain with spasm
  • Neck pain with spasm
  • Muscle strains
  • Usually short-term use

Dosing3

Prescription

  • 1500 mg four times daily initially
  • May reduce after first 2-3 days
  • Maintenance: 750-1000 mg as needed

OTC

  • Lower doses available
  • Follow package directions

Advantages

  • Less sedating than some alternatives
  • Lower abuse potential
  • Available OTC (some formulations)
  • Relatively safe profile
  • Short-term use generally well tolerated

Side Effects

  • Drowsiness
  • Dizziness
  • Lightheadedness
  • Nausea
  • Blurred vision
  • Headache
  • May cause urine discoloration (harmless)

Precautions

  • May impair driving/machinery
  • Avoid alcohol
  • Caution in elderly
  • Caution with kidney disease
  • Not for myasthenia gravis
  • Contains propylene glycol in IV form

Duration of Use

  • Generally for acute episodes
  • Usually 2-3 weeks maximum
  • Not typically for chronic use
  • Part of comprehensive approach
  • Combine with PT, stretching

Compared to Other Muscle Relaxants

  • Less sedating than cyclobenzaprine
  • Lower abuse risk than carisoprodol
  • OTC availability advantage
  • May be less effective for some
