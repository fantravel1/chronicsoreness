Muscle Relaxants

Medications for muscle spasm and tension

What Are Muscle Relaxants?

Muscle relaxants are a group of medications used to treat muscle spasms, spasticity, and associated pain. They work through various mechanisms in the central nervous system rather than directly on muscles.1

Types of Muscle Relaxants

Antispasmodics (for muscle spasm)

  • Cyclobenzaprine (Flexeril) - Most commonly prescribed
  • Methocarbamol (Robaxin)
  • Carisoprodol (Soma) - Higher abuse potential
  • Metaxalone (Skelaxin)
  • Orphenadrine (Norflex)

Antispastics (for spasticity)2

  • Baclofen - GABA-B agonist
  • Tizanidine (Zanaflex) - Alpha-2 agonist
  • Dantrolene - Acts on muscle directly

Common Uses

  • Acute muscle spasm (back pain, neck pain)
  • Spasticity (MS, spinal cord injury, stroke)
  • Fibromyalgia (cyclobenzaprine)
  • Tension headaches

Side Effects

  • Drowsiness (most common)
  • Dizziness
  • Dry mouth
  • Cognitive impairment
  • Dependence risk with some

Important Considerations3

  • Usually for short-term use (2-3 weeks)
  • Take at bedtime to minimize daytime drowsiness
  • Avoid alcohol
  • Use caution in elderly
  • Don't stop baclofen abruptly (withdrawal risk)
  • Limited evidence for long-term use
