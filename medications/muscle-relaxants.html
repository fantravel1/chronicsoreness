Muscle Relaxants

Medications for muscle spasm and tension

What Are Muscle Relaxants?

Muscle relaxants are medications used to reduce muscle spasm and associated pain. They work in the central nervous system rather than directly on muscles. They're often used for acute back pain and muscle injuries.1

Common Types2

Cyclobenzaprine (Flexeril)

  • Most commonly prescribed
  • Related to tricyclic antidepressants
  • Causes significant drowsiness
  • Usually short-term use (2-3 weeks)

Methocarbamol (Robaxin)

  • Less sedating for some
  • Can be used longer-term
  • Available OTC in some countries

Tizanidine (Zanaflex)

  • Alpha-2 agonist
  • Also used for spasticity
  • Can lower blood pressure

Baclofen (Lioresal)

  • GABA agonist
  • Primarily for spasticity
  • Must taper off slowly

Common Uses

  • Acute back pain
  • Muscle spasms
  • Neck pain
  • Spasticity (certain types)
  • Fibromyalgia (some)

Side Effects3

  • Drowsiness (most common)
  • Dizziness
  • Dry mouth
  • Weakness
  • Fatigue

Important Considerations

  • Can impair driving
  • Avoid with alcohol
  • May cause dependence with long-term use
  • Often more effective at night
  • Not for everyone with chronic pain
