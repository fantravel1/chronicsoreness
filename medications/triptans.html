Triptans

First-line migraine-specific medications

What Are Triptans?

Triptans are a class of medications specifically designed to treat migraines and cluster headaches. They work by activating serotonin (5-HT1B/1D) receptors, causing blood vessel constriction and blocking pain pathways.1

How They Work

  • Constrict dilated blood vessels
  • Inhibit release of inflammatory peptides
  • Block pain signal transmission
  • Work on cause, not just symptoms

Available Triptans2

  • Sumatriptan (Imitrex) - Original, multiple forms
  • Rizatriptan (Maxalt) - Fast-acting
  • Eletriptan (Relpax) - Good efficacy
  • Zolmitriptan (Zomig) - Nasal spray available
  • Naratriptan (Amerge) - Longer-lasting, gentler
  • Frovatriptan (Frova) - Long half-life
  • Almotriptan (Axert) - Fewer side effects

Forms Available

  • Oral tablets
  • Orally disintegrating tablets
  • Nasal spray
  • Injection

Proper Use

  • Take early in migraine attack
  • Can repeat dose after 2 hours if needed
  • Limit to 9-10 days per month
  • Don't use for aura without headache

Side Effects3

  • Chest tightness or pressure
  • Tingling
  • Flushing
  • Drowsiness
  • Neck stiffness

Contraindications

  • Coronary artery disease
  • Uncontrolled hypertension
  • History of stroke/TIA
  • Hemiplegic or basilar migraine
  • Within 24 hours of ergots or other triptans
