Pregabalin (Lyrica)

A medication for neuropathic pain and fibromyalgia

What Is Pregabalin?

Pregabalin (brand name Lyrica) is an anticonvulsant medication FDA-approved for several chronic pain conditions. It's chemically related to gabapentin but has more predictable absorption and can be dosed twice daily.1

FDA-Approved Pain Uses

  • Diabetic peripheral neuropathy
  • Postherpetic neuralgia
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Spinal cord injury-related pain

How It Works

  • Binds to calcium channels in nervous system
  • Reduces release of excitatory neurotransmitters
  • Decreases abnormal nerve signaling

Dosing

  • Starting: 75mg twice daily or 50mg three times daily
  • Typical: 150-300mg daily in divided doses
  • Maximum: 600mg daily
  • Adjust for kidney function

Side Effects2

  • Dizziness and drowsiness
  • Weight gain
  • Peripheral edema (swelling)
  • Blurred vision
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Dry mouth

Warnings

  • Controlled substance (Schedule V)
  • Don't stop suddenly - taper
  • Avoid alcohol
  • May affect driving
  • Monitor for mood changes

Effectiveness3

About 30-50% of people achieve meaningful pain relief. Works best for neuropathic pain conditions.
