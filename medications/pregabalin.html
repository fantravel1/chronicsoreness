Pregabalin (Lyrica)

FDA-approved for multiple pain conditions

What Is Pregabalin?

Pregabalin is similar to gabapentin but with more predictable absorption and faster onset. It's FDA-approved for several chronic pain conditions and is considered a first-line treatment for many types of neuropathic pain.1

FDA-Approved Uses

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Diabetic peripheral neuropathy
  • Postherpetic neuralgia
  • Neuropathic pain from spinal cord injury
  • Epilepsy (adjunct)

Pregabalin vs. Gabapentin2

  • More linear absorption (predictable dosing)
  • Twice daily dosing (vs. 3 times)
  • Faster onset of effect
  • Similar efficacy
  • More expensive (generic now available)
  • Schedule V controlled substance

Dosing

  • Start: 75 mg twice daily or 50 mg three times daily
  • Increase: After 3-7 days based on response
  • Target: 150-300 mg/day
  • Maximum: 600 mg/day
  • Reduce dose if kidney problems

Side Effects3

  • Dizziness
  • Drowsiness
  • Peripheral edema
  • Weight gain
  • Dry mouth
  • Blurred vision
  • Cognitive effects

Important Information

  • Controlled substance (DEA Schedule V)
  • Some abuse potential
  • Taper gradually when stopping
  • May cause angioedema (rare)
