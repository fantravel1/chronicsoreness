Topical NSAIDs

Pain relief applied directly to the skin

What Are Topical NSAIDs?

Topical NSAIDs are anti-inflammatory pain medications applied directly to the skin over a painful area. They provide localized pain relief with fewer systemic side effects than oral NSAIDs. Common examples include diclofenac gel (Voltaren) and topical ibuprofen.1

Available Products

Prescription

  • Diclofenac 1% gel (Voltaren Gel)
  • Diclofenac 1.5% solution (Pennsaid)
  • Diclofenac patch (Flector)
  • Ketoprofen gel

Over-the-Counter

  • Diclofenac 1% gel (Voltaren Arthritis Pain)
  • Various topical ibuprofen products

How They Work2

  • Absorbed through the skin
  • Concentrate in underlying tissues
  • Reduce inflammation locally
  • Lower blood levels than oral NSAIDs

What They Treat

  • Osteoarthritis (especially knees, hands)
  • Muscle strains and sprains
  • Tendinitis
  • Localized musculoskeletal pain

Effectiveness3

  • Good evidence for knee/hand osteoarthritis
  • Similar efficacy to oral NSAIDs for superficial conditions
  • Less effective for deeper joints (hips, spine)
  • May take several days for full effect

Advantages Over Oral NSAIDs

  • Fewer GI side effects
  • Lower cardiovascular risk
  • Minimal kidney effects
  • Can be used alongside other pain medications
  • Good for localized pain

How to Use

  • Apply to clean, dry skin
  • Use the measured dose
  • Rub in gently
  • Wash hands after applying
  • Don't cover with bandages unless directed
  • Avoid sun exposure on treated areas

Side Effects

  • Skin irritation at application site
  • Redness, itching, dryness
  • Rash (discontinue if severe)
  • Systemic effects rare but possible

Precautions

  • Don't apply to broken or irritated skin
  • Avoid eyes and mucous membranes
  • Check for NSAID allergies
  • Use caution if on blood thinners
  • Discuss with doctor if using multiple NSAIDs
