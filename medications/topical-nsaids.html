Topical NSAIDs

Targeted pain relief applied to the skin

What Are Topical NSAIDs?

Topical NSAIDs are anti-inflammatory medications applied directly to the skin over painful areas. They provide localized relief with less systemic absorption than oral NSAIDs, reducing the risk of side effects.1

Available Products

  • Diclofenac gel (Voltaren) - Most studied, OTC available
  • Diclofenac solution - For knee osteoarthritis
  • Diclofenac patch - Extended release
  • Ketoprofen gel
  • Piroxicam gel

Conditions Treated2

  • Osteoarthritis (especially knee, hand)
  • Acute soft tissue injuries
  • Sprains and strains
  • Tendinitis
  • Localized musculoskeletal pain

How to Use

  • Apply to clean, dry skin
  • Use prescribed amount
  • Rub in gently
  • Wash hands after application
  • Don't cover with bandages unless directed
  • Avoid broken or irritated skin
  • Don't apply near eyes or mucous membranes

Benefits3

  • Effective for localized pain
  • Lower systemic levels than oral NSAIDs
  • Reduced GI side effects
  • Good option for older adults
  • Can be used with other medications
  • OTC availability (diclofenac gel)

Side Effects

Common

  • Skin irritation at application site
  • Rash or itching
  • Dryness

Less Common

  • Systemic NSAID effects (with extensive use)
  • Sun sensitivity

Precautions

  • Avoid sun exposure to treated areas
  • Not for deep tissue or widespread pain
  • Caution with blood thinners
  • Don't use before or after heart surgery
  • Talk to doctor if pregnant or breastfeeding
