Ketamine for Pain

An NMDA receptor antagonist for severe pain

What Is Ketamine?

Ketamine is an anesthetic medication that, at sub-anesthetic doses, is being used to treat certain chronic pain conditions. It works differently than opioids and other pain medications, blocking NMDA receptors involved in pain signaling and central sensitization. It's typically reserved for refractory cases.1

How It Works2

  • NMDA receptor antagonist
  • Blocks pain pathway signaling
  • May reduce central sensitization
  • Has antidepressant effects
  • May "reset" pain processing

Conditions That May Respond

  • Complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS)
  • Neuropathic pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Refractory chronic pain
  • Cancer pain
  • Post-surgical pain

Delivery Methods3

IV Infusions

  • Most common for pain
  • Done in clinic setting
  • Duration varies (hours to days)
  • Monitored closely

Other Routes

  • Intranasal (Spravato for depression)
  • Oral or sublingual
  • Topical creams
  • Research ongoing on best methods

What to Expect

  • Monitored in clinical setting
  • May feel dissociative effects
  • Dizziness, nausea possible
  • Cannot drive after treatment
  • Effects may take hours to days
  • May need multiple treatments

Side Effects

  • Dissociation ("out of body")
  • Dizziness
  • Nausea
  • Elevated blood pressure
  • Visual disturbances
  • Fatigue

Risks and Concerns

  • Potential for abuse
  • Bladder problems with chronic use
  • Psychological effects
  • Blood pressure elevation
  • Not for everyone

Who Should Avoid

  • Uncontrolled hypertension
  • History of psychosis
  • Pregnancy
  • Certain heart conditions
  • Substance abuse history (caution)

Finding Treatment

  • Specialized pain clinics
  • Some anesthesiology practices
  • Growing availability
  • Usually not insurance covered
