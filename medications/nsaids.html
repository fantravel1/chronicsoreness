NSAIDs

Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs for pain

What Are NSAIDs?

Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) are medications that reduce pain, inflammation, and fever. They work by blocking cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes that produce prostaglandins, chemicals involved in inflammation and pain.1

Common NSAIDs

Over-the-Counter

  • Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin)
  • Naproxen (Aleve)
  • Aspirin

Prescription

  • Celecoxib (Celebrex) - COX-2 selective
  • Meloxicam (Mobic)
  • Diclofenac (Voltaren)
  • Indomethacin
  • Ketorolac (short-term only)

Effective For2

  • Osteoarthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Ankylosing spondylitis
  • Acute musculoskeletal pain
  • Menstrual cramps
  • Headaches
  • Post-operative pain

Risks and Side Effects3

Gastrointestinal

  • Stomach ulcers and bleeding
  • Dyspepsia, nausea
  • Risk increases with age, history, alcohol use

Cardiovascular

  • Increased risk of heart attack, stroke
  • Higher with longer use and higher doses
  • Naproxen may have lower CV risk

Kidney

  • Can worsen kidney function
  • Fluid retention
  • Caution with existing kidney disease

Safe Use Tips

  • Use lowest effective dose
  • Use for shortest time needed
  • Take with food
  • Consider GI protection (PPI) if high risk
  • Avoid if history of GI bleeding, heart disease, kidney disease
  • Topical NSAIDs have fewer systemic effects
