Lidocaine Patches

Targeted relief for localized pain

What Are Lidocaine Patches?

Lidocaine patches deliver local anesthetic through the skin to numb painful areas. They work by blocking sodium channels in nerve fibers, reducing pain signals. They're particularly useful for localized nerve pain.1

Available Forms

  • Lidoderm - 5% prescription patch
  • Generic 5% - Prescription
  • OTC patches - Lower concentration (4%)
  • Lidocaine cream/gel - Topical alternatives

FDA-Approved Uses2

  • Postherpetic neuralgia (after shingles)

Off-Label Uses

  • Diabetic neuropathy
  • Low back pain
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Other localized pain
  • Myofascial pain

How to Use

  • Apply to intact, non-irritated skin
  • Up to 3 patches at once (prescription)
  • Wear 12 hours on, 12 hours off
  • Can cut patches to size
  • Apply directly over painful area

Advantages3

  • Minimal systemic absorption
  • Few drug interactions
  • Can use with other treatments
  • No sedation or cognitive effects
  • No dependence potential

Side Effects

  • Skin irritation at application site
  • Redness
  • Rash (discontinue if occurs)
  • Systemic effects rare at proper doses

Precautions

  • Avoid on broken skin
  • Don't use with heating pads
  • Allergy to local anesthetics
  • Store properly, dispose safely
