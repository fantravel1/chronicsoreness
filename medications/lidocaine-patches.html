Lidocaine Patches

Topical numbing for localized pain

What Are Lidocaine Patches?

Lidocaine patches are adhesive patches containing the local anesthetic lidocaine. Applied directly to painful areas, they provide targeted relief by numbing the skin and underlying tissue. The prescription version (Lidoderm) contains 5% lidocaine, while lower-strength versions are available over-the-counter.1

How They Work2

  • Lidocaine penetrates skin
  • Blocks sodium channels in nerves
  • Prevents pain signals from transmitting
  • Works locally without systemic effects
  • Peak effect in 4-12 hours

FDA-Approved Use

  • Postherpetic neuralgia (after shingles)
  • Often used off-label for other conditions

Off-Label Uses

  • Chronic low back pain
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Neuropathic pain
  • Myofascial pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Diabetic neuropathy

How to Use3

  • Apply to clean, dry, intact skin
  • Cover painful area directly
  • Can cut patches to fit
  • Up to 3 patches at once (prescription)
  • Wear for 12 hours on, 12 hours off
  • Don't apply to broken skin

Benefits

  • Targeted local relief
  • Minimal systemic absorption
  • Few drug interactions
  • Generally well tolerated
  • Non-addictive
  • Can use with other treatments

Side Effects

  • Skin irritation at site
  • Redness or itching
  • Rash (uncommon)
  • Systemic effects rare with proper use

Precautions

  • Don't exceed recommended number
  • Avoid damaged or inflamed skin
  • Remove before MRI
  • Allergies to local anesthetics
  • Keep away from heat sources

OTC vs Prescription

OTC (4% or less)

  • Lower concentration
  • Various brands available
  • Good for mild pain

Prescription (5%)

  • Higher concentration
  • Brand: Lidoderm
  • May have generic options
  • Insurance may cover

Tips for Best Results

  • Apply consistently
  • Clean and dry skin first
  • Rotate exact application site
  • Combine with other treatments
