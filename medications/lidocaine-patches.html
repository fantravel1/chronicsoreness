Lidocaine Patches

Topical local anesthetic for pain

What Are Lidocaine Patches?

Lidocaine patches (brand name Lidoderm) deliver local anesthetic through the skin to numb the underlying area. They're FDA-approved for post-herpetic neuralgia (shingles pain) but are commonly used for other localized pain conditions. They provide targeted relief with minimal systemic absorption.1

How They Work

  • Lidocaine blocks nerve signals locally
  • Numbs pain receptors in the skin
  • Reduces nerve sensitivity
  • Minimal absorption into bloodstream
  • Works on local area only

Conditions That May Benefit2

  • Post-herpetic neuralgia (approved use)
  • Localized nerve pain
  • Diabetic neuropathy (localized)
  • Low back pain (localized)
  • Osteoarthritis (some evidence)
  • Chronic regional pain

How to Use3

  • Apply to intact, non-irritated skin
  • Up to 3 patches at once (usually)
  • Wear for 12 hours, then remove for 12 hours
  • Can be cut to fit area
  • Apply to painful area, not necessarily where pain originates

Benefits

  • Minimal systemic side effects
  • Easy to use
  • Can be used with other medications
  • Non-addictive
  • Provides continuous delivery

Side Effects

  • Skin irritation (most common)
  • Redness at application site
  • Itching
  • Rash (stop using if occurs)
  • Systemic effects rare with proper use

Precautions

  • Don't apply to broken or inflamed skin
  • Avoid near eyes or mucous membranes
  • Don't use with heating pads
  • Tell doctor about heart conditions
  • Allergic to local anestheticsâ€”don't use

Forms Available

  • 5% lidocaine patches (prescription)
  • OTC lidocaine patches (lower strength)
  • Lidocaine cream and gel
  • Generic versions available

Tips for Success

  • Clean and dry skin before application
  • Hair can be clipped (don't shave)
  • Press firmly to ensure adhesion
  • Rotate application sites if possible
  • Give adequate trial (several weeks)
