Corticosteroids

Powerful anti-inflammatory medications

How They Work

Corticosteroids are synthetic versions of cortisol that powerfully reduce inflammation. They work by suppressing immune responses and blocking inflammatory chemicals.1

Forms

Oral Steroids

  • Prednisone, prednisolone
  • Methylprednisolone (Medrol dose pack)
  • Dexamethasone
  • Used for short bursts typically

Injectable Steroids2

  • Joint injections
  • Epidural steroid injections
  • Trigger point injections
  • Longer acting (triamcinolone, methylprednisolone)

Conditions Treated

  • Inflammatory arthritis flares
  • Radiculopathy
  • Cluster headaches (bridge therapy)
  • Autoimmune conditions
  • Severe osteoarthritis flares
  • Bursitis, tendinitis

Short-Term Side Effects3

  • Increased appetite, weight gain
  • Insomnia
  • Mood changes
  • Elevated blood sugar
  • Fluid retention

Long-Term Risks

  • Osteoporosis
  • Adrenal suppression
  • Diabetes
  • Cataracts
  • Weight gain
  • Skin thinning
  • Infection risk

Important Considerations

  • Short-term use preferred when possible
  • Must taper if used more than 1-2 weeks
  • Limit injection frequency (3-4x/year per joint)
  • Monitor blood sugar in diabetics
