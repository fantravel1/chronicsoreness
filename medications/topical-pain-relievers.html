Topical Pain Relievers

Localized treatment applied directly to painful areas

Advantages of Topicals

  • Treat pain at the source
  • Fewer systemic side effects
  • Lower drug interactions
  • Can combine with oral medications
  • Good for localized pain

Types of Topical Pain Relievers

Lidocaine1

  • Patches (Lidoderm), creams, gels
  • Numbs the area (local anesthetic)
  • Good for neuropathic pain, PHN
  • Prescription patches, OTC creams
  • Apply to intact skin only

Capsaicin2

  • Derived from chili peppers
  • Depletes substance P (pain chemical)
  • Initial burning sensation (decreases with use)
  • Low concentration OTC (0.025-0.1%)
  • High concentration prescription (8% patch)

Topical NSAIDs3

  • Diclofenac gel (Voltaren)
  • Good for osteoarthritis, sprains
  • Less GI side effects than oral
  • Apply to joint areas

Counterirritants

  • Menthol, camphor, methyl salicylate
  • Create cooling/warming sensation
  • Distract from pain
  • OTC products (IcyHot, Bengay)

Application Tips

  • Apply to clean, dry, intact skin
  • Wash hands after applying (or use gloves)
  • Don't apply heat over topicals
  • Follow dosing instructions
  • Avoid eyes, mucous membranes
