Medical Cannabis

An evolving area of pain treatment

Overview

Medical cannabis is increasingly used for chronic pain, though evidence is still developing. The cannabis plant contains over 100 cannabinoids, with THC and CBD being the most studied.1

Key Compounds

  • THC - Psychoactive, analgesic, antiemetic
  • CBD - Non-psychoactive, anti-inflammatory, anxiolytic
  • Other cannabinoids and terpenes may contribute

Evidence for Pain2

Moderate Evidence

  • Neuropathic pain
  • Cancer pain (adjunct)
  • MS-related spasticity and pain

Limited/Mixed Evidence

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Arthritis
  • Headache disorders
  • More research needed

Forms Available

  • Inhaled (flower, vape)
  • Oral (oils, capsules, edibles)
  • Topical
  • Sublingual

Risks and Side Effects3

  • Cognitive impairment
  • Dizziness, sedation
  • Anxiety or paranoia (some people)
  • Cannabis use disorder risk
  • Respiratory issues (if smoked)
  • Drug interactions

Considerations

  • Legal status varies by location
  • Quality control issues
  • May not be covered by insurance
  • Discuss with healthcare provider
  • Start low, go slow
  • Not first-line treatment
