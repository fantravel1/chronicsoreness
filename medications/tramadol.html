Tramadol

An atypical opioid pain reliever

What Is Tramadol?

Tramadol is a centrally acting pain reliever with a dual mechanism: weak opioid activity plus effects on serotonin and norepinephrine. It's considered a weaker opioid but still carries risks of dependence and other concerns.1

Brand Names

  • Ultram (immediate-release)
  • Ultram ER (extended-release)
  • ConZip
  • Also in combination products

Uses

  • Moderate to moderately severe pain
  • Chronic pain (with caution)
  • Sometimes for neuropathic pain

Dosing2

  • Immediate-release: 50-100 mg every 4-6 hours
  • Maximum: 400 mg/day
  • Extended-release: 100-300 mg once daily
  • Start low, especially in elderly

Side Effects3

  • Nausea, dizziness
  • Constipation
  • Headache
  • Drowsiness
  • Seizures (especially at high doses)
  • Serotonin syndrome (with certain medications)

Important Warnings

  • Schedule IV controlled substance
  • Can cause physical dependence
  • Seizure risk (especially with SSRIs)
  • Avoid with MAOIs
  • Lower dose in elderly, kidney/liver disease
  • Some people are ultra-rapid metabolizers (risk)

Drug Interactions

  • SSRIs, SNRIs (serotonin syndrome risk)
  • MAOIs (contraindicated)
  • Other CNS depressants
  • CYP2D6 inhibitors/inducers
