Tramadol

Understanding this atypical pain medication

What Is Tramadol?

Tramadol is a pain medication with a dual mechanismâ€”weak opioid effects plus inhibition of serotonin and norepinephrine reuptake. It's used for moderate to moderately severe pain.1

How It Works

  • Binds to opioid receptors (weak)
  • Inhibits serotonin and norepinephrine reuptake
  • Both mechanisms contribute to pain relief

Forms and Dosing

  • Immediate-release: 50-100mg every 4-6 hours
  • Extended-release: Once daily dosing
  • Maximum: 400mg daily (300mg in elderly)

Side Effects2

  • Nausea and dizziness
  • Constipation
  • Drowsiness
  • Headache
  • Sweating

Serious Risks

  • Seizures - Especially at high doses
  • Serotonin syndrome - With other serotonergic drugs
  • Respiratory depression - Especially with other CNS depressants
  • Dependence and addiction - Schedule IV controlled substance
  • Withdrawal - Can occur with prolonged use

Drug Interactions

  • SSRIs and SNRIs (serotonin syndrome risk)
  • MAOIs (contraindicated)
  • Other opioids
  • Benzodiazepines
  • CYP2D6 inhibitors

Effectiveness

Provides modest pain relief. Not recommended as first-line for chronic non-cancer pain due to risks.3
