Baclofen

Muscle relaxant for spasticity

What Is Baclofen?

Baclofen (brand name Lioresal) is a muscle relaxant that works on the central nervous system. It's primarily used for spasticity from conditions like multiple sclerosis and spinal cord injury. It may also be used for some pain conditions.1

How It Works

  • GABA-B receptor agonist
  • Reduces muscle spasms
  • Works in spinal cord
  • Decreases nerve signals to muscles

Uses2

Primary Uses

  • Spasticity from MS
  • Spinal cord injury spasticity
  • Cerebral palsy spasticity

Other Uses

  • Trigeminal neuralgia
  • Chronic pain (sometimes)
  • Muscle spasms

Dosing

  • Start low (5mg 2-3 times daily)
  • Increase gradually
  • Usual dose: 40-80mg daily in divided doses
  • Maximum: 80mg daily (oral)
  • Also available as intrathecal pump

Side Effects3

Common

  • Drowsiness
  • Dizziness
  • Weakness
  • Fatigue
  • Nausea
  • Headache

Less Common

  • Confusion
  • Mood changes
  • Constipation
  • Urinary frequency

Important Warnings

  • Do not stop suddenly - Can cause withdrawal (seizures, hallucinations)
  • Taper gradually under medical supervision
  • Avoid alcohol
  • May cause drowsinessâ€”avoid driving initially

Intrathecal Baclofen

  • Delivered directly to spinal fluid via pump
  • Much lower doses needed
  • For severe spasticity not controlled orally
  • Requires surgical implant
