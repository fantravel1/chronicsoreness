Baclofen

Muscle relaxant for spasticity and pain

What Is Baclofen?

Baclofen is a muscle relaxant and antispasticity medication that works on the spinal cord. It's primarily used to treat muscle spasticity from conditions like multiple sclerosis, spinal cord injury, and cerebral palsy. It may also help with certain types of muscle-related pain.1

How It Works2

  • GABA-B receptor agonist
  • Acts primarily at spinal cord level
  • Reduces muscle tone and spasms
  • Decreases reflex activity
  • May have some pain-relieving effects

Approved Uses

  • Spasticity from multiple sclerosis
  • Spasticity from spinal cord conditions
  • Muscle stiffness and spasms

Off-Label Uses

  • Trigeminal neuralgia
  • Chronic muscle pain
  • Alcohol withdrawal
  • Hiccups
  • Some headache types

Dosing3

  • Start low: 5 mg three times daily
  • Increase gradually
  • Typical range: 40-80 mg/day
  • Maximum: usually 80 mg/day oral
  • Intrathecal pump for severe spasticity

Common Side Effects

  • Drowsiness
  • Dizziness
  • Weakness
  • Nausea
  • Headache
  • Constipation
  • Confusion (especially elderly)

Serious Risks

  • Withdrawal syndrome (can be severe)
  • Never stop suddenly
  • Respiratory depression (high doses)
  • Seizures on abrupt discontinuation
  • Intrathecal pump complications

Important Precautions

  • Taper dose slowly when stopping
  • Caution with kidney disease
  • May worsen muscle weakness
  • Avoid alcohol
  • May impair driving/machinery
  • Increased fall risk in elderly

Intrathecal Baclofen

  • Pump delivers directly to spinal fluid
  • Much lower doses needed
  • Fewer systemic side effects
  • For severe spasticity
  • Requires surgical implant
  • Regular pump refills needed
