Naproxen

Longer-acting anti-inflammatory relief

What Is Naproxen?

Naproxen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) with a longer duration of action than ibuprofen, allowing for twice-daily dosing. It's effective for arthritis, menstrual pain, and various musculoskeletal conditions.1

Brand Names

  • Aleve (OTC)
  • Naprosyn (Rx)
  • Anaprox
  • Naprelan (extended-release)

Uses

  • Osteoarthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Ankylosing spondylitis
  • Tendinitis and bursitis
  • Menstrual cramps
  • Gout attacks
  • Migraine

Dosing2

  • OTC (Aleve) - 220 mg every 8-12 hours
  • OTC maximum - 660 mg per day
  • Prescription - 250-500 mg twice daily
  • Prescription max - 1,250 mg first day, then 1,000 mg

Advantages

  • Longer lasting (12 hours)
  • Twice-daily dosing
  • May have slightly lower cardiovascular risk than some NSAIDs

Risks3

  • Same NSAID risks as ibuprofen
  • GI bleeding and ulcers
  • Cardiovascular risks
  • Kidney effects
  • Higher risk with longer-term use

Who Should Avoid

  • Prior GI bleeding
  • Kidney disease
  • Cardiovascular disease
  • Late pregnancy
  • On blood thinners
