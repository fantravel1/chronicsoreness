Naproxen

A common NSAID for pain and inflammation

What Is Naproxen?

Naproxen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) available both over-the-counter (Aleve) and by prescription (Naprosyn). Its long duration of action allows twice-daily dosing.1

Uses

  • Osteoarthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Ankylosing spondylitis
  • Acute gout
  • Menstrual pain
  • Headaches and migraines
  • General pain and inflammation

How It Works

  • Inhibits COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes
  • Reduces prostaglandin production
  • Decreases inflammation, pain, and fever

Dosing

  • OTC: 220mg every 8-12 hours (max 660mg/day)
  • Prescription: 250-500mg twice daily
  • Maximum: 1500mg/day for limited time
  • Take with food

Side Effects2

  • Stomach upset, nausea
  • Heartburn
  • Dizziness
  • Headache
  • Drowsiness

Serious Risks

  • GI bleeding and ulcers
  • Cardiovascular events (heart attack, stroke)
  • Kidney damage
  • Increased blood pressure
  • Allergic reactions

Who Should Avoid

  • History of GI bleeding or ulcers
  • Heart disease or stroke history
  • Kidney disease
  • Before surgery
  • Third trimester pregnancy
