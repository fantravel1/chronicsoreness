Ibuprofen

A widely used anti-inflammatory pain reliever

What Is Ibuprofen?

Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to treat pain, inflammation, and fever. It works by blocking enzymes (COX-1 and COX-2) that produce prostaglandins, chemicals that cause inflammation and pain.1

Brand Names

  • Advil
  • Motrin
  • Nurofen
  • Many generic versions

Uses

  • Arthritis pain
  • Muscle aches
  • Headaches and migraines
  • Menstrual cramps
  • Dental pain
  • Minor injuries

Dosing2

  • OTC dose - 200-400 mg every 4-6 hours
  • OTC maximum - 1,200 mg per day
  • Prescription - Up to 800 mg per dose, 3,200 mg/day
  • Take with food to reduce stomach upset

Risks and Side Effects3

GI Issues

  • Stomach pain, nausea
  • Ulcers and bleeding (especially long-term)

Cardiovascular

  • Increased heart attack/stroke risk (at high doses, long-term)
  • May raise blood pressure

Kidney

  • Can reduce kidney function
  • Risk increases with dehydration

Who Should Avoid

  • History of GI bleeding or ulcers
  • Kidney disease
  • Heart disease or high blood pressure
  • Third trimester of pregnancy
  • On blood thinners (consult doctor)
