Tizanidine

Muscle relaxant for spasticity and spasms

What Is Tizanidine?

Tizanidine is a centrally acting muscle relaxant primarily used for spasticity (from conditions like multiple sclerosis or spinal cord injury) and sometimes for muscle spasms from musculoskeletal conditions. Brand name is Zanaflex. It works differently than other muscle relaxants, acting as an alpha-2 agonist in the brain.1

How It Works2

  • Alpha-2 adrenergic agonist
  • Acts in spinal cord and brain
  • Reduces excitatory signals to muscles
  • Decreases muscle tone
  • Different mechanism than benzodiazepines

Approved Uses

  • Spasticity from MS
  • Spasticity from spinal cord injury

Off-Label Uses

  • Muscle spasms from musculoskeletal conditions
  • Low back pain with spasm
  • Tension headaches
  • Chronic daily headache

Dosing3

  • Start low: 2-4 mg
  • Usually taken at bedtime initially
  • Can be taken up to 3 times daily
  • Maximum 36 mg per day
  • Increase gradually
  • Take with or without food (consistently)

Common Side Effects

  • Drowsiness/sedation (significant)
  • Dry mouth
  • Dizziness
  • Weakness
  • Fatigue
  • Constipation
  • Low blood pressure

Serious Concerns

  • Liver toxicity (monitor)
  • Severe low blood pressure
  • Rebound hypertension if stopped suddenly
  • Significant drug interactions

Important Precautions

  • Liver function tests needed
  • Don't stop suddenly
  • Avoid with fluvoxamine
  • Caution with ciprofloxacin
  • Interacts with CYP1A2 inhibitors
  • May impair driving

Compared to Other Muscle Relaxants

  • More sedating for many
  • Better for spasticity than acute spasm
  • Different mechanism than cyclobenzaprine
  • May be useful when others fail
  • Helpful for sleep if taken at night

Food Effects

  • Food affects absorption
  • Take consistently with or without food
  • Switching can change effect
