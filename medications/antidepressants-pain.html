Antidepressants for Pain

Pain relief through neurotransmitter modulation

Why Antidepressants for Pain?

Certain antidepressants have pain-relieving properties independent of their effects on mood. They work on the same neurotransmitters (serotonin, norepinephrine) involved in pain pathways. These medications can reduce pain even in people who aren't depressed.1

Types Used for Pain2

SNRIs (Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors)

  • Duloxetine (Cymbalta): FDA-approved for multiple pain conditions
  • Venlafaxine (Effexor): Used off-label for pain
  • Milnacipran (Savella): FDA-approved for fibromyalgia

Tricyclic Antidepressants (TCAs)

  • Amitriptyline: Most studied for pain
  • Nortriptyline: Better tolerated than amitriptyline
  • Desipramine: Fewer side effects
  • Used at lower doses than for depression

How They Help Pain

  • Enhance descending pain inhibition pathways
  • Increase serotonin and norepinephrine in pain circuits
  • Block pain signal transmission in spinal cord
  • May improve sleep (TCAs)
  • Reduce central sensitization

Conditions They Treat3

  • Diabetic neuropathy (duloxetine FDA-approved)
  • Fibromyalgia (duloxetine, milnacipran approved)
  • Chronic musculoskeletal pain (duloxetine approved)
  • Neuropathic pain
  • Chronic headaches
  • Low back pain

Common Side Effects

SNRIs

  • Nausea (often improves)
  • Dry mouth
  • Constipation
  • Dizziness
  • Sweating
  • Sexual dysfunction

TCAs

  • Drowsiness
  • Dry mouth
  • Constipation
  • Weight gain
  • Blurred vision
  • Heart rhythm effects (at higher doses)

Starting Treatment

  • Start at low dose
  • Increase gradually
  • Pain relief may take 2-4 weeks
  • Full effect may take 6-8 weeks
  • TCAs often taken at bedtime (drowsiness)

Important Warnings

  • Don't stop suddenly (withdrawal symptoms)
  • TCAs dangerous in overdose
  • Drug interactions (serotonin syndrome risk)
  • May affect blood pressure
  • Black box warning for young adults (suicide risk)

Not the Same as Being Depressed

  • Pain relief mechanism separate from mood effects
  • Works at lower doses for pain
  • Being prescribed one doesn't mean "it's all in your head"
  • Pain has real biological pathways these drugs target
