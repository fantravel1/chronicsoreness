Cyclobenzaprine

Common muscle relaxant for acute spasms

What Is Cyclobenzaprine?

Cyclobenzaprine is one of the most commonly prescribed muscle relaxants for acute muscle spasms and musculoskeletal pain. Brand names include Flexeril and Amrix. It's structurally similar to tricyclic antidepressants and is intended for short-term use alongside rest and physical therapy.1

How It Works2

  • Acts primarily in the brain stem
  • Reduces muscle hyperactivity
  • Doesn't directly relax muscles
  • May affect alpha motor neurons
  • Sedation contributes to effect

Uses

  • Acute muscle spasms
  • Muscle strains and sprains
  • Low back pain with spasm
  • Neck pain with spasm
  • Usually short-term (2-3 weeks)

Dosing3

Immediate Release

  • 5-10 mg three times daily
  • Often started at lower dose
  • Taken with or without food

Extended Release

  • 15-30 mg once daily
  • Take at same time each day

Common Side Effects

  • Drowsiness (most common)
  • Dry mouth
  • Dizziness
  • Fatigue
  • Constipation
  • Blurred vision
  • Headache

Important Precautions

  • Very sedatingâ€”affects driving
  • Avoid alcohol
  • Avoid in elderly (Beers criteria)
  • Not for use with MAO inhibitors
  • Caution with heart disease
  • Can cause urinary retention
  • Avoid with glaucoma

Drug Interactions

  • Other CNS depressants
  • MAO inhibitors (avoid)
  • Tramadol (serotonin syndrome risk)
  • Other serotonergic drugs
  • Anticholinergic drugs

Not for Chronic Use

  • Evidence supports short-term use only
  • Effectiveness decreases over time
  • Not appropriate for chronic pain
  • Tolerance may develop

Elderly Concerns

  • High anticholinergic burden
  • Increased fall risk
  • Cognitive effects
  • Generally avoided in >65
  • If used, lower doses
