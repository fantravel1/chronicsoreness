SNRIs for Pain

Antidepressants with pain-relieving properties

What Are SNRIs?

Serotonin-norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors (SNRIs) are antidepressants that also have pain-relieving properties. They work by increasing levels of serotonin and norepinephrine, which are involved in pain modulation.1

How They Help Pain

  • Enhance descending pain inhibition pathways
  • Norepinephrine particularly important for pain
  • May help even without depression
  • Can improve sleep and mood

SNRIs Used for Pain2

Duloxetine (Cymbalta)

  • FDA approved for fibromyalgia
  • FDA approved for diabetic neuropathy
  • FDA approved for chronic musculoskeletal pain
  • Typical dose: 30-60 mg/day

Milnacipran (Savella)

  • FDA approved for fibromyalgia
  • More norepinephrine activity
  • Twice daily dosing

Venlafaxine (Effexor)

  • Used off-label for pain
  • Neuropathic pain, migraine prevention
  • Higher doses more effective for pain

Conditions Treated

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Diabetic peripheral neuropathy
  • Chronic low back pain
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Migraine prevention

Side Effects3

  • Nausea (often improves)
  • Dry mouth
  • Constipation
  • Dizziness
  • Sweating
  • Sexual dysfunction
  • Blood pressure increase (monitor)

Important Notes

  • Takes 2-4 weeks for pain benefit
  • Don't stop abruptly - taper
  • Monitor blood pressure
  • Drug interactions (serotonin syndrome risk)
