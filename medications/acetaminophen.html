Acetaminophen (Tylenol)

A common over-the-counter pain reliever

What Is Acetaminophen?

Acetaminophen (paracetamol outside the US) is one of the most commonly used over-the-counter pain relievers. Unlike NSAIDs, it doesn't reduce inflammation significantly but can help with pain and fever.1

How It Works

  • Exact mechanism not fully understood
  • Acts in the central nervous system
  • May affect prostaglandin synthesis centrally
  • Affects serotonin and cannabinoid pathways

Uses

  • Mild to moderate pain
  • Headaches
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Back pain
  • Menstrual cramps
  • Fever

Effectiveness for Chronic Pain2

  • Osteoarthritis - Modest benefit at best
  • Low back pain - Limited evidence of benefit
  • May help some individuals
  • Often used as first-line due to safety profile

Dosing

  • Regular strength: 325-650mg every 4-6 hours
  • Extra strength: 1000mg every 6-8 hours
  • Maximum: 3000-4000mg/day
  • Lower max for elderly, liver disease, alcohol use

Safety Considerations3

Advantages

  • Doesn't cause GI bleeding like NSAIDs
  • No cardiovascular risks like NSAIDs
  • Safe with kidney disease
  • Generally safe in pregnancy

Liver Toxicity (Major Risk)

  • Don't exceed maximum daily dose
  • Watch for acetaminophen in combination products
  • Avoid with heavy alcohol use
  • Caution with liver disease

Important Notes

  • Many medications contain acetaminophen (cold medicines, prescription pain pills)
  • Check all labels to avoid accidental overdose
  • Overdose can cause severe liver damage
