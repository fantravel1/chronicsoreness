Acetaminophen

A common first-line pain reliever

What Is Acetaminophen?

Acetaminophen (known as paracetamol outside the US) is one of the most commonly used pain relievers and fever reducers worldwide. Unlike NSAIDs, it doesn't have significant anti-inflammatory effects, but it's generally easier on the stomach.1

Brand Names

  • Tylenol
  • Panadol
  • Many generic versions
  • Found in many combination products

Uses

  • Mild to moderate pain
  • Fever reduction
  • Headaches
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Often combined with opioids

Dosing2

  • Standard dose - 325-650 mg every 4-6 hours
  • Maximum - 3,000-4,000 mg per day (consult doctor)
  • Lower for - Liver disease, heavy alcohol use
  • Check all medications for hidden acetaminophen

Safety Concerns3

Liver Toxicity

  • Leading cause of acute liver failure
  • Risk with overdose or chronic high doses
  • Increased risk with alcohol
  • Check combination products (cold medicines, pain pills)

Advantages

  • Gentler on stomach than NSAIDs
  • Safe for most people at recommended doses
  • Can use with blood thinners
  • Generally safe in pregnancy (consult doctor)

When to Avoid

  • Liver disease
  • Heavy alcohol use (3+ drinks/day)
  • Already taking other acetaminophen products
