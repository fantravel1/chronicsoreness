Acetaminophen

A common pain reliever

What Is Acetaminophen?

Acetaminophen (called paracetamol in many countries, brand name Tylenol) is a widely used over-the-counter pain reliever and fever reducer. Unlike NSAIDs, it has minimal anti-inflammatory effects. Its exact mechanism of action is not fully understood.1

How It Works

  • Mechanism not completely understood
  • Acts centrally in the brain
  • Reduces pain perception
  • Lowers fever
  • Minimal anti-inflammatory effect

Advantages2

  • Doesn't irritate stomach
  • Doesn't affect bleeding
  • Safe for most people when used correctly
  • Can combine with NSAIDs (alternating)
  • Generally safe in pregnancy
  • Works for many types of pain

Best For

  • Mild to moderate pain
  • Headaches
  • Osteoarthritis (some evidence)
  • Post-surgical pain
  • Fever reduction
  • When NSAIDs are contraindicated

Dosing3

  • Typical adult dose: 325-650mg every 4-6 hours
  • Maximum: 3,000-4,000mg per day (follow label)
  • Lower max dose if liver disease or alcohol use
  • Don't exceed recommended dose

Liver Safety (Critical)

  • Can cause liver damage in overdose
  • Leading cause of acute liver failure in US
  • Found in many combination products (check labels!)
  • Alcohol increases liver risk
  • Stay within daily limits

Hidden Sources of Acetaminophen

  • Cold and flu medications
  • Sleep aids (PM formulations)
  • Prescription pain medications
  • Migraine medications
  • Always read ingredient labels

Who Should Be Careful

  • Liver disease
  • Heavy alcohol use
  • Taking multiple products with acetaminophen
  • Certain medications that affect liver

Effectiveness for Chronic Pain

  • Limited evidence for chronic pain
  • May work better for acute pain
  • First-line for some conditions (like osteoarthritis)
  • Individual response varies

Safe Use Tips

  • Read all labels for acetaminophen content
  • Track total daily dose from all sources
  • Don't exceed maximum daily dose
  • Limit alcohol
  • Talk to doctor if using long-term
