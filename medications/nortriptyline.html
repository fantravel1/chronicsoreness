Nortriptyline

Tricyclic antidepressant for nerve pain

What Is Nortriptyline?

Nortriptyline (brand name Pamelor) is a tricyclic antidepressant (TCA) that's commonly used at low doses to treat chronic pain, especially neuropathic pain. For pain, it's used at much lower doses than for depression and works through different mechanisms.1

How It Helps Pain

  • Affects pain pathways in spinal cord and brain
  • Increases norepinephrine and serotonin
  • Independent of antidepressant effect
  • May help with sleep

Conditions Treated2

  • Neuropathic pain
  • Postherpetic neuralgia
  • Diabetic neuropathy
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic headaches
  • Chronic back pain

Dosing

  • Start low: usually 10-25mg at bedtime
  • Increase slowly over weeks
  • Pain doses typically 25-75mg
  • May take 2-4 weeks to work
  • Take at bedtime (causes drowsiness)

Side Effects3

Common

  • Drowsiness
  • Dry mouth
  • Constipation
  • Blurred vision
  • Weight gain
  • Dizziness

Less Common but Important

  • Urinary retention
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Confusion (especially in elderly)

Precautions

  • May affect heart rhythmâ€”caution with heart disease
  • Don't stop suddenly
  • Interacts with many medications
  • Use caution in elderly
  • Avoid with glaucoma or urinary retention
  • Don't combine with MAOIs

Why Nortriptyline Over Amitriptyline?

  • Often better tolerated
  • Less sedating
  • Fewer anticholinergic effects
  • Similar effectiveness

  • Take at bedtime
  • Stay hydrated for dry mouth
