Amitriptyline

A classic tricyclic for chronic pain

What Is Amitriptyline?

Amitriptyline is a tricyclic antidepressant (TCA) that's been used for decades to treat chronic pain at doses lower than those used for depression. It affects multiple neurotransmitters involved in pain perception.1

Pain Conditions Treated

  • Neuropathic pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Migraine prevention
  • Tension headache prevention
  • Chronic back pain
  • IBS

How It Works2

  • Blocks reuptake of serotonin and norepinephrine
  • Blocks sodium channels
  • Anticholinergic effects
  • Antihistamine effects (helps sleep)
  • Pain relief independent of antidepressant effect

Dosing for Pain

  • Start: 10-25 mg at bedtime
  • Increase: By 10-25 mg weekly
  • Typical pain dose: 25-75 mg at bedtime
  • Maximum: 150 mg/day for pain
  • Lower than depression doses (150-300 mg)

Side Effects3

  • Drowsiness (often helpful for sleep)
  • Dry mouth
  • Constipation
  • Weight gain
  • Blurred vision
  • Urinary retention
  • Orthostatic hypotension

Cautions

  • Avoid in elderly (Beers criteria)
  • Dangerous in overdose
  • Cardiac effects (get ECG if concerns)
  • Avoid with glaucoma
  • Avoid with urinary retention

Tips

  • Take at bedtime (sedating)
  • Give it 4-6 weeks to work
  • Side effects often improve over time
  • Stay hydrated (for dry mouth)
