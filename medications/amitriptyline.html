Amitriptyline

A tricyclic antidepressant used for chronic pain

What Is Amitriptyline?

Amitriptyline is a tricyclic antidepressant (TCA) commonly used at low doses for chronic pain conditions. At pain-relieving doses, it works differently than at antidepressant doses.1

Uses in Pain Management

  • Neuropathic pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Migraine prevention
  • Tension headache prevention
  • Chronic low back pain
  • Interstitial cystitis

How It Works for Pain2

  • Blocks reuptake of norepinephrine and serotonin
  • Affects pain processing in spinal cord
  • Improves sleep quality
  • May have direct analgesic effects

Dosing for Pain

  • Start: 10-25mg at bedtime
  • Increase gradually
  • Typical pain dose: 25-75mg
  • Lower than antidepressant doses (150-300mg)

Side Effects

  • Drowsiness (often desired - take at bedtime)
  • Dry mouth
  • Constipation
  • Weight gain
  • Blurred vision
  • Urinary retention
  • Dizziness

Precautions3

  • Avoid in elderly if possible (anticholinergic effects)
  • Cardiac effects - use caution with heart conditions
  • Glaucoma risk
  • Don't combine with MAOIs
  • May affect driving

Timeline

May take 2-6 weeks to see full pain-relieving effect. Sleep improvement often comes sooner.
