Capsaicin

Heat from chili peppers for pain

What Is Capsaicin?

Capsaicin is the compound that makes chili peppers hot. When applied to the skin, it initially causes a burning sensation, then gradually desensitizes pain nerves. It's available in OTC creams and prescription patches for various pain conditions.1

How It Works

  • Activates TRPV1 receptors (heat/pain sensors)
  • Causes substance P depletion over time
  • Desensitizes nerve fibers
  • Reduces pain signal transmission
  • Requires consistent use for effect

Forms Available2

Over-the-Counter

  • Low-dose creams (0.025%-0.1%)
  • Applied 3-4 times daily
  • Takes 2-4 weeks for full effect
  • Examples: Zostrix, Capzasin

Prescription

  • High-dose patch (8% - Qutenza)
  • Applied by healthcare provider
  • Single application lasts months
  • For postherpetic neuralgia

Conditions Treated3

  • Postherpetic neuralgia (shingles pain)
  • Diabetic neuropathy
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Muscle pain
  • Fibromyalgia (limited evidence)

How to Use OTC Creams

  • Apply thin layer to affected area
  • Rub in completely
  • Use 3-4 times daily
  • Continue for at least 4 weeks
  • Wash hands thoroughly after (or use gloves)
  • Don't apply to broken skin

Side Effects

  • Burning sensation (decreases with use)
  • Redness
  • Stinging
  • Warmth at application site
  • Sneezing if inhaled

  • Don't skip applications
  • Keep away from eyes and mucous membranes
  • Don't use with heating pad
  • Give it adequate time to work
