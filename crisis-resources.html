⚠ When to Seek Emergency Care

  • Sudden severe pain that is different from your usual chronic pain
  • Chest pain, difficulty breathing, or signs of a heart attack
  • Sudden weakness, numbness, or difficulty speaking (stroke symptoms)
  • Thoughts of harming yourself or others
  • Fever with severe pain (possible infection)
  • Pain after an injury or accident
  • Severe abdominal pain with vomiting blood

Mental Health Crisis Support

Living with chronic pain can be emotionally overwhelming. If you're struggling with depression, anxiety, or thoughts of suicide, please reach out. These services are free and confidential.

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
Free, confidential support for people in distress. Trained counselors available 24/7 for anyone experiencing emotional distress or suicidal thoughts.
📞 Call 988 💬 Text 988 💻 Online Chat
Available 24/7, 365 days a year
Crisis Text Line
Free, 24/7 text-based support for anyone in crisis. Text with a trained crisis counselor from anywhere in the US.
💬 Text HOME to 741741
Available 24/7
SAMHSA National Helpline
Treatment referral service for substance abuse and mental health. Free, confidential, 24/7 information and referrals in English and Spanish.
📞 1-800-662-4357 🌐 Website
Available 24/7, 365 days a year
Veterans Crisis Line
Confidential support for veterans and their families. Connect with caring, qualified responders with the Department of Veterans Affairs.
📞 Dial 988, Press 1 💬 Text 838255 💻 Online Chat
Available 24/7

Immediate Self-Care During a Pain Flare

While waiting for help or during a difficult moment, try these grounding techniques:

Box Breathing

Breathe in for 4 counts, hold for 4, breathe out for 4, hold for 4. Repeat 4 times.

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

Name 5 things you see, 4 you hear, 3 you feel, 2 you smell, 1 you taste.

Ice or Heat

Apply an ice pack or heating pad to the painful area. Even holding ice can help distract from emotional pain.

Change Position

If possible, gently change your position. Lie down, sit up, or find a more comfortable arrangement.

Reach Out

Text a friend, family member, or call a helpline. You don't have to face this moment alone.

Gentle Movement

If safe, try gentle stretching or walking to another room. Small movements can shift your focus.

Outside the United States?

The resources above are primarily US-based. For international crisis support, visit Find A Helpline to locate mental health support in your country, or IASP Crisis Centres for international suicide prevention resources.
