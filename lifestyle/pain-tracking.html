Pain Tracking

Understanding your pain patterns

Why Track Pain?

Keeping a pain diary helps you identify patterns, triggers, and what helps. It provides valuable information for your healthcare providers and helps you become more aware of factors affecting your pain. Tracking can also reveal progress that might not be obvious day-to-day.1

What to Track2

Pain Details

  • Pain level (0-10 scale)
  • Location(s) of pain
  • Type of pain (sharp, aching, burning)
  • Duration of pain episodes
  • Time of day

Context

  • Activities before pain changed
  • Weather
  • Sleep quality and duration
  • Stress level
  • Mood
  • Menstrual cycle (if applicable)

Treatments

  • Medications taken and timing
  • Other treatments used (heat, ice, etc.)
  • What helped or didn't help

Function

  • Activities you could/couldn't do
  • Impact on daily life
  • Work or school attendance

Methods3

Paper Diary

  • Simple and private
  • No technology needed
  • Create your own or use templates

Apps

  • Easy to use on the go
  • Automatic graphs and reports
  • Reminders to log
  • Examples: PainScale, CatchMyPain, Manage My Pain

Spreadsheets

  • Customizable
  • Easy to analyze patterns
  • Syncs across devices

Tips for Success

  • Keep it simple (you'll be more likely to do it)
  • Track at consistent times
  • Don't let tracking increase focus on pain
  • Look for patterns weekly, not daily
  • Share insights with your providers
  • Track function and wins too, not just pain

Common Patterns to Look For

  • Time of day patterns
  • Activity triggers
  • Weather effects
  • Sleep-pain connections
  • Stress-pain connections
  • Medication effectiveness timing
