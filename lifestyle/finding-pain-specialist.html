Finding a Pain Specialist

Building your treatment team

When to See a Pain Specialist

Pain specialists are physicians with additional training in diagnosing and treating complex pain conditions. Consider seeing one when pain persists despite standard treatment, affects daily function significantly, or when your primary care doctor recommends it. Finding the right specialist can make a significant difference in your care.1

Types of Pain Specialists2

Pain Medicine Physicians

  • Board certified in pain medicine
  • Background may vary (anesthesiology, PM&R, neurology)
  • Comprehensive pain management
  • May do procedures and prescribe

Physiatrists (PM&R)

  • Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation
  • Focus on function and quality of life
  • Non-surgical approach
  • Often coordinate care

Other Specialists

  • Neurologists (nerve-related pain)
  • Rheumatologists (inflammatory conditions)
  • Orthopedic surgeons (structural issues)
  • Neurosurgeons (spine problems)

Types of Pain Clinics3

Comprehensive/Multidisciplinary

  • Multiple specialists together
  • Physical therapy, psychology included
  • Holistic approach
  • Gold standard for complex pain

Procedure-Focused

  • Emphasis on injections, procedures
  • May be appropriate for some
  • Ask about comprehensive services

Questions to Ask

  • What is your training/certification?
  • Do you treat my type of pain?
  • What treatments do you offer?
  • Do you have a multidisciplinary team?
  • What is your approach to opioids?
  • How do you measure progress?

How to Find One

  • Ask primary care for referral
  • Check insurance provider directory
  • Professional organizations (AAPM)
  • Academic medical centers
  • Patient reviews and recommendations

Preparing for First Visit

  • Gather medical records
  • List all medications
  • Bring imaging on CD
  • Write down your pain history
  • Note treatments already tried
  • List your questions

Red Flags to Avoid

  • Promises of complete cure
  • One-size-fits-all approach
  • Pressure to do procedures
  • Won't explain options
  • Doesn't listen to concerns
  • Cash-only/excessive costs

Building Your Team

  • Pain specialist coordinates
  • Physical therapist
  • Mental health provider
  • Primary care doctor
  • Others as needed

If First Visit Doesn't Go Well

  • Consider second opinion
  • Find someone you're comfortable with
  • Partnership is important
  • You can change providers
