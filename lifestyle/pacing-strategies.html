Pacing Strategies

Balancing activity and rest

What Is Pacing?

Pacing is a self-management strategy that involves breaking activities into manageable chunks and balancing activity with rest. The goal is to maintain consistent activity levels and avoid the "boom-bust" cycle that often worsens chronic pain.1

The Boom-Bust Cycle

  • Boom: Overdo on good days
  • Bust: Crash and need extended rest
  • Creates unpredictable symptom pattern
  • Often leads to gradually declining function
  • Pacing helps break this cycle

Key Principles2

  • Set baseline activity levels you can sustain
  • Plan regular rest breaks
  • Spread activities throughout day/week
  • Stop before pain increases significantly
  • Gradually increase activity over time

Practical Strategies

Time-Based Pacing

  • Set timers for activities
  • Take breaks at set intervals
  • Don't wait until pain forces you to stop

Task-Based Pacing

  • Break large tasks into smaller steps
  • Alternate between different types of activities
  • Spread tasks across multiple days

Finding Your Baseline3

  • Track activities and pain levels
  • Identify how long you can do activities comfortably
  • Set your baseline at 80% of that
  • Build consistency before increasing

Common Challenges

  • Feeling "lazy" for resting
  • Pressure to do more on good days
  • Difficulty saying no
  • Planning can feel overwhelming

Tips for Success

  • Plan activities in advance
  • Use timers and reminders
  • Schedule rest as important
  • Be patient with progress
