Traveling with Chronic Pain

Tips for more comfortable journeys

Travel Is Possible

Chronic pain doesn't mean giving up travel, but it does require extra planning and flexibility. With preparation and the right strategies, you can enjoy trips while managing your pain. The key is knowing your limits, planning ahead, and being willing to adapt.1

Planning Ahead

  • Build in rest days
  • Don't over-schedule
  • Research accessibility at destinations
  • Book accommodations carefully
  • Consider travel insurance
  • Plan for worst-case scenarios

Medication Considerations2

  • Bring extra medication (delays happen)
  • Keep meds in original containers
  • Carry prescriptions and doctor's letter
  • Pack medications in carry-on
  • Know rules for controlled substances
  • Research medication laws at destination

Flying Tips

  • Request wheelchair assistance if needed
  • Book aisle seats for movement
  • Consider bulkhead or exit row
  • Get up and move regularly
  • Use compression stockings for long flights
  • Bring neck pillow and lumbar support
  • Pre-board for extra time

Road Trips

  • Plan frequent stops (every 1-2 hours)
  • Use cruise control when possible
  • Adjust seating for comfort
  • Bring heat/ice packs
  • Stretch at stops
  • Share driving if possible

Packing Essentials

  • All medications (plus extra)
  • Heating pad (portable)
  • Ice packs or instant cold packs
  • TENS unit
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Supportive pillow
  • Topical pain relief
  • Mobility aids if used

At Your Destination

  • Pace your activities
  • Prioritize what matters most
  • Rest when needed
  • Don't push through severe pain
  • Maintain routines when possible
  • Stay hydrated

Hotel Tips3

  • Request ground floor if stairs are hard
  • Ask about bed firmness
  • Check accessibility features
  • Request room near elevators
  • Ask about in-room refrigerator for ice

If Pain Flares

  • Have a plan for flares
  • Know location of urgent care
  • Rest when needed
  • Use your tools (heat, ice, meds)
  • Be willing to modify plans
