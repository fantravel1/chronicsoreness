Disability Benefits

Financial support when pain prevents work

Overview

When chronic pain significantly limits your ability to work, disability benefits may provide financial support. Understanding the options and process can help you navigate this challenging system.1

Types of Benefits (US)

Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI)

  • Based on work history and credits earned
  • Must have worked enough recent years
  • Benefit amount based on earnings history
  • Includes Medicare after 24 months

Supplemental Security Income (SSI)

  • Need-based program
  • For limited income and resources
  • No work history requirement
  • Often includes Medicaid

Qualifying for Disability2

  • Unable to do previous work
  • Unable to adjust to other work
  • Condition expected to last 12+ months or result in death
  • Must have medical evidence

Documenting Your Condition3

  • Regular medical treatment records
  • Detailed descriptions of limitations
  • Functional capacity evaluation
  • Mental health documentation if applicable
  • Medication records and side effects

The Application Process

  • Initial application (often denied)
  • Reconsideration
  • Administrative Law Judge hearing
  • Appeals Council review
  • Federal court

Tips for Success

  • Keep detailed records
  • Follow all treatments
  • Be honest about limitations
  • Consider a disability attorney
  • Don't give up after initial denial
