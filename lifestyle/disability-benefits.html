Disability Benefits

Understanding support for chronic pain

Overview

When chronic pain prevents you from working, disability benefits may provide financial support. The process can be challenging, especially for pain conditions, but benefits are available for those who qualify. Understanding the system helps you navigate it more effectively.1

Types of Benefits (U.S.)2

Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI)

  • For those who have worked and paid SS taxes
  • Based on work credits earned
  • Amount depends on earnings history
  • Includes Medicare after 24 months

Supplemental Security Income (SSI)

  • Need-based program
  • For limited income/resources
  • Includes Medicaid in most states
  • Doesn't require work history

What SSA Considers

  • Medical evidence of condition
  • Severity and duration
  • Impact on ability to work
  • Expected to last 12+ months or result in death
  • Cannot do previous work or adjust to other work

Challenges with Pain Claims3

  • Pain is subjective
  • May lack objective findings
  • Initial denial rates high
  • Documentation crucial
  • Consistency in records matters

Building Your Case

Medical Documentation

  • Regular treatment records
  • Detailed doctor notes
  • Functional limitations documented
  • Medications and side effects
  • Consistent reporting of symptoms

Other Evidence

  • Work history
  • Third-party statements
  • Pain diaries/journals
  • Functional capacity evaluation

The Application Process

  • Apply online, phone, or in person
  • Provide detailed work history
  • List all medical providers
  • Describe daily activities affected
  • Process takes months (often 3-6+)

If Denied

  • Most applications initially denied
  • Appeal within 60 days
  • Reconsideration first step
  • Administrative Law Judge hearing
  • Consider disability attorney

Working with an Attorney

  • No upfront cost (paid from back benefits)
  • Fee limited by law (25% up to max)
  • Helpful for appeals process
  • Know the system

Other Options

  • Private disability insurance
  • State disability programs
  • Veterans disability
  • Workplace accommodations
