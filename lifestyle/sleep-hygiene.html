Sleep Hygiene

Building habits for better sleep

Why Sleep Matters for Pain

Sleep and pain have a bidirectional relationshipâ€”pain disrupts sleep, and poor sleep increases pain sensitivity. Improving sleep is one of the most effective ways to improve pain management.1

Sleep Schedule

  • Same bedtime and wake time daily
  • Even on weekends (within 1 hour)
  • Avoid naps >30 minutes after 3 PM
  • If unable to sleep after 20 min, get up

Sleep Environment2

  • Cool - 65-68Â°F (18-20Â°C)
  • Dark - Blackout curtains, eye mask
  • Quiet - Earplugs, white noise
  • Comfortable - Good mattress and pillows
  • Bed for sleep and intimacy only

Evening Routine

  • Wind down 1-2 hours before bed
  • Limit screens (or use blue light filter)
  • Relaxation techniques
  • Warm bath or shower
  • Avoid heavy meals late
  • Limit fluids before bed

Substances to Limit3

  • Caffeine - None after early afternoon
  • Alcohol - Disrupts sleep quality
  • Nicotine - Stimulant
  • Cannabis - May help initially but affects quality

Daytime Habits

  • Morning light exposure
  • Regular exercise (not too close to bedtime)
  • Manage stress
  • Address pain before bed

When to Seek Help

  • Persistent insomnia despite good hygiene
  • Suspected sleep apnea
  • Restless legs syndrome
  • Consider CBT for insomnia (CBT-I)
