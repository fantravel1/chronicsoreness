Sleep Hygiene

Better sleep despite pain

The Pain-Sleep Connection

Pain and sleep have a bidirectional relationship. Pain makes it harder to sleep, and poor sleep makes pain worse. About 50-80% of people with chronic pain have sleep problems. Improving sleep is a key part of pain management.1

Your Bed

  • Supportive mattress (not too soft or firm)
  • Comfortable pillows
  • Consider body pillows for positioning
  • Replace mattress every 7-10 years

Sleep Schedule

  • Go to bed and wake at same time daily
  • Yes, even on weekends
  • Consistency trains your body clock
  • Wake time is most important

Evening Routine3

Wind Down

  • Start 30-60 minutes before bed
  • Dim lights
  • Relaxing activities (reading, gentle stretching)
  • Warm bath or shower
  • Relaxation exercises

Avoid Before Bed

  • Screens (blue light affects melatonin)
  • Caffeine (stop 6+ hours before bed)
  • Alcohol (disrupts sleep quality)
  • Large meals
  • Intense exercise
  • Stimulating activities

Pain-Specific Tips

  • Take pain medication timed for sleep
  • Use pillows for positioning/support
  • Try gentle stretching before bed
  • Relaxation techniques for pain
  • Heat or ice before bed if helpful

If You Can't Sleep

  • Don't watch the clock
  • If awake 20+ minutes, get up
  • Do something quiet and boring
  • Return to bed when sleepy
  • Avoid napping if sleep is poor

When to Seek Help

  • Sleep problems persisting despite good habits
  • Loud snoring or breathing pauses
  • Excessive daytime sleepiness
  • Consider sleep study or CBT for insomnia
