Travel with Chronic Pain

Making trips manageable

Planning Ahead

Successful travel with chronic pain requires advance planning. While it may take extra effort, travel is often possible with the right preparation and realistic expectations.1

Before You Go

  • Talk to your doctor about travel plans
  • Get enough medication (plus extra)
  • Request doctor's letter for medications
  • Research medical facilities at destination
  • Consider travel insurance
  • Plan rest days into itinerary

Flying Tips2

  • Request wheelchair/mobility assistance
  • Board early if available
  • Request aisle seat (easier movement)
  • Get up and move regularly
  • Bring neck pillow, cushion
  • Stay hydrated
  • Pack medications in carry-on
  • Consider compression socks

Road Trip Strategies

  • Take regular breaks (every 1-2 hours)
  • Stretch at rest stops
  • Use lumbar support
  • Adjust seat position frequently
  • Split driving with others
  • Plan shorter driving days

Packing Essentials3

  • All medications (in original bottles)
  • Doctor's letter for controlled substances
  • Heat/cold packs (portable)
  • TENS unit if you use one
  • Comfortable shoes
  • Supportive pillows
  • List of medications and conditions

At Your Destination

  • Request accessible room if needed
  • Pace activities
  • Build in rest days
  • Don't try to do everything
  • Have backup plans
  • Know local emergency numbers

Managing Expectations

  • Travel may increase pain temporarily
  • Flexibility is key
  • Focus on what you can do
  • Rest before big activities
  • It's okay to say no to activities
