Working from Home with Chronic Pain

Making remote work work for you

The Opportunity

Working from home can be a significant advantage for people with chronic pain. It eliminates commuting, allows for flexible positioning and breaks, and gives you control over your environment. However, without proper setup and boundaries, it can also lead to worsening pain. Here's how to make it work for you.1

Advantages

  • No painful commute
  • Control over your environment
  • Flexibility in positioning
  • Easier to take breaks
  • Can lie down during breaks
  • Use heating pads, ice while working
  • Wear comfortable clothing

Challenges

  • May have poor ergonomic setup
  • Harder to maintain boundaries
  • Less movement throughout day
  • Isolation and loneliness
  • Distractions
  • Work-life blurring

Setting Up Your Space2

Desk and Chair

  • Adjustable chair essential
  • Feet flat on floor
  • Monitor at eye level
  • Keyboard at elbow height
  • Consider standing desk option

Alternative Setups

  • Standing desk converters
  • Kneeling chairs
  • Saddle stools
  • Laptop stands and separate keyboard
  • Multiple work positions

Managing Pain While Working

  • Change positions frequently
  • Set movement reminders
  • Do micro-breaks every 30 minutes
  • Stretch between tasks
  • Use voice-to-text when possible
  • Keep pain relief tools nearby

Structuring Your Day3

  • Work during your best hours
  • Build in rest periods
  • Block time for movement
  • Set clear start/end times
  • Don't skip lunch breaks
  • Separate work and rest spaces

Communicating with Employers

  • Discuss accommodation needs
  • Request ergonomic equipment
  • Be clear about availability
  • Document arrangements
  • Know your rights

Avoiding Common Mistakes

  • Working from couch or bed regularly
  • Skipping all breaks
  • Working longer hours than at office
  • Ignoring pain signals
  • Not moving for hours

Self-Care While Remote

  • Maintain social connections
  • Get outside daily if possible
  • Keep regular hours when you can
  • Don't isolate completely
  • Keep up with appointments
