Communicating with Doctors

Making the most of appointments

Why Communication Matters

Good communication with your healthcare providers is essential for effective pain management. Appointments are often short, so being prepared helps ensure your concerns are addressed and your care is optimized.1

Before Your Appointment2

Prepare

  • Write down your questions (prioritize top 3)
  • List all current medications
  • Note what you've tried and how it worked
  • Keep a pain diary if helpful
  • Bring relevant medical records

Pain Diary Elements

  • Pain level (0-10)
  • Location and type of pain
  • What makes it better or worse
  • How it affects daily activities
  • Sleep quality
  • Medication effects

During the Appointment

Describe Your Pain3

  • Location - Where exactly?
  • Quality - Sharp, burning, aching?
  • Intensity - Scale of 0-10
  • Timing - When does it occur?
  • Duration - How long does it last?
  • Impact - How does it affect your life?

Be Honest

  • Don't minimize or exaggerate
  • Share what's really happening
  • Mention if treatments aren't working
  • Discuss all treatments you're using

Ask Questions

  • "What do you think is causing my pain?"
  • "What are my treatment options?"
  • "What are the risks and benefits?"
  • "What should I expect?"
  • "When should I follow up?"

If You Feel Unheard

  • Speak up respectfully
  • "I don't feel my concerns are being addressed"
  • Ask for a longer appointment
  • Bring a support person
  • Consider a second opinion
  • Find a provider who listens

After the Appointment

  • Review any written instructions
  • Fill prescriptions promptly
  • Follow up as directed
  • Call if you have questions

Building a Care Team

  • Primary care provider
  • Pain specialist if needed
  • Physical therapist
  • Mental health provider
  • Coordinate between providers
