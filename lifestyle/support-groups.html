Support Groups

Finding community in the pain journey

Why Support Groups Help

Living with chronic pain can be isolating. Support groups connect you with others who understand what you're going through, providing emotional support, practical tips, and a sense of community.1

Benefits of Peer Support2

  • Reduced feelings of isolation
  • Validation of experiences
  • Learning coping strategies from others
  • Practical tips and resources
  • Emotional support
  • Hope from seeing others managing
  • Opportunity to help others

Types of Support Groups

In-Person Groups

  • Hospital or clinic-based
  • Community center groups
  • Condition-specific organizations
  • Church or faith-based groups

Online Groups

  • Social media groups (Facebook, Reddit)
  • Forums and message boards
  • Virtual meetings (Zoom)
  • Accessible from home
  • Available 24/7

Finding a Group3

  • Ask your healthcare provider
  • Contact condition-specific organizations
  • Search online (Pain Connection, etc.)
  • Check local hospitals and community centers
  • Look for local chapters of national organizations

Making the Most of Support Groups

  • Try different groups to find good fit
  • Be open but protect your privacy
  • Give and receive support
  • Focus on coping, not just venting
  • Be cautious about medical advice
  • Remember everyone's experience is different
