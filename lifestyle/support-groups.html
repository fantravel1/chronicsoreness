Support Groups

Finding community with chronic pain

Why Support Groups Help

Chronic pain can be isolating. Support groups connect you with others who truly understand what you're going through. Research shows that social support can actually reduce pain perception and improve coping.1

Benefits2

  • Reduced feelings of isolation
  • "I'm not alone" realization
  • Learning from others' experiences
  • Practical tips and strategies
  • Emotional support
  • Increased hope
  • Safe space to express feelings
  • Sense of belonging

Types of Support Groups

In-Person Groups

  • Hospital or clinic-based
  • Community organizations
  • Disease-specific organizations
  • Local pain foundations

Online Groups

  • Forums and message boards
  • Social media groups (Facebook, Reddit)
  • Video-based support groups
  • Apps for chronic pain community

Professionally Led vs. Peer-Led

  • Professional facilitation provides structure
  • Peer-led groups offer shared experience
  • Both can be valuable

Finding Groups3

  • Ask your doctor or pain clinic
  • Check disease-specific organizations
  • Search online for chronic pain groups
  • Hospital community programs
  • Mental health centers
  • Places of worship

Making the Most of It

  • Give it a few sessions before judging
  • Share when you're comfortable
  • Listen to others
  • Take what helps, leave what doesn't
  • Keep information confidential
  • Avoid comparing pain

If a Group Isn't Right

  • Try a different group
  • Online might suit you better than in-person (or vice versa)
  • Condition-specific might help
  • One-on-one peer support options exist

Starting Your Own

If you can't find a group, consider starting one. Partner with a local clinic, library, or community center. Even a small informal group can provide meaningful support.
