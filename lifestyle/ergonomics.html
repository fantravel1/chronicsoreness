Ergonomics for Chronic Pain

Optimizing your environment to reduce pain

What Is Ergonomics?

Ergonomics is the science of designing workspaces and activities to fit people's bodies, reducing strain and preventing injury. Good ergonomics can help manage and prevent chronic pain.1

Workspace Setup

Monitor

  • Top of screen at or slightly below eye level
  • About arm's length away
  • Directly in front of you
  • Reduce glare

Keyboard and Mouse

  • Elbows at 90-degree angle
  • Wrists neutral (not bent up or down)
  • Shoulders relaxed
  • Mouse close to keyboard

Chair

  • Feet flat on floor (or footrest)
  • Knees at 90 degrees
  • Lower back supported
  • Armrests at comfortable height

Ergonomic Equipment2

  • Ergonomic keyboard
  • Vertical mouse
  • Monitor arm
  • Sit-stand desk
  • Lumbar support
  • Footrest
  • Document holder

Beyond the Desk

  • Driving: Seat position, lumbar support
  • Sleeping: Mattress, pillow position
  • Lifting: Proper technique
  • Kitchen: Counter height, reaching

Movement Matters

  • No posture is perfect for hours
  • Change positions frequently
  • Take micro-breaks every 20-30 minutes
  • The best posture is the next one
