Ergonomics

Working smarter to reduce pain

What Is Ergonomics?

Ergonomics is the science of designing workspaces and tasks to fit the human body. Good ergonomics reduces physical stress on your body, preventing pain and injury while improving comfort and productivity.1

Desk Workstation Setup2

Chair

  • Feet flat on floor (or footrest)
  • Knees at 90 degrees or slightly more
  • Back supported, especially lower back
  • Armrests at elbow height
  • Seat pan supports thighs without pressure behind knees

Monitor

  • Top of screen at or slightly below eye level
  • Arm's length away
  • Directly in front of you
  • No glare on screen
  • Consider monitor arm for adjustability

Keyboard and Mouse

  • Elbows at 90-degree angle
  • Wrists neutral (not bent up or down)
  • Mouse close to keyboard
  • Keyboard at elbow height or slightly lower
  • Consider ergonomic keyboard/mouse

Movement and Breaks3

  • Move every 30-60 minutes
  • Microbreaks every 20-30 minutes
  • Stretch regularly
  • Consider sit-stand desk
  • Walk during phone calls
  • 20-20-20 rule for eyes

Laptop Ergonomics

  • Use external keyboard and mouse
  • Raise laptop to eye level
  • Don't work on couch or bed regularly
  • Consider laptop stand

Standing Desk Tips

  • Alternate sitting and standing
  • Use anti-fatigue mat
  • Monitor and keyboard at proper heights
  • Wear supportive shoes
  • Don't lock knees

Common Ergonomic Problems

  • Monitor too low (neck strain)
  • Chair too high or low
  • Mouse too far away (shoulder strain)
  • Keyboard too high (wrist strain)
  • Sitting too long without breaks
