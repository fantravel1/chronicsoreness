Ergonomics

Setting up your environment to reduce pain

What Is Ergonomics?

Ergonomics is the science of designing workspaces and activities to fit the person, reducing strain and preventing injury. For people with chronic pain, proper ergonomics can help reduce pain triggers, minimize strain on vulnerable areas, and allow longer, more comfortable activity.1

Computer Workstation Setup2

Chair

  • Feet flat on floor (or footrest)
  • Knees at about 90 degrees
  • Good lumbar support
  • Armrests at elbow height
  • Adjustable height

Monitor

  • Top of screen at eye level
  • About arm's length away
  • Reduce glare
  • Consider larger font size

Keyboard and Mouse

  • Elbows at 90-degree angle
  • Wrists neutral (not bent up or down)
  • Mouse close to keyboard
  • Consider ergonomic keyboard/mouse

Movement at Work3

  • Take breaks every 30-60 minutes
  • Stand and stretch
  • Change positions frequently
  • Consider sit-stand desk
  • Walk during phone calls

Home Ergonomics

Kitchen

  • Store frequently used items at waist height
  • Use reaching tools
  • Sit while prepping food
  • Ergonomic kitchen tools

Bedroom

  • Supportive mattress
  • Proper pillow height
  • Easy to get in/out of bed

Bathroom

  • Grab bars if needed
  • Non-slip mats
  • Shower chair or bench
  • Long-handled tools

Lifting and Carrying

  • Bend at knees, not waist
  • Keep load close to body
  • Avoid twisting while lifting
  • Push rather than pull when possible
  • Ask for help with heavy items

Driving

  • Adjust seat for good back support
  • Take breaks on long drives
  • Use lumbar support
  • Mirrors adjusted to minimize turning

Getting Professional Help

  • Occupational therapist
  • Ergonomic consultant
  • Physical therapist
  • Workplace ergonomic assessment
