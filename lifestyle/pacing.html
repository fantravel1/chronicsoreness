Pacing

Finding balance in activity and rest

What Is Pacing?

Pacing is a self-management strategy that involves balancing activity and rest to avoid triggering pain flares. It helps break the boom-bust cycle where overdoing it on good days leads to days of increased pain and inactivity.1

How Pacing Works2

  • Set time limits, not task completion goals
  • Take regular breaks before pain increases
  • Maintain consistent activity level day to day
  • Gradually increase activity over time
  • Plan ahead for demanding days

Practical Strategies

Time-Based Approach

  • Determine your baseline (sustainable time for activities)
  • Set timer for activity periods
  • Take scheduled breaks (5-15 minutes)
  • Switch between different types of tasks

Priority Setting

  • Identify must-do vs. nice-to-do tasks
  • Spread demanding tasks across days
  • Delegate when possible
  • Learn to say no

Finding Your Baseline3

  • Track activities and pain levels
  • Find the point before pain increases
  • Start at 80% of that level
  • Be consistent for 1-2 weeks
  • Gradually increase (10% at a time)

Common Challenges

  • Feeling lazy on good days
  • Guilt about not finishing tasks
  • External pressures and expectations
  • Unpredictable symptom patterns

Benefits

  • Fewer severe flares
  • More consistent daily function
  • Gradual activity increase over time
  • Better sense of control
  • Improved quality of life
