Medical Self-Advocacy

Speaking up for your healthcare needs

Why Advocacy Matters

Self-advocacy in healthcare means actively participating in your care, communicating your needs, and ensuring you receive appropriate treatment. People with chronic pain often face unique challenges, including not being believed, difficulty getting diagnosis, and navigating complex healthcare systems. Learning to advocate for yourself can improve your care.1

Challenges Pain Patients Face2

  • Not being believed
  • Invisible nature of pain
  • Short appointment times
  • Difficulty describing pain
  • Feeling dismissed
  • Complex conditions
  • Stigma around chronic pain

Preparing for Appointments

  • Write down your main concerns
  • Prioritize 2-3 key issues
  • List current medications
  • Note what's been tried
  • Track pain patterns
  • Write down questions
  • Bring someone if helpful

Communicating Effectively

  • Be specific about symptoms
  • Use pain scales
  • Describe impact on function
  • State your goals clearly
  • Ask questions
  • Repeat back to confirm understanding

Useful Phrases3

  • "I need you to hear me on this..."
  • "Can you help me understand..."
  • "What else could this be?"
  • "I'd like this documented in my chart"
  • "What are my options?"
  • "Can you explain your reasoning?"

Know Your Rights

  • Right to be treated with respect
  • Right to informed consent
  • Right to second opinions
  • Right to see your medical records
  • Right to ask questions
  • Right to refuse treatment

When You Feel Dismissed

  • Stay calm but firm
  • Ask for documentation
  • Request referral or second opinion
  • Consider finding new provider
  • Follow up in writing if needed

Building Good Relationships

  • Come prepared
  • Be honest about everything
  • Follow through on recommendations
  • Communicate between visits
  • Express appreciation
  • Give feedback constructively

Bringing Support

  • Family member or friend
  • Help take notes
  • Provide additional perspective
  • Emotional support
  • Professional patient advocates exist

After Appointments

  • Review what was discussed
  • Follow up on tests/referrals
  • Keep records
  • Note questions for next time
