Traveling with Chronic Pain

Tips for managing pain while on the go

Planning Ahead

Travel can be challenging with chronic pain, but with preparation, many people with pain conditions can travel successfully. Planning ahead is key to minimizing flares and maximizing enjoyment.1

Before You Go

  • Talk to your doctor about travel plans
  • Get enough medication (plus extra)
  • Request a doctor's letter for medications
  • Research healthcare options at destination
  • Consider travel insurance
  • Rest well before departure

Packing Essentials

  • Medications in carry-on (original bottles)
  • Pain relief supplies (heat/cold packs)
  • Comfortable travel pillow
  • Supportive footwear
  • Compression socks for long trips
  • TENS unit (if you use one)

Air Travel Tips2

  • Request wheelchair/assistance if needed
  • Pre-board for extra time
  • Request aisle seat for mobility
  • Get up and move hourly
  • Stretch in your seat
  • Stay hydrated
  • Consider TSA PreCheck for easier screening

Road Trip Tips

  • Take frequent breaks (every 1-2 hours)
  • Stretch at rest stops
  • Use lumbar support
  • Share driving if possible
  • Adjust seat position for comfort
  • Plan shorter driving days

At Your Destination

  • Rest after arrival
  • Don't overdo first day
  • Pace activities throughout trip
  • Build in rest days
  • Know your limits
  • Have backup plans if flares occur

Managing Time Zones

  • Adjust medication timing gradually
  • Set reminders for medications
  • Maintain sleep schedule as possible
  • Allow extra recovery time

Accommodations

  • Request accessible room if needed
  • Check for elevator availability
  • Ask about bed firmness
  • Consider locations near activities
