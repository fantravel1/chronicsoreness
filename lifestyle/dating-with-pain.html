Dating with Chronic Pain

Building new relationships

You Deserve Love

Dating with chronic pain can feel daunting. Concerns about when to share your condition, fear of rejection, and physical limitations can all create anxiety. But chronic pain doesn't make you unworthy of a loving relationship. Many people with chronic conditions find fulfilling partnerships.1

Common Concerns

  • When should I tell them?
  • Will they reject me?
  • Can I handle a relationship?
  • What if I have to cancel plans?
  • How will they react?
  • Am I a burden?

When to Disclose2

No One Right Answer

  • Depends on comfort level
  • Don't have to share on first date
  • But don't wait too long
  • Before it becomes serious makes sense
  • When it naturally comes up

How Much to Share

  • Start with basics
  • You don't owe full medical history
  • Share more as trust builds
  • Match their investment level

Practical Tips

Date Planning

  • Choose accessible locations
  • Plan for good pain times
  • Have backup plans
  • Suggest comfortable activities
  • It's okay to suggest sitting dates

Managing Symptoms

  • Time medications appropriately
  • Bring what you need (meds, heat pack)
  • Don't push through severe pain
  • Have exit strategy if needed

If You Need to Cancel

  • Be honest but brief
  • Suggest alternative plan
  • Don't over-apologize
  • Their response tells you a lot

Red Flags in Partners

  • Dismissing your pain
  • "You don't look sick"
  • Pressuring you to push through
  • Making you feel guilty
  • Lack of flexibility

Green Flags

  • Asks questions with genuine interest
  • Flexible about plans
  • Doesn't make it about them
  • Shows understanding
  • Actions match words

Working on Yourself3

  • Address internalized shame
  • Know your worth
  • You bring more than your pain
  • Therapy can help with self-esteem

Remember

  • Right person will accept you
  • Chronic pain is filter for wrong people
  • You have a lot to offer
  • Take things at your pace
