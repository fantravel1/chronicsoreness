Pain and Intimacy

Maintaining connection with chronic pain

The Challenge

Chronic pain can significantly affect intimate relationships and sexuality. Pain, fatigue, medications, and emotional factors can all impact desire, ability, and satisfaction. Many people feel embarrassed to discuss this topic, but it's a common concern and help is available.1

How Pain Affects Intimacy2

Physical Factors

  • Pain during physical activity
  • Limited range of motion
  • Fatigue and low energy
  • Medication side effects
  • Fear of triggering pain

Emotional Factors

  • Body image concerns
  • Depression and anxiety
  • Feeling undesirable
  • Guilt about limitations
  • Loss of spontaneity

Communication

  • Honest conversation with partner is essential
  • Share what helps and what hurts
  • Discuss needs and concerns
  • Talk outside the bedroom (less pressure)
  • Use "I" statements

Practical Strategies3

Timing

  • Plan for better times of day
  • When medications are at peak effect
  • When energy is highest
  • Spontaneity isn't the only path to intimacy

Positioning

  • Use supportive pillows
  • Try positions that reduce strain
  • Side-lying can reduce pressure
  • Experiment to find what works

Preparation

  • Warm bath or shower
  • Gentle stretching
  • Pain medication timing
  • Heat or ice therapy beforehand

Expanding Intimacy

  • Intimacy is more than intercourse
  • Touch, massage, cuddling
  • Emotional closeness
  • Quality time together
  • Verbal expressions of love

Medication Considerations

  • Some medications affect libido or function
  • Opioids commonly reduce sex hormones
  • Antidepressants can have sexual side effects
  • Discuss concerns with healthcare provider
  • Adjustments may be possible

When to Seek Help

  • Persistent sexual difficulties
  • Relationship strain
  • Physical problems (discuss with doctor)
  • Sex therapy or couples counseling
  • Pelvic floor physical therapy

For Partners

  • Be patient and understanding
  • Don't take it personally
  • Communicate your needs too
  • Find other ways to connect
  • Learn about their condition
