Assistive Devices

Tools to make life easier

Why Use Assistive Devices?

Assistive devices can reduce strain, protect joints, and help maintain independence. Using the right tools isn't giving upâ€”it's smart adaptation that can help you do more with less pain.1

Mobility Aids2

  • Canes - Reduce weight on painful joints
  • Walkers - Greater stability
  • Rollators - Walker with wheels and seat
  • Wheelchairs - For longer distances
  • Scooters - Powered mobility

Kitchen Aids

  • Electric can openers
  • Jar openers
  • Built-up utensil handles
  • Rocker knives
  • Lightweight pots and pans
  • Perching stools

Bathroom Aids

  • Grab bars
  • Raised toilet seats
  • Shower chairs
  • Handheld showerheads
  • Long-handled sponges

Dressing Aids

  • Button hooks
  • Zipper pulls
  • Long-handled shoehorns
  • Elastic shoelaces
  • Dressing sticks
  • Sock aids

Reaching and Gripping3

  • Reachers/grabbers
  • Key turners
  • Door lever handles
  • Built-up pen grips

Getting Started

  • Occupational therapist can assess needs
  • Try before you buy if possible
  • Insurance may cover some items
  • Many available at medical supply stores

Overcoming Reluctance

  • Using aids is practical, not weakness
  • Conserves energy for what matters
  • May prevent further damage
  • Maintains independence longer
