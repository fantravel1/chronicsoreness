Workplace Accommodations

Staying productive with chronic pain

Your Rights

The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) requires employers with 15+ employees to provide reasonable accommodations for qualified employees with disabilities, including chronic pain conditions that substantially limit major life activities.1

Common Accommodations2

Physical Workspace

  • Ergonomic chair or standing desk
  • Adjusted workstation height
  • Footrest, keyboard tray
  • Voice recognition software
  • Accessible parking

Schedule Modifications

  • Flexible start/end times
  • Frequent short breaks
  • Modified work schedule
  • Part-time options
  • Leave for medical appointments

Job Modifications

  • Remote work options
  • Modified job duties
  • Reassignment of marginal functions
  • Rest area access

Requesting Accommodations3

  1. Document your needs (doctor's note helpful)
  2. Request in writing
  3. Propose specific accommodations
  4. Engage in "interactive process"
  5. Follow up in writing

Tips for Success

  • Focus on what you can do
  • Be specific about needs
  • Propose solutions, not just problems
  • Keep documentation
  • Know your job's essential functions

What Employers Can't Do

  • Refuse to consider accommodations
  • Retaliate for requesting
  • Require you to accept unwanted accommodations
