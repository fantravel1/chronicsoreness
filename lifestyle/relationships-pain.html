Relationships and Pain

Navigating connections with chronic pain

How Pain Affects Relationships

Chronic pain impacts not just the person experiencing it, but their relationships as well. Roles may shift, activities change, and communication becomes more important than ever. Understanding these dynamics can help strengthen relationships.1

Common Challenges2

  • Changes in roles and responsibilities
  • Difficulty with physical activities together
  • Impact on intimacy
  • Feeling misunderstood
  • Cancelling plans unpredictably
  • Financial stress
  • Caregiver burnout

Communication Tips

For the Person with Pain

  • Be specific about what you need
  • Share how pain affects you
  • Don't assume others understand
  • Express appreciation for support
  • Ask about their experience too

For Partners/Loved Ones

  • Ask how they're feeling
  • Believe their experience
  • Offer specific help
  • Maintain your own interests
  • Share your feelings too

Maintaining Connection3

  • Find activities you can still do together
  • Create new traditions
  • Focus on quality time, not quantity
  • Be flexible with plans
  • Date nights don't have to mean going out

Intimacy and Pain

  • Pain often affects physical intimacy
  • Communication is essential
  • Explore adaptations and timing
  • Focus on connection, not performance
  • Consider counseling if needed

When to Seek Help

  • Relationship significantly suffering
  • Communication breaking down
  • Resentment building
  • Couples therapy can help
