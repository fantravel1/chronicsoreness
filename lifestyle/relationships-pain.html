Relationships and Pain

Navigating connections while living with chronic pain

Pain's Impact on Relationships

Chronic pain affects not just the individual but their relationships with partners, family, and friends. Role changes, communication challenges, and the invisible nature of pain can strain connections. But relationships can also be powerful sources of support.1

Common Challenges

  • Others not understanding invisible pain
  • Role changes in household
  • Reduced participation in activities
  • Financial stress from medical costs
  • Caregiver burnout
  • Intimacy changes
  • Social withdrawal

Communication Strategies2

  • Be specific about what you need
  • Explain pain in relatable terms
  • Share how pain affects your day
  • Ask for specific help (not general)
  • Express appreciation for support
  • Use "I" statements

For Partners/Family

  • Believe their experience
  • Don't assume you know what they need
  • Offer support without taking over
  • Take care of your own needs too
  • Learn about their condition
  • Attend appointments together sometimes

Maintaining Intimacy

  • Communicate openly about needs
  • Plan intimacy for better times
  • Explore different positions
  • Focus on closeness, not just physical
  • Be patient with changes
  • Consider couples counseling

Maintaining Friendships3

  • Be honest about limitations
  • Suggest activities you can do
  • Stay connected even when you can't be active
  • Accept that some friendships may change
  • Cultivate understanding friends

Getting Support

  • Support groups (in-person or online)
  • Couples/family therapy
  • Individual therapy
  • Caregiver support resources
