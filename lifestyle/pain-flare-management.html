Pain Flare Management

Coping when pain intensifies

What Is a Pain Flare?

A pain flare is a temporary increase in pain intensity or symptoms beyond your usual baseline. Flares are common in chronic pain conditions and can be triggered by various factorsâ€”or sometimes seem to happen without any clear reason. Having a plan for flares can help you cope more effectively.1

Common Flare Triggers2

  • Overdoing activities
  • Poor sleep
  • Stress or emotional upset
  • Weather changes
  • Illness or infection
  • Hormonal changes
  • Dietary factors
  • Skipping medications
  • Sometimes unknown

Immediate Strategies

Physical Comfort

  • Apply heat or ice (whichever helps)
  • Change position
  • Gentle stretching if tolerated
  • Supportive positioning with pillows
  • Take a warm bath

Medications

  • Take prescribed as-needed medications
  • Don't wait until pain is severe
  • Use topicals if helpful
  • Follow your flare plan

Rest vs. Activity

  • Rest more than usual but not completely
  • Gentle movement prevents stiffness
  • Avoid pushing through severe pain
  • Pace yourself

Mental Strategies3

  • Remind yourself flares are temporary
  • Use relaxation techniques
  • Distraction (TV, audiobooks, music)
  • Avoid catastrophic thinking
  • Practice self-compassion
  • Accept help from others

Creating a Flare Plan

  • Write it down when feeling better
  • List what helps during flares
  • Include medication instructions
  • Note who to contact if needed
  • Keep supplies accessible
  • Share plan with support people

Flare Kit Essentials

  • Heating pad/ice packs
  • Medications
  • Comfortable clothes
  • Phone/entertainment
  • Easy snacks and water
  • Relaxation aids
  • Emergency contact information

When to Seek Help

  • New or different symptoms
  • Pain much worse than typical flares
  • Fever, weakness, or other concerning signs
  • Symptoms not improving as expected
  • Thoughts of self-harm

After the Flare

  • Return to activities gradually
  • Reflect on possible triggers
  • Update your flare plan
  • Practice self-compassion
  • Don't try to "catch up" immediately
