Work Modifications

Accommodations for working with chronic pain

Working with Chronic Pain

Many people with chronic pain want to and can continue working with appropriate accommodations. Work provides income, structure, social connection, and sense of purpose. Understanding your rights and options helps you maintain employment while managing your condition.1

Legal Protections2

Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)

  • Covers employers with 15+ employees
  • Chronic pain may qualify as disability
  • Requires "reasonable accommodations"
  • Cannot discriminate based on disability
  • Must be able to perform essential job functions

FMLA (Family Medical Leave Act)

  • Up to 12 weeks unpaid leave annually
  • Can be taken intermittently
  • Job protection during leave
  • Covers employers with 50+ employees

Common Accommodations3

Physical Modifications

  • Ergonomic equipment (chair, keyboard, monitor)
  • Sit-stand desk
  • Footrest, lumbar support
  • Closer parking spot
  • Rest area access

Schedule Modifications

  • Flexible start/end times
  • Modified break schedule
  • Part-time or reduced hours
  • Telecommuting options
  • Leave for medical appointments

Job Modifications

  • Modified job duties
  • Job restructuring
  • Transfer to different position
  • Reduced lifting requirements

Requesting Accommodations

  • Put request in writing
  • Be specific about what you need
  • Explain how it will help you work
  • Provide medical documentation if requested
  • Engage in interactive process with employer

Communication Strategies

  • Focus on solutions, not just problems
  • Emphasize what you can do
  • Be honest but strategic about disclosure
  • Document everything
  • Know you don't have to disclose diagnosis

Self-Advocacy at Work

  • Know your rights
  • Keep records of accommodations and responses
  • Build relationships with HR
  • Consider involving union if applicable
  • Seek legal advice if facing discrimination

Self-Care at Work

  • Take breaks to stretch and move
  • Use good posture
  • Pace your activities
  • Practice stress management
  • Stay hydrated
  • Know your limits
