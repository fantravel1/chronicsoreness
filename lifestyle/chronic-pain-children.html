Parenting with Chronic Pain

Being present for your children

The Challenge

Parenting is demanding even without chronic pain. When you're dealing with persistent pain, fatigue, and limitations, meeting your children's needs while managing your health becomes a daily balancing act. Many parents with chronic pain struggle with guilt and fear they're not enough for their children.1

Common Concerns

  • Missing activities and events
  • Not being able to play like other parents
  • Children taking on caregiving roles
  • Impact on children's emotional well-being
  • Guilt about limitations
  • Partner carrying more burden

Talking to Children About Pain2

Keep It Age-Appropriate

  • Young children: simple explanations
  • "Mommy/Daddy has a boo-boo inside that doesn't go away"
  • Older children can understand more
  • Teens can learn about the condition

Key Messages

  • It's not their fault
  • They can't "catch" it
  • Doctors are helping
  • You still love being their parent
  • It's okay to ask questions

Strategies for Parenting3

Pacing Family Life

  • Plan rest before and after activities
  • Prioritize what matters most
  • It's okay to say no sometimes
  • Create predictable routines

Adapting Activities

  • Find activities you can do together
  • Quality time doesn't require physical activity
  • Reading, games, crafts, movies
  • Be present even if sitting down

Managing Guilt

  • You are more than your limitations
  • Children need emotional connection most
  • Modeling resilience teaches valuable lessons
  • Imperfect parenting is still good parenting
  • Seek support for your feelings

Preventing Parentification

  • Age-appropriate responsibilities are okay
  • Children shouldn't be primary caregivers
  • Don't share adult worries with them
  • Ensure they have time to be kids
  • Monitor for signs of stress

Getting Support

  • Accept help from family/friends
  • Coordinate with your partner
  • Hire help if possible
  • Connect with other parents with chronic pain
  • Family therapy if needed

Taking Care of Yourself

  • Your health matters for them too
  • Taking rest helps you be better parent
  • Model self-care for children
  • Don't feel guilty for self-care
