Financial Impact of Pain

Managing the economic burden of chronic pain

The Reality

Chronic pain often brings significant financial challenges. Medical costs, lost wages, reduced work capacity, and the expense of managing daily life with pain can create substantial economic burden. Understanding your options and resources can help reduce this stress.1

Common Financial Impacts2

  • Medical expenses (appointments, treatments)
  • Medication costs
  • Lost wages from missed work
  • Reduced earning capacity
  • Job loss or career change
  • Equipment and aids
  • Home modifications
  • Childcare and household help

Managing Medical Costs

  • Understand your insurance coverage
  • Ask about generic medications
  • Use patient assistance programs
  • Compare pharmacy prices
  • Ask about payment plans
  • Negotiate medical bills
  • Check for sliding scale fees

Work-Related Options3

If Still Working

  • Workplace accommodations (ADA)
  • Flexible schedules
  • Remote work options
  • FMLA for medical leave
  • Short-term disability insurance

If Unable to Work

  • Long-term disability insurance
  • Social Security Disability (SSDI)
  • Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
  • State disability programs

Disability Benefits

  • SSDI: based on work history
  • SSI: need-based
  • Application process can be long
  • Many are initially denied
  • Appeals often successful
  • Consider disability attorney

Other Resources

  • Patient advocacy foundations
  • Disease-specific organizations
  • Community assistance programs
  • State vocational rehabilitation
  • Nonprofit financial counseling

Reducing Costs

  • Prioritize treatments with best evidence
  • Consider group programs vs individual
  • Use free resources (apps, videos)
  • Community exercise programs
  • Support groups (free)

Emotional Aspect

  • Financial stress worsens pain
  • Guilt about not contributing
  • Relationship strain over money
  • Loss of independence
  • Seek support for these feelings
