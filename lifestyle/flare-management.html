Flare Management

Coping when pain increases

What Is a Pain Flare?

A flare is a temporary increase in pain symptoms, often with accompanying fatigue and other symptoms. Flares are a normal part of living with chronic pain - they don't mean you've done something wrong or that your condition has permanently worsened.1

Common Flare Triggers

  • Overdoing activities
  • Stress
  • Poor sleep
  • Weather changes
  • Illness or infection
  • Emotional distress
  • Hormonal changes
  • Sometimes no clear trigger

During a Flare2

Physical Strategies

  • Reduce (don't stop) activity
  • Use heat or cold
  • Gentle stretching if tolerated
  • Adjust medications as directed
  • Prioritize sleep

Mental Strategies

  • Remind yourself flares are temporary
  • Avoid catastrophizing
  • Use relaxation techniques
  • Distract with enjoyable activities
  • Reach out for support

Creating a Flare Plan3

  • Write plan when you're feeling okay
  • List what helps during flares
  • Include emergency contacts
  • Note when to seek medical care
  • Keep plan accessible

Recovery

  • Return to normal activity gradually
  • Don't try to "make up" for lost time
  • Use pacing strategies
  • Be patient with yourself

When to Seek Help

  • New or different symptoms
  • Flare lasting unusually long
  • Symptoms not responding to usual strategies
  • Feeling unable to cope
