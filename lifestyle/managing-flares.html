Managing Pain Flares

Getting through difficult periods

What Is a Flare?

A flare (or flare-up) is a temporary worsening of chronic pain symptoms. Flares are common and can be triggered by various factors or occur without clear cause. Learning to manage flares can reduce their impact and help you recover more quickly.

Common Triggers

  • Overactivity (doing too much)
  • Stress
  • Poor sleep
  • Weather changes
  • Illness or infection
  • Emotional distress
  • Changes in routine
  • Sometimes no identifiable cause

Creating a Flare Plan

Before Flares Happen

  • Write down what helps during flares
  • Have supplies ready (medications, heat packs, etc.)
  • Plan how to reduce responsibilities
  • Identify who can help
  • Know when to contact your doctor

What to Include

  • List of helpful strategies
  • Medication plan (what to take, when)
  • Phone numbers for support
  • Comfort items and where they are
  • Self-care activities that help

During a Flare

Physical Strategies

  • Rest (but don't become completely inactive)
  • Heat or cold therapy
  • Gentle movement when possible
  • Use medications as prescribed
  • Comfortable positioning

Mental Strategies

  • Remind yourself flares are temporary
  • Practice relaxation techniques
  • Distraction (TV, audiobooks, podcasts)
  • Avoid catastrophizing
  • Accept help from others

What to Avoid

  • Complete bed rest (usually worsens things)
  • Pushing through to maintain normal activity
  • Excessive medication use
  • Isolation
  • Blaming yourself

Gradual Return

  • Resume activity slowly
  • Don't rush back to full activity
  • Listen to your body
  • Expect some setbacks

When to Seek Help

  • Symptoms significantly different than usual
  • New or concerning symptoms
  • Flare not responding to usual strategies
  • Unable to function at all
  • Thoughts of self-harm

Learning from Flares

  • Note what triggered it (if known)
  • Record what helped
  • Identify patterns
  • Adjust prevention strategies
  • Share insights with healthcare team

Talking to Others

  • Let loved ones know you're struggling
  • Be specific about what help you need
  • Accept offers of support
  • You don't have to go through flares alone
