Sleep and Chronic Pain

Breaking the pain-sleep cycle

The Pain-Sleep Connection

Sleep problems and chronic pain have a bidirectional relationshipâ€”pain disrupts sleep, and poor sleep makes pain worse. Up to 88% of people with chronic pain report sleep difficulties. Addressing sleep can be one of the most impactful interventions for pain management.1

How Pain Affects Sleep2

  • Difficulty falling asleep
  • Frequent awakening
  • Difficulty finding comfortable position
  • Non-restorative sleep
  • Reduced deep sleep stages
  • Early morning awakening

How Poor Sleep Affects Pain

  • Increases pain sensitivity
  • Reduces pain threshold
  • Impairs pain inhibition
  • Increases inflammation
  • Reduces coping ability
  • Worsens mood (which affects pain)

Sleep Hygiene Basics3

Schedule

  • Consistent bed and wake times
  • Even on weekends
  • Avoid long daytime naps

Environment

  • Dark, quiet, cool room
  • Comfortable mattress and pillows
  • Remove electronics from bedroom

Pre-Sleep Routine

  • Wind down 30-60 minutes before bed
  • Avoid screens (blue light)
  • Relaxation techniques
  • Avoid caffeine after noon
  • Limit alcohol (disrupts sleep quality)

Pain-Specific Sleep Tips

  • Supportive pillows for positioning
  • Body pillow between knees (side sleepers)
  • Pillow under knees (back sleepers)
  • Gentle stretching before bed
  • Warm bath for muscle relaxation
  • Time medications for night coverage

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I)

  • Most effective treatment for chronic insomnia
  • Works well even with chronic pain
  • Changes thoughts and behaviors around sleep
  • Available in person, online, or via apps
  • Effects are long-lasting

When to Seek Help

  • Sleep problems persist despite good habits
  • Snoring or gasping at night (sleep apnea)
  • Excessive daytime sleepiness
  • Restless legs at night
  • Need sleep medications frequently

Sleep Medications

  • Should be short-term when possible
  • Discuss with doctor
  • Some pain medications cause drowsiness
  • Be aware of interactions
