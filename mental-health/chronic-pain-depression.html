Depression and Chronic Pain

Understanding and treating co-occurring conditions

The Connection

Depression and chronic pain frequently occur together. Up to 85% of people with chronic pain experience depression, and people with depression are more likely to develop chronic pain. The two conditions share biological pathways and each can make the other worse, but treating both can improve outcomes.1

Why They're Connected2

  • Share common brain chemicals (serotonin, norepinephrine)
  • Pain causes loss, disability, isolation
  • Depression increases pain perception
  • Both involve stress systems
  • Sleep problems affect both
  • Reduced activity worsens both

Signs of Depression

  • Persistent sad or empty mood
  • Loss of interest or pleasure
  • Fatigue and low energy
  • Sleep problems (too much or too little)
  • Appetite changes
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Feelings of worthlessness
  • Thoughts of death or suicide

How Depression Worsens Pain3

  • Increases pain sensitivity
  • Reduces motivation for treatment
  • Interferes with healthy behaviors
  • Impairs coping ability
  • Worsens sleep (which worsens pain)
