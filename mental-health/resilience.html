Resilience and Chronic Pain

Bouncing back and moving forward

What Is Resilience?

Resilience is the ability to adapt and cope in the face of adversity. For people with chronic pain, resilience doesn't mean pretending pain doesn't exist or "just pushing through." It means developing the psychological flexibility and resources to maintain well-being and pursue a meaningful life despite ongoing challenges.1

Resilience Is Not2

  • Never struggling
  • Being positive all the time
  • Ignoring pain
  • Going it alone
  • Fixedâ€”it fluctuates
  • Something you either have or don't

Components of Pain Resilience

Acceptance

  • Acknowledging reality of pain
  • Not fighting against it constantly
  • Redirecting energy to living

Self-Efficacy

  • Belief in ability to manage
  • Confidence in coping skills
  • Taking active role in care

Purpose and Meaning

  • Finding meaning despite pain
  • Maintaining valued activities
  • Goals beyond pain relief

Optimism

  • Realistic hope for future
  • Expecting good things can happen
  • Not toxic positivity

Factors That Build Resilience3

  • Social support
  • Problem-solving skills
  • Emotional regulation
  • Sense of control
  • Flexibility in thinking
  • Finding silver linings
  • Self-care practices

Developing Resilience

Build Connections

  • Nurture supportive relationships
  • Accept help from others
  • Join support groups

Focus on What You Can Control

  • Your responses and actions
  • Self-care behaviors
  • Setting small, achievable goals

Cultivate Wellness

  • Physical activity as able
  • Sleep hygiene
  • Mindfulness and relaxation
  • Enjoyable activities

Find Meaning

  • Help others
  • Pursue values
  • Growth from challenges

It's a Journey

  • Resilience takes time to develop
  • Bad days don't erase progress
  • Be patient with yourself
  • Professional help can accelerate growth
