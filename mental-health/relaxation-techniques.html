Relaxation Techniques

Calming body and mind to ease pain

Why Relaxation Helps Pain

Chronic pain often causes muscle tension, stress, and nervous system activation that can worsen pain. Relaxation techniques help break this cycle by reducing tension, lowering stress hormones, and activating the body's relaxation response.1

Deep Breathing2

Diaphragmatic Breathing

  • Sit or lie comfortably
  • Place one hand on chest, one on belly
  • Breathe in slowly through nose (4 counts)
  • Feel belly rise (chest stays still)
  • Exhale slowly through mouth (6-8 counts)
  • Practice 5-10 minutes daily

Progressive Muscle Relaxation (PMR)

  • Systematically tense and release muscle groups
  • Start at feet, work up to head
  • Tense each group for 5-7 seconds
  • Release and notice difference
  • Takes 15-20 minutes
  • Modified versions for painful areas

Guided Imagery3

  • Imagine peaceful, pleasant scene
  • Engage all senses
  • Can use recordings or scripts
  • Creates mental "vacation"

Body Scan

  • Lie down comfortably
  • Bring attention to each body part
  • Notice sensations without judgment
  • Release tension as you go
  • 15-20 minutes typically

Other Techniques

  • Autogenic training
  • Self-hypnosis
  • Meditation
  • Yoga nidra
  • Visualization

Tips for Success

  • Practice regularly (daily is best)
  • Start with short sessions
  • Find quiet, comfortable space
  • Be patient - it's a skill
  • Use recordings/apps for guidance
  • Practice when calm first

When to Use

  • Daily practice (prevention)
  • During pain flares
  • Before stressful situations
  • To help with sleep
