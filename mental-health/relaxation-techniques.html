Relaxation Techniques

Calming the nervous system

Why Relaxation Helps Pain

Chronic pain activates the stress response, which can amplify pain signals. Relaxation techniques help calm the nervous system, reduce muscle tension, and break the stress-pain cycle. Regular practice can actually change how your brain processes pain.1

Deep Breathing2

Diaphragmatic Breathing

  • Place hand on belly
  • Breathe in through nose - belly rises
  • Exhale slowly through mouth - belly falls
  • Practice 5-10 minutes daily

4-7-8 Breathing

  • Inhale for 4 counts
  • Hold for 7 counts
  • Exhale for 8 counts
  • Repeat 4 times

Progressive Muscle Relaxation3

  • Systematically tense and relax muscle groups
  • Start with feet, work up to face
  • Tense for 5 seconds, relax for 30 seconds
  • Notice difference between tension and relaxation
  • Takes about 15-20 minutes

Body Scan

  • Lie down comfortably
  • Focus attention on each body part in turn
  • Notice sensations without judgment
  • Release tension as you notice it
  • Move from toes to head

Guided Imagery

  • Imagine peaceful scene in detail
  • Engage all senses
  • Can use recordings or apps
  • Customize to what works for you

Autogenic Training

  • Self-suggestions of warmth and heaviness
  • "My arms are heavy and warm"
  • Promotes deep relaxation
  • Requires practice to master

Tips for Success

  • Practice daily (not just during pain)
  • Find a quiet, comfortable place
  • Start with short sessions
  • Use apps or recordings if helpful
  • Be patient - skills improve with practice
  • Try different techniques to find what works

Resources

  • Apps: Calm, Headspace, Insight Timer
  • YouTube guided relaxation videos
