Anxiety and Chronic Pain

Breaking the worry-pain cycle

The Anxiety-Pain Connection

Anxiety and chronic pain frequently occur together and can create a vicious cycle. Anxiety increases muscle tension, heightens pain perception, and leads to avoidance behaviors that can worsen both pain and disability. Understanding and addressing anxiety is an important part of comprehensive pain management.1

How Common Is It?2

  • 35-45% of chronic pain patients have anxiety
  • Much higher than general population
  • Often underrecognized and undertreated
  • May predate or follow pain onset

How Anxiety Worsens Pain

  • Increases muscle tension
  • Heightens nervous system sensitivity
  • Amplifies pain signals
  • Disrupts sleep (worsens pain)
  • Leads to avoidance behaviors
  • Hypervigilance to body sensations

Types of Pain-Related Anxiety3

Fear of Pain

  • Anticipating pain
  • Avoiding activities
  • Catastrophizing about pain

Fear of Movement

  • Kinesiophobia
  • Avoiding activity due to fear of injury
  • Leads to deconditioning

Health Anxiety

  • Worry about underlying disease
  • Excessive checking behaviors
  • Seeking repeated reassurance

Generalized Anxiety

  • Worry about many things
  • May include pain-related worries
  • Difficulty controlling worry

Signs of Anxiety with Pain

  • Excessive worry about pain worsening
  • Avoiding activities you used to enjoy
  • Physical symptoms (racing heart, sweating)
  • Sleep problems
  • Difficulty relaxing
  • Feeling on edge
  • Panic attacks

Treatment Approaches

Psychological

  • Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)
  • Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)
  • Exposure therapy for fear of movement
  • Relaxation training
  • Mindfulness

Medications

  • SSRIs (may help both anxiety and pain)
  • SNRIs (effective for both)
  • Buspirone
  • Caution with benzodiazepines

Self-Help Strategies

  • Regular physical activity
  • Breathing exercises
  • Limiting caffeine
  • Sleep hygiene
  • Gradual exposure to feared activities

Getting Help

  • Talk to your doctor
  • Mental health referral
  • Pain psychology
  • Integrated pain programs
