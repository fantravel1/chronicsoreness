Pain and Anxiety

Breaking the fear-pain cycle

Anxiety in Chronic Pain

Anxiety is common in chronic pain. Fear of pain, worry about the future, and hypervigilance can make pain worse and lead to avoidance of activities. Understanding this cycle is key to breaking it.1

Types of Pain-Related Anxiety2

Fear-Avoidance

  • Fear that movement will cause harm
  • Avoiding activities "just in case"
  • Leads to deconditioning
  • Creates disability beyond what pain causes

Kinesiophobia

  • Specific fear of movement
  • "If I move, I'll hurt myself"
  • Common after injuries

Health Anxiety

  • Worry about underlying cause
  • Fear of serious disease
  • Seeking repeated testing

The Fear-Avoidance Cycle

  1. Pain occurs
  2. Fear and catastrophizing
  3. Avoidance of activities
  4. Deconditioning and disability
  5. More pain, starting cycle again

Strategies for Anxiety

  • Deep breathing exercises
  • Progressive muscle relaxation
  • Grounding techniques
  • Mindfulness
  • Challenge anxious thoughts
  • Gradual exposure to feared activities

When to Seek Help

  • Anxiety significantly limits life
  • Panic attacks
  • Avoiding most activities
  • Constant worry about pain
