Sleep and Chronic Pain

Breaking the pain-sleep cycle

The Sleep-Pain Connection

Sleep and pain have a bidirectional relationship: pain disrupts sleep, and poor sleep increases pain sensitivity. Up to 88% of chronic pain patients report sleep problems, creating a cycle that can worsen both conditions.1

How Pain Disrupts Sleep2

  • Difficulty falling asleep
  • Frequent night waking
  • Less time in deep sleep
  • Early morning awakening
  • Unrefreshing sleep

How Poor Sleep Worsens Pain

  • Increases pain sensitivity
  • Reduces pain inhibition
  • Increases inflammation
  • Worsens mood
  • Reduces coping ability

Sleep Improvement Strategies3

Sleep Hygiene

  • Consistent sleep/wake times
  • Cool, dark, quiet bedroom
  • Avoid screens before bed
  • Limit caffeine and alcohol
  • Regular exercise (not late evening)

Pain-Specific Strategies

  • Proper pillow and mattress
  • Supportive positioning
  • Pre-bed relaxation routine
  • Timing of pain medications
  • Address nighttime pain

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I)

  • Most effective non-drug treatment
  • Works well for pain-related insomnia
  • Available in-person or online
  • Long-lasting benefits

When to Seek Help

  • Significant daytime impairment
  • Sleep problems lasting weeks
  • Snoring or breathing pauses
  • Restless legs
