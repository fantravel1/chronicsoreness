Mindfulness Meditation

Present-moment awareness for pain

What Is Mindfulness?

Mindfulness is paying attention to the present moment with openness and without judgment. When applied to chronic pain, it helps change your relationship with pain rather than trying to eliminate it. Research shows it can reduce pain intensity and improve quality of life.1

How It Helps Pain2

  • Reduces emotional reaction to pain
  • Decreases catastrophizing
  • Lowers stress and anxiety
  • Improves ability to cope
  • Changes brain's pain processing
  • Increases acceptance

Types of Mindfulness Practices

  • Breath awareness - Focus on breathing
  • Body scan - Systematic attention to body parts
  • Mindful movement - Yoga, walking
  • Loving-kindness - Cultivating compassion
  • Open awareness - Noticing whatever arises

Getting Started

  • Start with 5-10 minutes daily
  • Find comfortable position
  • Use guided meditations (apps, recordings)
  • Be patient with wandering mind
  • Practice regularly, not just during pain

MBSR for Pain3

Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) is an 8-week program developed specifically for chronic pain and stress:

  • Weekly 2.5-hour group sessions
  • Daily home practice
  • Includes meditation, yoga, body scan
  • Strong research support

Tips for Practicing with Pain

  • You don't have to focus on pain
  • Noticing pain without judgment is okay
  • Adjust position as needed
  • Shorter sessions are fine
  • Progress takes time
