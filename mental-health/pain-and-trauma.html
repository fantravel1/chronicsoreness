Trauma and Chronic Pain

Understanding the mind-body connection

The Connection

Trauma and chronic pain frequently co-occur and can influence each other in complex ways. People with trauma histories are more likely to develop chronic pain, and chronic pain itself can be traumatic. Understanding this connection is important for effective treatment of both conditions.1

What Is Trauma?

  • Event(s) that overwhelm ability to cope
  • Physical, emotional, sexual abuse
  • Accidents and injuries
  • Combat/violence exposure
  • Medical trauma
  • Neglect and abandonment
  • Adverse childhood experiences (ACEs)

How Trauma Affects Pain2

  • Sensitizes nervous system
  • Alters stress response systems
  • Increases inflammation
  • Affects pain processing in brain
  • May lead to hypervigilance to body sensations
  • Creates learned patterns of bracing/tension

Research Findings3

  • Higher rates of ACEs in chronic pain patients
  • PTSD common in chronic pain (up to 50%)
  • Shared brain pathways
  • Both involve central sensitization
  • Treatment of one may help the other

Signs Trauma May Be Involved

  • Pain began after traumatic event
  • Pain triggers emotional responses
  • Certain situations worsen pain
  • Pain in areas related to trauma
  • Treatments don't work as expected
  • Strong reactions to medical procedures

Trauma-Informed Care

  • Recognizes impact of trauma
  • Creates safety in treatment settings
  • Provides choice and control
  • Avoids retraumatization
  • Builds on strengths

Treatment Approaches

For Trauma

  • Trauma-focused therapy (EMDR, CPT)
  • Somatic experiencing
  • Trauma-sensitive yoga

For Both

  • Grounding techniques
  • Mindfulness-based approaches
  • Body-based therapies
  • Integrated treatment programs

Finding Help

  • Trauma-informed therapists
  • Pain psychologists with trauma training
  • EMDR-trained clinicians
  • Integrated pain programs

Important Notes

  • Having trauma doesn't mean pain isn't "real"
  • Pain is always physical AND psychological
  • Addressing trauma doesn't replace medical care
  • Go at your own pace
  • Recovery is possible
