Anger and Chronic Pain

Understanding and managing this common emotion

Anger Is Normal

If you're angry about having chronic pain, you're not alone. Anger is one of the most common emotional responses to chronic pain. It's a natural reaction to losing abilities, facing skepticism, and dealing with a condition that may not have been caused by anything you did. Understanding and managing anger is important for well-being and pain management.1

Why Chronic Pain Causes Anger2

  • Loss of abilities and roles
  • Feeling helpless
  • Not being believed
  • Treatment failures
  • Impact on relationships
  • Financial strain
  • Unfairness of situation
  • Uncertainty about future

Targets of Anger

  • Self (for having pain, limitations)
  • Healthcare providers
  • Family and friends
  • Insurance companies
  • Employers
  • The situation/universe

How Anger Affects Pain3

  • Increases muscle tension
  • Heightens nervous system arousal
  • May amplify pain perception
  • Disrupts sleep
  • Damages relationships (support)
  • Can lead to unhealthy behaviors

Unhealthy Anger Expression

  • Aggressive outbursts
  • Chronic hostility
  • Suppressing all anger
  • Taking it out on loved ones
  • Self-destructive behavior

Healthy Anger Management

Acknowledge It

  • Anger is valid and understandable
  • Don't judge yourself for feeling it
  • Name the emotion

Express It Appropriately

  • Talk to supportive people
  • Write in journal
  • Physical activity (when possible)
  • Creative expression

Work Through It

  • Identify what's underneath anger
  • Often grief, fear, hurt
  • Process the losses
  • Challenge unhelpful thoughts

Techniques That Help

  • Deep breathing
  • Taking a timeout
  • Progressive muscle relaxation
  • Mindfulness
  • Cognitive restructuring
  • Assertive communication

When to Seek Help

  • Anger feels out of control
  • Affecting relationships significantly
  • Leading to aggression
  • Accompanied by depression
