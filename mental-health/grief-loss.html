Grief & Loss

Mourning what chronic pain takes away

Understanding Grief in Chronic Pain

Chronic pain often involves significant lossesâ€”loss of health, abilities, roles, identity, and dreams. Grieving these losses is a normal and necessary part of adjusting to life with chronic pain.1

Types of Losses

  • Physical - Mobility, energy, abilities
  • Social - Relationships, social activities
  • Professional - Career, income, work identity
  • Personal - Independence, hobbies, future plans
  • Identity - Sense of self, who you were

Grief Is Not Linear2

Common experiences include:

  • Denial - This can't be happening
  • Anger - Why me? This isn't fair
  • Bargaining - If only I'd...
  • Depression - Sadness, withdrawal
  • Acceptance - Learning to live with new reality

These don't happen in order and may repeat.

Chronic Sorrow

  • Grief may return periodically
  • Triggered by reminders of losses
  • Normal for chronic conditions
  • Doesn't mean you're not coping

Healthy Grieving3

  • Acknowledge your losses
  • Allow yourself to feel sadness
  • Express emotions (talk, write, create)
  • Be patient with yourself
  • Seek support from understanding people
  • Consider grief counseling

Moving Forward

  • Honor what you've lost
  • Find new sources of meaning
  • Develop new identity that includes pain
  • Focus on what's still possible
  • Celebrate small victories
