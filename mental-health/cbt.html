Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Pain

Changing thoughts and behaviors to manage pain

What Is CBT for Pain?

Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) for chronic pain is a structured psychological treatment that helps people identify and change unhelpful thoughts and behaviors related to pain. It's one of the most evidence-based psychological treatments for chronic pain.1

How It Helps

  • Changes relationship to pain
  • Reduces pain catastrophizing
  • Improves coping skills
  • Decreases depression and anxiety
  • Improves function and quality of life
  • May reduce pain intensity

Key Components2

Cognitive Techniques

  • Identifying unhelpful thoughts
  • Challenging catastrophic thinking
  • Developing balanced perspectives
  • Reducing fear of movement

Behavioral Techniques

  • Activity pacing
  • Graded exposure to activities
  • Behavioral activation
  • Sleep hygiene
  • Relaxation training

Common Unhelpful Thoughts

  • "This pain will never get better"
  • "I can't do anything because of pain"
  • "If I move, I'll make it worse"
  • "No one understands my pain"

Evidence3

  • Strong evidence for effectiveness
  • Recommended in major guidelines
  • Benefits maintain after treatment ends
  • Works for various pain conditions

What to Expect

  • Usually 8-12 sessions
  • Structured, goal-oriented
  • Homework between sessions
  • Skills to use long-term
  • Individual or group format

Finding CBT for Pain

  • Pain psychologists
  • Multidisciplinary pain programs
  • Some physical therapists trained in CBT
  • Online CBT programs (emerging)
