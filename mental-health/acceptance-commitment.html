Acceptance and Commitment Therapy

ACT for chronic pain management

What Is ACT?

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT, pronounced "act") is an evidence-based psychological treatment that helps people develop psychological flexibilityâ€”the ability to be present, accept difficult experiences, and take action toward a valued life. For chronic pain, ACT focuses on living well despite pain, rather than fighting to eliminate it.1

Core Principles2

Acceptance

  • Making room for pain without struggle
  • Not resignation or giving up
  • Willingness to experience discomfort
  • Reduces suffering from fighting pain

Cognitive Defusion

  • Unhooking from unhelpful thoughts
  • Seeing thoughts as thoughts, not facts
  • Reducing thought control over behavior

Present Moment Awareness

  • Being here now
  • Mindful attention
  • Not stuck in past or future

Self-as-Context

  • You are more than your pain
  • Observer self perspective

Values

  • Clarifying what matters to you
  • Using values as compass for action

Committed Action

  • Taking steps toward values
  • Even when pain is present

Research Evidence3

  • Strong evidence for chronic pain
  • Improves function and quality of life
  • Reduces disability
  • May reduce pain intensity
  • Helps with depression and anxiety

ACT vs. Traditional Approaches

  • Focus on function, not just pain reduction
  • Values-driven rather than symptom-driven
  • Acceptance rather than control
  • Emphasis on quality of life

What ACT Doesn't Mean

  • Giving up on treatment
  • Pretending pain doesn't exist
  • Being passive
  • Liking or wanting pain

Accessing ACT

  • Psychologists trained in ACT
  • Pain management programs
  • Self-help books
  • Online programs
  • Apps (ACT Coach, others)

Questions to Consider

  • What would you do if pain wasn't stopping you?
  • What matters most in your life?
  • Are you living according to your values?
