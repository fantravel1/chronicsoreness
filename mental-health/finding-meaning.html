Finding Meaning with Pain

Living a fulfilling life despite chronic pain

Beyond Pain Reduction

While reducing pain is important, it's not the only measure of a good life with chronic pain. Research shows that people who find meaning, purpose, and connection can live fulfilling lives even when pain persists. This isn't about giving up on treatmentâ€”it's about living fully while also managing pain.1

What Meaning Looks Like

  • Engaging in activities you value
  • Contributing to others or community
  • Pursuing adapted versions of important goals
  • Finding purpose in new ways
  • Maintaining connections with others
  • Personal growth despite challenges

Identifying Your Values2

Questions to Consider

  • What matters most to you in life?
  • What kind of person do you want to be?
  • What would you do if pain wasn't limiting you?
  • What brings you satisfaction or joy?
  • What do you want your life to be about?

Common Values

  • Family and relationships
  • Creativity and self-expression
  • Learning and growth
  • Helping others
  • Health and well-being
  • Nature and environment
  • Spirituality or faith

Living Your Values with Pain3

  • Adapt activities rather than abandon them
  • Find new ways to express what matters
  • Small steps toward meaningful goals
  • Accept limitations while staying engaged
  • Quality over quantity

Sources of Meaning

Relationships

  • Deepening connections
  • Being present with loved ones
  • Supporting others who understand

Contribution

  • Volunteering within your capacity
  • Mentoring others
  • Advocacy for chronic pain awareness

Creativity

  • Art, writing, music
  • Problem-solving
  • Adapting and innovating

Growth

  • Learning new things
  • Developing new skills
  • Personal development

Post-Traumatic Growth

  • Some people grow from difficult experiences
  • New appreciation for life
  • Deeper relationships
  • New possibilities
  • Greater personal strength
  • Spiritual development

This Is Not...

  • Pretending pain doesn't exist
  • Toxic positivity
  • Giving up on treatment
  • Saying pain is a gift
  • Pressure to "find the silver lining"

Resources
