CBT for Chronic Pain

Changing thoughts to change pain

What Is CBT for Pain?

Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) for chronic pain helps you identify and change unhelpful thoughts and behaviors related to pain. It's the most studied psychological treatment for chronic pain and is recommended in treatment guidelines worldwide.1

The CBT Model2

  • Thoughts, feelings, behaviors, and body sensations are connected
  • Changing one affects the others
  • Unhelpful patterns can be identified and changed
  • You can learn skills to manage pain better

What CBT Targets

Unhelpful Thoughts

  • Catastrophizing ("This will never get better")
  • All-or-nothing thinking
  • Focusing only on pain
  • Negative predictions

Unhelpful Behaviors

  • Avoidance of activities
  • Overdoing on good days
  • Reduced activity overall
  • Social withdrawal

CBT Techniques3

Cognitive Techniques

  • Identifying negative thoughts
  • Evaluating evidence for thoughts
  • Developing balanced thinking
  • Problem-solving

Behavioral Techniques

  • Activity pacing
  • Graded exposure to feared activities
  • Pleasant activity scheduling
  • Relaxation training

What to Expect

  • Typically 8-12 sessions
  • Structured sessions with specific goals
  • Homework between sessions
  • Active participation required
  • Skills you keep for life

Evidence3

  • Reduces pain intensity
  • Decreases disability
  • Improves mood
  • Reduces catastrophizing
  • Effects can be long-lasting

Finding CBT

  • Pain psychologists
  • Clinical psychologists with pain training
  • Pain rehabilitation programs
  • Online CBT programs

It's Not About "Thinking Away" Pain

CBT doesn't claim pain is "all in your head." It acknowledges pain is real while teaching practical skills to reduce suffering and improve function.
