Journaling for Pain

Writing your way to better coping

Why Journal?

Journaling can help process the emotional impact of chronic pain, identify patterns and triggers, and improve communication with healthcare providers. Research shows expressive writing can have health benefits.1

Types of Pain Journaling2

Pain Tracking

  • Record pain levels
  • Note activities and triggers
  • Track medications and effects
  • Identify patterns

Expressive Writing

  • Write about emotions
  • Process difficult experiences
  • No rules about grammar
  • Private and uncensored

Gratitude Journaling

  • Note positive moments
  • Shift focus from pain
  • Build positive perspective

Benefits3

  • Reduces emotional distress
  • Improves self-awareness
  • Identifies triggers and patterns
  • Helps communicate with doctors
  • Provides sense of control
  • Tracks what helps

Getting Started

  • Choose format (paper, app, computer)
  • Start small (5-10 minutes)
  • Be consistent but flexible
  • Write honestly
  • Don't worry about perfection

Prompts to Try

  • How is pain affecting my life today?
  • What am I grateful for despite pain?
  • What helped me cope this week?
  • What would I tell someone newly diagnosed?
  • What are my values and how can I honor them?

Cautions

  • Don't ruminate excessively
  • Balance difficult emotions with positive
  • Seek professional help if overwhelmed
