Pain Acceptance

A path to living well with chronic pain

What Is Pain Acceptance?

Pain acceptance is a willingness to experience pain without trying to avoid, control, or judge itâ€”while continuing to pursue meaningful life activities. It's not about giving up or liking pain.1

What Acceptance Is NOT

  • Giving up on treatment
  • Believing nothing can help
  • Passive resignation
  • Liking or wanting pain
  • Pretending pain doesn't exist
  • Denying emotions about pain

What Acceptance IS2

  • Acknowledging pain is present
  • Reducing the struggle against pain
  • Focusing on what you can do
  • Continuing meaningful activities
  • Not letting pain define you
  • Flexible response to pain

Why Acceptance Helps

  • Fighting pain increases suffering
  • Reduces emotional distress
  • Frees energy for living
  • Improves functioning
  • Associated with less disability
  • Better quality of life

Components of Acceptance3

Activity Engagement

  • Participating in activities despite pain
  • Not letting pain stop meaningful pursuits

Pain Willingness

  • Not avoiding or controlling pain at all costs
  • Allowing pain to be present

Developing Acceptance

  • Mindfulness practice
  • Identifying values
  • Gradual engagement in activities
  • ACT or similar therapy
  • Self-compassion
