Anxiety and Chronic Pain

Understanding and managing the connection

The Anxiety-Pain Connection

Anxiety and chronic pain frequently occur together and can worsen each other. People with chronic pain are much more likely to have anxiety disorders. Understanding this connection can help break the cycle and improve both conditions.1

How Anxiety Affects Pain2

  • Increases muscle tension
  • Heightens nervous system arousal
  • Amplifies pain perception
  • Leads to hypervigilance to body sensations
  • Can cause avoidance that increases disability
  • Disrupts sleep, which worsens pain

How Pain Causes Anxiety

  • Uncertainty about the future
  • Fear of pain getting worse
  • Worry about being believed
  • Concerns about diagnosis
  • Financial and work worries
  • Loss of control

Signs of Anxiety3

  • Excessive worry
  • Restlessness
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Irritability
  • Sleep problems
  • Physical symptoms (racing heart, sweating)
  • Avoiding activities due to fear

Treatment Approaches

Psychological Treatments

  • Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)
  • Acceptance and commitment therapy (ACT)
  • Exposure therapy for feared activities
  • Mindfulness-based approaches

Relaxation Techniques

  • Deep breathing exercises
  • Progressive muscle relaxation
  • Meditation
  • Yoga

Medications

  • Some antidepressants help both (SNRIs)
  • Buspirone for anxiety
  • Short-term anti-anxiety medications (use caution)

Self-Help Strategies

  • Regular exercise (reduces both anxiety and pain)
  • Good sleep hygiene
  • Limit caffeine and alcohol
  • Practice relaxation daily
  • Gradual exposure to feared activities
  • Challenge anxious thoughts

When to Get Help

  • Anxiety significantly impacts daily life
  • Avoiding important activities due to fear
  • Constant worry about pain
  • Physical symptoms of anxiety
  • Sleep significantly affected
