Anxiety and Chronic Pain

Breaking the cycle of pain and fear

The Anxiety-Pain Connection

Anxiety and chronic pain are closely linked. Anxiety can heighten pain perception, and chronic pain often leads to anxiety about the future, activities, and worsening symptoms. This creates a cycle that can perpetuate both conditions.1

How Anxiety Affects Pain

  • Increases muscle tension
  • Heightens pain sensitivity (hypervigilance)
  • Activates stress response
  • Disrupts sleep
  • Leads to avoidance behaviors
  • Amplifies attention to pain signals

Fear-Avoidance2

Fear of pain or re-injury can lead to avoiding activities, which causes:

  • Physical deconditioning
  • Loss of confidence
  • Social isolation
  • Increased disability
  • Paradoxically, more pain over time

Pain Catastrophizing

  • Rumination - Can't stop thinking about pain
  • Magnification - Expecting the worst
  • Helplessness - Feeling nothing can be done

Breaking the Cycle3

Cognitive Strategies

  • Challenge catastrophic thoughts
  • Recognize pain doesn't always mean harm
  • Reframe unhelpful thinking patterns

Behavioral Strategies

  • Graded exposure to feared activities
  • Gradual return to meaningful activities
  • Pacing - not too much, not too little

Relaxation Techniques

  • Deep breathing
  • Progressive muscle relaxation
  • Mindfulness meditation

Treatment Options

  • Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)
  • Acceptance and commitment therapy (ACT)
  • Medications (SNRIs help both)
  • Physical therapy with graded exposure
