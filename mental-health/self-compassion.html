Self-Compassion

Treating yourself with kindness

What Is Self-Compassion?

Self-compassion means treating yourself with the same kindness, understanding, and care you would offer a good friend who is suffering. For people with chronic pain, self-compassion is particularly importantâ€”and often particularly difficult.1

Three Components2

Self-Kindness

  • Being gentle with yourself
  • Not harsh self-criticism
  • Understanding when you can't do things

Common Humanity

  • Suffering is part of being human
  • You're not alone in this
  • Others have similar struggles

Mindfulness

  • Acknowledge suffering without over-identifying
  • Notice thoughts without getting swept away
  • Balanced awareness

Why It Helps Chronic Pain

  • Reduces secondary suffering
  • Decreases anxiety and depression
  • Improves pain coping
  • Reduces self-blame
  • Better emotional regulation

Common Obstacles3

  • Feeling undeserving
  • Fear it will make you weak
  • Confusing it with self-pity
  • Cultural messages about toughness
  • Critical inner voice

Practicing Self-Compassion

  • Self-compassion break - Pause and offer yourself kindness
  • Compassionate self-talk - What would you say to a friend?
  • Self-compassion phrases - "May I be kind to myself"
  • Physical gestures - Hand on heart
  • Writing exercises - Compassionate letters to yourself
