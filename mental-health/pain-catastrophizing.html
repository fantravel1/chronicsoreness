Pain Catastrophizing

Breaking the cycle of negative pain thinking

What Is Pain Catastrophizing?

Pain catastrophizing is a pattern of negative thinking about pain that includes magnifying the threat, feeling helpless, and ruminating on pain. It's not your fault or a character flawâ€”it's a common response that can be changed. Research shows it significantly impacts pain experience and outcomes.1

Three Components2

Rumination

  • "I can't stop thinking about the pain"
  • Constantly dwelling on pain
  • Difficulty focusing on other things

Magnification

  • "Something terrible is going to happen"
  • Expecting the worst
  • Exaggerating the threat of pain

Helplessness

  • "There's nothing I can do"
  • Feeling powerless to cope
  • Giving up trying to manage pain

Why It Matters3

  • Increases pain intensity experienced
  • Leads to greater disability
  • Predicts poorer treatment outcomes
  • Associated with depression and anxiety
  • Reduces effectiveness of treatments
  • Can be changed with intervention

It's Not Your Fault

  • Catastrophizing is a learned pattern
  • Often develops as protective response
  • Commonâ€”many people do it
  • Not a sign of weakness
  • Can be unlearned

Strategies to Reduce Catastrophizing

Recognize the Pattern

  • Notice catastrophic thoughts
  • Write them down
  • Identify triggers

Challenge the Thoughts

  • Is this thought realistic?
  • What's the evidence?
  • What would you tell a friend?
  • What's a more balanced view?

Build Coping Skills

  • Relaxation techniques
  • Mindfulness meditation
  • Activity pacing
  • Focus on what you can control

Professional Help

  • Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)
  • Acceptance and commitment therapy (ACT)
  • Pain psychology
  • Pain rehabilitation programs
