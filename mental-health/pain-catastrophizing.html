Pain Catastrophizing

When thoughts about pain spiral

What Is Pain Catastrophizing?

Pain catastrophizing is a pattern of negative, exaggerated thinking about pain. It involves ruminating about pain, feeling helpless, and expecting the worst. While understandable responses to suffering, these thought patterns can worsen pain experience and outcomes.1

Three Components2

Rumination

  • "I can't stop thinking about the pain"
  • "I keep wondering if it will ever end"
  • Repetitive focus on pain

Magnification

  • "Something terrible is going to happen"
  • "This must be something serious"
  • Expecting the worst

Helplessness

  • "There's nothing I can do"
  • "I can't stand this anymore"
  • Feeling out of control

Why It Matters

  • Predicts worse pain outcomes
  • Increases pain intensity
  • Leads to more disability
  • Interferes with treatment
  • Associated with depression
  • Can become self-fulfilling

This Is NOT Your Fault

  • Catastrophizing is a natural response to suffering
  • It's not weakness or making up pain
  • The brain evolved to protect us from threats
  • But these patterns can be changed

Strategies to Reduce Catastrophizing3

Cognitive Strategies

  • Notice catastrophic thoughts
  • Challenge: Is this thought helpful? Accurate?
  • Consider alternative perspectives
  • Focus on what you CAN do

Behavioral Strategies

  • Stay engaged in activities
  • Gradual exposure to avoided activities
  • Pacing
  • Social connection

Mind-Body Approaches

  • Mindfulness - observe without judgment
  • Relaxation techniques
  • Distraction

Getting Help

CBT with a pain psychologist is particularly effective for reducing catastrophizing. This is a treatable pattern.
