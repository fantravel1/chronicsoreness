Acceptance & Commitment Therapy

Living fully despite pain

What Is ACT?

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) is a form of behavioral therapy that helps people accept what is out of their control and commit to actions that enrich their lives. For chronic pain, ACT focuses on living meaningfully rather than eliminating pain.1

Core Principles2

Acceptance

  • Allowing pain to be present without fighting it
  • Not the same as giving up or liking pain
  • Reducing struggle with pain

Cognitive Defusion

  • Changing relationship with thoughts
  • Thoughts are just thoughts, not facts
  • Creating distance from unhelpful thoughts

Present Moment Awareness

  • Being here now (mindfulness)
  • Not lost in past or future
  • Noticing experiences without judgment

Self as Context

  • You are more than your pain
  • Observing self vs. thinking self
  • Pain is something you have, not who you are

Values

  • What matters most to you
  • Life directions, not goals
  • Guide for meaningful action

Committed Action

  • Taking steps aligned with values
  • Even when pain is present
  • Building patterns of effective action

ACT vs. Traditional Approaches3

  • Focus on function, not pain reduction
  • Accept rather than control thoughts/feelings
  • Emphasis on values and meaning
  • Willingness to experience discomfort

Evidence

  • Shown effective for chronic pain
  • Improves functioning and quality of life
  • Reduces disability and depression
