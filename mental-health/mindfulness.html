Mindfulness for Pain

Present-moment awareness as a pain management tool

What Is Mindfulness?

Mindfulness is the practice of paying attention to the present moment with acceptance and without judgment. For chronic pain, it changes your relationship to pain rather than eliminating it, often reducing suffering and improving function.1

How It Helps Pain

  • Reduces reactivity to pain
  • Decreases catastrophizing
  • Separates physical sensation from emotional response
  • Reduces stress and muscle tension
  • Improves emotional regulation
  • Increases acceptance

MBSR (Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction)2

  • Developed by Jon Kabat-Zinn
  • 8-week structured program
  • Weekly classes + home practice
  • Includes body scan, sitting meditation, gentle yoga
  • Strong evidence for chronic pain

Core Practices

Body Scan

  • Systematic attention through body
  • Notice sensations without judgment
  • Release tension

Breath Awareness

  • Focus on breathing
  • Notice when mind wanders
  • Gently return attention

Mindful Movement

  • Gentle stretching with awareness
  • Yoga, walking meditation

Evidence3

  • Reduces pain intensity and unpleasantness
  • Improves physical function
  • Reduces depression and anxiety
  • Effective for low back pain, fibromyalgia
  • Changes brain activity related to pain

Getting Started

  • MBSR programs (in-person or online)
  • Guided meditation apps (Headspace, Calm, Insight Timer)
  • Start with short sessions (5-10 minutes)
  • Consistency matters more than duration
  • Be patient - it's a skill that develops

Common Challenges

  • "I can't clear my mind" - That's not the goal; notice thoughts and return
  • "It makes me more aware of pain" - Normal initially; relationship to pain changes
  • "I don't have time" - Even brief practice helps
