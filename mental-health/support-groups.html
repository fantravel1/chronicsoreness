Support Groups

Connecting with others who understand

Why Support Groups Help

Living with chronic pain can be isolating. Friends and family may not fully understand. Support groups provide a space to connect with others who truly "get it." Research shows that peer support can reduce isolation, improve coping, and enhance quality of life for people with chronic pain.1

Benefits2

  • Feeling understood and validated
  • Reduced isolation
  • Shared coping strategies
  • Practical tips from experience
  • Emotional support
  • Hope from seeing others cope
  • Giving support helps too

Types of Groups3

In-Person

  • Local community groups
  • Hospital or clinic-based
  • Condition-specific organizations
  • General chronic pain groups

Online

  • Facebook groups
  • Reddit communities
  • Dedicated forums
  • Video chat support groups
  • Apps with community features

Finding Groups

  • Ask your healthcare provider
  • Search condition-specific organizations
  • Check local hospitals and pain clinics
  • Search online (Facebook, Meetup)
  • National pain organizations

What Happens in Groups

  • Share experiences
  • Discuss challenges
  • Share what's working
  • Sometimes have speakers
  • Often informal discussion
  • Usually facilitated

Getting the Most Out of Groups

  • Try a few meetings before deciding
  • Participate at your comfort level
  • Share and listen
  • Be open to different perspectives
  • Take breaks if overwhelming

Potential Concerns

  • Some groups may be negative or complaining
  • Medical advice from non-professionals
  • May hear difficult stories
  • Not all groups are right for everyone
  • Try different groups if needed

Online Safety

  • Protect personal information
  • Verify medical information
  • Report harassment
  • Take breaks from social media

If No Groups Available

  • Online options accessible anywhere
  • Consider starting a local group
  • One-on-one peer support programs
  • Ask hospital about starting group
