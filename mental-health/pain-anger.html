Pain and Anger

Managing frustration with chronic pain

Anger Is Normal

Feeling angry about chronic pain is completely understandable. Pain has taken things from you, changed your life, and isn't fair. Anger is a normal response to loss, injustice, and ongoing suffering. The goal isn't to never feel angry, but to manage anger so it doesn't make things worse.1

Why Chronic Pain Causes Anger

  • Loss of abilities and activities
  • Feeling misunderstood or dismissed
  • Unfairness of the situation
  • Loss of control
  • Failed treatments and disappointments
  • Medical system frustrations
  • Impact on work, relationships, finances

The Anger-Pain Cycle2

  • Pain triggers anger
  • Anger increases muscle tension
  • Tension can worsen pain
  • More pain leads to more anger
  • Breaking this cycle is important

Healthy Ways to Express Anger

  • Acknowledge and name the feeling
  • Physical release (within your limits)
  • Writing or journaling
  • Talking to understanding people
  • Creative expression
  • Advocacy and channeling into action

Unhealthy Anger Patterns

  • Suppressing all anger (leads to buildup)
  • Explosive outbursts
  • Taking anger out on loved ones
  • Dwelling on angry thoughts
  • Becoming bitter

Coping Strategies3

In the Moment

  • Pause before reacting
  • Deep breathing
  • Count to 10 (or 100)
  • Remove yourself from situation if needed
  • Use relaxation techniques

Ongoing

  • Regular exercise (as tolerated)
  • Mindfulness practice
  • Processing emotions in therapy
  • Support groups
  • Finding meaning and purpose

Cognitive Approaches

  • Identify triggering thoughts
  • Challenge unhelpful thinking
  • Reframe situations when possible
  • Accept what can't be changed
  • Focus on what you can control

When to Get Help

  • Anger is frequent and intense
  • Damaging relationships
  • Leading to aggression
  • Can't control outbursts
  • Contributing to depression

Professional Support

  • Pain psychologists understand this
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy helps
  • Anger management programs
  • Support groups for chronic pain
