Accessibility Statement

Our commitment to making chronic pain resources accessible to everyone.

Our Commitment

ChronicSoreness is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We continually improve the user experience for everyone and apply the relevant accessibility standards.

We believe that everyone deserves access to information about chronic pain management, regardless of ability. Living with chronic pain is challenging enough without facing barriers to finding help and support.

Conformance Status

We strive to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible for people with disabilities.

WCAG 2.1 Level AA Target

Accessibility Features

We've implemented numerous accessibility features throughout our website:

Keyboard Navigation

All functionality is accessible via keyboard. Use Tab to navigate, Enter to activate, and Escape to close dialogs.

Screen Reader Support

Semantic HTML, ARIA labels, and proper heading structure ensure compatibility with assistive technologies.

Skip Links

Skip to main content links allow keyboard users to bypass repetitive navigation.

Color Contrast

Text maintains sufficient contrast ratios against backgrounds for readability.

Dark Mode

Toggle between light and dark themes based on preference or to reduce eye strain.

Responsive Design

Content adapts to all screen sizes and supports zoom up to 200% without loss of functionality.

Focus Indicators

Clear visual focus indicators help keyboard users track their position on the page.

Alt Text

Images include descriptive alternative text for users who cannot see them.

Keyboard Shortcuts

Navigate our site efficiently using these keyboard shortcuts:

Action Shortcut
Navigate to next interactive element Tab
Navigate to previous interactive element Shift + Tab
Activate link or button Enter
Close dialog or dropdown Escape
Scroll page Space or Arrow keys
Navigate headings (screen reader) H

Assistive Technology Compatibility

Our website is designed to be compatible with:

  • Screen Readers: NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver, TalkBack
  • Voice Control: Dragon NaturallySpeaking, Voice Control (iOS/macOS)
  • Screen Magnification: ZoomText, system zoom features
  • Alternative Input Devices: Switch controls, eye tracking

Known Limitations

While we strive for full accessibility, some areas are still being improved:

  • Some interactive tools (like the Symptom Checker) may have complex interactions that we're working to simplify
  • Third-party embedded content may not fully meet our accessibility standards
  • PDF documents linked from our site may need accessibility improvements

We are actively working to address these limitations and welcome your feedback.

Browser and Device Support

For the best experience, we recommend using modern browsers:

  • Google Chrome (latest 2 versions)
  • Mozilla Firefox (latest 2 versions)
  • Apple Safari (latest 2 versions)
  • Microsoft Edge (latest 2 versions)

Our site is fully responsive and works on desktop computers, tablets, and mobile devices.

Chronic Pain & Accessibility

We understand that chronic pain can affect how you interact with digital content. That's why we've designed our site with features that may help:

  • Reading Time Estimates: Know how long content will take before committing energy
  • Print-Friendly Pages: Print resources to read offline during difficult days
  • Simple Navigation: Find what you need without excessive clicking or scrolling
  • Dark Mode: Reduce screen brightness during migraine-sensitive times
  • Large Click Targets: Buttons and links are sized for easy interaction

Report an Accessibility Issue

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of ChronicSoreness. Please let us know if you encounter barriers:

  • Contact Form: Use our contact page and select "Accessibility Issue"
  • Describe the Issue: Tell us what page you were on and what barrier you encountered
  • Assistive Technology: Let us know what assistive technology you use, if any

We try to respond to accessibility feedback within 5 business days.

Continuous Improvement

We are committed to continuously improving accessibility:

  • Regular accessibility audits of new and existing content
  • Training for content creators on accessibility best practices
  • User testing with people who use assistive technologies
  • Staying current with accessibility guidelines and best practices
