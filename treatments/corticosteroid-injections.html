Corticosteroid Injections

Targeted anti-inflammatory treatment

What Are Corticosteroid Injections?

Corticosteroid (cortisone) injections deliver anti-inflammatory medication directly to a painful area. They can provide relief for joints, tendons, bursae, and spinal structures. While not a cure, they can reduce inflammation and pain, often allowing patients to participate better in physical therapy.1

How They Work2

  • Reduce local inflammation
  • Suppress immune response
  • Decrease pain signals
  • Usually combined with local anesthetic
  • Effects can last weeks to months

Common Uses

Joint Injections

  • Knee osteoarthritis
  • Hip arthritis
  • Shoulder arthritis
  • Facet joint pain

Soft Tissue

  • Bursitis
  • Tendinitis
  • Tennis elbow
  • Trigger finger
  • Plantar fasciitis

Spine

  • Epidural steroid injections
  • Facet joint injections
  • Sacroiliac joint injections

What to Expect3

Before

  • Review medications with doctor
  • May need to hold blood thinners
  • Usually outpatient procedure

During

  • Area cleaned and numbed
  • May use ultrasound or X-ray guidance
  • Brief discomfort during injection
  • Takes just minutes

After

  • May have immediate relief (anesthetic)
  • Steroid effect takes 2-7 days
  • Some soreness at injection site
  • Rest joint for 1-2 days

Potential Risks

  • Injection site pain
  • Temporary blood sugar elevation
  • Facial flushing
  • Infection (rare)
  • Tendon weakening (repeated injections)
  • Skin depigmentation
  • Fat atrophy

Limitations

  • Relief is often temporary
  • Limited number recommended (usually 3-4/year)
  • Repeated injections may damage tissue
  • Not effective for everyone
  • Doesn't fix underlying problem

Maximizing Benefit

  • Use window of relief for PT
  • Address underlying causes
  • Part of comprehensive plan
  • Space injections appropriately
