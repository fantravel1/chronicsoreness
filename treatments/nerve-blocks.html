Nerve Blocks

Targeted injections for pain diagnosis and treatment

What Are Nerve Blocks?

Nerve blocks are injections of medication near specific nerves to interrupt pain signals. They can be diagnostic (to identify pain source) or therapeutic (to provide relief).1

Types of Nerve Blocks

  • Peripheral nerve blocks - Target specific nerves
  • Sympathetic blocks - Stellate ganglion, lumbar sympathetic
  • Epidural injections - Into epidural space
  • Facet joint blocks
  • SI joint injections
  • Trigger point injections

Conditions Treated

  • Sciatica and radiculopathy
  • Complex regional pain syndrome
  • Neuropathic pain
  • Headaches (occipital blocks)
  • Facet joint pain
  • SI joint dysfunction
  • Cancer pain

Medications Used

  • Local anesthetics (lidocaine, bupivacaine)
  • Corticosteroids (for inflammation)
  • Sometimes combined

The Procedure2

  • Usually outpatient
  • Guided by fluoroscopy or ultrasound
  • Local numbing first
  • Takes 15-30 minutes typically
  • May feel pressure or brief pain

What to Expect

  • Relief may be immediate (local anesthetic) or delayed (steroid)
  • Duration varies: days to months
  • May need repeat injections
  • Not a cure - provides window for rehab

Risks

  • Infection (rare)
  • Bleeding
  • Nerve damage (rare)
  • Temporary weakness or numbness
  • Steroid side effects with repeated injections
