Nerve Blocks

Targeted injections for pain

What Are Nerve Blocks?

Nerve blocks are injections that deliver medication directly to specific nerves or groups of nerves to block pain signals. They can be used for diagnosis (to identify pain sources), treatment (to provide relief), or as a precursor to other procedures like radiofrequency ablation.1

Types of Nerve Blocks2

By Purpose

  • Diagnostic: Determine if a specific nerve is the pain source
  • Therapeutic: Provide longer-term pain relief
  • Prognostic: Predict response to other treatments

Common Locations

  • Facet joint blocks (spine)
  • Epidural injections
  • Sympathetic blocks
  • Peripheral nerve blocks
  • Trigger point injections
  • Sacroiliac joint injections

Medications Used

  • Local anesthetics (lidocaine, bupivacaine)
  • Corticosteroids (reduce inflammation)
  • Combination of both commonly used

What to Expect3

Before the Procedure

  • Review of medical history
  • May need to stop blood thinners
  • Usually outpatient procedure
  • Arrange transportation home

During the Procedure

  • Area is cleaned and numbed
  • Imaging (fluoroscopy or ultrasound) guides needle placement
  • Medication injected at target location
  • Usually takes 15-30 minutes

After the Procedure

  • Brief observation period
  • Temporary numbness or weakness possible
  • May have increased pain initially
  • Relief timing varies by block type

Duration of Relief

  • Local anesthetic: Hours
  • With steroid: Days to weeks to months
  • Varies widely between individuals
  • May need repeat injections

Risks

  • Infection (rare)
  • Bleeding
  • Nerve injury (rare)
  • Allergic reaction
  • Temporary weakness or numbness
  • Steroid side effects if repeated frequently

When They're Used

  • Back and neck pain
  • Arthritis pain
  • Neuropathic pain
  • Complex regional pain syndrome
  • Headaches
  • Cancer pain

Realistic Expectations

  • May not provide complete relief
  • Part of comprehensive treatment plan
  • Effect may diminish over time
  • Works best combined with physical therapy
