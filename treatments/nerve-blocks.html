Nerve Blocks

Interrupting pain signals at their source

What Are Nerve Blocks?

Nerve blocks are injections of local anesthetic (with or without steroid) near specific nerves to interrupt pain signals. They can be diagnostic (to identify pain source) or therapeutic (to provide relief). Effects are typically temporary but can last weeks to months.1

Types of Nerve Blocks2

Peripheral Nerve Blocks

  • Occipital nerve block (headaches)
  • Suprascapular nerve block (shoulder pain)
  • Intercostal nerve block (rib pain)
  • Lateral femoral cutaneous block (meralgia paresthetica)
  • Genicular nerve block (knee pain)

Sympathetic Blocks

  • Stellate ganglion block (upper extremity CRPS)
  • Lumbar sympathetic block (lower extremity)
  • Celiac plexus block (abdominal pain)
  • Superior hypogastric block (pelvic pain)
  • Ganglion impar block (coccyx/perineal pain)

Purposes

  • Diagnostic - Confirm pain is from specific nerve
  • Therapeutic - Provide pain relief
  • Prognostic - Predict response to longer-lasting procedures

The Procedure3

  • Usually outpatient
  • Image guidance (ultrasound or fluoroscopy)
  • Skin numbed first
  • Needle guided to target nerve
  • Medication injected

What to Expect

  • Numbness in affected area (temporary)
  • Pain relief varies (hours to months)
  • May need repeat injections
  • Can lead to radiofrequency ablation if effective
