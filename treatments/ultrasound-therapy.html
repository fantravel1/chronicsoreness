Therapeutic Ultrasound

Deep tissue treatment with sound waves

What Is Therapeutic Ultrasound?

Therapeutic ultrasound uses high-frequency sound waves to treat soft tissue injuries and pain. Unlike diagnostic ultrasound (imaging), therapeutic ultrasound delivers energy to tissues to promote healing. It's commonly used in physical therapy settings, though evidence for its effectiveness varies.1

How It Works2

Thermal Effects

  • Deep tissue heating
  • Increases blood flow
  • Improves tissue extensibility
  • May reduce muscle spasm

Non-Thermal Effects

  • Cavitation (micro-bubbles)
  • Acoustic streaming
  • May enhance tissue healing
  • Pulsed mode minimizes heat

Common Uses

  • Tendinitis/tendinopathy
  • Muscle strains
  • Ligament injuries
  • Bursitis
  • Scar tissue
  • Joint contractures

The Treatment3

  • Gel applied to skin
  • Wand (transducer) moved in circles
  • Treatment lasts 5-10 minutes
  • May feel warmth
  • Should not be painful
  • Often part of larger PT session

Research Evidence

  • Evidence is mixed
  • Some studies show benefit
  • Others show no advantage over placebo
  • May help specific conditions
  • Often used with other treatments

Contraindications

  • Over cancer/tumors
  • Pregnant uterus
  • Over pacemakers
  • Over growth plates in children
  • Metal implants (use caution)
  • Infected areas
  • Over eyes, brain, heart

Phonophoresis

  • Ultrasound used to drive medication into tissue
  • Often anti-inflammatory gels
  • May enhance drug delivery
  • Evidence also limited

Realistic Expectations

  • Usually one part of treatment plan
  • Not a standalone cure
  • Combined with exercise, manual therapy
  • Results vary individually
  • Safe when properly applied

Questions to Ask

  • Why is ultrasound being recommended?
  • What outcomes can I expect?
  • How many sessions needed?
  • What other treatments are planned?
