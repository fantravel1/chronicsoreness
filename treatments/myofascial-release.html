Myofascial Release

Releasing fascial restrictions

What Is Myofascial Release?

Myofascial release (MFR) is a hands-on therapy that focuses on releasing restrictions in the fasciaâ€”the connective tissue that surrounds muscles, bones, and organs throughout the body. Therapists apply gentle, sustained pressure to areas of restriction to help restore movement and reduce pain.1

Understanding Fascia2

  • Continuous web of connective tissue
  • Surrounds every structure in body
  • Can become tight or restricted
  • Restrictions can cause pain
  • May affect areas distant from restriction

What Causes Fascial Restrictions?

  • Trauma and injury
  • Surgery and scarring
  • Inflammation
  • Poor posture
  • Repetitive movements
  • Prolonged immobility
  • Stress and tension

Techniques3

Direct MFR

  • Pressure applied to restriction
  • Working with tissue resistance
  • Sustained holds

Indirect MFR

  • Following tissue ease
  • Very gentle pressure
  • Waiting for release

What to Expect

  • Hands-on treatment
  • Sustained pressure (not massage)
  • Holds last 90-120+ seconds
  • May feel stretching or warmth
  • Should not be extremely painful
  • Treatment may be slow and gentle

Conditions That May Benefit

  • Myofascial pain syndrome
  • Chronic neck and back pain
  • Headaches
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Post-surgical adhesions
  • Scar tissue restrictions
  • TMJ dysfunction

Research Status

  • Growing body of research
  • Some positive findings for pain
  • Evidence quality varies
  • Mechanisms still being studied
  • Part of many treatment plans

Self-Myofascial Release

  • Foam rolling
  • Massage balls
  • Can be helpful adjunct
  • Different from hands-on treatment
  • Learn proper technique

Finding Treatment

  • Physical therapists
  • Massage therapists (trained in MFR)
  • Occupational therapists
  • Ask about specific MFR training

After Treatment

  • May feel sore next day
  • Drink water
  • Effects may be gradual
  • Multiple sessions often needed
