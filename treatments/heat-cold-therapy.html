Heat & Cold Therapy

Simple, accessible pain relief methods

Overview

Heat and cold therapy are simple, inexpensive, and effective methods for managing many types of pain. Understanding when to use each can maximize their benefits.1

Heat Therapy (Thermotherapy)

How It Works

  • Increases blood flow
  • Relaxes muscles
  • Decreases joint stiffness
  • Provides comfort

Best For2

  • Chronic pain and stiffness
  • Muscle tension and spasms
  • Arthritis (morning stiffness)
  • Before stretching or exercise

Methods

  • Heating pads
  • Hot water bottles
  • Warm baths/showers
  • Microwaveable heat packs
  • Warm towels

Cold Therapy (Cryotherapy)

How It Works

  • Reduces blood flow (decreases swelling)
  • Numbs pain
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Slows nerve conduction

Best For3

  • Acute injuries (first 48-72 hours)
  • Inflammatory flares
  • After exercise
  • Swelling

Methods

  • Ice packs (wrapped in cloth)
  • Frozen gel packs
  • Ice massage
  • Cold baths

Safety Guidelines

  • Limit to 15-20 minutes at a time
  • Use barrier between skin and ice
  • Check skin frequently
  • Avoid on areas with poor circulation
  • Don't sleep with heating pad on
