Osteopathic Treatment

Hands-on treatment from DOs

What Is Osteopathic Medicine?

Osteopathic medicine is a branch of medicine practiced by Doctors of Osteopathic Medicine (DOs). DOs are fully licensed physicians who can prescribe medications and perform surgery. In addition, they may use hands-on techniques called osteopathic manipulative treatment (OMT).1

What Is OMT?2

  • Hands-on diagnosis and treatment
  • Uses various manual techniques
  • Aims to improve body function
  • Based on understanding body's structure affects function
  • Can be integrated with conventional medicine

OMT Techniques

Soft Tissue

  • Stretching and pressure on muscles
  • Reduces tension and improves circulation

Muscle Energy

  • Patient actively contracts muscles against resistance
  • Followed by stretching

High-Velocity, Low-Amplitude (HVLA)

  • Quick thrust to restore joint motion
  • Similar to chiropractic adjustment

Counterstrain

  • Finding tender points
  • Positioning to relieve tension
  • Very gentle technique

Myofascial Release

  • Sustained pressure on restricted areas
  • Releases fascial restrictions

Conditions Treated3

  • Low back pain
  • Neck pain
  • Headaches
  • Joint pain
  • Post-surgical pain
  • Pregnancy-related pain

Evidence

  • Best evidence for low back pain
  • May reduce medication use
  • Generally safe
  • Part of comprehensive care

DOs vs. MDs

  • Both fully licensed physicians
  • Same residency programs
  • DOs have additional training in OMT
  • Not all DOs practice OMT
  • Philosophy emphasizes whole-person care

What to Expect

  • Physical examination including palpation
  • Assessment of body mechanics
  • Hands-on treatment (usually 15-30 minutes)
  • May be combined with other treatments
  • Often covered by insurance

Finding Treatment

  • Find a DO who practices OMT
  • Not all DOs incorporate OMT into practice
  • Neuromusculoskeletal medicine specialists focus on OMT
  • Ask specifically if they offer OMT
