Pain Education

Understanding pain to manage it better

What Is Pain Neuroscience Education?

Pain neuroscience education (PNE) teaches people how pain works from a biological and neurological perspective. Research shows that understanding pain can actually reduce pain intensity, improve function, and decrease fear. Knowledge is genuinely therapeutic for chronic pain.1

Key Concepts2

Pain Is Produced by the Brain

  • Pain is an output of the brain, not just input from tissues
  • The brain decides whether to produce pain
  • Many factors influence this decision

Pain Doesn't Equal Damage

  • Pain and tissue damage don't always match
  • You can have damage without pain
  • You can have pain without damage
  • Chronic pain often persists after healing

The Nervous System Can Change

  • Pain pathways can become sensitized
  • But they can also desensitize
  • The brain can learn to turn down pain

Why Education Helps3

  • Reduces fear of pain and movement
  • Decreases catastrophizing
  • Changes how brain processes pain
  • Improves engagement with treatment
  • Validated in research studies

What Pain Education Teaches

  • How nerves transmit signals
  • How the brain interprets danger
  • Why pain can persist after healing
  • How thoughts and emotions affect pain
  • Why hurt doesn't always mean harm
  • How to calm the nervous system

This Doesn't Mean

  • Your pain isn't real
  • It's all psychological
  • You're making it up
  • You can just think pain away

Resources to Learn More

  • "Explain Pain" by Butler and Moseley
  • "Why Do I Hurt?" by Louw

Applying Knowledge

  • Recognize when fear is driving behavior
  • Gradually return to activities
  • Reframe what pain means
  • Combine with other treatments
