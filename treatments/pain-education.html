Pain Neuroscience Education

Understanding how pain works

What Is Pain Education?

Pain neuroscience education (PNE) teaches people about the biology and neuroscience of pain. Understanding how pain worksâ€”especially that pain doesn't always equal tissue damageâ€”can reduce fear, improve function, and even decrease pain intensity. It's an evidence-based treatment component.1

Key Concepts2

Pain Is in the Brain

  • Pain is an output of the brain, not an input
  • The brain decides if something is painful
  • Many factors influence this decision

Pain Doesn't Equal Damage

  • You can have pain without tissue damage
  • You can have damage without pain
  • Chronic pain often exists after tissues heal

The Nervous System Can Change

  • Nerves can become sensitized
  • Pain system can become overprotective
  • These changes can be reversed

Many Factors Affect Pain

  • Stress and emotions
  • Sleep
  • Beliefs about pain
  • Social factors
  • Previous experiences

Benefits of Pain Education3

  • Reduces fear of movement
  • Decreases catastrophizing
  • Improves function
  • Can reduce pain intensity
  • Improves treatment outcomes
  • Empowers self-management

This Is Not...

  • Saying your pain isn't real
  • "It's all in your head"
  • A replacement for other treatments
  • A quick fix

Resources

  • Books: "Explain Pain," "Why Do I Hurt?"
  • Videos: "Understanding Pain" (YouTube)
  • Physical therapists trained in PNE
  • Pain psychology

How to Use This Knowledge

  • Movement is safe even with some pain
  • Hurt doesn't always mean harm
  • Gradual exposure to feared activities
  • Address factors that sensitize your system
  • Combine with active treatments
