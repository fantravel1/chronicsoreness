Intrathecal Drug Pumps

Delivering medication directly to the spine

What Is an Intrathecal Pump?

An intrathecal drug delivery system is an implanted pump that delivers pain medication directly into the spinal fluid. Because medication goes directly to pain receptors in the spinal cord, very small doses can provide significant relief.1

How It Works

  • Pump implanted under skin (usually abdomen)
  • Catheter runs from pump to spinal canal
  • Medication released directly into spinal fluid
  • Programmable dosing
  • Pump refilled every 1-6 months

Medications Used2

  • Morphine (most common)
  • Hydromorphone
  • Ziconotide
  • Baclofen (for spasticity)
  • Combinations

Who May Benefit

  • Failed back surgery syndrome
  • Cancer pain
  • Complex regional pain syndrome
  • Severe spasticity
  • Poor response to oral medications
  • Intolerable oral medication side effects

The Process3

Trial Phase

  • Temporary catheter placement
  • Test medication effectiveness
  • Usually requires 50%+ pain relief

Permanent Implantation

  • Surgical procedure
  • Hospital stay often required
  • Recovery takes several weeks

Considerations

  • Requires ongoing refills and maintenance
  • Battery replacement every 5-7 years
  • Potential complications (infection, catheter issues)
  • MRI compatibility varies
  • Significant commitment
