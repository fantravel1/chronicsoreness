Intrathecal Pain Pumps

Targeted drug delivery for severe pain

What Is an Intrathecal Pump?

An intrathecal pump is a surgically implanted device that delivers pain medication directly into the spinal fluid. By delivering medication to the spine rather than through the bloodstream, much smaller doses can provide significant relief with fewer systemic side effects. It's typically reserved for severe, chronic pain that hasn't responded to other treatments.1

How It Works2

  • Pump reservoir holds medication
  • Catheter delivers to intrathecal space
  • Medication bathes spinal cord/nerves
  • 1/300th of oral dose may be effective
  • Programmable delivery rates

Components

Pump

  • Implanted in abdomen
  • Contains medication reservoir
  • Programmable by external device
  • Battery life ~7 years

Catheter

  • Thin tube to spinal canal
  • Tunneled under skin
  • Connects pump to spine

Who Might Benefit3

  • Failed back surgery syndrome
  • Cancer pain
  • Chronic pancreatitis
  • CRPS (complex regional pain syndrome)
  • Arachnoiditis
  • Patients who respond to oral opioids but have intolerable side effects

Trial Period

  • Required before permanent implant
  • Temporary catheter placement
  • Tests response to intrathecal medication
  • 50%+ pain relief typically needed

Medications Used

  • Morphine (FDA approved)
  • Ziconotide (FDA approved)
  • Hydromorphone (off-label)
  • Local anesthetics (off-label)
  • Sometimes combinations

The Procedure

  • Surgical implantation
  • General or local anesthesia
  • Hospital stay may be needed
  • Recovery takes weeks

Ongoing Care

  • Regular pump refills (every 1-6 months)
  • Done in clinic
  • Dose adjustments as needed
  • Monitoring for complications
  • Battery replacement surgery eventually

Risks and Complications

  • Infection
  • Catheter problems
  • Medication side effects
  • Granuloma formation (rare)
  • Spinal headache
  • Device malfunction

Considerations

  • Major commitment
  • Requires ongoing care
  • MRI compatibility varies
  • Travel considerations
  • Insurance coverage varies
