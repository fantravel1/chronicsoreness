TENS Therapy

Portable electrical pain relief

What Is TENS?

Transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS) is a non-invasive pain relief method using a small battery-operated device that delivers mild electrical impulses through electrodes placed on the skin.1

How It Works

  • Gate control - Electrical signals block pain transmission
  • Endorphin release - May stimulate natural pain relief
  • Effects often temporary but helpful

Types of TENS2

Conventional (High Frequency)

  • 80-120 Hz
  • Tingling sensation
  • Quick onset, shorter duration

Acupuncture-Like (Low Frequency)

  • 1-10 Hz
  • Muscle twitching sensation
  • Slower onset, longer relief

Conditions Treated

  • Low back pain
  • Neck pain
  • Arthritis pain
  • Neuropathic pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Post-surgical pain
  • Labor pain

How to Use3

  • Place electrodes around painful area
  • Start at low intensity, increase gradually
  • Should feel comfortable tingling
  • Use 20-60 minutes per session
  • Can use multiple times daily

Safety Considerations

  • Avoid with pacemakers/implanted devices
  • Don't place over heart, carotid sinus, or pregnant uterus
  • Don't use on broken skin
  • May cause skin irritation
