TENS Therapy

Electrical stimulation for pain relief

What Is TENS?

Transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS) is a therapy that uses low-voltage electrical currents delivered through electrodes placed on the skin. TENS units are portable devices that can be used at home for pain management. While evidence is mixed, many people find TENS helpful as part of their pain management toolkit.1

How It Works2

Gate Control Theory

  • Stimulates sensory nerves
  • May "close the gate" on pain signals
  • Competes with pain transmission

Endorphin Release

  • Low-frequency TENS may trigger endorphins
  • Natural pain-relieving chemicals
  • Can provide longer-lasting relief

Using TENS

Electrode Placement

  • Usually around or near painful area
  • Various placement patterns
  • Follow instructions or PT guidance
  • Clean, dry skin

Settings

  • High frequency (80-120 Hz): gate control
  • Low frequency (1-10 Hz): endorphins
  • Intensity: strong but comfortable
  • May need to experiment

What to Expect

  • Tingling or buzzing sensation
  • Should not be painful
  • Effect often during or shortly after use
  • Sessions typically 15-60 minutes
  • Can use multiple times daily

Research Evidence3

  • Mixed results in studies
  • Some evidence for chronic pain
  • May help osteoarthritis
  • Low back pain evidence limited
  • Generally considered safe
  • Individual response varies

Conditions Often Tried

  • Low back pain
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Neuropathic pain
  • Neck pain
  • Labor pain

Safety Precautions

  • Not over pacemakers
  • Not on front of neck
  • Not over pregnant uterus
  • Avoid broken skin
  • Not while driving or in water
  • Not near metallic implants

Getting a TENS Unit

  • Available without prescription
  • Pharmacies, online retailers
  • Some insurance covers with prescription
  • Physical therapists can instruct
  • Range of prices

Tips for Success

  • Try different electrode placements
  • Experiment with settings
  • Give it adequate trial
  • Replace electrodes regularly
  • Use as part of overall plan
