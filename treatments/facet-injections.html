Facet Joint Injections

Diagnostic and therapeutic injections for spine pain

What Are Facet Joints?

Facet joints are small joints on either side of the spine that provide stability and guide movement. They can become painful due to arthritis, injury, or degenerative changes. Facet joint pain is a common cause of neck and back pain.1

Types of Injections

Intra-articular Facet Injection

  • Injection directly into the joint
  • Steroid and local anesthetic
  • Diagnostic and therapeutic

Medial Branch Block

  • Targets nerves supplying the facet joint
  • Primarily diagnostic
  • If successful, may proceed to RFA

Facet Pain Characteristics2

  • Axial pain (doesn't radiate far)
  • Worse with extension, rotation
  • May refer to buttocks, thigh (lumbar) or shoulder, arm (cervical)
  • Tender over facet joints
  • Morning stiffness

The Procedure

  • Outpatient procedure
  • Fluoroscopic (X-ray) guidance
  • Local anesthetic at skin
  • Needle guided to target
  • Contrast confirms placement
  • Medication injected
  • Takes 15-30 minutes

Effectiveness3

  • Diagnostic blocks highly valuable
  • Therapeutic benefit variable
  • Relief may be temporary
  • Can guide further treatment (RFA)

Risks

  • Temporary pain increase
  • Bleeding
  • Infection (rare)
  • Allergic reaction
  • Steroid side effects

After the Procedure

  • Track pain response (pain diary)
  • May have temporary leg weakness
  • Results help determine next steps
