Epidural Steroid Injections

Delivering anti-inflammatory medication to the spine

What Are Epidural Steroid Injections?

Epidural steroid injections (ESIs) deliver corticosteroid medication into the epidural space around the spinal nerves. The steroid reduces inflammation, which can relieve pain from nerve irritation. They're commonly used for radicular pain (sciatica) from disc herniations or spinal stenosis.1

Types of Approaches

  • Interlaminar - Between vertebrae, targets central epidural space
  • Transforaminal - Through the foramen, more targeted
  • Caudal - Through sacral hiatus, often for lower lumbar

Conditions Treated2

  • Herniated disc with radiculopathy
  • Spinal stenosis
  • Degenerative disc disease
  • Post-surgical pain
  • Some cases of chronic back pain

The Procedure

  • Outpatient, takes 15-30 minutes
  • Local anesthesia
  • Fluoroscopic (X-ray) guidance
  • Contrast dye confirms placement
  • Steroid and anesthetic injected

What to Expect3

  • May have temporary numbness/weakness
  • Pain relief may take days to weeks
  • Duration varies (weeks to months)
  • Can be repeated (usually limit of 3-4/year)

Potential Side Effects

  • Temporary increase in pain
  • Headache
  • Elevated blood sugar (diabetics)
  • Facial flushing
  • Rare: infection, bleeding, nerve injury
