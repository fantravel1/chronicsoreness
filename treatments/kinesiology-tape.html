Kinesiology Tape

Elastic tape for pain and support

What Is Kinesiology Tape?

Kinesiology tape (often called KT tape or K-tape) is an elastic adhesive tape designed to support muscles and joints while allowing full range of motion. Originally developed in the 1970s, it gained popularity after being seen on Olympic athletes. It's used for pain relief, swelling reduction, and support.1

How It's Proposed to Work2

  • Lifts skin slightly
  • May improve circulation
  • Proposed to reduce pressure on tissues
  • Sensory feedback to brain
  • May alter pain perception
  • Exact mechanisms unclear

Common Uses

  • Muscle pain and strains
  • Joint pain
  • Tendinitis
  • Posture support
  • Swelling (edema)
  • Sports injuries
  • Bruising

Evidence Base3

  • Research results are mixed
  • Some studies show modest benefits
  • Others show no advantage over sham
  • May help some individuals
  • Likely safe to try
  • Placebo effect possible

How to Apply

  • Clean, dry skin
  • Round corners to prevent peeling
  • Various application patterns
  • Apply with stretch as directed
  • Rub to activate adhesive
  • Can last several days

Professional Application

  • Physical therapists can apply and teach
  • Different techniques for different problems
  • Proper application may matter
  • Learn correct method first

Advantages

  • Non-invasive
  • Allows movement
  • Can wear during activity
  • Water-resistant
  • Relatively inexpensive
  • Low risk of harm

Limitations

  • Evidence isn't strong
  • Not a cure
  • Effects may be modest
  • Proper application important
  • Skin irritation possible

Precautions

  • Skin allergies or sensitivities
  • Don't apply over wounds
  • Fragile skin (elderly)
  • Active infection
  • DVT or blood clots
  • Remove if irritation occurs

Removal Tips

  • Remove slowly
  • Pull parallel to skin
  • Use oil to loosen adhesive
  • Remove after shower (easier)
