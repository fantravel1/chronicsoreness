Spinal Cord Stimulation

Electrical impulses to interrupt pain signals

What Is Spinal Cord Stimulation?

Spinal cord stimulation (SCS) is a pain treatment that uses electrical impulses delivered by an implanted device to interrupt pain signals traveling to the brain. It's used for chronic pain that hasn't responded to other treatments.1

How It Works

  • Thin electrodes placed in epidural space
  • Connected to pulse generator (battery)
  • Electrical impulses block pain signals
  • Replaces pain with mild tingling (or none with newer technology)
  • Patient controls stimulation with remote

Conditions Treated2

  • Failed back surgery syndrome
  • Complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS)
  • Chronic leg or arm pain
  • Peripheral neuropathy
  • Angina (refractory)
  • Peripheral vascular disease

The Procedure

Trial Period

  • Temporary leads placed first
  • Test for 5-7 days
  • 50% pain relief = successful trial
  • If successful, proceed to permanent implant

Permanent Implant

  • Outpatient or overnight procedure
  • Leads and generator implanted
  • Generator in buttock or abdomen
  • Programmable settings

Types of Stimulation3

  • Traditional SCS - Creates tingling sensation
  • High-frequency SCS - No tingling
  • Burst stimulation - Mimics natural nerve patterns
  • Dorsal root ganglion (DRG) - Targets specific areas

Benefits

  • 50%+ pain relief in many patients
  • Reduced medication use
  • Improved function
  • Reversible and adjustable
  • Trial before permanent implant

Risks and Considerations

  • Infection
  • Lead migration
  • Hardware failure
  • Need for revision surgery
  • Battery replacement every 5-10 years
  • MRI compatibility varies
