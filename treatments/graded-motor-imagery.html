Graded Motor Imagery

Retraining the brain for pain relief

What Is Graded Motor Imagery?

Graded motor imagery (GMI) is a treatment approach that uses mental exercises to retrain the brain's perception of a painful body part. It's based on the understanding that chronic pain involves changes in how the brain processes information about the body. GMI aims to normalize these brain maps and reduce pain.1

The Theory2

  • Chronic pain changes brain body maps
  • Brain representation becomes disrupted
  • This contributes to ongoing pain
  • Mental exercises can reorganize maps
  • Gradual approach prevents flare-ups

The Three Stages

Stage 1: Left/Right Discrimination

  • Looking at pictures of body parts
  • Identifying if image is left or right
  • Activates brain representation
  • Without physical movement
  • Apps and cards available

Stage 2: Imagined Movements

  • Imagine moving the painful part
  • No actual movement
  • Visualize different movements
  • Activates motor planning areas

Stage 3: Mirror Therapy

  • Use mirror to reflect unaffected limb
  • Brain "sees" pain-free movement
  • Can reduce pain perception
  • Move both limbs (hidden painful one)

Conditions That May Benefit3

  • Complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS)
  • Phantom limb pain
  • Stroke-related pain
  • Chronic limb pain
  • Some musculoskeletal conditions

Evidence

  • Best evidence for CRPS
  • Promising for phantom limb pain
  • Mixed results for other conditions
  • Research ongoing
  • Low risk, potential benefit

How to Do It

  • Usually guided by trained therapist
  • Progress through stages gradually
  • Don't skip stages
  • Practice regularly (multiple times/day)
  • Track progress and symptoms

Resources

  • Recognise apps (NOI group)
  • Physical therapists trained in GMI
  • Pain specialists
  • Online resources
