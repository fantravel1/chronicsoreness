Graded Motor Imagery

Retraining the brain to reduce pain

What Is Graded Motor Imagery?

Graded Motor Imagery (GMI) is a brain-based treatment that aims to gradually retrain the brain's representation of a painful body part. It progresses through stages that activate the motor cortex without initially requiring movement of the painful area.1

The Science Behind GMI2

  • Chronic pain involves brain changes
  • Body representation in brain becomes altered
  • GMI aims to normalize these representations
  • Gradual progression reduces threat

The Three Stages

1. Laterality Recognition

  • Identify left vs. right body parts in images
  • Activates body representation without movement
  • Use apps or flash cards
  • Practice until quick and accurate

2. Explicit Motor Imagery

  • Imagine moving the affected body part
  • Visualize specific movements
  • Should feel real but not cause pain
  • Start simple, progress to complex

3. Mirror Therapy

  • Use mirror to reflect unaffected side
  • Move unaffected side while watching reflection
  • Brain "sees" affected side moving painlessly
  • Progress to actual movement

Conditions That May Benefit3

  • Complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS)
  • Phantom limb pain
  • Chronic limb pain
  • Post-stroke pain
  • Some other chronic pain conditions

Important Considerations

  • Requires consistent daily practice
  • Progress through stages in order
  • Work with trained therapist initially
  • May take weeks to months
