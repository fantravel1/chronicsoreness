Biofeedback

Learning to control body functions for pain relief

What Is Biofeedback?

Biofeedback is a technique that teaches you to control physiological processes that are normally involuntary, such as heart rate, muscle tension, and skin temperature. Sensors provide real-time feedback that helps you learn to make changes.1

How It Works

  • Sensors detect body signals
  • Signals displayed on monitor (visual/audio)
  • Learn to change responses through practice
  • Skills transfer to daily life

Types of Biofeedback2

  • EMG (electromyography) - Muscle tension
  • Thermal - Skin temperature (blood flow)
  • Heart rate variability (HRV) - Heart rhythm
  • EEG (neurofeedback) - Brain waves
  • GSR (galvanic skin response) - Sweat/stress

Conditions Treated

  • Tension headaches
  • Migraines
  • Chronic back pain
  • TMJ disorders
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Raynaud's phenomenon
  • Stress-related pain

Evidence3

  • Strong evidence - Tension headaches, migraines
  • Moderate evidence - TMJ, chronic low back pain
  • Some evidence - Fibromyalgia

What to Expect

  • Initial assessment
  • Sensors attached (non-invasive)
  • Practice relaxation techniques while watching feedback
  • Learn patterns that reduce symptoms
  • Home practice often assigned
  • Usually 8-10 sessions

Benefits

  • Non-invasive and safe
  • No medications
  • Teaches self-regulation skills
  • Skills last beyond treatment
  • Empowering - puts you in control
