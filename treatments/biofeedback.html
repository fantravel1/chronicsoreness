Biofeedback

Learning to control your body's responses to reduce pain

Key Takeaways

  • Biofeedback uses sensors to provide real-time information about body functions
  • Teaches you to consciously control normally unconscious processes
  • Strong evidence for headaches and migraines, good evidence for other pain conditions
  • No side effects and can be learned for home practice

What Is Biofeedback?

Biofeedback is a technique that uses electronic monitoring to give you real-time information about physiological processes in your bodyâ€”such as muscle tension, heart rate, skin temperature, and brain waves. With this feedback, you can learn to consciously influence these processes to improve health and reduce pain.1

How It Works

  1. Sensors are attached to your body to measure specific functions
  2. These measurements are displayed on a screen in real time
  3. A trained therapist guides you in techniques to change the readings
  4. With practice, you learn to control these functions without the equipment
Grade A Evidence
for biofeedback in tension headache and migraine treatment2

Types of Biofeedback

EMG (Electromyography) Biofeedback

  • Measures muscle tension
  • Most commonly used for pain conditions
  • Teaches muscle relaxation
  • Used for headaches, TMJ, back pain, fibromyalgia

Thermal Biofeedback

  • Measures skin temperature (usually finger)
  • Indicates blood flow and relaxation
  • Warm hands = relaxed state
  • Effective for migraines and Raynaud's

Heart Rate Variability (HRV) Biofeedback

  • Measures variations in heartbeat timing
  • Reflects autonomic nervous system balance
  • Training can reduce stress response
  • Used for stress, anxiety, and various pain conditions

Neurofeedback (EEG Biofeedback)

  • Measures brain wave activity
  • Trains specific brain wave patterns
  • Used for chronic pain, fibromyalgia, migraines
  • More specialized and time-intensive

Conditions Biofeedback Helps

Research supports biofeedback for several conditions:3

Strong Evidence

  • Tension-type headaches: EMG biofeedback highly effective
  • Migraines: Thermal and EMG biofeedback both effective
  • TMJ disorders: EMG biofeedback for jaw muscle tension

Good Evidence

  • Chronic low back pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Raynaud's phenomenon
  • Chronic pelvic pain
  • Irritable bowel syndrome

Emerging Evidence

  • Neck pain
  • CRPS (complex regional pain syndrome)
  • Phantom limb pain
  • Chronic fatigue syndrome

What to Expect

Initial Session

  • Assessment of your condition and goals
  • Explanation of biofeedback principles
  • Baseline measurements
  • Introduction to relaxation techniques
  • Practice with the equipment

Ongoing Sessions

  • Typically 8-12 sessions, sometimes more
  • Sessions usually 30-60 minutes
  • Progressive skill development
  • Home practice assigned between sessions
  • Gradual reduction in equipment dependence

The Training Process

  1. Learn awareness of body responses
  2. Practice techniques to change responses
  3. Use feedback to refine your control
  4. Apply skills without equipment
  5. Generalize skills to daily life situations

Techniques Used with Biofeedback

Diaphragmatic Breathing

  • Slow, deep belly breathing
  • Activates parasympathetic nervous system
  • Foundational technique for most biofeedback

Progressive Muscle Relaxation

  • Systematically tensing and relaxing muscles
  • Increases awareness of tension
  • EMG biofeedback provides objective feedback

Autogenic Training

  • Self-suggestions of warmth and heaviness
  • Often used with thermal biofeedback
  • Promotes deep relaxation

Guided Imagery

  • Visualization of peaceful scenes
  • Can enhance other techniques
  • Biofeedback confirms effectiveness

Finding Biofeedback Treatment

Practitioners

  • Psychologists with biofeedback training
  • Physical therapists
  • Certified biofeedback practitioners
  • Pain clinics and rehabilitation centers

Certification

  • BCIA (Biofeedback Certification International Alliance)
  • Look for BCIA-certified practitioners
  • Ask about training and experience with your condition

Home Devices

  • Consumer devices available for home practice
  • HRV devices (heart rate variability)
  • Temperature feedback devices
  • Apps that use phone sensors
  • Best used after professional training

Benefits of Biofeedback

  • No side effects: Non-invasive and safe
  • Self-empowering: You develop active control
  • Long-lasting: Skills can be used indefinitely
  • Drug-free: May reduce medication needs
  • Objective feedback: See your progress in real time
  • Transferable skills: Apply to many situations

Research Support

The Association for Applied Psychophysiology and Biofeedback (AAPB) has established evidence-based efficacy ratings. Biofeedback for tension headaches and migraines has the highest level of evidence (Level 4-5: Efficacious to Highly Efficacious).4
