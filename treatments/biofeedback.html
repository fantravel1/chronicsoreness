Biofeedback

Learning to control body responses

What Is Biofeedback?

Biofeedback uses electronic sensors to measure body functions (like muscle tension or heart rate) and displays this information in real-time. This allows you to learn to consciously control these typically automatic processes to reduce pain and stress.1

Types of Biofeedback2

EMG (Electromyography)

  • Measures muscle tension
  • Common for headaches, TMJ, back pain
  • Learn to relax specific muscles

Thermal Biofeedback

  • Measures skin temperature
  • Indicates blood flow
  • Used for migraines, Raynaud's

Heart Rate Variability (HRV)

  • Measures heart rhythm patterns
  • Reflects stress response
  • Trains relaxation

Neurofeedback (EEG)

  • Measures brain waves
  • Trains brain activity patterns

Conditions Treated

  • Tension headaches
  • Migraines
  • TMJ disorders
  • Chronic back pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Pelvic floor dysfunction

What to Expect3

  • Sessions with trained therapist
  • Sensors attached to body
  • Visual or audio feedback
  • Practice relaxation techniques
  • Usually 8-12 sessions
  • Home practice between sessions

Benefits

  • Non-invasive
  • No medications
  • Skills transfer to daily life
  • Increases sense of control
  • Good evidence for some conditions
