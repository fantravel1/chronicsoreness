Biofeedback

Learning to control body functions

What Is Biofeedback?

Biofeedback is a technique that teaches you to control physiological functions that are normally automatic, such as muscle tension, heart rate, and breathing. Sensors provide real-time feedback about body processes, helping you learn to make changes that can reduce pain and stress.1

How It Works

  • Sensors monitor body functions
  • Information displayed on screen or through sounds
  • Therapist guides you to make changes
  • Practice helps you learn voluntary control
  • Eventually can use techniques without sensors

Types of Biofeedback2

EMG (Electromyography)

  • Measures muscle tension
  • Good for tension headaches, TMJ, neck/back pain
  • Helps learn to relax specific muscles

Thermal (Temperature)

  • Measures skin temperature
  • Used for migraines, Raynaud's
  • Teaches blood flow regulation

Heart Rate Variability (HRV)

  • Monitors heart rate patterns
  • Helps with stress and anxiety
  • Promotes nervous system balance

Neurofeedback (EEG)

  • Monitors brain wave activity
  • Can help with chronic pain conditions
  • More specialized training needed

Conditions That May Benefit3

  • Tension headaches and migraines
  • TMJ disorders
  • Chronic back pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Raynaud's phenomenon
  • Stress-related pain

What to Expect

  • Sessions typically 30-60 minutes
  • Usually need 8-20 sessions
  • Homework practice recommended
  • Non-invasive and pain-free
  • Skills can be used lifelong

Evidence

  • Strong evidence for tension headaches
  • Good evidence for migraines
  • Moderate evidence for various pain conditions
  • Often combined with other treatments

Finding Treatment

  • Certified biofeedback practitioners
  • Pain clinics often offer it
  • Some psychologists specialize in it
  • Home devices available for some types
