Kinesiology Taping

Elastic tape for support and sensory feedback

What Is Kinesiology Tape?

Kinesiology tape (K-tape) is elastic therapeutic tape applied to the skin to provide support, reduce pain, and improve function without restricting movement. It became popular after being seen on Olympic athletes.1

Proposed Mechanisms

  • Lifts skin to improve circulation
  • Provides sensory feedback
  • Supports muscles without limiting range
  • May reduce swelling
  • Psychological/placebo component

What the Evidence Shows2

  • Some benefit for pain in certain conditions
  • May help with function short-term
  • Mixed results in research
  • Generally considered safe
  • May work as part of comprehensive treatment

Common Applications

  • Shoulder pain
  • Low back pain
  • Knee pain (patellofemoral)
  • Neck pain
  • Plantar fasciitis
  • Swelling/edema

Application Tips3

  • Clean, dry skin
  • No lotions before application
  • Round the corners of tape
  • Apply 30 min before sweating
  • Can leave on 3-5 days
  • Remove gently (with oil if needed)

Precautions

  • Not for fragile or damaged skin
  • Avoid on areas of infection
  • Watch for skin irritation
  • Consider latex allergies

Getting Started

  • Learn from a trained professional first
  • Different techniques for different goals
  • Online tutorials available
  • Pre-cut applications available
