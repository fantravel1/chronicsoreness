Osteopathic Manipulative Treatment

Hands-on care from osteopathic physicians

What Is OMT?

Osteopathic manipulative treatment (OMT) is a set of hands-on techniques used by doctors of osteopathic medicine (DOs) to diagnose, treat, and prevent illness. It involves moving muscles and joints using stretching, gentle pressure, and resistance.1

Techniques Used2

  • High-velocity, low-amplitude (HVLA) - Quick thrust
  • Muscle energy - Patient contracts against resistance
  • Counterstrain - Positioning to relieve tender points
  • Myofascial release - Sustained pressure on fascia
  • Cranial osteopathy - Gentle skull/sacrum techniques
  • Soft tissue - Massage-like techniques

Conditions Treated

  • Low back pain
  • Neck pain
  • Headaches
  • Shoulder and hip problems
  • Postural issues
  • Sometimes other conditions

What to Expect3

  • Full medical evaluation
  • Structural examination
  • Treatment session (15-30 minutes)
  • May feel popping or stretching
  • Should not be painful
  • May have brief soreness after

Evidence

  • Best evidence for low back pain
  • Comparable to other manual therapies
  • Part of multimodal approach
  • Generally safe

DOs vs. Chiropractors

  • DOs are fully licensed physicians
  • Can prescribe medications
  • OMT is one part of care
  • Some DOs use OMT, many don't
