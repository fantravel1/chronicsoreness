Peripheral Nerve Stimulation

Targeting specific nerves for pain relief

What Is Peripheral Nerve Stimulation?

Peripheral nerve stimulation (PNS) is a neuromodulation technique where electrodes are placed near specific peripheral nerves to deliver mild electrical impulses. Unlike spinal cord stimulation which targets the spinal cord, PNS directly targets the nerves responsible for transmitting pain signals from a specific area.1

How It Works2

  • Electrodes placed near target nerve
  • Mild electrical pulses delivered
  • Interrupts pain signal transmission
  • May work by gate control mechanism
  • Modulates nerve activity
  • Patient controls stimulation

Conditions Treated

  • Post-surgical neuropathic pain
  • Peripheral nerve injuries
  • Occipital neuralgia
  • CRPS (complex regional pain syndrome)
  • Post-amputation pain
  • Localized chronic pain
  • Some headache conditions

The Procedure3

Trial Period

  • Temporary electrode placement
  • Tests effectiveness for days to weeks
  • Determines if permanent helpful

Permanent Implant

  • Small electrode(s) placed near nerve
  • Generator implanted under skin
  • Outpatient or short stay procedure
  • Programming session after healing

Types of Systems

  • Traditional implanted systems
  • External powered systems
  • Ultrasound-guided temporary systems
  • Newer rechargeable devices
  • Technology advancing rapidly

Benefits

  • Targets specific pain area
  • Can be trialed first
  • Reversible (can be removed)
  • Adjustable settings
  • May reduce medication need

Risks and Considerations

  • Infection
  • Lead migration
  • Hardware malfunction
  • Skin irritation
  • May lose effectiveness
  • Not effective for everyone

Candidate Selection

  • Well-defined pain source
  • Failed conservative treatments
  • Positive response to nerve blocks
  • Psychological evaluation
  • Realistic expectations

Living with PNS

  • Regular device checks
  • Battery monitoring
  • Some activity restrictions
  • MRI compatibility varies
  • Carry device ID card
