Pelvic Floor Physical Therapy

Specialized therapy for pelvic conditions

What Is Pelvic Floor PT?

Pelvic floor physical therapy is specialized treatment focused on the muscles, ligaments, and connective tissues of the pelvic floor. These muscles support bladder, bowel, and reproductive organs. Dysfunction can cause pain, incontinence, and other symptoms.1

The Pelvic Floor

  • Bowl-shaped group of muscles
  • Supports pelvic organs
  • Controls bladder and bowel
  • Functions in sexual activity
  • Can be too tight or too weak

Conditions Treated2

  • Pelvic pain
  • Painful intercourse
  • Vulvodynia
  • Interstitial cystitis
  • Chronic prostatitis
  • Urinary incontinence
  • Constipation
  • Post-surgical rehabilitation
  • Pregnancy-related issues

What to Expect3

Evaluation

  • Detailed history
  • External examination
  • Internal examination (with consent)
  • Assessment of muscle function

Treatment Techniques

  • Manual therapy (internal and external)
  • Trigger point release
  • Stretching
  • Strengthening exercises
  • Relaxation and down-training
  • Biofeedback
  • Electrical stimulation
  • Education

For Tight vs. Weak Muscles

Overactive/Tight Pelvic Floor

  • Focus on relaxation and lengthening
  • Breathing techniques
  • Gentle stretches
  • Kegels may NOT be appropriate

Weak Pelvic Floor

  • Strengthening exercises
  • Kegels when appropriate
  • Coordination training

Finding a Pelvic PT

  • Look for specialized training in pelvic health
  • Ask your doctor for referral
  • Check APTA or professional directories
  • Available for both women and men
