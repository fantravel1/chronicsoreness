Stem Cell Therapy

Regenerative approaches to pain

What Is Stem Cell Therapy?

Stem cell therapy is a type of regenerative medicine that uses stem cells to promote tissue repair. For pain conditions, the goal is to reduce inflammation and potentially regenerate damaged tissue. While promising, it's important to understand what current evidence supports.1

Types of Regenerative Treatments

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP)

  • Made from your own blood
  • Concentrated platelets and growth factors
  • Most studied regenerative treatment
  • Used for tendon and joint problems

Bone Marrow Aspirate Concentrate

  • Harvested from hip bone
  • Contains stem cells and growth factors
  • More invasive than PRP

Adipose-Derived Stem Cells

  • From fat tissue (liposuction)
  • Less studied than bone marrow
  • Regulatory concerns in some regions

Conditions Being Studied2

  • Knee osteoarthritis (most evidence)
  • Tendon injuries
  • Disc degeneration
  • Hip osteoarthritis
  • Rotator cuff injuries

Current Evidence3

  • PRP: Moderate evidence for knee OA and some tendon problems
  • Stem cells: Limited but emerging evidence
  • More research needed
  • Results vary widely between studies
  • Not proven to regrow cartilage in humans

Important Cautions

  • Not FDA-approved for most uses
  • Usually not covered by insurance
  • Can be expensive ($2,000-$10,000+)
  • Beware of clinics making bold claims
  • Risks include infection and immune reactions
  • No guarantee of benefit

Questions to Ask

  • What specific treatment is being offered?
  • What does the evidence show for my condition?
  • What are realistic expectations?
  • What are the risks?
  • Why is this being recommended over proven treatments?

Red Flags

  • Claims to cure multiple unrelated conditions
  • No discussion of risks or limitations
  • Pressure to decide quickly
  • Very high costs
  • No follow-up plan
