Stem Cell Therapy

Emerging regenerative treatment

What Is Stem Cell Therapy?

Stem cell therapy for musculoskeletal pain involves injecting cells with regenerative potential into damaged tissues. The goal is to promote healing and reduce inflammation. This is an emerging field with growing but still limited evidence.1

Types of Stem Cells Used2

  • Bone marrow concentrate (BMC) - From hip bone
  • Adipose-derived cells - From fat tissue
  • Umbilical cord tissue - Allogeneic (from donors)

Conditions Being Studied

  • Knee osteoarthritis
  • Hip osteoarthritis
  • Disc degeneration
  • Tendon injuries
  • Cartilage defects

Current Evidence3

  • Some promising early results
  • Many studies are small or low quality
  • Long-term outcomes unknown
  • Not FDA-approved for most uses
  • More research needed

Important Considerations

  • Often not covered by insurance
  • Costs can be significant
  • Beware of exaggerated claims
  • Seek reputable providers
  • Consider proven treatments first

Red Flags

  • Claims to cure everything
  • No discussion of risks
  • Pressure to commit quickly
  • Multiple injections at multiple sites
  • No follow-up plan

The Future

Regenerative medicine is a promising field, but it's important to distinguish hope from hype. Evidence is building but not yet conclusive for most applications.
