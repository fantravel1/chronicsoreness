PRP Therapy

Platelet-rich plasma for healing and pain relief

What Is PRP?

Platelet-rich plasma (PRP) is a concentrated solution of platelets derived from your own blood. Platelets contain growth factors that may promote tissue healing. PRP is injected into injured areas to potentially stimulate repair.1

How It Works

  • Blood drawn from patient
  • Centrifuged to concentrate platelets
  • PRP injected into target area
  • Growth factors released
  • May stimulate tissue healing

Conditions Treated2

Better Evidence

  • Knee osteoarthritis
  • Lateral epicondylitis (tennis elbow)
  • Patellar tendinopathy

Emerging/Mixed Evidence

  • Rotator cuff injuries
  • Achilles tendinopathy
  • Hip osteoarthritis
  • Plantar fasciitis
  • Muscle injuries

What to Expect

  • Office procedure (30-60 minutes)
  • Blood draw first
  • Wait while blood processed
  • Injection with or without ultrasound guidance
  • May have soreness for days
  • Full effect takes weeks to months

Evidence3

  • Results vary by condition
  • Quality of studies variable
  • Different PRP preparations make comparison difficult
  • Generally safe (your own blood)
  • Usually not covered by insurance

Advantages

  • Uses patient's own blood (low rejection risk)
  • Minimally invasive
  • Potential for tissue healing

Limitations

  • Expensive out-of-pocket
  • Evidence still emerging
  • Results variable
  • Not standardized
