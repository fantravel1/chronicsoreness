Cupping Therapy

Ancient technique using suction

What Is Cupping?

Cupping therapy is an ancient healing practice that involves placing cups on the skin to create suction. This draws the skin and superficial muscle layer into the cup. Used in traditional Chinese medicine for thousands of years, cupping has gained attention in Western healthcare for musculoskeletal pain conditions.1

Types of Cupping2

Dry Cupping

  • Most common type
  • Suction only
  • Cups left in place 5-15 minutes

Wet Cupping

  • Combines suction with small incisions
  • Controlled bleeding
  • Traditional in some cultures
  • Higher infection risk

Moving/Sliding Cupping

  • Cups moved along muscles
  • Uses oil for gliding
  • Similar to massage effect

How Suction Is Created

  • Fire (traditional method)
  • Hand pump
  • Silicone cups (squeeze and release)
  • Electric pumps

Proposed Mechanisms

  • Increases blood flow to area
  • Releases myofascial restrictions
  • Stimulates nervous system
  • May reduce inflammation
  • Mechanisms not fully understood

Research Evidence3

  • Some studies show benefit for pain
  • Evidence quality often low
  • Difficult to blind participants
  • May help low back pain, neck pain
  • More research needed
  • Placebo effect possible

What to Expect

  • Sensation of pulling/tightness
  • Should not be painful
  • Session lasts 15-30 minutes
  • May feel relaxed afterward
  • Temporary relief for some

The Marks

  • Circular bruise-like marks common
  • Not true bruises (no trauma)
  • Fade over 3-10 days
  • Color varies by individual

Safety Considerations

  • Generally safe when done properly
  • Avoid on broken skin
  • Caution with blood thinners
  • Not over varicose veins
  • Avoid during pregnancy (some areas)
  • Risk of burns with fire cupping
  • Infection risk with wet cupping

Finding a Practitioner

  • Licensed acupuncturists
  • Some massage therapists
  • Some physical therapists
  • Ask about training and experience
