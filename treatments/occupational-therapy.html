Occupational Therapy

Helping you do what matters despite pain

What Is Occupational Therapy?

Occupational therapy (OT) helps people participate in meaningful activities ("occupations") of daily life despite illness, injury, or disability. For chronic pain, OTs focus on improving function, adapting activities, and enhancing quality of life.1

What OTs Address

  • Self-care activities (bathing, dressing, grooming)
  • Home management (cooking, cleaning, laundry)
  • Work and productivity
  • Leisure and recreation
  • Sleep and rest
  • Social participation

OT Interventions for Pain2

  • Activity analysis and modification - Breaking tasks into manageable steps
  • Energy conservation - Pacing strategies
  • Ergonomic assessment - Home and work setup
  • Adaptive equipment - Tools to reduce strain
  • Splinting - For joint protection
  • Hand therapy - For upper extremity conditions

Conditions Commonly Treated

  • Arthritis (RA, OA)
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic fatigue
  • Hand and upper extremity pain
  • CRPS
  • Post-surgical rehabilitation

The OT Process3

  • Comprehensive evaluation of abilities and challenges
  • Goal setting based on your priorities
  • Intervention plan tailored to you
  • Skill building and practice
  • Home program development

Benefits

  • Improved independence
  • Reduced disability
  • Better quality of life
  • Practical, real-world focus
  • Client-centered approach
