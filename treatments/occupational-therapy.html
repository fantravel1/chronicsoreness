Occupational Therapy

Helping you do what matters

What Is Occupational Therapy?

Occupational therapy (OT) helps people participate in daily activities ("occupations") despite illness, injury, or disability. For chronic pain, OTs focus on adapting activities, modifying environments, and teaching strategies to maintain quality of life.1

What OTs Do for Pain2

  • Assess how pain affects daily activities
  • Teach energy conservation techniques
  • Recommend adaptive equipment
  • Modify home/work environments
  • Teach joint protection strategies
  • Address hand function
  • Help with pacing and planning

Areas Addressed

  • Self-care (dressing, bathing, grooming)
  • Home management
  • Work and productivity
  • Leisure activities
  • Sleep positioning
  • Driving

Techniques and Strategies3

Energy Conservation

  • Prioritize important activities
  • Plan and pace activities
  • Eliminate unnecessary steps
  • Use rest breaks effectively

Joint Protection

  • Use larger, stronger joints
  • Avoid sustained gripping
  • Reduce repetitive motions
  • Maintain good posture

Adaptive Equipment

  • Assess need for assistive devices
  • Train in proper use
  • Recommend home modifications

Conditions Benefiting from OT

  • Arthritis
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic fatigue
  • Hand and upper extremity conditions
  • Any condition affecting daily function

Finding an OT

  • Ask your doctor for referral
  • Look for experience in chronic pain
  • Often covered by insurance
