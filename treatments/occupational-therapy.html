Occupational Therapy

Helping you do what matters despite pain

What Is Occupational Therapy?

Occupational therapy (OT) helps people participate in the activities ("occupations") that are meaningful to themâ€”from self-care to work to leisure. For chronic pain, occupational therapists help you find ways to do daily activities with less pain and more efficiency, adapting how you do things rather than giving them up.1

How OT Differs from PT

  • PT focuses on movement and physical function
  • OT focuses on daily activities and participation
  • OT addresses the "how" of daily life
  • Both are valuable and complementary

What OTs Do for Pain2

  • Assess how pain affects daily activities
  • Teach energy conservation techniques
  • Recommend adaptive equipment
  • Modify how tasks are done
  • Address hand and arm problems
  • Help with return to work
  • Activity pacing education

Areas Addressed3

Self-Care

  • Bathing and dressing
  • Grooming
  • Eating and meal preparation
  • Medication management

Home Management

  • Cleaning and housework
  • Laundry
  • Shopping
  • Home organization

Work

  • Ergonomic assessment
  • Workstation setup
  • Job task analysis
  • Return to work planning

Leisure

  • Adapting hobbies
  • Finding new activities
  • Social participation

Techniques Used

  • Activity analysis and modification
  • Pacing and energy conservation
  • Splinting and bracing
  • Hand therapy
  • Cognitive strategies
  • Relaxation techniques

Adaptive Equipment

  • Long-handled tools
  • Jar openers
  • Ergonomic kitchen tools
  • Dressing aids
  • Bathroom safety equipment

Finding an OT

  • Ask doctor for referral
  • Look for experience with chronic pain
  • Available in hospitals, clinics, home health
  • May be covered by insurance
