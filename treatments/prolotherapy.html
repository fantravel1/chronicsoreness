Prolotherapy

Regenerative injection therapy for joint and soft tissue pain

What Is Prolotherapy?

Prolotherapy (proliferative therapy) involves injecting an irritant solution (usually dextrose) into damaged ligaments, tendons, or joints to stimulate the body's natural healing response.1

How It Works (Proposed)

  • Irritant solution triggers inflammation
  • Inflammatory response brings healing factors
  • Stimulates tissue repair and strengthening
  • Multiple treatments typically needed

Solutions Used

  • Dextrose (sugar water) - most common
  • Saline
  • Other irritants

Conditions Treated

  • Chronic ligament sprains
  • Tendinopathy
  • SI joint dysfunction
  • Low back pain
  • Osteoarthritis (some cases)
  • Hypermobility-related instability

Evidence2

  • Mixed results in studies
  • Some evidence for knee OA
  • Less evidence for other conditions
  • Considered experimental by many
  • Usually not covered by insurance

What to Expect

  • Multiple injections per session
  • Usually 3-6 sessions, 2-6 weeks apart
  • Temporary increased pain after treatment
  • Results take time to appear

Safety

  • Generally well-tolerated
  • Main risk is injection site pain
  • Infection (rare)
  • Nerve irritation (rare)
