Prolotherapy

Stimulating the body's healing response

What Is Prolotherapy?

Prolotherapy (proliferative therapy) involves injecting an irritant solution, typically dextrose (sugar water), into damaged ligaments, tendons, or joints. The goal is to stimulate the body's natural healing response and strengthen weakened connective tissue.1

How It Works

  • Injection causes local inflammation
  • Triggers healing cascade
  • New collagen is deposited
  • Tissue may become stronger
  • Multiple treatments usually needed

Common Solutions Used2

  • Dextrose - Most common, well-studied
  • Morrhuate sodium - Less common
  • Phenol-glucose-glycerin - P2G
  • Sometimes combined with local anesthetic

Conditions Treated

  • Chronic low back pain
  • Sacroiliac joint dysfunction
  • Tendinopathies
  • Ligament injuries
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Joint instability

The Procedure3

  • Multiple injections per session
  • Typically 4-6 treatments, 2-6 weeks apart
  • Brief pain during injection
  • Soreness for 2-5 days after
  • Avoid NSAIDs (may interfere with healing)

Evidence

  • Mixed evidence overall
  • Some positive studies for back pain, OA
  • Better evidence for tendinopathies
  • More research needed
  • Generally safe when done properly
