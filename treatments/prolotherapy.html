Prolotherapy

Injection therapy to stimulate healing

What Is Prolotherapy?

Prolotherapy (proliferative therapy) is an injection treatment that aims to stimulate the body's natural healing processes. A solution, typically containing dextrose (sugar water), is injected into painful tendons, ligaments, or joints to trigger an inflammatory response that promotes tissue repair and strengthening.1

How It Works2

  • Irritant solution injected into damaged tissue
  • Triggers localized inflammation
  • Inflammation stimulates healing cascade
  • New collagen production
  • Tissue strengthening over time

What's Injected

  • Dextrose (most common)
  • Sometimes lidocaine added
  • Various concentrations used
  • Some practitioners add other substances

Conditions Treated

  • Chronic low back pain
  • Knee osteoarthritis
  • Tennis elbow
  • Achilles tendinopathy
  • Ligament sprains
  • Joint instability
  • Chronic tendon injuries

What to Expect3

During Treatment

  • Multiple injections at affected sites
  • May be uncomfortable during injection
  • Sessions take 15-30 minutes
  • Usually 3-6 treatments needed
  • Treatments spaced 2-6 weeks apart

After Treatment

  • Soreness at injection sites (2-4 days)
  • Avoid NSAIDs (may interfere with healing)
  • Rest briefly then gradually resume activity
  • Full benefit takes weeks to months

Evidence

  • Mixed research results
  • Best evidence for knee OA, chronic back pain
  • Some studies show significant benefit
  • More research needed
  • Generally considered safe

Risks and Side Effects

  • Injection site pain (common)
  • Swelling and stiffness
  • Infection (rare)
  • Nerve damage (rare)
  • Allergic reaction (rare)

Finding Treatment

  • Not mainstream in all areas
  • Sports medicine, physiatry physicians
  • Some osteopaths and naturopaths
  • Often not covered by insurance
