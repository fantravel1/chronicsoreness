Epidural Steroid Injections

Targeted anti-inflammatory treatment for spine pain

What Are Epidural Steroid Injections?

Epidural steroid injections (ESIs) deliver corticosteroid medication into the epidural space around spinal nerves. The goal is to reduce inflammation and pain from irritated nerve roots. They're commonly used for radicular pain (pain radiating to arms or legs).1

Types of Approaches

  • Interlaminar - Between vertebrae from back
  • Transforaminal - Through neural foramen (more targeted)
  • Caudal - Through sacral hiatus

Conditions Treated2

  • Herniated disc with radiculopathy
  • Spinal stenosis
  • Degenerative disc disease
  • Sciatica
  • Cervical radiculopathy

The Procedure

  • Outpatient procedure
  • Fluoroscopy (X-ray) guidance
  • Local anesthetic at skin
  • Contrast dye confirms placement
  • Steroid injected
  • Takes 15-30 minutes

What to Expect After

  • May feel temporary leg weakness or numbness
  • Effects may take days to weeks
  • Relief duration varies (weeks to months)
  • May be repeated (usually max 3-4/year)

Evidence3

  • Short-term relief often achieved
  • Long-term benefits less clear
  • May help avoid or delay surgery
  • Best for acute radicular symptoms
  • Less effective for pure axial back pain

Risks

  • Temporary pain increase
  • Headache (if dural puncture)
  • Infection (rare)
  • Bleeding (rare)
  • Nerve injury (rare)
  • Steroid side effects (blood sugar, flushing)
