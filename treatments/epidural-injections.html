Epidural Steroid Injections

Spine injections for back and leg pain

What Are Epidural Injections?

Epidural steroid injections (ESIs) deliver anti-inflammatory medication directly into the epidural space around the spinal nerves. They're one of the most common interventional procedures for back and neck pain, particularly when pain radiates into the arms or legs due to nerve irritation.1

How They Work2

  • Steroid reduces inflammation around nerves
  • Local anesthetic provides immediate relief
  • Targets specific area of spine
  • May reduce nerve swelling and irritation
  • Allows natural healing to occur

Conditions Treated

  • Herniated disc
  • Spinal stenosis
  • Sciatica
  • Degenerative disc disease
  • Radiculopathy (nerve root pain)
  • Spondylolisthesis

Types of Approaches

Interlaminar

  • Enters between vertebrae
  • More general spread
  • Good for midline pain

Transforaminal

  • Enters through nerve opening
  • More targeted to specific nerve
  • Often used for leg/arm pain

Caudal

  • Enters through sacrum
  • Used for lower spine issues
  • Less precise targeting

What to Expect3

Before

  • Review medications with doctor
  • May need to stop blood thinners
  • Arrange for driver home

During

  • Usually done under fluoroscopy (X-ray)
  • Takes 15-30 minutes
  • Positioned face down
  • Skin numbed first
  • Pressure sensation during injection

After

  • Observation for 15-30 minutes
  • Rest day of procedure
  • May have temporary leg weakness
  • Full effect in 2-7 days

Effectiveness

  • 50-75% experience some relief
  • Relief duration varies widely
  • Some get weeks, others months
  • May be repeated if helpful
  • Usually limit 3-4 per year

Risks

  • Temporary pain increase
  • Headache
  • Infection (rare)
  • Bleeding (rare)
  • Nerve damage (very rare)
  • Steroid side effects (with repeated use)
