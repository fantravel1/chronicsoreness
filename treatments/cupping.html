Cupping Therapy

Ancient practice gaining modern attention

What Is Cupping?

Cupping is an ancient therapy where special cups are placed on the skin to create suction. The suction draws tissue up into the cup, increasing blood flow to the area. It's used for pain, inflammation, and relaxation.1

Types of Cupping

  • Dry cupping - Suction only
  • Wet cupping - Includes small incisions (less common)
  • Fire cupping - Heat creates suction
  • Silicone cups - Squeezable for suction
  • Pump cups - Mechanical suction

Proposed Mechanisms2

  • Increases local blood flow
  • Releases myofascial restrictions
  • Stimulates healing response
  • May affect pain signaling
  • Relaxation effects

Conditions Treated

  • Low back pain
  • Neck and shoulder pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Headaches
  • Muscle tension

What to Expect

  • Cups left on 5-20 minutes
  • Sensation of pressure/pulling
  • Should not be painful
  • Circular marks are normal
  • Marks fade in days to 2 weeks

Evidence3

  • Some positive research for certain conditions
  • Many studies have methodological limitations
  • May be helpful as part of treatment plan
  • More high-quality research needed

Precautions

  • Avoid on broken skin
  • Not for those on blood thinners
  • Avoid during pregnancy (certain areas)
  • See qualified practitioner
