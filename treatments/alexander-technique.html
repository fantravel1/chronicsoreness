Alexander Technique

Relearning how to move with ease

What Is Alexander Technique?

The Alexander Technique is an educational method that teaches people to recognize and overcome habitual movement patterns that may be contributing to pain and tension. Developed over a century ago, it focuses on awareness of how we use our bodies and making subtle changes to reduce strain and improve coordination.1

Core Principles2

  • Awareness of habitual patterns
  • Inhibition (pausing before reacting)
  • Direction (thinking of length and ease)
  • Primary control (head-neck-back relationship)
  • Constructive conscious control
  • Use affects function

How It Works

  • One-on-one lessons with trained teacher
  • Gentle hands-on guidance
  • Verbal instruction
  • Applied to everyday activities
  • Sitting, standing, walking, lying down
  • Gradually learn new patterns

Evidence for Pain3

  • Research support for back pain
  • ATEAM trial showed lasting benefits
  • Reduced pain and disability
  • Effects maintained at one year
  • May help neck pain
  • Used for repetitive strain

What Lessons Are Like

  • Usually 30-45 minutes
  • Wear comfortable clothing
  • No special equipment needed
  • Teacher guides with light touch
  • Practice everyday movements
  • Time on table (semi-supine)

Benefits

  • Reduced muscle tension
  • Improved posture
  • Better body awareness
  • Skills for self-management
  • Can complement other treatments
  • No negative side effects

Who May Benefit

  • Chronic back and neck pain
  • Tension-related headaches
  • Repetitive strain injuries
  • Musicians and performers
  • Those with postural issues
  • Anyone interested in body awareness

  • Training takes 3+ years
  • Professional organizations list teachers
  • May not be covered by insurance
