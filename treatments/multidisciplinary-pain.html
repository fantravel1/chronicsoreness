Multidisciplinary Pain Programs

Comprehensive team approach to chronic pain

What Are Multidisciplinary Pain Programs?

Multidisciplinary (or interdisciplinary) pain rehabilitation programs bring together a team of specialists to address chronic pain comprehensively. They're considered the gold standard for complex, persistent pain that hasn't responded to other treatments.1

The Team May Include

  • Pain physician
  • Physical therapist
  • Psychologist
  • Occupational therapist
  • Nurses
  • Social worker
  • Pharmacist
  • Other specialists as needed

Program Components2

  • Education - Understanding pain science
  • Physical rehabilitation - Exercise, stretching, pacing
  • Psychological treatment - CBT, coping skills
  • Medication management - Often reduction/optimization
  • Functional restoration - Return to activities
  • Vocational counseling (if relevant)

Program Formats

  • Intensive outpatient - Several hours daily, weeks
  • Inpatient - 24-hour programs
  • Less intensive - Few days per week

Who Benefits3

  • Complex chronic pain
  • Pain with significant disability
  • Failed other treatments
  • High medication use (including opioids)
  • Significant psychological distress
  • Motivated for active participation

Evidence

  • Strong evidence for effectiveness
  • Reduces pain intensity
  • Improves function
  • Reduces healthcare use
  • Improves return to work
  • Reduces medication use

Challenges

  • Requires significant time commitment
  • Not available everywhere
  • Insurance coverage varies
  • May need travel/lodging
