Chiropractic Care

Spinal manipulation for pain relief

What Is Chiropractic?

Chiropractic is a healthcare profession focused on disorders of the musculoskeletal and nervous systems. Chiropractors primarily use hands-on spinal manipulation (adjustments) to restore joint mobility and reduce pain. It's one of the most common complementary treatments for back and neck pain.1

What Chiropractors Do2

Spinal Manipulation (Adjustment)

  • Controlled force applied to joints
  • Aims to restore proper movement
  • Often produces a "popping" sound

Other Treatments

  • Soft tissue therapy
  • Exercise recommendations
  • Ergonomic advice
  • Heat/cold therapy
  • Electrical stimulation

Evidence3

What Research Shows

  • Low back pain: Moderate evidence of benefit
  • Neck pain: Some evidence of benefit
  • Headaches: Some evidence for certain types
  • Comparable to other conservative treatments

Guidelines

  • Recommended as option for acute/chronic low back pain
  • Part of multimodal approach
  • Should include active patient participation

What to Expect

First Visit

  • Health history
  • Physical examination
  • May include X-rays
  • Treatment plan discussion

Treatment

  • Typically 15-30 minute visits
  • Multiple visits often recommended
  • Adjustment may cause brief discomfort
  • Mild soreness afterward is common

Safety

  • Generally safe when performed by licensed chiropractor
  • Serious complications are rare
  • Mild side effects common (soreness, stiffness)
  • Certain conditions require caution or avoidance

When to Avoid

  • Severe osteoporosis
  • Spinal cord compression
  • Cancer in the spine
  • Increased stroke risk (for neck manipulation)
  • Certain inflammatory conditions

Choosing a Chiropractor

  • Check licensing and credentials
  • Look for evidence-based practice
  • Be wary of excessive X-rays or long treatment plans
  • Should include exercise and self-care advice
  • Should refer out when appropriate

Red Flags

  • Claims to cure unrelated conditions
  • Excessive treatment frequency
  • Discourages other medical care
  • High-pressure sales tactics
