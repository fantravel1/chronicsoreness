Chiropractic Care

Spinal manipulation and musculoskeletal treatment

What Is Chiropractic Care?

Chiropractic is a healthcare profession focused on disorders of the musculoskeletal and nervous systems. The primary treatment is spinal manipulation (adjustments), often combined with other manual therapies and exercises.1

Techniques Used

  • Spinal manipulation - High-velocity, low-amplitude thrusts
  • Mobilization - Gentler, low-velocity movements
  • Soft tissue therapy
  • Instrument-assisted manipulation
  • Flexion-distraction
  • Exercise prescription

Evidence for Effectiveness2

Good Evidence

  • Acute and chronic low back pain
  • Neck pain
  • Cervicogenic headache

Some Evidence

  • Migraine (as adjunct)
  • Shoulder pain
  • Extremity conditions

What to Expect

  • Initial evaluation and history
  • Physical examination
  • Treatment plan development
  • Adjustments and other therapies
  • Home exercises often prescribed

Safety and Risks3

  • Generally safe when performed by trained professional
  • Common: temporary soreness, stiffness
  • Rare: serious complications (stroke with neck manipulation - very rare)
  • Contraindications: severe osteoporosis, spinal cord compression, inflammatory arthritis

Choosing a Chiropractor

  • Look for licensed practitioners
  • Evidence-based approach preferred
  • Should include exercise/self-management
  • Avoid long-term treatment contracts
