Pain Neuroscience Education

Understanding pain to change it

What Is Pain Neuroscience Education?

Pain neuroscience education (PNE), also called "explain pain," teaches people about the biology and neuroscience of pain. Understanding how pain works can change pain beliefs, reduce fear, and improve outcomes.1

Key Concepts Taught2

Pain â‰  Damage

  • Pain is an output of the brain
  • Tissue damage and pain don't always match
  • Pain is protective, not always accurate

The Nervous System Changes

  • Central sensitization
  • Nerves can become overly sensitive
  • Alarm system can be recalibrated

Many Factors Influence Pain

  • Thoughts and beliefs
  • Emotions and stress
  • Sleep and activity
  • Social factors
  • Past experiences

Benefits of PNE3

  • Reduces fear of pain and movement
  • Decreases catastrophizing
  • Can reduce pain intensity
  • Improves function
  • Empowers patients
  • Enhances other treatments

What PNE Is NOT

  • Saying pain is "all in your head"
  • Denying pain is real
  • A standalone cure
  • Replacement for medical care

How to Learn More

  • Books: "Explain Pain," "Why Do I Hurt?"
  • Physical therapists trained in PNE
  • Pain management programs
  • Online resources and videos
