Physical Therapy

A cornerstone of chronic pain treatment

What Is Physical Therapy?

Physical therapy (PT) is a healthcare profession focused on helping people improve movement, manage pain, and restore function. For chronic pain, physical therapists use exercise, manual therapy, education, and other techniques to help reduce pain and improve quality of life. PT is recommended as first-line treatment for many chronic pain conditions.1

Why PT for Chronic Pain2

  • Exercise is among most effective pain treatments
  • Addresses physical deconditioning
  • Corrects movement patterns
  • Reduces fear of movement
  • Empowers self-management
  • Low risk of side effects

What PTs Do

Assessment

  • Evaluate movement and function
  • Identify impairments
  • Assess strength, flexibility, posture
  • Understand your goals

Treatment Approaches3

  • Therapeutic exercise
  • Manual therapy
  • Education about pain
  • Modalities (heat, ice, TENS)
  • Movement retraining
  • Home exercise programs

Types of Physical Therapy

  • Orthopedic (musculoskeletal)
  • Neurological
  • Pelvic floor
  • Aquatic therapy
  • Vestibular
  • Sports rehabilitation

What to Expect

  • Initial evaluation (45-60 minutes)
  • Follow-up sessions (30-60 minutes)
  • Frequency varies (1-3x/week common)
  • Duration depends on condition
  • Home exercises assigned

Finding a Good PT

  • Look for experience with your condition
  • Ask about treatment approach
  • Certified specialists available (OCS, etc.)
  • Should focus on active treatments
  • Good communication important

Maximizing Benefits

  • Do your home exercises
  • Communicate about pain levels
  • Ask questions
  • Be patientâ€”progress takes time
  • Provide feedback on what's working

When PT May Not Help

  • If only using passive treatments
  • If not doing home exercises
  • If PT isn't addressing your specific issues
  • May need different PT or approach
