Physical Therapy

Movement-based treatment for pain

What Is Physical Therapy?

Physical therapy (PT) uses movement, exercise, manual therapy, and education to treat pain and improve function. It's a cornerstone of chronic pain management and is recommended as first-line treatment for many musculoskeletal conditions.1

What PTs Do2

  • Assess movement and function
  • Identify contributing factors
  • Develop individualized treatment plans
  • Teach exercises and self-management
  • Use manual techniques
  • Provide education about pain

Techniques Used

Exercise Therapy

  • Stretching and flexibility
  • Strengthening
  • Aerobic conditioning
  • Balance training
  • Functional training

Manual Therapy

  • Joint mobilization
  • Soft tissue massage
  • Trigger point release

Modalities

  • Heat and cold
  • Electrical stimulation
  • Ultrasound
  • Dry needling

Conditions Treated3

  • Back and neck pain
  • Arthritis
  • Post-surgical rehabilitation
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Headaches
  • Sports injuries
  • Most musculoskeletal pain

What to Expect

  • Initial evaluation (45-60 minutes)
  • Treatment sessions (30-60 minutes)
  • Home exercise program
  • Progress reassessment
  • Typically 6-12 sessions

Keys to Success

  • Do your home exercises
  • Communicate openly with your PT
  • Be patient with progress
  • Active participation matters most
