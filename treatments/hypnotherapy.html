Hypnotherapy

Using focused attention to manage pain

What Is Hypnotherapy?

Clinical hypnotherapy uses guided relaxation, focused attention, and suggestions to achieve a state of heightened awareness. In this state, people may be more open to suggestions that can help reduce pain perception and change unhelpful thought patterns.1

How It Works2

  • Creates focused state of attention
  • Reduces awareness of distractions
  • Increases suggestibility
  • May alter pain processing in brain
  • Not sleep or loss of control

What Happens in a Session

  • Discussion of goals
  • Induction (relaxation techniques)
  • Deepening of relaxed state
  • Therapeutic suggestions
  • Return to normal awareness
  • Often learn self-hypnosis

Evidence for Pain3

  • Research supports use for chronic pain
  • May reduce pain intensity
  • Can improve coping
  • Effects may be long-lasting
  • Works better for some than others

Conditions Studied

  • Chronic pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Irritable bowel syndrome
  • Headaches
  • Cancer pain
  • Procedural pain

Hypnotizability

  • People vary in responsiveness
  • About 10-15% highly hypnotizable
  • Most can benefit to some degree
  • Can improve with practice

Finding a Provider

  • Look for licensed health professionals
  • Training in clinical hypnosis
  • Experience with chronic pain
