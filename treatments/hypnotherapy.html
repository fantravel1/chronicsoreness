Hypnotherapy for Pain

Using hypnosis to manage chronic pain

What Is Clinical Hypnotherapy?

Clinical hypnotherapy uses guided relaxation and focused attention to achieve a heightened state of awareness, sometimes called a trance. In this state, people may be more open to suggestions that can help change pain perception, reduce anxiety, and improve coping. It's an evidence-based treatment for chronic pain.1

How It Works for Pain2

  • Changes how the brain processes pain signals
  • Reduces the emotional component of pain
  • Decreases anxiety and muscle tension
  • May alter pain perception directly
  • Brain imaging shows real changes during hypnosis

What Happens in a Session

  • Induction: Guided relaxation techniques
  • Deepening: Increasing focus and relaxation
  • Therapeutic suggestions: Pain-specific imagery and suggestions
  • Emergence: Returning to normal awareness
  • Sessions typically last 30-60 minutes

Common Techniques

  • Glove anesthesia: Imagining numbness spreading
  • Pain displacement: Moving pain to different location
  • Dissociation: Separating from the painful body part
  • Reframing: Changing interpretation of sensations
  • Time distortion: Shortening perceived duration

Evidence for Pain3

  • Research supports use for chronic pain
  • Effective for fibromyalgia, IBS, headaches
  • May reduce pain intensity and unpleasantness
  • Can decrease medication use
  • Benefits may last after treatment ends

Who Can Be Hypnotized?

  • Most people can achieve some level of hypnosis
  • Hypnotizability varies among individuals
  • Motivation and expectations matter
  • Not about being "weak-willed"
  • You remain in control throughout

Self-Hypnosis

  • Can learn self-hypnosis for ongoing use
  • Practice reinforces benefits
  • Audio recordings can guide practice
  • Useful for daily pain management

Finding a Practitioner

  • Look for licensed healthcare providers with hypnosis training
  • Psychologists, physicians, or social workers
  • Ask about specific pain experience
  • Check credentials and training
