TENS Therapy

Electrical stimulation for pain relief

What Is TENS?

TENS (Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation) is a method of pain relief that uses a small battery-operated device to deliver mild electrical impulses through electrodes placed on the skin. It's a non-invasive, drug-free option that many people use for various types of chronic pain.1

How It Works2

Gate Control Theory

  • Electrical signals may "close the gate" to pain
  • Non-painful input blocks pain signals
  • Stimulates large nerve fibers

Endorphin Release

  • May stimulate natural painkillers
  • Low-frequency TENS especially

Types of TENS Settings

  • High frequency (80-120 Hz): tingling, quick relief
  • Low frequency (1-10 Hz): muscle twitching, longer-lasting effect
  • Burst mode: alternating patterns
  • Modulated: varies to prevent habituation

Conditions It May Help3

  • Chronic low back pain
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Neck pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Neuropathic pain
  • Labor pain
  • Post-surgical pain

Using a TENS Unit

  • Place electrodes on or near painful area
  • Start at lowest intensity
  • Increase until you feel tingling (not painful)
  • Sessions typically 20-60 minutes
  • Can use multiple times daily

Electrode Placement Tips

  • Place around (not directly on) painful area
  • Clean, dry skin
  • Follow device instructions
  • Experiment with placement
  • Replace electrodes when less sticky

Effectiveness

  • Research results are mixed
  • Many people find it helpful
  • Safe with minimal side effects
  • Works better for some than others
  • Low cost to try

Safety and Precautions

Don't Use If

  • You have a pacemaker or defibrillator
  • You have epilepsy (without medical guidance)
  • Over cancerous tissue
  • During pregnancy (over abdomen)

Avoid Placing Electrodes

  • On front of neck
  • Over eyes
  • On broken skin or wounds
  • Over numb areas

Getting a TENS Unit

  • Available over-the-counter
  • Range from $20-$100+
  • May be covered by insurance with prescription
  • Physical therapists can teach proper use
