TENS Therapy

Electrical stimulation for pain

What Is TENS?

TENS (transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation) uses a battery-powered device to deliver low-voltage electrical impulses through electrodes placed on the skin. It's a non-invasive, drug-free option for managing various types of pain.1

How It Works

  • Electrodes placed on or near painful area
  • Electrical impulses travel through skin
  • May block pain signals to brain (gate theory)
  • May stimulate endorphin release
  • Creates tingling or buzzing sensation

Types of TENS2

Conventional (High-frequency)

  • 80-100 Hz frequency
  • Low intensity, comfortable sensation
  • Works during use
  • Most common setting

Acupuncture-like (Low-frequency)

  • 2-4 Hz frequency
  • Higher intensity, muscle twitching
  • Effects may last longer

Conditions Used For3

  • Back and neck pain
  • Arthritis
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Neuropathic pain
  • Post-surgical pain
  • Menstrual pain
  • Labor pain
  • Sports injuries

How to Use

  • Place electrodes on or around painful area
  • Start at low intensity
  • Increase until tingling felt (not painful)
  • Use for 15-30 minutes at a time
  • Can use multiple times daily
  • Follow manufacturer instructions

Electrode Placement Tips

  • Place on or around painful area
  • Clean, dry skin
  • Don't place over wounds or rashes
  • Experiment with placement
  • Replace electrodes when they lose stickiness

Precautions

  • Don't use with pacemaker without doctor approval
  • Avoid placing over heart
  • Don't use on front of neck
  • Avoid during pregnancy without medical advice
  • Don't use while driving or in water
  • Not for unexplained pain

Getting a TENS Unit

  • Available OTC at pharmacies
  • May be prescribed by doctor
  • Insurance may cover with prescription
  • Prices range from $20-$100+
