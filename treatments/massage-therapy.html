Massage Therapy

Hands-on healing for pain relief

How Massage Helps Pain

Massage therapy involves manual manipulation of soft tissues to promote healing and relaxation. It can reduce muscle tension, improve circulation, and decrease pain through multiple mechanisms.1

Benefits for Chronic Pain

  • Reduces muscle tension and spasm
  • Improves circulation
  • Decreases stress hormones
  • Releases endorphins
  • Improves sleep
  • Reduces anxiety
  • Increases range of motion

Types of Massage2

Swedish Massage

  • Long, flowing strokes
  • Gentle to moderate pressure
  • Good for relaxation and general pain

Deep Tissue Massage

  • Targets deeper muscle layers
  • Firm pressure
  • For chronic muscle tension

Myofascial Release

  • Focuses on fascia (connective tissue)
  • Sustained pressure
  • For myofascial pain

Trigger Point Therapy

  • Targets specific tender points
  • Direct pressure on knots
  • For referred pain patterns

Conditions Helped3

  • Low back pain
  • Neck and shoulder pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Tension headaches
  • Myofascial pain
  • Osteoarthritis

What to Expect

  • Sessions typically 30-90 minutes
  • Communicate pain levels and preferences
  • Some soreness possible after
  • Regular sessions often more effective
