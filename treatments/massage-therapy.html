Massage Therapy

Hands-on treatment for pain relief

What Is Massage Therapy?

Massage therapy involves manipulating soft tissuesâ€”muscles, tendons, ligaments, and fasciaâ€”to reduce pain, relax muscles, improve circulation, and promote well-being. It's one of the oldest healing practices and is commonly used for chronic pain management.1

Types of Massage2

Swedish Massage

  • Most common type
  • Long flowing strokes
  • Gentle to moderate pressure
  • Relaxation focused

Deep Tissue Massage

  • Targets deeper muscle layers
  • More intense pressure
  • For chronic muscle tension
  • May cause temporary soreness

Myofascial Release

  • Targets fascia (connective tissue)
  • Sustained pressure
  • Addresses restrictions

Trigger Point Therapy

  • Focuses on "knots" in muscles
  • Direct pressure on trigger points
  • Can refer pain to other areas

Benefits for Pain3

  • Reduces muscle tension
  • Improves blood flow
  • Decreases stress hormones
  • Increases endorphins
  • Improves sleep
  • Reduces anxiety
  • Increases range of motion

Conditions That May Benefit

  • Back and neck pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Tension headaches
  • Myofascial pain syndrome
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Sports injuries
  • General muscle soreness

What to Expect

  • Sessions typically 30-90 minutes
  • Discuss pain areas and pressure preferences
  • Draping for comfort and modesty
  • May feel sore for 1-2 days after deep work
  • Drink water after session

Finding a Therapist

  • Look for licensed massage therapists (LMT)
  • Ask about experience with your condition
  • Check reviews
  • May be covered by insurance

Precautions

  • Avoid with blood clots
  • Caution over fractures or wounds
  • Inform therapist of all health conditions
  • Communicate if pressure is too much
