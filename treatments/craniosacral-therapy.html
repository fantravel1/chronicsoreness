Craniosacral Therapy

Gentle hands-on bodywork

What Is Craniosacral Therapy?

Craniosacral therapy (CST) is a gentle, hands-on approach that uses light touch to examine and potentially release restrictions in the soft tissues surrounding the central nervous system. Practitioners believe this can improve body function and relieve pain. It's considered a complementary therapy.1

The Theory2

  • Based on concept of "craniosacral rhythm"
  • Cerebrospinal fluid has rhythmic movement
  • Restrictions can affect this rhythm
  • Gentle touch releases restrictions
  • Developed from osteopathy

What to Expect

During a Session

  • Remain fully clothed
  • Lie on treatment table
  • Very light touch (5 grams of pressure)
  • Practitioner touches head, spine, sacrum
  • Sessions typically 45-60 minutes
  • Deeply relaxing for many

After Treatment

  • May feel relaxed or energized
  • Some feel temporary symptoms
  • Drink water
  • Multiple sessions often recommended

Conditions It May Help3

  • Headaches and migraines
  • Chronic neck/back pain
  • TMJ dysfunction
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Stress-related conditions
  • Post-concussion symptoms

Evidence

  • Limited high-quality research
  • Some studies show benefits for headache, neck pain
  • Mechanism not scientifically validated
  • Relaxation response may contribute
  • Generally considered safe

Finding a Practitioner

  • Massage therapists
  • Osteopaths
  • Physical therapists
  • Chiropractors
  • Look for specific CST training

Precautions

  • Avoid with recent head injury
  • Caution with bleeding disorders
  • Not for acute stroke
  • Inform practitioner of conditions

Considerations

  • Generally not covered by insurance
  • Scientific basis debated
  • Many find it helpful
  • Low risk if done by trained provider
  • Part of broader treatment plan
