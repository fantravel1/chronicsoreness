Hyaluronic Acid Injections

Viscosupplementation for joint pain

What Is Viscosupplementation?

Hyaluronic acid (HA) injections, also called viscosupplementation, involve injecting a gel-like substance into joints (most commonly the knee). HA is naturally found in joint fluid and helps with lubrication and shock absorption.1

How It Works

  • Supplements depleted HA in arthritic joints
  • May improve joint lubrication
  • May have anti-inflammatory effects
  • May stimulate natural HA production

Products Available

  • Synvisc-One (single injection)
  • Supartz (5 injections)
  • Hyalgan (3-5 injections)
  • Euflexxa (3 injections)
  • Gel-One (single injection)

Evidence2

  • Results vary in studies
  • Some show modest benefit over placebo
  • May work better in mild-moderate OA
  • Effect may last 6 months
  • Guidelines vary on recommendations

The Procedure

  • Office procedure
  • Knee cleaned and numbed
  • Injection into joint space
  • Takes only minutes
  • May be single or series of injections

Who May Benefit3

  • Knee osteoarthritis (most studied)
  • Inadequate response to other treatments
  • Can't take or don't want oral medications
  • Not ready for/can't have surgery

Side Effects

  • Temporary injection site pain
  • Swelling
  • Rarely: pseudo-septic reaction (severe but temporary)
  • Allergic reaction (rare)

After the Injection

  • Avoid strenuous activity for 48 hours
  • Ice if swollen
  • Effects may take weeks to notice
  • Can be repeated if beneficial
