Dry Needling

Treating trigger points with needles

What Is Dry Needling?

Dry needling is a technique where thin filiform needles are inserted into trigger pointsâ€”tight, painful knots in musclesâ€”to release them and reduce pain. Called "dry" because no medication is injected. It's used primarily for myofascial pain and is typically performed by physical therapists or other trained practitioners.1

How It Works2

  • Needle inserted into trigger point
  • May cause local twitch response
  • Releases tight muscle band
  • Interrupts pain cycle
  • Improves blood flow to area
  • May reset nerve signals

Dry Needling vs. Acupuncture

  • Different theoretical basis
  • Acupuncture: Traditional Chinese Medicine, meridians
  • Dry needling: Western anatomical approach
  • Similar needles used
  • Different training and philosophy
  • Dry needling targets specific muscles

What Trigger Points Are

  • Hyperirritable spots in muscle
  • Taut bands of muscle fiber
  • Can refer pain to other areas
  • Common in myofascial pain
  • Feel like knots or nodules

Conditions Treated3

  • Myofascial pain syndrome
  • Neck pain
  • Low back pain
  • Tension headaches
  • Shoulder pain
  • Hip pain
  • Sports injuries

What to Expect

  • Brief needle insertion
  • May feel muscle twitch
  • Some discomfort during
  • Treatment quick (minutes)
  • Soreness after is common
  • Effects may be immediate or gradual

Research Evidence

  • Growing body of research
  • Some positive findings for myofascial pain
  • Evidence quality varies
  • More research needed
  • Often used with other treatments

Side Effects

  • Soreness (most common)
  • Bruising
  • Bleeding
  • Temporary symptom increase
  • Fatigue
  • Serious complications rare

Who Performs It

  • Physical therapists (in many states)
  • Chiropractors
  • Some physicians
  • Varies by state/country
  • Ensure proper training

Contraindications

  • Bleeding disorders
  • On blood thinners (caution)
  • Immune compromised
  • Local infection
  • Needle phobia
  • Pregnancy (certain areas)
