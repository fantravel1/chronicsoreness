Dry Needling

Releasing trigger points with thin needles

What Is Dry Needling?

Dry needling is a treatment where thin, solid (filiform) needles are inserted into muscle trigger points to release tension and reduce pain. It's called "dry" because no medication is injected. It's often performed by physical therapists and is different from acupuncture.1

How It Works2

  • Needle inserted into trigger point
  • May cause local twitch response
  • Releases muscle tension
  • Increases blood flow to area
  • May reduce chemical irritants
  • Stimulates healing response

Conditions Treated

  • Myofascial pain syndrome
  • Chronic muscle pain
  • Tension headaches
  • Neck and back pain
  • Hip and gluteal pain
  • Shoulder pain
  • Sports injuries

Dry Needling vs. Acupuncture

  • Dry needling: Based on Western medicine, targets anatomical trigger points
  • Acupuncture: Based on traditional Chinese medicine, targets meridians
  • Same type of needle, different philosophy

What to Expect3

  • Brief pinch when needle inserted
  • May feel aching or twitch in muscle
  • Treatment lasts 10-30 minutes
  • Soreness for 24-48 hours after is common
  • Usually combined with other PT treatments

Benefits

  • Quick procedure
  • Can provide rapid relief
  • Minimal side effects
  • Can reach deep muscles
  • Often combined with exercise therapy

Side Effects

  • Temporary soreness
  • Bruising
  • Bleeding
  • Fatigue
  • Rare: infection, nerve injury

Finding a Practitioner

  • Physical therapists with dry needling training
  • Chiropractors (in some states)
  • Check credentials and experience
  • Regulations vary by state/country
