Dry Needling

Understanding this treatment for muscle pain and trigger points

What Is Dry Needling?

Dry needling is a technique where thin needles are inserted into trigger points, muscles, ligaments, or near nerves to treat pain and movement problems. It's called "dry" because no medication is injectedâ€”the needle itself provides the therapeutic effect.1

How It Works

  • Causes local twitch response in muscle
  • Releases muscle tension and trigger points
  • Increases blood flow to area
  • Stimulates healing response
  • May affect pain signaling

Conditions Treated

  • Myofascial pain syndrome
  • Muscle tension and spasm
  • Neck and back pain
  • Headaches (tension-type, cervicogenic)
  • Shoulder pain
  • Hip and leg pain
  • Plantar fasciitis
  • Tennis/golfer's elbow

What to Expect

  • Brief discomfort as needle enters trigger point
  • Local twitch response (muscle jump)
  • Achiness for 24-48 hours after treatment
  • Usually multiple sessions needed
  • Often combined with exercise and stretching

Dry Needling vs. Acupuncture2

  • Same tools - Both use thin, solid needles
  • Different theory - Dry needling based on Western anatomy; acupuncture based on traditional Chinese medicine
  • Different targets - Dry needling targets trigger points; acupuncture uses meridian points
  • Different practitioners - Dry needling often by PTs; acupuncture by licensed acupuncturists

Research Evidence3

  • Moderate evidence for myofascial pain
  • May be effective for trigger points
  • Evidence still emerging for many conditions
  • More research needed

Safety

  • Generally safe when performed by trained practitioners
  • Minor bleeding or bruising possible
  • Post-treatment soreness common
  • Serious complications rare
