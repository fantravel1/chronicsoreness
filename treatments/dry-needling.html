Dry Needling

Targeting trigger points with thin needles

What Is Dry Needling?

Dry needling is a technique where thin filiform needles are inserted into myofascial trigger points, muscles, or connective tissue to relieve pain and improve function. "Dry" refers to no medication being injected.1

How It Works

  • Releases muscle tension and knots
  • Improves blood flow to area
  • Stimulates local twitch response
  • May reset muscle to normal state
  • Can reduce referred pain

Dry Needling vs. Acupuncture2

  • Different theoretical frameworks
  • Similar needles used
  • Dry needling based on Western anatomy
  • Targets trigger points specifically
  • Often performed by physical therapists

Conditions Treated

  • Myofascial pain syndrome
  • Tension headaches
  • Low back pain
  • Neck pain
  • Shoulder pain
  • Tennis elbow
  • Plantar fasciitis

What to Expect3

  • Brief discomfort or muscle twitch
  • Session lasts 15-30 minutes
  • Soreness for 24-48 hours after
  • Often combined with stretching/exercise
  • Multiple sessions typically needed

Potential Side Effects

  • Temporary soreness
  • Bruising
  • Fatigue
  • Rare: pneumothorax (if near chest)
