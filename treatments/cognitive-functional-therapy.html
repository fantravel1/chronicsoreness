Cognitive Functional Therapy

Addressing the whole person with chronic pain

What Is CFT?

Cognitive Functional Therapy (CFT) is an evidence-based approach for chronic musculoskeletal pain that integrates psychological and physical elements. It addresses beliefs, emotions, and behaviors alongside movement retraining.1

Key Components2

Making Sense of Pain

  • Understanding pain neuroscience
  • Addressing unhelpful beliefs
  • Reducing fear of movement
  • Personalized explanation

Functional Integration

  • Identifying provocative postures/movements
  • Graded exposure to feared activities
  • Relaxation of protective muscle guarding
  • Building confidence in body

Lifestyle Factors

  • Sleep
  • Stress management
  • Physical activity
  • Social connection

What Makes CFT Different

  • Highly individualized
  • Addresses multiple factors at once
  • Patient is active participant
  • Not passive treatment
  • Builds self-efficacy

Evidence3

  • Shown effective for chronic low back pain
  • Better outcomes than usual care
  • Improvements in pain and function
  • Cost-effective

Who May Benefit

  • Chronic back or neck pain
  • Pain without structural cause
  • Fear of movement
  • High stress or anxiety about pain
  • Failed other treatments

Finding a Provider

  • Physical therapists trained in CFT
  • Growing availability worldwide
  • Often multiple sessions needed
