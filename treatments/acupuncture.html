Acupuncture

An ancient practice with modern applications for pain

What Is Acupuncture?

Acupuncture involves inserting thin needles into specific points on the body. Traditionally part of Chinese medicine, it's now used worldwide for pain management. Research suggests it may work through neurological and biochemical mechanisms.1

Proposed Mechanisms

  • Stimulates release of endorphins
  • Activates descending pain inhibition
  • Modulates neurotransmitters
  • Local tissue effects
  • Placebo/contextual effects (debated)

Evidence for Pain Conditions2

Good Evidence

  • Chronic low back pain
  • Neck pain
  • Osteoarthritis (knee)
  • Chronic headache/migraine prevention

Moderate Evidence

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Shoulder pain
  • Post-operative pain

What to Expect

  • Initial assessment and history
  • Insertion of thin, sterile needles
  • Needles left in place 15-30 minutes
  • Minimal to mild discomfort
  • May feel relaxed or energized after
  • Usually 6-12 sessions for chronic conditions

Safety3

  • Very safe when performed by trained practitioner
  • Common: minor bruising, temporary soreness
  • Rare: infection (with proper technique), pneumothorax (extremely rare)
  • Use sterile, single-use needles

Types of Acupuncture

  • Traditional Chinese - Based on meridian theory
  • Medical/Western - Anatomically based
  • Electroacupuncture - Electrical stimulation added
  • Auricular - Ear acupuncture
