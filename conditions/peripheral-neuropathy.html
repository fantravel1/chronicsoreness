Peripheral Neuropathy

Nerve damage affecting limbs

What Is Peripheral Neuropathy?

Peripheral neuropathy is damage to the peripheral nervesâ€”the nerves outside the brain and spinal cord. It most commonly affects the feet and hands in a "stocking-glove" pattern. There are over 100 types of peripheral neuropathy.1

Types2

  • Sensory neuropathy - Affects sensation
  • Motor neuropathy - Affects muscle movement
  • Autonomic neuropathy - Affects involuntary functions
  • Combined - Multiple nerve types affected

Symptoms

Sensory

  • Numbness or reduced sensation
  • Tingling or "pins and needles"
  • Burning or shooting pain
  • Extreme sensitivity to touch

Motor

  • Muscle weakness
  • Muscle cramps
  • Difficulty with coordination
  • Foot drop

Autonomic

  • Blood pressure changes
  • Digestive problems
  • Sweating abnormalities
  • Bladder dysfunction

Causes

  • Diabetes (most common)
  • Alcohol abuse
  • Vitamin deficiencies (B12, B1, E)
  • Autoimmune diseases
  • Infections (HIV, shingles)
  • Medications (chemotherapy)
  • Kidney or liver disease
  • Inherited conditions
  • Idiopathic (unknown cause)

Treatment3

Treat the Cause

  • Blood sugar control for diabetes
  • Stop alcohol if applicable
  • Replace vitamin deficiencies
  • Treat underlying conditions

Pain Management

  • Anticonvulsants (gabapentin, pregabalin)
  • Antidepressants (duloxetine, amitriptyline)
  • Topical treatments (lidocaine, capsaicin)
  • Pain medications

Other Treatments

  • Physical therapy
  • TENS
  • Foot care

Self-Care

  • Check feet daily for injuries
  • Wear proper footwear
  • Avoid temperature extremes
  • Exercise as tolerated
  • Manage blood sugar if diabetic
