Peripheral Neuropathy

Damage to peripheral nerves causing pain and numbness

What Is Peripheral Neuropathy?

Peripheral neuropathy refers to damage to the peripheral nerves - the nerves outside the brain and spinal cord. It often causes weakness, numbness, and pain, usually in the hands and feet, but can affect other areas. Over 20 million Americans have some form.1

Causes2

  • Diabetes - Most common cause
  • Chemotherapy
  • Alcohol abuse
  • Vitamin deficiencies (B12, B1)
  • Autoimmune diseases
  • Infections (HIV, Lyme, shingles)
  • Kidney or liver disease
  • Hereditary conditions
  • Idiopathic (unknown - 30%)

Types

  • Sensory neuropathy - Affects sensation
  • Motor neuropathy - Affects muscle control
  • Autonomic neuropathy - Affects automatic functions
  • Mixed - Combination

Symptoms

Sensory

  • Numbness, tingling ("pins and needles")
  • Burning or shooting pain
  • Sensitivity to touch
  • Loss of temperature/pain sensation

Motor

  • Muscle weakness
  • Cramps
  • Loss of coordination
  • Foot drop

Diagnosis

  • Neurological examination
  • Nerve conduction studies/EMG
  • Blood tests (glucose, B12, etc.)
  • Skin biopsy (small fiber)

Treatment3

Treat Underlying Cause

  • Blood sugar control for diabetes
  • Vitamin supplementation
  • Stop offending medications/toxins

Pain Management

  • Anticonvulsants (gabapentin, pregabalin)
  • Antidepressants (duloxetine, TCAs)
  • Topical treatments (lidocaine, capsaicin)
  • TENS
