Chronic Migraine

When migraines become frequent

What Is Chronic Migraine?

Chronic migraine is defined as headaches occurring 15 or more days per month for more than 3 months, with at least 8 days having migraine features. It affects about 1-2% of the population and is highly disabling.1

Symptoms2

Migraine Features

  • Moderate to severe pain
  • Throbbing or pulsating quality
  • Usually one-sided
  • Worse with physical activity
  • Nausea and/or vomiting
  • Sensitivity to light and sound
  • Some attacks may have aura

Risk Factors for Chronic Migraine

  • Medication overuse
  • Obesity
  • Depression and anxiety
  • Stressful life events
  • Sleep disorders
  • Caffeine overuse
  • Frequent episodic migraines

Treatment3

Preventive Medications

  • Beta-blockers (propranolol, metoprolol)
  • Antidepressants (amitriptyline, venlafaxine)
  • Anticonvulsants (topiramate, valproate)
  • CGRP monoclonal antibodies (erenumab, etc.)
  • Botox (OnabotulinumtoxinA)

Acute Treatment

  • Triptans
  • NSAIDs
  • Gepants (ubrogepant, rimegepant)
  • Limit to avoid medication overuse

Lifestyle Modifications

  • Regular sleep schedule
  • Regular meals
  • Exercise
  • Stress management
  • Trigger avoidance

Medication Overuse Headache

  • Using acute medications too frequently can worsen headaches
  • Limit triptans/ergots to 10 days/month
  • Limit simple analgesics to 15 days/month
  • May need supervised withdrawal

When to Seek Specialist Care

  • Headaches not responding to treatment
  • Needing acute medications frequently
  • Significant impact on quality of life
  • Consider headache specialist or neurologist
