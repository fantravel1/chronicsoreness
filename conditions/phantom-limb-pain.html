Phantom Limb Pain

Pain felt in a missing or amputated limb

What Is Phantom Limb Pain?

Phantom limb pain (PLP) is the sensation of pain in a limb that has been amputated or is missing from birth. It affects 60-80% of amputees and is distinct from stump (residual limb) pain.1

Related Phenomena

  • Phantom sensation - Non-painful awareness of missing limb
  • Stump pain - Pain at amputation site
  • Phantom limb pain - Pain felt in missing limb

Characteristics2

  • Burning, stabbing, shooting, cramping
  • Often intermittent
  • May feel limb in abnormal position
  • Can be triggered by weather, stress, touch to stump
  • Most common in distal parts (fingers, toes)

Why It Happens

  • Brain's body map still includes missing limb
  • Nervous system reorganization
  • Peripheral nerve damage
  • Spinal cord changes
  • Brain plasticity (cortical reorganization)

Treatment3

Medications

  • Gabapentin, pregabalin
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • NMDA receptor antagonists
  • Opioids (limited role)

Mirror Therapy

  • Uses mirror to create visual illusion
  • Patient moves intact limb, sees "phantom" moving
  • Can reduce pain and restore sense of control
  • Evidence-based approach

Other Approaches

  • TENS (transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation)
  • Virtual reality therapy
  • Spinal cord stimulation
  • Graded motor imagery
  • Acupuncture
