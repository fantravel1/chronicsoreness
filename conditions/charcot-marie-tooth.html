Charcot-Marie-Tooth Disease

The most common inherited nerve disorder

What Is CMT?

Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease (CMT) is a group of inherited disorders that damage peripheral nerves. It's the most common inherited neurological disorder, affecting about 1 in 2,500 people.1

Types

  • CMT1 - Demyelinating (affects myelin sheath)
  • CMT2 - Axonal (affects nerve axon)
  • CMT3 - Severe early onset (Dejerine-Sottas)
  • CMT4 - Autosomal recessive
  • CMTX - X-linked

Symptoms2

  • Muscle weakness (feet, ankles first)
  • Foot deformities (high arches, hammertoes)
  • Difficulty walking, tripping
  • Numbness in feet and hands
  • Muscle atrophy (lower legs)
  • Hand weakness (later)
  • Pain (neuropathic or musculoskeletal)

Progression

  • Usually begins in childhood/adolescence
  • Slowly progressive
  • Severity varies widely
  • Most maintain ability to walk
  • Life expectancy usually normal

Pain in CMT

  • Neuropathic pain common (30-50%)
  • Musculoskeletal pain from gait changes
  • Cramping
  • Often undertreated

Management3

  • Physical therapy
  • Occupational therapy
  • Orthotics (AFOs)
  • Pain management
  • Surgical correction of deformities
  • Genetic counseling
