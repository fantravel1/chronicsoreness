Cluster Headaches

One of the most painful headache types

What Are Cluster Headaches?

Cluster headaches are one of the most severe types of pain a person can experience. They occur in cyclical patterns or "clusters" of attacks, followed by remission periods. The pain is typically one-sided, often described as a burning or piercing sensation around the eye. Though not life-threatening, they significantly impact quality of life.1

Symptoms2

Pain Characteristics

  • Extremely severe, one-sided pain
  • Centered around or behind one eye
  • Burning, piercing, or sharp quality
  • Rapid onset (peaks in 5-10 minutes)
  • Lasts 15 minutes to 3 hours
  • Often at same time daily

Associated Symptoms (same side as pain)

  • Tearing of eye
  • Eye redness
  • Drooping eyelid
  • Nasal congestion or runny nose
  • Facial sweating
  • Restlessness and agitation

Patterns3

Episodic

  • Clusters last weeks to months
  • Remissions last months to years
  • More common type

Chronic

  • Attacks occur for over a year
  • No remission or less than 3 months
  • About 10-15% of cases

Triggers

  • Alcohol (during cluster period)
  • Strong smells
  • Heat
  • Bright light
  • High altitude

Treatment

Acute (Abort Attack)

  • High-flow oxygen (most effective)
  • Sumatriptan injection
  • Sumatriptan or zolmitriptan nasal spray
  • OTC painkillers don't work (too slow)

Preventive

  • Verapamil (first-line)
  • Lithium
  • Corticosteroids (short-term bridge)
  • Topiramate
  • Greater occipital nerve block

Living with Cluster Headaches

  • Keep oxygen and medication readily available
  • Avoid triggers during clusters
  • Maintain regular sleep schedule
  • Connect with support groups
  • See a headache specialist

Diagnosis

  • Based on symptom description
  • Physical and neurological exam
  • MRI to rule out other causes
  • Often misdiagnosed initially
