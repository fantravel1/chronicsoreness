Cluster Headaches

Severe headaches in cyclical patterns

What Are Cluster Headaches?

Cluster headaches are one of the most painful conditions known, sometimes called "suicide headaches" due to their severity. They occur in cyclical patterns or clusters, often at the same time each day, and affect about 0.1% of the population.1

Symptoms2

  • Excruciating pain around or behind one eye
  • Pain on one side of head
  • Attacks last 15 minutes to 3 hours
  • Restlessness and agitation during attack
  • Red, watering eye on affected side
  • Stuffy or runny nostril
  • Droopy eyelid
  • Forehead and facial sweating

Patterns

Episodic Cluster Headaches

  • Occur for weeks to months (cluster period)
  • Followed by remission periods
  • Attacks may occur at same time daily
  • Often wake person from sleep

Chronic Cluster Headaches

  • No extended remission periods
  • Less common (10-15% of cases)

Treatment3

Acute Treatment

  • High-flow oxygen (most effective)
  • Triptans (sumatriptan injection or nasal spray)
  • Zolmitriptan nasal spray

Preventive Treatment

  • Verapamil (first-line)
  • Lithium
  • Topiramate
  • Corticosteroids (bridge therapy)
  • Greater occipital nerve block

Triggers to Avoid

  • Alcohol (during cluster period)
  • Nitrates
  • Strong odors
  • Altitude changes

Living with Clusters

  • Keep oxygen available at home
  • Carry acute treatment with you
  • Maintain regular sleep schedule
  • Seek specialized headache care
  • Connect with support groups
