Cluster Headaches

One of the most severe pain conditions known

What Are Cluster Headaches?

Cluster headaches are a type of trigeminal autonomic cephalgia characterized by severe, strictly unilateral pain with autonomic symptoms. They occur in "clusters" of frequent attacks followed by remission periods. They're often called "suicide headaches" due to their extreme intensity.1

Symptoms

  • Severe, excruciating unilateral pain
  • Pain around or behind eye
  • Duration: 15-180 minutes
  • Restlessness, agitation (unlike migraine)
  • 1-8 attacks per day during cluster period

Autonomic Features2

  • Tearing (lacrimation)
  • Eye redness (conjunctival injection)
  • Nasal congestion or runny nose
  • Eyelid swelling
  • Forehead/facial sweating
  • Pupil constriction (miosis)
  • Drooping eyelid (ptosis)

Pattern

  • Episodic - Cluster periods last weeks to months, then remission
  • Chronic - No remission or <3 months remission
  • Often occur at same time daily (circadian pattern)
  • More common in spring and fall

Treatment

Acute Treatment3

  • High-flow oxygen (100%, 12-15 L/min) - first line
  • Sumatriptan injection
  • Zolmitriptan nasal spray

Preventive Treatment

  • Verapamil (first line)
  • Short-term prednisone bridge
  • Lithium
  • Greater occipital nerve blocks
