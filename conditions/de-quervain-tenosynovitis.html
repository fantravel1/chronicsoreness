De Quervain's Tenosynovitis

Painful inflammation on the thumb side of the wrist

What Is De Quervain's?

De Quervain's tenosynovitis is a painful condition affecting the tendons on the thumb side of the wrist. It occurs when the tendons that move the thumb become irritated or constricted as they pass through a narrow tunnel.1

Symptoms

  • Pain at base of thumb/thumb-side wrist
  • Swelling near the thumb
  • Pain with gripping or pinching
  • Pain when turning the wrist
  • Difficulty moving thumb
  • "Sticking" or "catching" sensation

Causes and Risk Factors2

  • Repetitive hand or wrist motions
  • New parents (lifting baby)
  • Texting/smartphone use
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Pregnancy
  • Women more affected

Diagnosis

  • Finkelstein's test (positive)
  • Tenderness over radial styloid
  • Clinical diagnosis usually
  • Ultrasound or MRI if needed

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Rest from aggravating activities
  • Thumb spica splint
  • Ice
  • NSAIDs
  • Ergonomic modifications

Corticosteroid Injection

  • Often very effective
  • May cure condition

Surgery

  • Release of tendon sheath
  • For refractory cases

Prevention

  • Ergonomic device use
  • Take breaks from repetitive tasks
  • Proper lifting technique
