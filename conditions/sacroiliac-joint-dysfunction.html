Sacroiliac Joint Dysfunction

When the SI joint causes lower back and buttock pain

What Is SI Joint Dysfunction?

Sacroiliac (SI) joint dysfunction refers to pain arising from abnormal movement or inflammation of the SI joint, which connects the spine to the pelvis. It accounts for 15-30% of chronic low back pain cases.1

Symptoms

  • Lower back pain (one or both sides)
  • Buttock pain
  • Pain radiating to hip, groin, thigh
  • Worse with sitting, standing from sitting
  • Pain climbing stairs
  • Pain with weight shift to one leg
  • Morning stiffness

Causes2

  • Pregnancy and childbirth
  • Leg length discrepancy
  • Gait abnormalities
  • Previous lumbar fusion
  • Trauma
  • Inflammatory arthritis
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Infection (rare)

Diagnosis

  • Provocative physical tests (FABER, compression)
  • At least 3 positive tests
  • Diagnostic SI joint injection (gold standard)
  • X-ray, CT, MRI

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Physical therapy
  • SI joint belt
  • NSAIDs
  • Activity modification
  • Core strengthening

Interventional

  • SI joint injections
  • Radiofrequency ablation
  • Prolotherapy

Surgical

  • SI joint fusion
