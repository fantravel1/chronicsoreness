Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome

Genetic connective tissue disorders

What Is Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome?

Ehlers-Danlos syndromes (EDS) are a group of inherited connective tissue disorders affecting collagen. They cause joint hypermobility, skin hyperextensibility, and tissue fragility. There are 13 types, with hypermobile EDS (hEDS) being most common.1

Types2

  • Hypermobile EDS (hEDS) - Most common, joint hypermobility, chronic pain
  • Classical EDS - Very stretchy, fragile skin
  • Vascular EDS - Rare but serious, arterial fragility
  • Kyphoscoliotic EDS - Severe muscle weakness, scoliosis
  • Other rare types

Common Symptoms

  • Joint hypermobility
  • Frequent joint dislocations/subluxations
  • Chronic widespread pain
  • Easy bruising
  • Soft, stretchy skin
  • Poor wound healing
  • Fatigue

Associated Conditions

  • POTS (postural orthostatic tachycardia)
  • MCAS (mast cell activation syndrome)
  • Gastrointestinal issues
  • Chronic fatigue
  • Anxiety
  • TMJ dysfunction

Management3

  • Physical therapy (joint stabilization)
  • Low-impact exercise
  • Joint protection strategies
  • Pain management
  • Bracing when needed
  • Treatment of associated conditions
  • Genetic counseling

Exercise Considerations

  • Focus on strengthening, not stretching
  • Low-impact activities (swimming, cycling)
  • Avoid hyperextension
  • Proprioception training
