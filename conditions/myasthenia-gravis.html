Myasthenia Gravis

When muscles tire easily

What Is Myasthenia Gravis?

Myasthenia gravis (MG) is an autoimmune neuromuscular disease causing weakness in skeletal muscles. Antibodies block or destroy receptors at the neuromuscular junction, preventing muscle contraction. Weakness worsens with activity and improves with rest.1

Symptoms2

Ocular (Most Common Initially)

  • Drooping eyelid (ptosis)
  • Double vision

Bulbar

  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Slurred speech
  • Chewing difficulty

Generalized

  • Arm and leg weakness
  • Neck weakness
  • Breathing difficulty (severe)

Key Characteristic

  • Fluctuating weakness
  • Worse with activity
  • Better after rest
  • Worse later in day

Diagnosis

  • Antibody tests (AChR, MuSK)
  • Nerve conduction studies
  • Ice pack test
  • CT/MRI for thymus

Treatment3

  • Cholinesterase inhibitors (pyridostigmine)
  • Immunosuppressants
  • Thymectomy
  • Plasma exchange (severe)
  • IVIG
  • Newer biologics

Myasthenic Crisis

Respiratory failure is a medical emergency. Seek immediate care for breathing difficulty.
