Tennis Elbow

Lateral epicondylitis explained

What Is Tennis Elbow?

Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis) is a painful condition affecting the outer elbow where forearm extensor tendons attach. Despite its name, most cases aren't from tennis but from repetitive arm and wrist movements.1

Causes

  • Repetitive wrist extension
  • Computer mouse use
  • Gripping activities
  • Painting, plumbing, carpentry
  • Racquet sports (incorrect technique)

Symptoms2

  • Pain on outer elbow
  • Pain with gripping
  • Weak grip strength
  • Pain shaking hands
  • Pain lifting objects
  • Pain turning doorknobs

Treatment3

Conservative (Usually Effective)

  • Activity modification
  • Forearm brace (counterforce strap)
  • Eccentric exercises (key treatment)
  • Ice
  • NSAIDs (short-term)

Additional Options

  • Physical therapy
  • Corticosteroid injection (temporary relief)
  • PRP injection
  • Shockwave therapy

Surgery

  • Only 5-10% need surgery
  • After 6-12 months conservative care

Eccentric Exercise (Tyler Twist)

  • Use flexible bar (FlexBar)
  • Twist bar with affected wrist
  • Slowly release
  • 3 sets of 15, twice daily
  • Evidence-based treatment

Prognosis

80-90% improve with conservative treatment. Average recovery time is 6-12 months.
