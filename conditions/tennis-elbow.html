Tennis Elbow

Lateral epicondylitis - outer elbow pain

What Is Tennis Elbow?

Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis) is a painful condition affecting the outer part of the elbow where forearm extensor tendons attach. Despite its name, most cases aren't from tennis - it's caused by repetitive wrist and arm motions.1

Symptoms

  • Pain on outer elbow
  • Pain with gripping or lifting
  • Weak grip strength
  • Pain with wrist extension
  • Tenderness at lateral epicondyle
  • Pain may radiate into forearm

Causes/Risk Factors2

  • Repetitive wrist/forearm movements
  • Computer mouse use
  • Manual labor (plumbing, painting, carpentry)
  • Racquet sports (with poor technique)
  • Age 30-50 most common

Diagnosis

  • Clinical examination
  • Pain with resisted wrist extension
  • Tenderness at lateral epicondyle
  • Imaging usually not needed (MRI/US can confirm)

Treatment3

Conservative (First Line)

  • Activity modification - Avoid aggravating activities
  • Eccentric exercises - Key component of rehab
  • Counterforce brace - Strap below elbow
  • Wrist brace - Rest extensors
  • Ice
  • NSAIDs - Short-term

Other Options

  • Physical therapy
  • Corticosteroid injection (short-term relief only)
  • PRP injections
  • Shockwave therapy
  • Surgery (rarely needed)

Eccentric Exercise Example

  • Hold light weight, palm down
  • Use other hand to extend wrist up
  • Slowly lower weight (3-5 seconds)
  • 3 sets of 15, twice daily

Prognosis

Most cases resolve within 1-2 years. Eccentric exercises speed recovery. Corticosteroid injections provide short-term relief but may delay healing long-term.
