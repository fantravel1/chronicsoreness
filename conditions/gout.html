Gout

When uric acid crystals attack joints

What Is Gout?

Gout is a form of inflammatory arthritis caused by uric acid crystals depositing in joints. It causes sudden, severe attacks of pain, redness, and swelling, most commonly in the big toe. It affects about 4% of adults and is more common in men.1

Symptoms of a Gout Attack2

  • Sudden severe joint pain (often at night)
  • Intense swelling
  • Redness and warmth
  • Extreme sensitivity to touch
  • Limited range of motion
  • Attacks last days to weeks

Common Sites

  • Big toe (podagra) - most common
  • Ankle
  • Knee
  • Wrist
  • Fingers
  • Elbow

Risk Factors

  • High uric acid levels
  • Male sex
  • Obesity
  • Certain medications (diuretics)
  • Diet high in purines (red meat, shellfish)
  • Alcohol (especially beer)
  • Kidney disease
  • Family history

Treatment3

During an Attack

  • NSAIDs (indomethacin, naproxen)
  • Colchicine
  • Corticosteroids
  • Ice, rest, elevation

Preventing Attacks

  • Urate-lowering therapy (allopurinol, febuxostat)
  • Dietary modifications
  • Weight management
  • Limit alcohol
  • Stay hydrated

Dietary Considerations

  • Limit: Red meat, organ meats, shellfish, beer
  • Moderate: Fish, poultry
  • Encouraged: Low-fat dairy, vegetables, cherries
