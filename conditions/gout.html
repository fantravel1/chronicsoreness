Gout

Inflammatory arthritis from uric acid crystal deposits

What Is Gout?

Gout is a type of inflammatory arthritis caused by deposition of monosodium urate crystals in joints. It causes sudden, severe attacks of pain, swelling, and redness. The big toe is classically affected, but any joint can be involved.1

Symptoms

  • Sudden, intense joint pain (often night)
  • Peak pain within 12-24 hours
  • Severe swelling, redness, warmth
  • Extreme tenderness
  • Limited range of motion
  • Attacks last days to weeks

Risk Factors2

  • Elevated uric acid (hyperuricemia)
  • Male sex (more common)
  • Obesity
  • Diet high in purines (red meat, organ meats, shellfish)
  • Alcohol (especially beer)
  • Sugar-sweetened beverages
  • Certain medications (diuretics)
  • Kidney disease

Diagnosis

  • Clinical presentation
  • Joint fluid analysis (gold standard - crystals)
  • Serum uric acid (can be normal during attack)
  • Imaging (ultrasound, dual-energy CT)

Treatment3

Acute Attacks

  • NSAIDs (indomethacin, naproxen)
  • Colchicine (best if started early)
  • Corticosteroids (oral or injected)
  • Ice, rest, elevation

Prevention (Urate-Lowering)

  • Allopurinol - Most common
  • Febuxostat
  • Target uric acid <6 mg/dL
  • Start after acute attack resolves

Lifestyle Changes

  • Limit high-purine foods
  • Reduce alcohol (especially beer)
  • Stay hydrated
  • Achieve healthy weight
  • Limit fructose/sugary drinks
