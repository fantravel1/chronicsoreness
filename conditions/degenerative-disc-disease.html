Degenerative Disc Disease

Age-related changes in spinal discs

What Is Degenerative Disc Disease?

Degenerative disc disease (DDD) refers to age-related changes in spinal discs that can cause pain. Despite its name, it's not truly a disease but a natural part of aging. However, not everyone with disc degeneration has pain.1

Understanding Disc Changes

  • Discs lose water content with age
  • Disc height decreases
  • Annulus (outer ring) can develop tears
  • Nucleus (inner gel) may bulge or herniate
  • Disc becomes less flexible

Symptoms2

  • Low back or neck pain
  • Pain worse with sitting, bending, lifting
  • Pain better with movement, position changes
  • Pain that comes and goes
  • Radiating pain if nerve involvement
  • Muscle spasms

Diagnosis

  • Clinical history and exam
  • X-rays show disc space narrowing
  • MRI shows disc hydration, herniations
  • Note: Imaging findings don't always correlate with pain

Treatment3

Conservative Care

  • Physical therapy - core strengthening
  • NSAIDs, acetaminophen
  • Activity modification
  • Heat/ice
  • Weight management

Interventional Options

  • Epidural steroid injections
  • Facet injections (if facets involved)
  • Surgery (fusion, disc replacement) - last resort

Prognosis

Most people improve with conservative treatment. Disc degeneration is common and doesn't always cause symptoms. Staying active is generally beneficial.
