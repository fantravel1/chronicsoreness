Degenerative Disc Disease

Understanding spinal disc changes

What Is Degenerative Disc Disease?

Degenerative disc disease (DDD) isn't actually a diseaseâ€”it's the natural wear and tear on spinal discs that occurs with age. Almost everyone shows disc changes on imaging by age 50, but not everyone has pain. When disc changes do cause symptoms, they can be managed with various treatments.1

What Happens to Discs2

  • Discs lose water content (dehydration)
  • Become less flexible
  • Disc height decreases
  • May develop tears in outer layer
  • Normal aging process
  • Some people more affected than others

Risk Factors

  • Age
  • Genetics
  • Smoking
  • Obesity
  • Physically demanding work
  • Previous injury
  • Sedentary lifestyle

Symptoms3

  • Many people have no symptoms
  • Pain varies from mild to severe
  • Worse with sitting, bending, lifting
  • May improve with movement
  • Pain may come and go
  • Stiffness
  • Can radiate to buttocks/thighs

Diagnosis

  • History and physical exam
  • X-rays show disc space narrowing
  • MRI shows disc condition
  • Important: imaging findings don't always match symptoms
  • Clinical correlation needed

Important to Know

  • Disc changes on MRI are common and normal
  • Doesn't mean you'll have severe pain
  • Often improves over time
  • Surgery rarely needed
  • Name can sound scarier than it is

Treatment

Conservative (First Line)

  • Staying active (important!)
  • Physical therapy
  • NSAIDs
  • Heat/ice
  • Core strengthening
  • Weight management

Additional Options

  • Muscle relaxants
  • Epidural injections
  • Pain psychology
  • Acupuncture

Surgery (Rarely Needed)

  • Disc replacement
  • Spinal fusion
  • Only for severe, refractory cases

Lifestyle Management

  • Stay physically active
  • Good posture
  • Proper lifting techniques
  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Don't smoke
  • Core strengthening
