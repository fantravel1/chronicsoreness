Degenerative Disc Disease

Understanding age-related disc changes

What Is Degenerative Disc Disease?

Degenerative disc disease (DDD) describes changes to spinal discs that occur with aging. Despite its name, it's not truly a disease but a natural process. The discs lose hydration and become less flexible, potentially causing pain.1

What Happens to Discs

  • Loss of water content
  • Disc height decreases
  • Tears in outer layer (annulus)
  • Less shock absorption
  • Can lead to bulging or herniation

Symptoms2

  • Pain in back or neck
  • Pain worse with sitting
  • Pain with bending, twisting
  • Periods of severe pain (flares)
  • Relief with movement, position changes
  • Some have no symptoms despite changes

Risk Factors

  • Age (begins as early as 20s)
  • Genetics
  • Smoking
  • Obesity
  • Heavy physical work
  • Sedentary lifestyle

Diagnosis

  • MRI shows disc changes
  • X-ray shows disc space narrowing
  • Imaging findings don't always correlate with pain
  • Clinical evaluation essential

Treatment3

  • Physical therapy
  • Exercise and movement
  • Pain medications (as needed)
  • Heat/cold therapy
  • Epidural injections
  • Surgery (rare, for severe cases)
