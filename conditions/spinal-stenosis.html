Spinal Stenosis

When the spinal canal narrows

What Is Spinal Stenosis?

Spinal stenosis is narrowing of the spaces within the spine, which can put pressure on nerves. It most commonly occurs in the lower back (lumbar) and neck (cervical). It's usually caused by age-related changes and is most common after age 50.1

Types

  • Lumbar stenosis - Lower back, most common
  • Cervical stenosis - Neck, potentially more serious
  • Central stenosis - Main canal narrowed
  • Foraminal stenosis - Nerve exit points narrowed

Symptoms2

Lumbar Stenosis

  • Leg pain with walking (neurogenic claudication)
  • Relief with sitting or bending forward
  • Can walk farther with cart support
  • Back pain
  • Numbness/tingling in legs

Cervical Stenosis

  • Neck pain
  • Arm weakness or numbness
  • Balance problems
  • Difficulty with fine motor tasks

Causes

  • Degenerative changes (most common)
  • Bone spurs
  • Thickened ligaments
  • Disc bulging
  • Congenital narrow canal

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Physical therapy (flexion exercises)
  • Activity modification
  • Pain medications
  • Epidural steroid injections

Surgery

  • Decompression (laminectomy)
  • Sometimes with fusion
  • For severe symptoms or weakness
