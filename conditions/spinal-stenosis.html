Spinal Stenosis

When the spinal canal narrows

What Is Spinal Stenosis?

Spinal stenosis occurs when the spaces within your spine narrow, putting pressure on the nerves that travel through it. Most commonly affects the lower back (lumbar stenosis) or neck (cervical stenosis). It's typically caused by wear-and-tear changes from osteoarthritis.1

Types of Spinal Stenosis

Lumbar Stenosis

  • Most common form
  • Affects lower back
  • Causes leg symptoms
  • Often due to degenerative changes

Cervical Stenosis

  • Affects the neck
  • Can affect arms and legs
  • May cause balance problems
  • More serious if severe

Symptoms2

Lumbar Stenosis Symptoms

  • Neurogenic claudication (leg pain with walking)
  • Pain relieved by sitting or bending forward
  • Numbness or tingling in legs
  • Weakness in legs or feet
  • Back pain (may or may not be present)

Cervical Stenosis Symptoms

  • Neck pain
  • Arm numbness or weakness
  • Balance and walking difficulties
  • Bladder or bowel problems (severe cases)

Causes

  • Osteoarthritis and bone spurs
  • Thickened ligaments
  • Herniated discs
  • Congenital narrowing (born with it)
  • Spinal injuries
  • Tumors (rare)

Treatment Options3

Conservative Treatment

  • Physical therapy
  • Anti-inflammatory medications
  • Epidural steroid injections
  • Activity modification
  • Assistive devices for walking

Surgical Options

  • Laminectomy (decompression surgery)
  • Laminotomy
  • Spinal fusion (sometimes needed)
  • Minimally invasive options available

Living with Stenosis

  • Bending forward often relieves symptoms
  • Using a walker or shopping cart can help
  • Cycling often better tolerated than walking
  • Core strengthening helps stability
  • Many people manage well without surgery
