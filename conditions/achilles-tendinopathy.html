Achilles Tendinopathy

Overuse injury of the body's largest tendon

What Is Achilles Tendinopathy?

Achilles tendinopathy is a condition involving pain, swelling, and impaired function of the Achilles tendon. It's common in runners and middle-aged "weekend warriors." The term "tendinopathy" is preferred over "tendinitis" as the condition often involves degeneration rather than inflammation.1

Types

  • Midportion - Most common (2-6 cm above heel)
  • Insertional - At heel bone attachment

Symptoms2

  • Pain and stiffness along tendon
  • Morning stiffness
  • Pain with activity (esp. starting exercise)
  • Swelling or thickening
  • Tenderness to touch

Risk Factors

  • Sudden increase in activity
  • Tight calf muscles
  • Flat feet or overpronation
  • Running hills
  • Improper footwear
  • Fluoroquinolone antibiotics
  • Age, obesity, diabetes

Treatment3

Conservative (Most Cases)

  • Relative rest, activity modification
  • Eccentric strengthening exercises
  • Calf stretching
  • Ice after activity
  • Heel lifts
  • Proper footwear

Additional Options

  • Physical therapy
  • Shockwave therapy
  • PRP injections
  • Night splints

Surgery

  • Reserved for refractory cases
  • Debridement or repair
