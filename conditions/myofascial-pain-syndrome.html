Myofascial Pain Syndrome

Chronic muscle pain from trigger points

What Is Myofascial Pain Syndrome?

Myofascial pain syndrome (MPS) is a chronic pain disorder caused by sensitivity and tension in myofascial tissuesâ€”the muscles and their surrounding connective tissue. It's characterized by trigger points that cause pain locally and in referred patterns.1

Trigger Points

  • Hyperirritable spots in taut muscle bands
  • Palpable nodules
  • Cause local and referred pain
  • Can be active (cause pain) or latent
  • Twitch response when pressed

Symptoms2

  • Deep, aching muscle pain
  • Pain that persists or worsens
  • Tender knots in muscles
  • Referred pain patterns
  • Limited range of motion
  • Sleep disturbance
  • Muscle weakness

Common Locations

  • Neck and shoulders (trapezius)
  • Low back (quadratus lumborum)
  • Jaw (masseters)
  • Hip (gluteals)
  • Legs

Causes

  • Muscle overuse or injury
  • Poor posture
  • Stress and anxiety
  • Repetitive motions
  • Inactivity
  • Sleep problems
  • Nutritional deficiencies

Treatment3

Manual Therapy

  • Trigger point release
  • Myofascial release
  • Massage therapy
  • Stretching

Interventional

  • Trigger point injections
  • Dry needling

Other Treatments

  • Physical therapy
  • Heat therapy
  • Spray and stretch
  • Ultrasound
  • Medications (NSAIDs, muscle relaxants)
