Myofascial Pain Syndrome

Trigger points and referred pain

What Is Myofascial Pain Syndrome?

Myofascial pain syndrome (MPS) is a chronic pain condition characterized by sensitive points in muscles called trigger points. These trigger points can cause pain in seemingly unrelated parts of the body (referred pain). MPS is distinct from fibromyalgia, though they can coexist.1

Trigger Points2

  • Taut bands in muscle
  • Tender spots that refer pain elsewhere
  • Active trigger points cause spontaneous pain
  • Latent trigger points hurt only when pressed
  • Can cause muscle weakness and stiffness

Symptoms

  • Deep, aching muscle pain
  • Pain that persists or worsens
  • Knots in muscles
  • Pain in unexpected locations (referred)
  • Difficulty sleeping
  • Muscle stiffness

Common Locations

  • Neck and shoulders
  • Upper back
  • Lower back
  • Jaw muscles
  • Hip and buttock muscles

Causes

  • Muscle overuse or injury
  • Repetitive motions
  • Poor posture
  • Stress and tension
  • Inactivity
  • Other pain conditions

Treatment3

Physical Approaches

  • Trigger point release (manual)
  • Stretch and spray technique
  • Massage therapy
  • Dry needling
  • Physical therapy
  • Stretching exercises

Injections

  • Trigger point injections
  • Local anesthetic
  • Sometimes corticosteroids

Self-Care

  • Heat application
  • Self-massage tools (balls, foam rollers)
  • Stretching
  • Posture improvement
  • Stress management
