Autoimmune disease affecting moisture-producing glands

What Is Sj√∂gren's Syndrome?

Sj√∂gren's syndrome is a chronic autoimmune disease where the immune system attacks moisture-producing glands. While dry eyes and dry mouth are hallmark symptoms, it can also cause fatigue, joint pain, and affect multiple organ systems.1

Types

  • Primary - Occurs alone
  • Secondary - Occurs with another autoimmune disease (RA, lupus)

Symptoms2

Glandular (Sicca)

  • Dry eyes (gritty, burning)
  • Dry mouth (difficulty swallowing)
  • Dental problems
  • Vaginal dryness
  • Dry skin

Extraglandular

  • Fatigue (often severe)
  • Joint pain
  • Muscle pain
  • Brain fog
  • Peripheral neuropathy
  • Lung, kidney involvement (some)

Diagnosis

  • Symptom assessment
  • Eye tests (Schirmer test, staining)
  • Blood tests (SSA, SSB antibodies, ANA)
  • Salivary gland biopsy (sometimes)
  • Often takes years to diagnose

Treatment3

Symptom Management

  • Artificial tears
  • Saliva substitutes
  • Prescription eye drops (cyclosporine)
  • Prescription medications for saliva (pilocarpine)

Systemic Treatment

  • Hydroxychloroquine
  • NSAIDs for joint pain
  • Immunosuppressants for organ involvement
  • Biologics (rituximab) in severe cases

Lifestyle

  • Stay hydrated
  • Use humidifiers
  • Avoid dry environments
  • Good dental hygiene
  • Pace activities (fatigue management)
