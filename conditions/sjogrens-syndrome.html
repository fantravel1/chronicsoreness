What Is Sj√∂gren's Syndrome?

Sj√∂gren's syndrome is an autoimmune disease where immune cells attack moisture-producing glands. The hallmark symptoms are dry eyes and dry mouth, but it also causes fatigue, joint pain, and can affect multiple organ systems.1

Types

  • Primary - Occurs alone
  • Secondary - With another autoimmune disease (RA, lupus)

Symptoms2

Classic (Sicca)

  • Dry eyes (burning, gritty)
  • Dry mouth (difficulty swallowing, dental problems)

Systemic

  • Fatigue (often profound)
  • Joint pain
  • Skin rashes
  • Dry skin
  • Muscle pain
  • Brain fog

Diagnosis

  • Clinical symptoms
  • Blood tests (SSA/SSB antibodies, ANA)
  • Eye tests (Schirmer test)
  • Sometimes lip biopsy

Treatment3

For Dryness

  • Artificial tears
  • Saliva substitutes
  • Prescription eye drops (cyclosporine)
  • Pilocarpine or cevimeline

For Systemic Symptoms

  • Hydroxychloroquine
  • NSAIDs for joint pain
  • Immunosuppressants if needed

Self-Management

  • Stay hydrated
  • Use humidifiers
  • Avoid dry environments
  • Good dental hygiene essential
  • Protect eyes from wind
