Cervicogenic Headache

Headaches originating from the cervical spine

What Is Cervicogenic Headache?

Cervicogenic headache is a secondary headache caused by disorders of the cervical spine and its component structures. Pain is referred from cervical structures (joints, discs, muscles) to the head.1

Symptoms

  • Unilateral headache (side-locked)
  • Starts in neck, spreads to head
  • Pain triggered by neck movement or posture
  • Reduced neck range of motion
  • Same-side shoulder or arm pain
  • Non-throbbing, non-pulsating quality

Diagnostic Criteria2

  • Evidence of cervical source
  • Clinical signs of neck involvement
  • Pain relieved by diagnostic block
  • Headache resolves after treating cervical disorder

Common Causes

  • Upper cervical facet joint dysfunction
  • C2-3 disc degeneration
  • Atlantoaxial joint problems
  • Muscle tightness (upper trapezius, suboccipitals)
  • Whiplash injury

Treatment3

Physical Therapy

  • Cervical spine mobilization
  • Postural correction
  • Strengthening exercises
  • Ergonomic modifications

Interventional

  • Greater occipital nerve blocks
  • Cervical facet injections
  • Radiofrequency ablation
