Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (ME/CFS)

A complex, debilitating illness

What Is ME/CFS?

Myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome (ME/CFS) is a complex, multi-system illness characterized by profound fatigue that isn't improved by rest and worsens with physical or mental exertion.1

Core Symptoms

  • Profound fatigue - New, not lifelong
  • Post-exertional malaise (PEM) - Worsening after activity
  • Unrefreshing sleep
  • Cognitive impairment - "Brain fog"
  • Orthostatic intolerance

Other Common Symptoms

  • Widespread pain
  • Headaches
  • Sore throat, swollen lymph nodes
  • Sensitivities (light, sound, chemicals)
  • Temperature dysregulation
  • GI symptoms

Diagnosis2

  • Clinical diagnosis based on symptoms
  • No specific diagnostic test
  • Must rule out other conditions
  • Often takes years to diagnose

Management

  • Pacing - Stay within "energy envelope"
  • Rest - Allow recovery
  • Symptom management - Pain, sleep, etc.
  • Treat comorbidities
  • Avoid pushing through - Can worsen condition

Important Notes3

  • GET (graded exercise therapy) controversial; can harm
  • This is a physiological illness, not psychological
  • Severity varies widely
  • Research is ongoing
