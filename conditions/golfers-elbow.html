Golfer's Elbow

Medial epicondylitis explained

What Is Golfer's Elbow?

Golfer's elbow (medial epicondylitis) affects the inner elbow where flexor tendons attach. Less common than tennis elbow, it results from repetitive wrist flexion and gripping activities.1

Causes

  • Repetitive wrist flexion
  • Golf (especially poor technique)
  • Throwing sports
  • Weight training
  • Carpentry
  • Computer work

Symptoms2

  • Pain on inner elbow
  • Pain with wrist flexion
  • Pain with gripping
  • Weakness in grip
  • May radiate down forearm
  • Stiffness

Comparison to Tennis Elbow

  • Inner vs. outer elbow
  • Flexor vs. extensor tendons
  • Less common than tennis elbow
  • Similar treatment approach

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Rest from aggravating activities
  • Ice
  • Forearm brace
  • NSAIDs
  • Stretching and strengthening
  • Eccentric exercises

Additional Options

  • Physical therapy
  • Corticosteroid injection
  • PRP therapy

Prevention

  • Proper technique
  • Gradual increase in activity
  • Warm up before activities
  • Strengthen forearm muscles

Prognosis

Most cases resolve with conservative treatment in 3-6 months. Surgery is rarely needed.
