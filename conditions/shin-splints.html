Shin Splints

Medial tibial stress syndrome from overuse

What Are Shin Splints?

Shin splints, medically known as medial tibial stress syndrome (MTSS), is pain along the inner edge of the shinbone. It's one of the most common exercise-related leg injuries, affecting up to 35% of runners and military recruits.1

Symptoms

  • Pain along inner border of tibia
  • Tenderness to touch
  • Mild swelling
  • Pain at start of exercise that may ease
  • Pain returns after exercise
  • Eventually pain throughout activity

Risk Factors2

  • Sudden increase in activity
  • Running on hard surfaces
  • Worn-out footwear
  • Flat feet or high arches
  • Weak hip/core muscles
  • Female sex
  • Previous shin splints

Differential Diagnosis

  • Stress fracture (localized pain, worse with hopping)
  • Chronic exertional compartment syndrome
  • Nerve entrapment

Treatment3

Acute Phase

  • Relative rest (not complete rest)
  • Cross-train with low-impact activities
  • Ice massage
  • NSAIDs short-term

Rehabilitation

  • Gradual return to activity
  • Calf stretching and strengthening
  • Hip and core strengthening
  • Gait retraining if needed

Prevention

  • Proper footwear
  • Orthotics if indicated
  • Gradual training increases (10% rule)
  • Varied running surfaces
