Shin Splints

Exercise-related lower leg pain

What Are Shin Splints?

Shin splints, medically known as medial tibial stress syndrome (MTSS), is pain along the inner edge of the shinbone (tibia). It's one of the most common exercise-related leg injuries, affecting up to 35% of runners and military recruits.1

Symptoms2

  • Pain along inner shin bone
  • Tenderness along the tibia
  • Mild swelling in lower leg
  • Pain during and after exercise
  • Pain that eases with rest
  • May progress to constant pain if untreated

Causes

  • Sudden increase in activity
  • Running on hard surfaces
  • Improper footwear
  • Flat feet or high arches
  • Weak ankle or hip muscles
  • Poor running mechanics

Diagnosis

  • Physical examination
  • Tenderness along lower two-thirds of tibia
  • X-ray (to rule out stress fracture)
  • MRI or bone scan if stress fracture suspected

Treatment3

Initial Care

  • Rest from aggravating activities
  • Ice (15-20 minutes several times daily)
  • NSAIDs for pain and inflammation
  • Compression if swelling present

Rehabilitation

  • Calf stretching and strengthening
  • Eccentric exercises
  • Low-impact cross-training
  • Gradual return to activity
  • Gait retraining if needed

Additional Options

  • Orthotics or arch supports
  • Physical therapy
  • Kinesiology taping

Prevention

  • Increase training gradually (10% rule)
  • Wear proper footwear
  • Run on softer surfaces when possible
  • Strengthen lower leg muscles
  • Include cross-training
