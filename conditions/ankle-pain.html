Chronic Ankle Pain

Understanding persistent ankle problems

Common Causes of Chronic Ankle Pain

Chronic ankle pain can result from previous injuries that didn't heal properly, arthritis, or ongoing tendon problems. The ankle is a weight-bearing joint that undergoes significant stress during daily activities.1

Causes2

Post-Traumatic

  • Chronic ankle instability (after sprains)
  • Post-traumatic arthritis
  • Impingement (scar tissue)
  • Osteochondral lesions

Degenerative

  • Ankle osteoarthritis
  • Posterior tibial tendon dysfunction

Inflammatory

  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Gout
  • Tendinitis

Symptoms

  • Pain with walking or standing
  • Swelling
  • Stiffness
  • Instability ("giving way")
  • Catching or locking

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Physical therapy
  • Balance and proprioception training
  • Ankle braces or supports
  • Orthotics
  • Pain medications
  • Corticosteroid injections

Surgery

  • Arthroscopy
  • Ligament reconstruction
  • Ankle fusion or replacement (severe arthritis)

Prevention of Recurrence

  • Strengthen ankle muscles
  • Balance training
  • Wear supportive footwear
  • Use bracing during sports
