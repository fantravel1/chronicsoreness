Trigger Finger

A condition causing finger catching and locking

What Is Trigger Finger?

Trigger finger, or stenosing tenosynovitis, is a condition where a finger gets stuck in a bent position and then snaps straight like a trigger being released. It occurs when inflammation narrows the space within the tendon sheath.1

Symptoms

  • Finger stiffness, especially in morning
  • Popping or clicking sensation
  • Tender nodule at base of finger
  • Finger catching or locking in bent position
  • Finger stuck in bent position (severe)
  • Most common: ring finger, thumb

Risk Factors2

  • Repetitive gripping
  • Diabetes (much higher risk)
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Gout
  • Women more affected
  • Age 40-60

Grading

  • Grade I - Pain, tenderness, no catching
  • Grade II - Catching, can actively extend
  • Grade III - Locking, needs passive extension
  • Grade IV - Fixed flexion contracture

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Rest from repetitive gripping
  • Splinting at night
  • NSAIDs
  • Gentle stretching

Corticosteroid Injection

  • Very effective first-line treatment
  • 70-90% success rate
  • May be repeated

Surgery

  • A1 pulley release
  • Percutaneous or open
  • For refractory cases
