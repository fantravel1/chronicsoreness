Hemicrania Continua

Continuous one-sided headache with a specific treatment

What Is Hemicrania Continua?

Hemicrania continua (HC) is a primary headache disorder characterized by continuous, strictly unilateral pain with fluctuating intensity. The defining feature is complete response to indomethacin.1

Key Features2

  • Unilateral (always same side)
  • Continuous (no pain-free periods)
  • Moderate baseline with severe exacerbations
  • Complete response to indomethacin (required)

Associated Symptoms

During exacerbations, at least one of:

  • Tearing or eye redness
  • Nasal congestion or runny nose
  • Eyelid swelling
  • Forehead sweating
  • Miosis (pupil constriction) or ptosis
  • Restlessness or agitation

Diagnosis

  • Clinical features
  • Indomethacin trial (diagnostic)
  • Typical dose: 25-75 mg three times daily
  • Response usually within 1-2 days

Treatment3

First Line

  • Indomethacin (most effective)
  • Use lowest effective dose
  • Take with food or PPI to protect stomach

If Indomethacin Not Tolerated

  • COX-2 inhibitors (celecoxib)
  • Topiramate
  • Gabapentin
  • Greater occipital nerve blocks

Importance of Diagnosis

HC is often misdiagnosed for years. Proper diagnosis is crucial because indomethacin provides complete relief.
