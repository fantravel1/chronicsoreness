Central Sensitization

When the nervous system amplifies pain

What Is Central Sensitization?

Central sensitization is a condition where the central nervous system (brain and spinal cord) becomes hypersensitive and amplifies pain signals. It's like the nervous system's "volume knob" for pain gets turned up too high. This is a real neurological change, not imagined pain.1

How It Works2

  • Normal pain signals are amplified
  • Non-painful stimuli become painful (allodynia)
  • Pain response is exaggerated (hyperalgesia)
  • Pain may spread to uninjured areas
  • Nervous system becomes "wound up"

Signs of Central Sensitization

  • Pain spreads beyond original injury site
  • Light touch causes pain
  • Sensitivity to sounds, lights, smells
  • Pain that continues after healing
  • Fatigue and cognitive difficulties
  • Sleep problems

Conditions Associated with CS3

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic fatigue syndrome
  • Chronic low back pain
  • Temporomandibular disorders
  • Migraine and tension headaches
  • Irritable bowel syndrome
  • Some chronic whiplash

What Causes It?

  • Prolonged or severe pain
  • Repeated injuries
  • Chronic stress
  • Sleep deprivation
  • Anxiety and fear of pain
  • Genetic factors

Treatment Approaches

Education

  • Understanding pain neuroscience
  • Learning about central sensitization
  • Reduces fear and catastrophizing

Movement and Exercise

  • Graded exercise therapy
  • Gradual exposure to movement
  • Helps "retrain" the nervous system

Psychological Approaches

  • Cognitive behavioral therapy
  • Mindfulness-based stress reduction
  • Relaxation training

Medications

  • Anticonvulsants
  • SNRIs
  • Low-dose tricyclics

Hope for Improvement
