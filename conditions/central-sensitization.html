Central Sensitization

When the nervous system amplifies pain

What Is Central Sensitization?

Central sensitization is a process where the central nervous system (brain and spinal cord) becomes hypersensitive, amplifying pain signals and lowering pain thresholds. The nervous system essentially "turns up the volume" on pain, causing you to experience more pain than expected or pain from things that shouldn't normally hurt.1

Key Features2

  • Allodynia (pain from non-painful stimuli)
  • Hyperalgesia (increased pain response)
  • Widespread pain (beyond original site)
  • Pain that persists after tissue healing
  • Heightened sensitivity to multiple stimuli

How It Develops

  • Prolonged pain input changes nervous system
  • Nerve cells become more excitable
  • Pain inhibition pathways become less effective
  • Brain areas change how they process pain
  • Pain can persist even after original cause resolved

Conditions Associated with Central Sensitization3

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic fatigue syndrome
  • Chronic low back pain
  • Migraine
  • IBS
  • TMJ disorders
  • Whiplash
  • CRPS

Signs of Central Sensitization

  • Pain disproportionate to tissue damage
  • Widespread pain
  • Sensitivity to light, sound, touch
  • Pain from light touch or pressure
  • Multiple coexisting pain conditions
  • Limited response to treatments

This Does NOT Mean

  • Pain is imaginary
  • Pain is "all in your head"
  • You're making it up
  • Pain is psychological (though psychology affects it)

Treatment Approaches

  • Pain neuroscience education
  • Graded exposure to movement
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy
  • Medications (centrally acting)
  • Mindfulness and stress reduction
  • Sleep improvement
  • Exercise (carefully paced)

Why Understanding Helps

  • Explains why pain persists
  • Reduces fear and catastrophizing
  • Guides appropriate treatment
  • Knowledge itself can reduce pain
  • Central sensitization can improve
