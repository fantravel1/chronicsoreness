Central Sensitization

When the nervous system amplifies pain

What Is Central Sensitization?

Central sensitization is a condition where the central nervous system (brain and spinal cord) becomes hypersensitive, amplifying pain signals and sometimes creating pain in the absence of tissue damage. It helps explain why chronic pain can persist even after an injury has healed and why pain can spread beyond the original site.1

How It Develops2

  • Prolonged pain input changes nervous system
  • Pain processing neurons become more excitable
  • "Volume turned up" on pain signals
  • Brain and spinal cord changes structure
  • Normal inhibition reduced
  • Pain becomes self-perpetuating

Signs and Features

  • Pain disproportionate to tissue damage
  • Pain spreading beyond injury site
  • Allodynia (pain from non-painful stimuli)
  • Hyperalgesia (increased pain response)
  • Sensitivity to light, sound, smells
  • Cognitive difficulties ("brain fog")
  • Fatigue
  • Sleep problems

Conditions Associated3

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic fatigue syndrome
  • Irritable bowel syndrome
  • Temporomandibular disorders
  • Tension headaches
  • Some chronic low back pain
  • Chronic whiplash
  • Complex regional pain syndrome

Why Understanding It Helps

  • Explains pain without clear "damage"
  • Pain is still real (nervous system problem)
  • Different treatment approach needed
  • Reduces self-blame
  • Guides treatment choices

Treatment Approaches

Education

  • Understanding pain neuroscience
  • Reduces fear and catastrophizing
  • Shown to improve outcomes

Medications

  • Central-acting medications
  • SNRIs, tricyclics
  • Anticonvulsants

Physical Approaches

  • Graded exercise
  • Pacing
  • Desensitization techniques

Psychological

  • CBT for chronic pain
  • Acceptance and commitment therapy
  • Mindfulness
  • Stress reduction

What Doesn't Help

  • More surgeries for "structural" problems
  • Excessive rest
  • Avoiding all activity
  • Searching for tissue-based explanation
