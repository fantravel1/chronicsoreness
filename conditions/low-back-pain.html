Chronic Low Back Pain

The most common cause of disability worldwide

About Low Back Pain

Low back pain is the leading cause of disability globally. While most acute episodes resolve, about 20% become chronic. Chronic low back pain lasting >12 weeks requires a comprehensive management approach.1

Potential Sources2

  • Intervertebral discs
  • Facet joints
  • Sacroiliac joints
  • Muscles and ligaments
  • Nerve roots
  • Vertebral bodies

Contributing Factors

  • Deconditioning
  • Obesity
  • Poor posture
  • Psychological factors
  • Sleep problems
  • Work-related factors
  • Central sensitization

Important Points

  • Imaging findings often don't correlate with pain
  • "Nonspecific" is common - no specific cause found
  • Serious causes are rare but must be ruled out
  • Multifactorial approach often needed

Red Flags

  • Bowel/bladder dysfunction
  • Saddle numbness
  • Progressive weakness
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fever
  • History of cancer

Evidence-Based Treatment3

First-Line (Strong Evidence)

  • Exercise (any type)
  • Staying active
  • Education and reassurance
  • NSAIDs (short-term)
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy

Second-Line

  • Physical therapy
  • Massage
  • Spinal manipulation
  • Acupuncture
  • Duloxetine

Multidisciplinary Rehabilitation

  • Combination of physical and psychological approaches
  • Most effective for complex, chronic cases
