Trigeminal Neuralgia

One of the most severe pain conditions known

What Is Trigeminal Neuralgia?

Trigeminal neuralgia (TN), also called tic douloureux, is a chronic pain condition affecting the trigeminal nerve, which carries sensation from the face to the brain. It causes extreme, sporadic, sudden burning or shock-like facial pain.1

Symptoms

  • Sudden, severe, shock-like pain
  • Attacks lasting seconds to minutes
  • Pain triggered by everyday activities
  • Usually affects one side of face
  • Episodes can increase over time
  • Pain-free intervals between attacks

Common Triggers2

  • Touching the face
  • Chewing or talking
  • Brushing teeth
  • Shaving
  • Cold wind on face
  • Applying makeup
  • Smiling

Causes

  • Blood vessel pressing on nerve (most common)
  • Multiple sclerosis
  • Tumor compressing nerve
  • Facial trauma or surgical injury
  • Stroke

Treatment3

Medications (First Line)

  • Carbamazepine
  • Oxcarbazepine
  • Baclofen
  • Lamotrigine

Surgical Options

  • Microvascular decompression (MVD)
  • Stereotactic radiosurgery (Gamma Knife)
  • Percutaneous procedures
