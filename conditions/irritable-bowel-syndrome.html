Irritable Bowel Syndrome

A common disorder affecting the gut-brain connection

What Is IBS?

Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) is a chronic functional gastrointestinal disorder characterized by recurrent abdominal pain associated with changes in bowel habits. It affects 10-15% of the population and often overlaps with other chronic pain conditions.1

Subtypes

  • IBS-D - Diarrhea predominant
  • IBS-C - Constipation predominant
  • IBS-M - Mixed pattern
  • IBS-U - Unclassified

Symptoms2

  • Abdominal pain related to bowel movements
  • Change in stool frequency or form
  • Bloating and distension
  • Mucus in stool
  • Urgency
  • Incomplete evacuation

The Gut-Brain Connection

  • Bidirectional communication between gut and brain
  • Stress can trigger or worsen symptoms
  • Altered gut sensitivity
  • Often coexists with anxiety/depression

Treatment3

Diet

  • Low FODMAP diet
  • Fiber modification
  • Identify trigger foods
  • Regular meal timing

Medications

  • Antispasmodics
  • Low-dose antidepressants
  • Laxatives or anti-diarrheals
  • Newer IBS-specific medications

Mind-Body Approaches

  • Gut-directed hypnotherapy
  • CBT for IBS
  • Stress management
  • Relaxation techniques
