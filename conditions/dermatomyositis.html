Dermatomyositis

Inflammatory muscle disease with skin involvement

What Is Dermatomyositis?

Dermatomyositis is an inflammatory myopathy characterized by muscle weakness and distinctive skin rashes. It's an autoimmune condition that affects both adults and children. The hallmark is the combination of proximal muscle weakness with characteristic cutaneous findings.1

Characteristic Skin Signs2

Heliotrope Rash

  • Violet or dusky red rash on eyelids
  • Often with swelling
  • Pathognomonic (highly characteristic)

Gottron Papules

  • Raised, scaly patches over knuckles
  • Also on elbows and knees
  • Violaceous (purple) color

Other Skin Findings

  • V-sign: Rash on chest and neck
  • Shawl sign: Rash on upper back and shoulders
  • Mechanic's hands: Rough, cracked skin on fingers
  • Photosensitivity

Muscle Symptoms

  • Progressive weakness in proximal muscles
  • Difficulty climbing stairs
  • Trouble rising from chairs
  • Difficulty lifting arms
  • May have muscle pain
  • Can affect swallowing muscles

Diagnosis

  • Clinical examination of skin and muscles
  • Elevated muscle enzymes (CK, aldolase)
  • Myositis-specific antibodies
  • EMG shows myopathic changes
  • Muscle biopsy
  • MRI of muscles

Treatment3

For Muscles

  • Corticosteroids (first-line)
  • Methotrexate or azathioprine
  • IVIG for refractory cases
  • Physical therapy (essential)

For Skin

  • Sun protection (critical)
  • Topical steroids
  • Hydroxychloroquine
  • Methotrexate

Associated Conditions

  • Interstitial lung disease
  • Increased cancer risk (adults)
  • Calcinosis (calcium deposits, especially in children)
  • Heart involvement (rare)

Living with Dermatomyositis

  • Strict sun protection is essential
  • Regular cancer screening for adults
  • Maintain exercise within tolerance
  • Monitor for lung involvement
  • Regular follow-up with rheumatologist
