TMJ Disorders

Temporomandibular joint dysfunction and jaw pain

What Are TMJ Disorders?

Temporomandibular disorders (TMD) are a group of conditions affecting the jaw joint, muscles of mastication, and associated structures. They affect 5-12% of the population and are more common in women.1

Types of TMD

  • Myofascial pain - Most common; pain in jaw muscles
  • Internal derangement - Disc displacement
  • Degenerative joint disease - Osteoarthritis

Symptoms2

  • Jaw pain or tenderness
  • Pain while chewing
  • Clicking, popping, or grating sounds
  • Limited jaw opening
  • Jaw locking
  • Ear pain or fullness
  • Headaches
  • Facial pain

Contributing Factors

  • Bruxism (teeth grinding/clenching)
  • Stress and anxiety
  • Jaw injury
  • Arthritis
  • Poor posture
  • Nail biting, gum chewing

Treatment3

Conservative (First Line)

  • Soft diet
  • Heat or ice application
  • Jaw rest (avoid wide opening)
  • NSAIDs
  • Physical therapy
  • Stress management

Other Treatments

  • Oral splints/night guards
  • Muscle relaxants
  • Trigger point injections
  • Botox for muscle spasm
  • Surgery (rarely needed)
