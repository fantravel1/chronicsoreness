TMJ Disorders

Understanding jaw pain and dysfunction

What Are TMJ Disorders?

Temporomandibular disorders (TMD) affect the jaw joint (TMJ) and the muscles that control jaw movement. They're a common cause of facial pain and can cause clicking, popping, or pain when opening the mouth. Many cases are temporary and respond to conservative treatment.1

The TMJ

  • Temporomandibular joint
  • Connects jawbone to skull
  • Complex joint (slides and hinges)
  • Has a disc between bones
  • Surrounded by muscles

Types of TMD2

Myofascial Pain

  • Pain in muscles controlling jaw
  • Most common type
  • Often related to clenching/grinding

Internal Derangement

  • Displaced disc
  • Jaw displacement
  • May cause clicking or locking

Arthritis

  • Degenerative changes in joint
  • Osteoarthritis or inflammatory

Symptoms3

  • Jaw pain or tenderness
  • Pain around ear
  • Difficulty or pain chewing
  • Clicking or popping sounds
  • Jaw locking (open or closed)
  • Limited jaw opening
  • Facial pain
  • Headaches
  • Ear symptoms (fullness, ringing)

Causes and Risk Factors

  • Bruxism (teeth grinding/clenching)
  • Stress
  • Arthritis
  • Jaw injury
  • Poor posture
  • Genetics
  • Other chronic pain conditions

Self-Care

  • Soft foods during flares
  • Avoid wide jaw opening
  • Moist heat or ice
  • Jaw relaxation exercises
  • Avoid gum chewing
  • Stress management
  • Good posture

Treatment

Conservative (First Line)

  • Self-care measures
  • NSAIDs or pain relievers
  • Physical therapy
  • Oral appliances (night guards)
  • Stress management

Additional Options

  • Muscle relaxants
  • Trigger point injections
  • Botox injections
  • Arthrocentesis (joint wash)

Surgery (Rarely Needed)

  • For structural problems
  • When conservative treatment fails
  • Various procedures available

Prognosis

  • Many cases resolve on their own
  • Conservative treatment often effective
  • Avoid irreversible treatments initially
  • Some become chronic
