Post-Concussion Syndrome

When concussion symptoms persist

What Is Post-Concussion Syndrome?

Post-concussion syndrome (PCS) occurs when concussion symptoms persist for weeks, months, or even years after the initial head injury. It affects 10-30% of people who experience concussion.1

Symptoms2

Physical

  • Headache (most common)
  • Dizziness
  • Fatigue
  • Light sensitivity
  • Noise sensitivity
  • Sleep disturbance
  • Balance problems

Cognitive

  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Memory problems
  • Mental fogginess
  • Slowed processing

Emotional

  • Irritability
  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Emotional lability

Risk Factors

  • Previous concussions
  • Pre-existing mental health conditions
  • Migraine history
  • Female sex
  • Age (older adults)

Treatment3

Symptom-Specific Treatment

  • Headache management (based on headache type)
  • Vestibular rehabilitation (for dizziness)
  • Cognitive rehabilitation
  • Mental health treatment
  • Sleep optimization

General Approaches

  • Gradual return to activity
  • Aerobic exercise (sub-symptom threshold)
  • Cognitive-behavioral therapy
  • Medication for specific symptoms

Prognosis

Most people recover within 3 months. Some have prolonged symptoms, but improvement is still possible.
