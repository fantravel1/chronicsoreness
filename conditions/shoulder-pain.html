Shoulder Pain

Understanding chronic shoulder problems

Shoulder Anatomy

The shoulder is the most mobile joint in the body, but this mobility comes at the cost of stability. The shoulder complex includes the glenohumeral joint, rotator cuff muscles, and surrounding structures that can all be sources of pain.1

Common Causes of Shoulder Pain2

Rotator Cuff Problems

  • Rotator cuff tendinopathy
  • Rotator cuff tears (partial or full)
  • Impingement syndrome

Other Conditions

  • Frozen shoulder (adhesive capsulitis)
  • Shoulder arthritis
  • AC joint problems
  • Biceps tendinitis
  • Labral tears
  • Bursitis

Referred Pain

  • Cervical spine problems
  • Heart (left shoulder)
  • Gallbladder (right shoulder)

Warning Signs

  • Sudden inability to lift arm
  • Trauma with deformity
  • Chest pain with shoulder pain
  • Fever with joint pain

Treatment3

Conservative (First Line for Most)

  • Physical therapy - stretching and strengthening
  • Activity modification
  • NSAIDs
  • Ice
  • Posture correction

Injections

  • Subacromial injection
  • Glenohumeral joint injection
  • Hydrodilatation (for frozen shoulder)

Surgery

  • Arthroscopy
  • Rotator cuff repair
  • Shoulder replacement (severe arthritis)
