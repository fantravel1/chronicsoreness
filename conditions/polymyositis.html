Polymyositis

Inflammatory disease of muscles

What Is Polymyositis?

Polymyositis is an inflammatory myopathy causing chronic muscle inflammation and weakness. It primarily affects proximal muscles (shoulders, hips, thighs). It's part of a group of conditions called inflammatory myopathies.1

Related Conditions

  • Dermatomyositis - With skin involvement
  • Inclusion body myositis - Different pattern, older adults

Symptoms2

  • Progressive muscle weakness
  • Difficulty rising from chairs
  • Trouble climbing stairs
  • Difficulty lifting arms overhead
  • Muscle pain and tenderness
  • Fatigue
  • Swallowing difficulty (some cases)

Diagnosis

  • Elevated muscle enzymes (CK, aldolase)
  • EMG abnormalities
  • Muscle biopsy
  • MRI showing inflammation
  • Autoantibodies

Treatment3

Medications

  • Corticosteroids (first-line)
  • Methotrexate
  • Azathioprine
  • IVIG
  • Rituximab (refractory cases)

Rehabilitation

  • Physical therapy essential
  • Maintain range of motion
  • Strengthen when inflammation controlled

Prognosis

Most respond to treatment, though it may take months. Some have chronic symptoms requiring long-term immunosuppression.
