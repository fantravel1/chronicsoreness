Polymyositis

Inflammatory muscle disease

What Is Polymyositis?

Polymyositis is a type of inflammatory myopathy that causes muscle inflammation and weakness. It primarily affects the proximal muscles (those closest to the trunk) and is considered an autoimmune condition where the immune system attacks muscle tissue.1

Symptoms2

Muscle Symptoms

  • Progressive weakness in shoulders, hips, and thighs
  • Difficulty climbing stairs
  • Trouble rising from seated position
  • Difficulty lifting arms overhead
  • May have muscle pain

Other Symptoms

  • Fatigue
  • Difficulty swallowing (dysphagia)
  • Shortness of breath
  • Joint pain
  • Fever in some cases

Diagnosis

  • Elevated muscle enzymes (CK, aldolase)
  • Myositis-specific antibodies
  • Electromyography (EMG)
  • Muscle biopsy
  • MRI of muscles

Treatment3

Medications

  • Corticosteroids (first-line treatment)
  • Methotrexate
  • Azathioprine
  • Mycophenolate mofetil
  • IVIG for resistant cases
  • Rituximab for refractory disease

Physical Therapy

  • Essential part of treatment
  • Helps maintain muscle strength
  • Prevents contractures
  • Should be tailored to disease activity

Associated Conditions

  • Interstitial lung disease
  • Heart involvement (rare)
  • Increased cancer risk (screening recommended)
  • May overlap with other autoimmune diseases

Living with Polymyositis

  • Regular monitoring of muscle enzymes
  • Balance rest and exercise
  • Watch for swallowing difficulties
  • Report breathing changes promptly
  • Sun protection if on immunosuppressants
  • Regular cancer screening

Prognosis

  • Most respond to treatment
  • Many achieve remission or low disease activity
  • Some require long-term medication
  • Lung involvement affects prognosis
