Migraines

A complex neurological condition causing severe head pain

What Is a Migraine?

Migraine is a primary headache disorder characterized by recurrent episodes of moderate to severe, often unilateral, pulsating headache. It affects approximately 12% of adults worldwide and is three times more common in women.1

Phases of Migraine

  • Prodrome - Hours to days before: mood changes, food cravings, neck stiffness
  • Aura - Visual, sensory, or speech disturbances (in ~25%)
  • Headache - 4-72 hours of pain with associated symptoms
  • Postdrome - Fatigue, cognitive difficulties after

Symptoms2

  • Pulsating/throbbing pain
  • Often unilateral (one-sided)
  • Moderate to severe intensity
  • Worsened by physical activity
  • Nausea and/or vomiting
  • Sensitivity to light (photophobia)
  • Sensitivity to sound (phonophobia)

Common Triggers

  • Stress (or stress letdown)
  • Hormonal changes
  • Sleep changes
  • Weather changes
  • Certain foods (alcohol, aged cheese, MSG)
  • Skipped meals
  • Bright lights, strong smells

Treatment

Acute Treatment

  • NSAIDs, acetaminophen (mild attacks)
  • Triptans (moderate-severe)
  • CGRP antagonists (gepants)
  • Anti-nausea medications

Preventive Treatment3

  • Beta-blockers (propranolol)
  • Anticonvulsants (topiramate)
  • Antidepressants (amitriptyline)
  • CGRP monoclonal antibodies
  • Botox (for chronic migraine)
