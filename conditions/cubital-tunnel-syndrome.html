Cubital Tunnel Syndrome

Ulnar nerve compression at the elbow

What Is Cubital Tunnel Syndrome?

Cubital tunnel syndrome is the second most common nerve entrapment syndrome after carpal tunnel. It occurs when the ulnar nerve is compressed at the elbow, causing numbness and weakness in the ring and small fingers.1

Symptoms

  • Numbness in ring and small fingers
  • Tingling along inner forearm
  • Weakness in grip
  • Hand clumsiness
  • Worse when elbow bent
  • May wake at night with symptoms
  • Muscle wasting in severe cases

Causes2

  • Prolonged elbow bending
  • Leaning on elbow
  • Prior elbow injury
  • Arthritis
  • Bone spurs
  • Cysts or ganglions
  • Repetitive activities

Diagnosis

  • Physical examination
  • Tinel's sign at elbow
  • Elbow flexion test
  • Nerve conduction study/EMG
  • X-ray or MRI

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Avoid pressure on elbow
  • Keep elbow straight at night (splint)
  • Elbow pad
  • NSAIDs
  • Activity modification

Surgery

  • Cubital tunnel release
  • Ulnar nerve transposition
  • Medial epicondylectomy
