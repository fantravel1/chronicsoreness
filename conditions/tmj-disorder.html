TMJ Disorder

When the jaw joint causes pain and dysfunction

What Is TMJ Disorder?

Temporomandibular joint disorder (TMD, often called TMJ) affects the jaw joint and surrounding muscles. It causes pain, difficulty chewing, and clicking or locking of the jaw. It's more common in women and typically appears between ages 20-40.1

Types

  • Muscle disorders - Pain in jaw muscles
  • Internal derangement - Disc displacement
  • Degenerative joint disease - Arthritis
  • Many people have more than one type

Symptoms2

  • Jaw pain or tenderness
  • Facial pain
  • Ear pain or fullness
  • Headaches
  • Clicking, popping, or grating sounds
  • Jaw locking (open or closed)
  • Difficulty chewing
  • Neck and shoulder pain

Contributing Factors

  • Teeth grinding (bruxism)
  • Jaw clenching
  • Stress and tension
  • Arthritis
  • Jaw injury
  • Poor posture

Treatment3

Self-Care

  • Soft diet during flares
  • Heat or ice
  • Avoid wide opening
  • Stress management
  • Jaw relaxation exercises

Professional Treatment

  • Physical therapy
  • Oral splints or night guards
  • Medications (muscle relaxants, NSAIDs)
  • Trigger point injections
  • Botox (for muscle-related TMD)
  • Surgery (rarely needed)

Prognosis

Most people improve with conservative treatment. Symptoms often fluctuate but tend to improve over time.
