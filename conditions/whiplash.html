Whiplash

Neck injury from sudden acceleration-deceleration

What Is Whiplash?

Whiplash occurs when the neck is suddenly forced backward then forward (or vice versa), straining muscles, ligaments, and other soft tissues. It's most commonly caused by rear-end car accidents but can occur from sports, falls, or assault.1

Symptoms

  • Neck pain and stiffness
  • Headaches (base of skull)
  • Reduced range of motion
  • Shoulder or upper back pain
  • Arm pain or tingling
  • Fatigue
  • Dizziness

Quebec Classification2

  • Grade 0 - No symptoms
  • Grade I - Pain, no physical signs
  • Grade II - Pain with musculoskeletal signs
  • Grade III - Pain with neurological signs
  • Grade IV - Fracture or dislocation

Risk Factors for Chronicity

  • High initial pain intensity
  • Prior neck pain
  • Headache at onset
  • Psychological distress
  • Older age
  • Female sex

Treatment3

Acute Phase

  • Stay active (avoid collars)
  • Gentle range of motion exercises
  • Pain medication if needed
  • Ice or heat

Ongoing Management

  • Physical therapy
  • Gradual return to activities
  • Exercise program
  • Address psychological factors

Prognosis

Most people recover within 3 months. About 25-40% develop chronic symptoms lasting beyond 6 months.
