Whiplash

Neck injury from rapid back-and-forth movement

What Is Whiplash?

Whiplash is a neck injury caused by rapid, forceful back-and-forth movement of the neck, like cracking a whip. The term "whiplash-associated disorders" (WAD) describes the range of symptoms that can follow. Most commonly results from rear-end car collisions.1

What Happens

  • Rapid extension then flexion of neck
  • Injury to muscles, ligaments, discs, nerves
  • Symptoms may be delayed hours to days

Symptoms2

Common

  • Neck pain and stiffness
  • Reduced range of motion
  • Headaches (base of skull)
  • Tenderness in shoulders, upper back

Other Symptoms

  • Dizziness
  • Fatigue
  • Arm pain or numbness
  • Concentration/memory problems
  • Sleep disturbance
  • Irritability

WAD Classification

  • Grade 0 - No symptoms
  • Grade I - Pain, stiffness, tenderness only
  • Grade II - Musculoskeletal signs (reduced ROM)
  • Grade III - Neurological signs
  • Grade IV - Fracture or dislocation

Prognosis3

  • Most (50-60%) recover within 3 months
  • About 25-40% have symptoms at 1 year
  • Some develop chronic pain
  • Psychological factors affect recovery

Treatment

Early Phase

  • Reassurance - most recover well
  • Stay active (not bed rest)
  • Gentle movement and stretching
  • Pain medications as needed
  • Ice or heat

Ongoing Management

  • Physical therapy
  • Exercises (strengthening, mobility)
  • Address psychological factors
  • Avoid collar/prolonged rest
