Whiplash

Neck injury from rapid movement

What Is Whiplash?

Whiplash is a neck injury caused by rapid back-and-forth movement of the head and neck, like the cracking of a whip. It commonly occurs in car accidents but can result from sports injuries, falls, or physical trauma. Many recover fully, but some develop chronic pain.1

How It Occurs

  • Most common: Rear-end car collisions
  • Sports impacts
  • Falls
  • Physical abuse
  • Rapid acceleration/deceleration forces

Symptoms2

Common Symptoms

  • Neck pain and stiffness
  • Worsening pain with movement
  • Loss of range of motion
  • Headaches (typically at skull base)
  • Tenderness in shoulders, upper back

Other Possible Symptoms

  • Dizziness
  • Fatigue
  • Arm pain or numbness
  • Blurred vision
  • Ringing in ears
  • Sleep problems
  • Difficulty concentrating

Whiplash-Associated Disorders (WAD)

  • WAD I: Neck pain only, no physical signs
  • WAD II: Neck pain with musculoskeletal signs
  • WAD III: Neck pain with neurological signs
  • WAD IV: Fracture or dislocation

Recovery3

  • Most recover within 3 months
  • About 25-40% develop chronic symptoms
  • Early movement is important
  • Prolonged collar use not recommended

Treatment

Early Phase

  • Ice/heat as comfortable
  • Over-the-counter pain relievers
  • Gentle movement (avoid immobilization)
  • Reassurance about recovery

For Ongoing Pain

  • Physical therapy
  • Exercises for strength and flexibility
  • Manual therapy
  • Addressing psychological factors
  • Pain management if chronic

Risk Factors for Chronic Pain

  • High initial pain intensity
  • Prior neck pain
  • Older age
  • Psychological distress
  • Fear-avoidance beliefs
  • Cold hypersensitivity early on

Prevention

  • Properly adjusted headrests in vehicles
  • Headrest should be at ear level or higher
  • Wear seatbelts
  • Protective gear in sports
