Whiplash

Understanding neck injuries and recovery

What Is Whiplash?

Whiplash is a neck injury caused by rapid back-and-forth movement of the neck, similar to cracking a whip. Most commonly caused by rear-end car accidents, whiplash can also result from sports injuries, falls, or physical abuse. While many recover within weeks, some develop chronic whiplash-associated disorders (WAD).1

Causes

  • Motor vehicle accidents (most common)
  • Contact sports injuries
  • Falls
  • Amusement park rides
  • Physical assault
  • Cycling accidents

Symptoms2

Common Symptoms

  • Neck pain and stiffness
  • Reduced range of motion
  • Headaches (often starting at skull base)
  • Pain with neck movement
  • Tenderness in shoulders, upper back

Other Possible Symptoms

  • Dizziness
  • Fatigue
  • Arm pain or numbness
  • Blurred vision
  • Ringing in ears
  • Sleep disturbances
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Memory problems

When to Seek Care

  • Any symptoms after accident
  • Severe neck pain
  • Arm weakness or numbness
  • Spreading pain
  • Symptoms worsening over time

Diagnosis

  • Physical examination
  • Assessment of range of motion
  • Neurological exam
  • X-rays (rule out fractures)
  • CT or MRI (if needed)

Treatment3

Early Phase

  • Ice for first 24-48 hours
  • Over-the-counter pain relievers
  • Stay active (avoid prolonged rest)
  • Gentle range of motion exercises

Ongoing Treatment

  • Physical therapy
  • Heat application
  • Muscle relaxants (short-term)
  • Cervical collar (limited use only)
  • Massage therapy

Recovery

  • Most improve within 2-3 months
  • Some have symptoms for months/years
  • Early movement aids recovery
  • Return to normal activities gradually

Risk Factors for Chronic WAD

  • High initial pain intensity
  • Older age
  • Prior neck pain
  • Psychological factors
  • Fear of movement
