Postherpetic Neuralgia

Persistent pain after shingles

What Is Postherpetic Neuralgia?

Postherpetic neuralgia (PHN) is nerve pain that persists for months or years after a shingles (herpes zoster) infection has healed. It's the most common complication of shingles and affects about 10-20% of shingles patients.1

Why It Happens

  • Shingles damages nerve fibers
  • Damaged nerves send abnormal signals
  • Pain persists after skin heals
  • Risk increases with age

Symptoms2

  • Burning, aching, or shooting pain
  • Extreme sensitivity to touch (allodynia)
  • Itching
  • Numbness
  • Pain in area where shingles rash occurred
  • May last months to years

Risk Factors

  • Age over 60
  • Severe acute shingles pain
  • Severe rash
  • Delayed antiviral treatment
  • Facial shingles

Prevention

  • Shingles vaccine - Best prevention
  • Early antiviral treatment for shingles

Treatment3

Medications

  • Lidocaine patches (first-line)
  • Capsaicin patches
  • Gabapentin or pregabalin
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • Opioids (if others fail)

Other Treatments

  • Nerve blocks
  • TENS
  • Spinal cord stimulation (severe cases)

Prognosis

Many cases improve over time. Some people have persistent pain requiring long-term management.
