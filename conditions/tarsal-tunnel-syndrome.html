Tarsal Tunnel Syndrome

Tibial nerve compression at the ankle

What Is Tarsal Tunnel Syndrome?

Tarsal tunnel syndrome (TTS) is compression of the posterior tibial nerve as it passes through the tarsal tunnel on the inside of the ankle. It's similar to carpal tunnel syndrome in the wrist.1

Symptoms

  • Burning, tingling in sole of foot
  • Numbness in foot
  • Sharp, shooting pain
  • Pain radiates to arch, heel, toes
  • Worse with prolonged standing/walking
  • May be worse at night

Causes2

  • Flat feet (overpronation)
  • Ankle injury or swelling
  • Varicose veins
  • Ganglion cyst
  • Diabetes
  • Arthritis
  • Repetitive stress

Diagnosis

  • Tinel's sign at tarsal tunnel
  • Clinical examination
  • Nerve conduction studies/EMG
  • MRI to identify compression cause

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Rest and ice
  • Supportive footwear
  • Custom orthotics
  • NSAIDs
  • Physical therapy

Medical

  • Corticosteroid injections
  • Neuropathic medications

Surgery

  • Tarsal tunnel release
  • Remove compressing mass
