Hand Arthritis

Managing finger and thumb joint pain

What Is Hand Arthritis?

Hand osteoarthritis commonly affects the finger joints and thumb base. It causes pain, stiffness, and can significantly impact daily activities. It's more common in women and tends to run in families.1

Common Locations2

  • DIP joints - Fingertips (Heberden's nodes)
  • PIP joints - Middle knuckles (Bouchard's nodes)
  • Thumb base - CMC joint (very common)
  • Wrist - Less common in OA

Symptoms

  • Joint pain and aching
  • Morning stiffness
  • Bony enlargements (nodes)
  • Reduced grip strength
  • Difficulty with fine motor tasks
  • Warmth and swelling during flares

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Hand exercises
  • Heat therapy
  • Splints (especially for thumb)
  • Joint protection techniques
  • Topical NSAIDs
  • Oral pain medications

Injections

  • Corticosteroid injections (thumb base)

Surgery (Rarely Needed)

  • Joint fusion
  • Joint replacement
  • Thumb base procedures

Joint Protection Tips

  • Use larger joints when possible
  • Avoid prolonged gripping
  • Use built-up handles on tools
  • Open jars with palm, not fingers
  • Use ergonomic devices
