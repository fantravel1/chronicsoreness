Frozen Shoulder

Adhesive capsulitis - progressive shoulder stiffness

What Is Frozen Shoulder?

Frozen shoulder (adhesive capsulitis) is a condition causing pain and progressive stiffness in the shoulder joint. The joint capsule becomes thickened and tight, with adhesions forming. It typically follows a predictable course over 1-3 years.1

Three Stages

  • Freezing (2-9 months) - Gradual onset of pain, increasing stiffness
  • Frozen (4-12 months) - Pain may decrease, stiffness peaks
  • Thawing (5-24 months) - Gradual return of motion

Symptoms2

  • Progressive loss of motion (all directions)
  • Pain, especially at night
  • Unable to reach behind back
  • Difficulty with daily activities
  • Both active and passive motion limited

Risk Factors

  • Diabetes (2-4x higher risk)
  • Thyroid disorders
  • Age 40-60
  • Female sex
  • Immobilization (after surgery, injury)
  • Other shoulder conditions

Diagnosis

  • Clinical examination (restricted active and passive ROM)
  • X-ray to rule out other causes
  • MRI can show thickened capsule

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Physical therapy - stretching within pain tolerance
  • NSAIDs for pain
  • Heat before exercise
  • Corticosteroid injections (especially in freezing stage)

Interventional

  • Hydrodilatation (injecting fluid to stretch capsule)
  • Manipulation under anesthesia
  • Arthroscopic capsular release

Prognosis

Most cases resolve eventually, but may take 1-3 years. Some people have persistent mild stiffness. Diabetes cases may take longer and be more resistant to treatment.
