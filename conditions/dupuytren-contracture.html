Dupuytren's Contracture

Progressive hand condition causing finger curling

What Is Dupuytren's Contracture?

Dupuytren's contracture is a hand condition where thickening of tissue under the palm gradually causes fingers to curl inward. It typically progresses slowly over years and most often affects the ring and little fingers.1

Symptoms and Progression

  • Nodules or lumps in palm
  • Pits or dimples in skin
  • Thick cords under skin
  • Gradual finger bending
  • Inability to flatten hand
  • Usually not painful

Risk Factors2

  • Northern European ancestry
  • Family history
  • Male sex (more common, more severe)
  • Age over 50
  • Diabetes
  • Smoking
  • Alcohol use
  • Epilepsy medications

When to Treat

Treatment is considered when:

  • Can't lay hand flat on table (tabletop test)
  • Contracture >30 degrees at MCP joint
  • Any contracture at PIP joint
  • Interfering with function

Treatment Options3

Collagenase Injection (Xiaflex)

  • Enzyme dissolves cord
  • Office procedure
  • Extension manipulation next day

Needle Aponeurotomy

  • Needle breaks up cord
  • Minimally invasive
  • Quick recovery

Surgery

  • Fasciectomy (cord removal)
  • For severe or recurrent cases
  • Longer recovery
