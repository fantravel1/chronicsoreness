Chronic Fatigue Syndrome

Understanding ME/CFS

What Is ME/CFS?

Myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome (ME/CFS) is a complex, chronic illness characterized by profound fatigue that is not improved by rest and worsens with activity. It often includes widespread pain, cognitive difficulties, and other symptoms. It affects an estimated 1-2.5 million Americans and can be severely disabling.1

Core Symptoms2

Required for Diagnosis

  • Substantial reduction in activity lasting 6+ months
  • Profound fatigue not relieved by rest
  • Post-exertional malaise (PEM)
  • Unrefreshing sleep

Plus One or Both

  • Cognitive impairment ("brain fog")
  • Orthostatic intolerance (symptoms worse when upright)

Post-Exertional Malaise (PEM)

  • Hallmark symptom of ME/CFS
  • Symptom worsening after physical/mental activity
  • May be delayed 24-72 hours
  • Can last days to weeks
  • Distinguishes from other fatigue

Other Common Symptoms

  • Muscle pain
  • Joint pain (without swelling)
  • Headaches
  • Sore throat
  • Tender lymph nodes
  • Sensitivities (light, sound, foods)
  • Temperature dysregulation

Diagnosis Challenges3

  • No specific diagnostic test
  • Diagnosis of exclusion
  • Often takes years to diagnose
  • Frequently misunderstood
  • Many doctors unfamiliar with condition

Management Approaches

Pacing

  • Activity management essential
  • Stay within "energy envelope"
  • Avoid boom-bust cycle
  • Rest before exhaustion

Sleep

  • Sleep hygiene important
  • May need medication for sleep
  • Even with sleep, still unrefreshed

Pain Management

  • Similar to fibromyalgia approaches
  • Low-dose medications
  • Gentle therapies

Orthostatic Intolerance

  • Increased fluid and salt (if appropriate)
  • Compression garments
  • Medications if needed

Important Cautions

  • Graded exercise therapy may harm some patients
  • Pushing through can worsen condition
  • Rest is not laziness
  • Condition is biological, not psychological

Living with ME/CFS

  • Pacing is fundamental
  • Adjust expectations
  • Connect with support groups
  • Advocate for yourself
  • Consider disability if needed
