Chronic Fatigue Syndrome

Understanding ME/CFS

What Is ME/CFS?

Myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome (ME/CFS) is a complex, chronic illness characterized by profound fatigue that is not improved by rest and worsens after physical or mental exertion. It significantly impacts daily function and quality of life.1

Core Symptoms2

Required for Diagnosis

  • Fatigue: Substantial reduction in activity lasting 6+ months, not lifelong
  • Post-exertional malaise (PEM): Symptoms worsen after physical or mental effort
  • Unrefreshing sleep: Sleep doesn't restore energy

Plus One of These

  • Cognitive impairment ("brain fog")
  • Orthostatic intolerance (symptoms when upright)

Other Common Symptoms

  • Pain (muscles, joints, headaches)
  • Sore throat
  • Tender lymph nodes
  • Sensitivities (light, sound, chemicals)
  • Temperature dysregulation
  • Digestive problems

Post-Exertional Malaise (PEM)

  • Hallmark symptom of ME/CFS
  • Crash after exertion (physical or mental)
  • May be delayed 24-72 hours
  • Can last days to weeks
  • Key to management is avoiding triggers

Diagnosis3

  • No single diagnostic test
  • Based on symptom criteria
  • Exclusion of other conditions
  • May take time to diagnose
  • Often misdiagnosed or dismissed

Management Strategies

Pacing (Most Important)

  • Stay within "energy envelope"
  • Avoid boom-bust cycles
  • Rest before exhaustion
  • Use heart rate monitoring if helpful

Symptom Management

  • Treat sleep problems
  • Pain management
  • Treat orthostatic intolerance
  • Cognitive strategies

Important Warnings

  • Graded exercise therapy (GET) can be harmful
  • Pushing through worsens condition
  • This is a physical illness, not psychological
  • Severity varies from mild to severe

Finding Care

  • Find a knowledgeable provider
  • ME/CFS clinics exist in some areas
  • Patient advocacy groups can help
  • Telehealth may be necessary for severe cases
