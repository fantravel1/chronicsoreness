Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (ME/CFS)

More than just being tired

What Is ME/CFS?

Myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome (ME/CFS) is a serious, long-term illness characterized by profound fatigue that isn't improved by rest and worsens with activity. It affects multiple body systems and can be severely disabling.1

Core Symptoms2

  • Profound fatigue - Not ordinary tiredness
  • Post-exertional malaise (PEM) - Symptoms worsen after physical or mental exertion
  • Unrefreshing sleep - Despite sleeping, not feeling rested
  • Cognitive difficulties - "Brain fog," memory, concentration problems

Additional Symptoms

  • Orthostatic intolerance (dizziness on standing)
  • Pain (muscle, joint, headache)
  • Sensitivity to light, sound, odors
  • Flu-like symptoms
  • Sore throat, tender lymph nodes

Severity Levels

  • Mild - Can work with modifications
  • Moderate - Reduced mobility, can't work
  • Severe - Housebound
  • Very severe - Bedbound

Management3

  • Pacing - Activity management to avoid PEM
  • Energy envelope management
  • Symptom treatment (pain, sleep, orthostatic intolerance)
  • Rest before reaching limits
  • Avoid overexertion

Important Notes

  • Graded exercise therapy is controversial and may worsen symptoms
  • This is a physical, not psychological, illness
  • Activity limits must be respected
