Neck Pain

Understanding chronic cervical spine problems

About Neck Pain

Neck pain is extremely common, affecting up to 70% of people at some point. While most episodes resolve, some develop chronic pain. The cervical spine supports the head, allows movement, and protects the spinal cord.1

Common Causes2

Mechanical/Degenerative

  • Cervical spondylosis (degenerative changes)
  • Facet joint arthritis
  • Disc degeneration
  • Muscle strain
  • Poor posture

Disc-Related

  • Herniated disc
  • Cervical radiculopathy (pinched nerve)

Other

  • Whiplash
  • Cervical stenosis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Fibromyalgia

Types of Neck Pain

  • Axial pain - Stays in neck region
  • Radicular pain - Radiates to arm (nerve root)
  • Myelopathy - Spinal cord compression (serious)

Red Flags

  • Weakness in arms or legs
  • Balance/coordination problems
  • Bowel/bladder changes
  • Severe or worsening symptoms
  • Fever with neck pain

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Physical therapy
  • Posture correction
  • Ergonomic modifications
  • NSAIDs, muscle relaxants
  • Heat
  • Staying active (not bed rest)

Interventional

  • Cervical epidural injection
  • Facet injections
  • Medial branch blocks/RFA

Surgery

  • For significant nerve compression
  • Myelopathy (spinal cord compression)
