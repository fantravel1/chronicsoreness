Arachnoiditis

Inflammation of the spinal membranes

What Is Arachnoiditis?

Arachnoiditis is a chronic condition caused by inflammation of the arachnoid membrane, one of the protective layers surrounding the spinal cord and nerves. The inflammation can cause the nerves to stick together and malfunction, leading to chronic pain and neurological symptoms. It's a challenging condition to treat.1

Causes2

  • Spinal surgery complications
  • Epidural injections or spinal procedures
  • Spinal infections (meningitis)
  • Spinal trauma
  • Chemical irritation (contrast dyes historically)
  • Chronic compression (disc herniation)
  • Sometimes cause unknown

Symptoms

  • Chronic, burning back and leg pain
  • Tingling, numbness in legs
  • Electric shock sensations
  • Muscle cramps and spasms
  • Bladder and bowel dysfunction
  • Sexual dysfunction
  • Weakness in legs
  • Symptoms often progressive

Diagnosis3

  • MRI (shows clumped nerve roots)
  • Clinical history and symptoms
  • May show "empty sac" appearance on MRI
  • Diagnosis can be challenging

Treatment Challenges

  • No cure currently
  • Treatment focuses on symptom management
  • Additional spinal procedures may worsen condition
  • Multidisciplinary approach needed

Management Options

Medications

  • Nerve pain medications (gabapentin, pregabalin)
  • Antidepressants for pain
  • Muscle relaxants
  • Opioids (controversial, carefully managed)

Other Treatments

  • Physical therapy
  • TENS
  • Spinal cord stimulation (some patients)
  • Pain psychology

Living with Arachnoiditis

  • Find experienced pain specialist
  • Pace activities carefully
  • Focus on function optimization
  • Psychological support important
  • Connect with others who understand

Cautions

  • Be cautious about additional spinal procedures
  • Discuss risks carefully with doctors
  • Some procedures may make worse
