Arachnoiditis

Inflammation of the spinal membrane surrounding nerves

What Is Arachnoiditis?

Arachnoiditis is a chronic pain condition caused by inflammation of the arachnoid, one of the membranes surrounding the spinal cord and nerve roots. The inflammation can cause scarring and nerve clumping, leading to chronic pain and neurological symptoms.1

Causes

  • Spinal surgery (most common currently)
  • Multiple spinal procedures
  • Epidural injections (rare)
  • Spinal infections (meningitis)
  • Trauma to spine
  • Contrast dyes (historical - improved formulations)

Symptoms2

  • Chronic, severe back and leg pain
  • Burning, stinging sensations
  • Numbness and tingling
  • Muscle cramps and spasms
  • Bladder/bowel dysfunction (some cases)
  • Sexual dysfunction
  • Symptoms often progressive

Diagnosis

  • MRI - Shows clumped nerve roots
  • Clinical history
  • CT myelogram
  • May take time to diagnose

Treatment3

There is no cure for arachnoiditis. Treatment focuses on managing symptoms:

Pain Management

  • Medications (nerve pain medications, anti-inflammatories)
  • Spinal cord stimulation
  • TENS
  • Intrathecal drug delivery (in severe cases)

Rehabilitation

  • Physical therapy
  • Aquatic therapy
  • Pain psychology

Prognosis

  • Chronic condition
  • May stabilize or progress slowly
  • Quality of life focus important
  • Multidisciplinary care recommended
