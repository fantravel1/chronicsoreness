Arachnoiditis

Spinal membrane inflammation

What Is Arachnoiditis?

Arachnoiditis is inflammation of the arachnoid membrane, one of the protective layers surrounding the spinal cord and nerve roots. This inflammation can cause scarring that leads the nerve roots to clump together, resulting in chronic and often severe pain. It's considered a challenging condition to treat.1

Causes2

  • Spinal surgery complications
  • Previous spinal injections (rare now)
  • Spinal infections (meningitis)
  • Trauma to spine
  • Myelograms with older contrast agents
  • Multiple lumbar punctures
  • Sometimes unknown cause

Symptoms

  • Chronic, persistent pain
  • Burning, stinging leg pain
  • Numbness and tingling
  • Muscle cramping and spasms
  • Bladder and bowel dysfunction
  • Sexual dysfunction
  • Weakness in legs
  • Symptoms may worsen over time

Types

  • Adhesive arachnoiditis (most common)
  • Ossifying arachnoiditis (bone formation)
  • Severity varies widely

Diagnosis3

  • MRI is main diagnostic tool
  • Shows nerve root clumping
  • Empty dural sac appearance
  • Clinical symptoms important
  • Exclusion of other causes

Treatment Challenges

  • No cure currently exists
  • Focus on symptom management
  • Multimodal approach needed
  • Avoid further spinal procedures if possible

Treatment Options

Medications

  • Neuropathic pain medications
  • Anti-inflammatories
  • Muscle relaxants
  • Sometimes opioids

Physical Approaches

  • Physical therapy
  • Pool therapy
  • Gentle movement
  • TENS

Interventional (with caution)

  • Spinal cord stimulation (carefully considered)
  • Intrathecal pumps (some cases)
  • Further procedures controversial

Living with Arachnoiditis

  • Pace activities
  • Gentle, regular movement
  • Psychological support important
  • Connect with support groups
  • Multi-disciplinary care team
