Arachnoiditis

Spinal membrane inflammation

What Is Arachnoiditis?

Arachnoiditis is a painful condition caused by inflammation of the arachnoid, one of the membranes (meninges) surrounding the spinal cord. This inflammation can lead to scar tissue formation and nerve damage, causing chronic pain and neurological symptoms.1

Causes2

  • Spinal surgery complications
  • Spinal injections (rare complication)
  • Spinal infections (meningitis)
  • Spinal injuries
  • Dye used in myelograms (historical)
  • Sometimes unknown cause

Symptoms

  • Chronic, burning lower back pain
  • Pain radiating down legs
  • Tingling, numbness, or weakness in legs
  • Bizarre sensations (crawling, water dripping)
  • Muscle cramps and spasms
  • Bladder, bowel, or sexual dysfunction
  • Symptoms often worse with activity

Diagnosis

  • MRI with contrast (best test)
  • CT myelogram
  • Clinical history and examination
  • May show clumped nerve roots

Treatment3

There is no cure for arachnoiditis. Treatment focuses on pain management and maintaining function.

Pain Management

  • Medications for neuropathic pain
  • Opioids (carefully monitored)
  • Muscle relaxants
  • Anti-inflammatory medications

Physical Approaches

  • Physical therapy
  • Water therapy
  • Gentle exercise as tolerated

Other Options

  • TENS
  • Spinal cord stimulation
  • Pain psychology
  • Intrathecal pump (severe cases)

Living with Arachnoiditis

  • Pace activities
  • Use assistive devices as needed
  • Stay as active as possible
  • Seek psychological support
  • Connect with support groups
