Hypermobility Spectrum Disorder

When flexible joints cause problems

What Is HSD?

Hypermobility spectrum disorder (HSD) describes symptomatic joint hypermobility that doesn't meet the criteria for hypermobile Ehlers-Danlos syndrome (hEDS). It can cause significant pain and functional problems despite being considered "less severe."1

Understanding Hypermobility

  • Joints move beyond normal range
  • Often runs in families
  • More common in women
  • Can be localized or generalized
  • Not everyone with hypermobility has problems

Symptoms

  • Chronic pain (joints, muscles)
  • Easy joint subluxations/dislocations
  • Fatigue
  • Poor proprioception (body awareness)
  • Soft tissue injuries
  • Possible dysautonomia symptoms

Management3

  • Physical therapy (strengthening focus)
  • Low-impact exercise
  • Proprioception training
  • Joint protection strategies
  • Pacing activities
  • Pain management
  • Supportive bracing when needed

Exercise Tips

  • Strengthen muscles to support joints
  • Avoid end-range stretching
  • Low-impact cardio (swimming, cycling)
  • Focus on control, not flexibility
