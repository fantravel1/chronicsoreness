Facet Joint Syndrome

When the spine's small joints cause pain

What Is Facet Joint Syndrome?

Facet joint syndrome occurs when the small joints at the back of the spine (facet or zygapophyseal joints) become painful. These joints allow spinal movement and can develop arthritis or become irritated, causing localized back or neck pain.1

Where It Occurs

  • Cervical - Neck, can refer to head/shoulders
  • Thoracic - Mid-back (less common)
  • Lumbar - Lower back, can refer to buttocks/thighs

Symptoms2

  • Localized spine pain
  • Pain worse with extension (bending backward)
  • Pain with twisting
  • Morning stiffness
  • Tenderness over joints
  • Referred pain (doesn't follow nerve patterns)
  • Pain eased by rest

Causes

  • Age-related degeneration
  • Previous spinal injury
  • Disc degeneration (increases facet load)
  • Poor posture
  • Repetitive stress
  • Obesity

Diagnosis

  • Clinical examination
  • Diagnostic nerve blocks (most definitive)
  • Imaging may show arthritis but doesn't confirm pain source

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Physical therapy
  • Anti-inflammatory medications
  • Heat therapy
  • Posture correction
  • Core strengthening

Interventional

  • Facet joint injections
  • Medial branch blocks
  • Radiofrequency ablation (for longer relief)
