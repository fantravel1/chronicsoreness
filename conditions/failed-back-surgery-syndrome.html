Failed Back Surgery Syndrome

When spine surgery doesn't relieve pain

What Is Failed Back Surgery Syndrome?

Failed back surgery syndrome (FBSS), also called post-laminectomy syndrome, refers to persistent or recurring pain following spinal surgery. It occurs in 10-40% of patients who undergo spine surgery.1

Possible Causes2

  • Wrong surgical diagnosis originally
  • Surgery at wrong level
  • Incomplete decompression
  • Recurrent disc herniation
  • Epidural fibrosis (scar tissue)
  • Spinal instability
  • Adjacent segment disease
  • Hardware problems
  • Central sensitization

Symptoms

  • Persistent back or leg pain
  • New pain in different location
  • Pain may be different quality
  • Numbness, tingling
  • Limited function

Evaluation

  • Detailed history (was there any relief?)
  • Physical examination
  • MRI (with gadolinium to see scar vs disc)
  • CT scan
  • Diagnostic injections

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Physical therapy
  • Pain rehabilitation program
  • Medications (neuropathic agents)
  • Psychological support

Interventional

  • Epidural steroid injections
  • Spinal cord stimulation (SCS)
  • Intrathecal drug delivery

Repeat Surgery

  • Only if clear structural problem
  • Lower success rates than first surgery
  • Careful patient selection
