Intercostal Neuralgia

Nerve pain along the ribs and chest wall

What Is Intercostal Neuralgia?

Intercostal neuralgia is neuropathic pain involving the intercostal nerves that run between the ribs. It causes pain along the chest wall that can wrap around from the back to the front.1

Symptoms

  • Sharp, stabbing, or burning pain
  • Pain follows rib distribution
  • May wrap from back to front
  • Worse with breathing, coughing, movement
  • Tenderness along the rib
  • Numbness or tingling

Causes2

  • Shingles (postherpetic neuralgia)
  • Thoracic surgery
  • Rib fractures
  • Chest trauma
  • Pregnancy
  • Tumors
  • Nerve entrapment

Diagnosis

  • Clinical history and examination
  • Rule out cardiac and pulmonary causes
  • Chest X-ray
  • MRI if needed
  • Diagnostic nerve block

Treatment3

Medications

  • NSAIDs
  • Neuropathic agents (gabapentin, pregabalin)
  • Topical lidocaine or capsaicin
  • Antidepressants

Interventional

  • Intercostal nerve blocks
  • Radiofrequency ablation
  • Thoracic epidural
