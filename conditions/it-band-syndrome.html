IT Band Syndrome

A common cause of lateral knee pain in athletes

What Is IT Band Syndrome?

Iliotibial band syndrome (ITBS) is an overuse injury causing pain on the outer side of the knee. The IT band is a thick band of tissue running from the hip to below the knee, and irritation occurs where it crosses the knee joint.1

Symptoms

  • Sharp or burning pain on outer knee
  • Pain typically worse with running/cycling
  • Pain often starts after certain distance/time
  • Worse going downhill or down stairs
  • May feel snapping over knee
  • Tender to touch at outer knee

Risk Factors2

  • Running (esp. distance)
  • Cycling
  • Sudden increase in training
  • Running on banked surfaces
  • Weak hip abductors
  • Leg length discrepancy
  • Excessive foot pronation

Diagnosis

  • Clinical examination
  • Noble compression test
  • Ober test for IT band tightness
  • MRI if diagnosis unclear

Treatment3

Acute Phase

  • Reduce or stop aggravating activity
  • Ice
  • NSAIDs

Rehabilitation

  • Hip abductor strengthening (key!)
  • Glute strengthening
  • Foam rolling (some benefit)
  • IT band stretching
  • Gait analysis/modification

Other Options

  • Corticosteroid injection
  • PRP
  • Surgery (rare, last resort)
