Osteonecrosis

Bone death from disrupted blood supply

What Is Osteonecrosis?

Osteonecrosis, also called avascular necrosis (AVN), occurs when blood flow to bone is disrupted, causing bone tissue to die. Without treatment, the bone can collapse. The hip is most commonly affected.1

Symptoms

  • Deep, aching joint pain
  • May have no symptoms early on
  • Pain increases with weight-bearing
  • Limited range of motion
  • Pain may radiate (hip to knee)
  • Collapse causes sudden worsening

Risk Factors2

  • Corticosteroid use (high dose, long term)
  • Excessive alcohol use
  • Trauma (hip fracture, dislocation)
  • Blood disorders (sickle cell)
  • Lupus and other autoimmune diseases
  • Radiation therapy
  • Gaucher's disease

Common Sites

  • Hip (femoral head) - most common
  • Knee
  • Shoulder
  • Ankle
  • Multiple joints possible

Staging

  • Stage 0-I: Early changes, possible reversal
  • Stage II: Bone changes visible on imaging
  • Stage III: Subchondral fracture (crescent sign)
  • Stage IV: Collapse and arthritis

Treatment3

Non-Surgical (Early Stage)

  • Reduce weight-bearing
  • Pain medications
  • Physical therapy
  • Address underlying cause

Surgical

  • Core decompression
  • Bone grafting
  • Osteotomy
  • Total joint replacement (late stage)
