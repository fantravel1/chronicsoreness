Osteonecrosis

Bone death from blood supply loss

What Is Osteonecrosis?

Osteonecrosis (also called avascular necrosis or AVN) occurs when bone tissue dies due to lack of blood supply. Without blood, the bone weakens and may collapse. It most commonly affects the hip but can occur in any bone.1

Common Sites

  • Hip (most common)
  • Knee
  • Shoulder
  • Ankle
  • Jaw (medication-related)

Symptoms2

  • May have no symptoms early on
  • Pain with activity initially
  • Progressive joint pain
  • Pain at rest as it worsens
  • Limited range of motion
  • If collapse occurs, sudden severe pain

Causes and Risk Factors

  • Trauma (fractures, dislocations)
  • Corticosteroid use (high doses)
  • Excessive alcohol use
  • Blood clotting disorders
  • Sickle cell disease
  • Cancer treatments
  • Sometimes unknown cause

Stages

  • Stage 0: No symptoms, normal imaging
  • Stage 1: Normal X-ray, abnormal MRI
  • Stage 2: Changes visible on X-ray
  • Stage 3: Bone starts to flatten
  • Stage 4: Joint collapse, arthritis

Treatment3

Non-Surgical

  • Pain medications
  • Reduced weight bearing
  • Physical therapy
  • Blood thinners (some cases)
  • Bisphosphonates (research ongoing)

Surgical Options

  • Core decompression (early stages)
  • Bone grafting
  • Osteotomy (reshaping bone)
  • Joint replacement (advanced cases)

Early Detection Key

Early diagnosis and treatment may prevent bone collapse and delay or avoid joint replacement.
