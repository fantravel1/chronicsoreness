Sciatica

Pain along the sciatic nerve

What Is Sciatica?

Sciatica refers to pain that radiates along the path of the sciatic nerveâ€”from the lower back through the hip and buttock and down each leg. It's actually a symptom of an underlying condition, not a diagnosis itself. Most cases resolve with conservative treatment.1

The Sciatic Nerve

  • Largest nerve in the body
  • Formed from nerve roots in lower spine (L4-S3)
  • Runs from lower back through buttock down leg
  • Controls muscles and provides sensation

Symptoms2

  • Pain radiating from lower back to buttock and down leg
  • Usually affects one side
  • Sharp, burning, or electric shock sensation
  • Numbness or tingling in leg or foot
  • Weakness in affected leg
  • Pain worse with sitting, coughing, sneezing

Common Causes

  • Herniated lumbar disc (most common)
  • Spinal stenosis
  • Piriformis syndrome
  • Degenerative disc disease
  • Spondylolisthesis

When to Seek Urgent Care

  • Loss of bladder or bowel control
  • Progressive weakness
  • Numbness in groin/saddle area
  • Severe, worsening symptoms
  • After significant trauma

Diagnosis3

  • Physical examination
  • Straight leg raise test
  • MRI if symptoms persist or severe
  • CT scan or X-rays in some cases
  • EMG/nerve conduction studies if needed

Treatment

Self-Care

  • Stay active (gentle movement)
  • Ice or heat
  • Over-the-counter pain relievers
  • Gentle stretching

Medical Treatment

  • Physical therapy
  • Prescription medications
  • Epidural steroid injections
  • Surgery (for severe or persistent cases)

Prognosis

  • Most improve within 4-6 weeks
  • About 80-90% recover without surgery
  • Staying active aids recovery
  • Some have recurrent episodes

Prevention

  • Regular exercise
  • Core strengthening
  • Good posture
  • Proper lifting mechanics
  • Maintain healthy weight
