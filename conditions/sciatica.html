Sciatica

Pain radiating along the sciatic nerve pathway

What Is Sciatica?

Sciatica refers to pain that radiates along the path of the sciatic nerve, from the lower back through the hip and buttock and down the leg. It typically affects one side and results from irritation or compression of the sciatic nerve roots.1

Causes

  • Herniated lumbar disc (most common)
  • Spinal stenosis
  • Piriformis syndrome
  • Spondylolisthesis
  • Bone spurs
  • Tumors (rare)

Symptoms2

  • Pain from low back to buttock to leg
  • Can extend to foot/toes
  • Usually one-sided
  • Burning, sharp, or electric quality
  • Numbness or tingling
  • Muscle weakness
  • Worse with coughing, sneezing, prolonged sitting

Red Flags - Seek Immediate Care

  • Loss of bladder/bowel control
  • Saddle numbness
  • Progressive weakness
  • Severe, worsening symptoms

Diagnosis

  • Physical exam (straight leg raise test)
  • MRI if symptoms persist or red flags
  • EMG/nerve conduction studies

Treatment3

Self-Care

  • Stay active (avoid prolonged rest)
  • Ice or heat
  • OTC pain medications
  • Gentle stretching

Medical Treatment

  • Physical therapy
  • Prescription medications
  • Epidural steroid injections
  • Surgery if conservative treatment fails

Prognosis

Most cases resolve within 4-6 weeks with conservative treatment. About 80-90% of people improve without surgery.
