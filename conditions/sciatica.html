Sciatica

Understanding sciatic nerve pain

What Is Sciatica?

Sciatica refers to pain that radiates along the path of the sciatic nerveâ€”from the lower back through the buttocks and down each leg. It's usually caused by compression of a nerve root in the lumbar spine. While it can be intensely painful, most cases resolve with conservative treatment within a few weeks to months.1

The Sciatic Nerve

  • Largest nerve in the body
  • Formed from nerve roots L4-S3
  • Runs from lower back to feet
  • Usually only affects one side

Causes2

  • Herniated disc (most common)
  • Spinal stenosis
  • Bone spurs
  • Spondylolisthesis
  • Piriformis syndrome
  • Tumor (rare)
  • Infection (rare)

Symptoms3

  • Pain from low back through buttock to leg
  • Sharp, shooting, burning pain
  • Numbness or tingling
  • Weakness in affected leg
  • Pain worse with sitting
  • Pain with coughing/sneezing
  • Usually one-sided

Red Flagsâ€”Seek Immediate Care

  • Loss of bladder/bowel control
  • Severe or progressive weakness
  • Numbness in groin/saddle area
  • Fever with back pain
  • Pain after major trauma

Diagnosis

  • Clinical history and exam
  • Straight leg raise test
  • MRI if symptoms persist or severe
  • EMG in select cases

Treatment

Self-Care

  • Stay moderately active
  • Ice or heat
  • OTC pain medications
  • Gentle stretching

Medical Treatment

  • NSAIDs
  • Muscle relaxants (short-term)
  • Oral steroids (short course)
  • Physical therapy
  • Epidural steroid injections

Surgery

  • Microdiscectomy
  • For severe, persistent cases
  • Progressive neurological deficit
  • Cauda equina syndrome (emergency)

Prognosis

  • Most improve within 4-6 weeks
  • Many resolve within 3 months
  • Some become chronic
  • Recurrence possible

Prevention

  • Core strengthening
  • Good posture
  • Proper lifting technique
  • Regular exercise
  • Healthy weight
