Lumbar Radiculopathy

Pinched nerve in the lower back

What Is Lumbar Radiculopathy?

Lumbar radiculopathy occurs when a nerve root in the lower back is compressed or inflamed, causing pain, numbness, or weakness that radiates down the leg. Sciatica is a type of lumbar radiculopathy affecting the sciatic nerve distribution.1

Causes2

  • Herniated lumbar disc (most common)
  • Lumbar spinal stenosis
  • Degenerative changes
  • Spondylolisthesis
  • Less common: tumors, infections, cysts

Symptoms

  • Pain radiating from back down leg
  • Sharp, shooting, or burning pain
  • Numbness or tingling in leg or foot
  • Weakness in leg muscles
  • Pain worse with sitting, coughing, sneezing
  • Usually affects one side

Nerve Root Patterns

  • L4: Front of thigh, inner lower leg
  • L5: Outer thigh, top of foot, big toe
  • S1: Back of thigh, outer foot, small toes

Red Flags (Seek Immediate Care)

  • Loss of bladder or bowel control
  • Numbness in groin/saddle area
  • Progressive weakness in legs
  • These may indicate cauda equina syndrome

Diagnosis3

  • Physical examination
  • Straight leg raise test
  • MRI (preferred imaging)
  • CT scan
  • EMG if diagnosis uncertain

Treatment

Conservative (First Line)

  • Activity as tolerated
  • Pain medications
  • Physical therapy
  • Epidural steroid injections

Surgery

  • Considered for severe or persistent cases
  • Microdiscectomy most common
  • Better for leg pain than back pain

Prognosis

  • Most improve within 6-12 weeks
  • About 80-90% recover without surgery
  • Leg pain often improves more than back pain
