Lumbar Radiculopathy

Sciatica and lower back nerve compression

What Is Lumbar Radiculopathy?

Lumbar radiculopathy occurs when a nerve root in the lower back is compressed, causing pain, numbness, or weakness that radiates down the leg. When it affects the sciatic nerve, it's commonly called sciatica.1

Common Causes

  • Herniated disc (most common)
  • Spinal stenosis
  • Degenerative disc disease
  • Spondylolisthesis
  • Bone spurs

Symptoms by Level2

  • L3 - Front of thigh pain, knee weakness
  • L4 - Inner shin pain, knee extension weakness
  • L5 - Outer leg/top of foot pain, foot drop (most common)
  • S1 - Back of leg/outer foot pain, ankle weakness

Red Flags (Seek Immediate Care)

  • Bladder or bowel dysfunction
  • Saddle numbness
  • Progressive weakness
  • Bilateral symptoms

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Activity modification (not bed rest)
  • Physical therapy
  • Pain medications
  • Epidural steroid injections

Surgery

  • Microdiscectomy (for disc herniations)
  • Laminectomy (for stenosis)
  • Usually reserved for failed conservative care

Prognosis

Most cases improve within 6-12 weeks with conservative treatment. The body often reabsorbs disc material over time.
