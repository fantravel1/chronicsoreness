Hip Bursitis

Trochanteric bursitis and lateral hip pain

What Is Hip Bursitis?

Hip bursitis, more accurately called greater trochanteric pain syndrome (GTPS), is a common cause of lateral hip pain. It involves inflammation of the bursa (a fluid-filled sac) over the bony prominence on the outside of the hip, along with possible tendon irritation. It affects about 10-25% of the general population.1

Symptoms2

  • Pain on the outer hip
  • Pain may radiate down the thigh
  • Worse when lying on affected side
  • Pain with walking, stairs, standing from sitting
  • Tenderness over hip bone
  • May disturb sleep

Causes and Risk Factors

  • Repetitive activities (walking, running, cycling)
  • Hip muscle weakness
  • Leg length differences
  • Hip or spine arthritis
  • Previous hip surgery
  • Obesity
  • More common in women

Diagnosis3

  • Physical examination
  • Tenderness over greater trochanter
  • Pain with certain movements
  • X-ray may rule out other conditions
  • MRI sometimes helpful

Treatment

Conservative (First-Line)

  • Activity modification
  • Ice application
  • NSAIDs
  • Physical therapy
  • Stretching and strengthening

Medical Treatment

  • Corticosteroid injection
  • May provide temporary relief
  • Can help with physical therapy

Physical Therapy

  • Hip strengthening (especially gluteals)
  • IT band stretching
  • Hip flexor stretching
  • Core strengthening
  • Gait analysis and correction

Self-Care

  • Sleep on opposite side with pillow between knees
  • Avoid crossing legs
  • Limit repetitive hip movements
  • Use cushion on hard chairs
  • Ice for 15-20 minutes when painful

Prognosis

  • Usually improves with conservative care
  • May take weeks to months
  • Can become chronic
  • Consistent exercises important
