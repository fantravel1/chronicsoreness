Interstitial Cystitis

Understanding bladder pain syndrome

What Is Interstitial Cystitis?

Interstitial cystitis (IC), also called bladder pain syndrome (BPS), is a chronic condition causing bladder pressure, bladder pain, and sometimes pelvic pain. The pain can range from mild discomfort to severe. It affects more women than men and can significantly impact daily life.1

Symptoms2

  • Bladder pain or pressure
  • Pain that worsens as bladder fills
  • Pain that improves after urinating
  • Urgent need to urinate (frequently)
  • Frequent urination (up to 60 times/day in severe cases)
  • Nocturia (waking to urinate)
  • Pelvic pain
  • Pain during sex

Triggers

  • Certain foods and drinks
  • Stress
  • Menstruation
  • Sexual activity
  • Prolonged sitting
  • Exercise (for some)

Common Food Triggers

  • Citrus fruits
  • Tomatoes
  • Caffeine
  • Alcohol
  • Artificial sweeteners
  • Spicy foods
  • Carbonated drinks

Diagnosis3

  • Clinical history and symptoms
  • Ruling out other conditions
  • Urinalysis (rule out infection)
  • Cystoscopy in some cases
  • Potassium sensitivity test (sometimes)
  • Often diagnosis of exclusion

Treatment Options

Lifestyle Changes

  • Dietary modifications (elimination diet)
  • Bladder training
  • Stress management
  • Heat application

Physical Therapy

  • Pelvic floor physical therapy
  • Often very helpful
  • Addresses muscle dysfunction

Oral Medications

  • Pentosan polysulfate (Elmiron)
  • Amitriptyline
  • Hydroxyzine
  • Cimetidine

Bladder Instillations

  • Medication placed directly in bladder
  • Various solutions used
  • DMSO sometimes helpful

Other Treatments

  • Nerve stimulation
  • Botox injections
  • Hydrodistention

Living with IC

  • Keep a symptom diary
  • Identify personal triggers
  • Plan around symptoms
  • Connect with support groups
  • Communicate with partners
