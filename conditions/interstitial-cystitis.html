Interstitial Cystitis

Understanding painful bladder syndrome

What Is Interstitial Cystitis?

Interstitial cystitis (IC), also called painful bladder syndrome, is a chronic condition causing bladder pressure, bladder pain, and sometimes pelvic pain. The severity ranges from mild discomfort to severe pain.1

Symptoms

  • Chronic pelvic pain
  • Persistent urge to urinate
  • Frequent urination (up to 60 times daily in severe cases)
  • Pain during bladder filling, relief after voiding
  • Pain with sexual intercourse
  • Symptoms may flare with certain foods, stress, menstruation

Causes

The exact cause is unknown. Theories include:2

  • Defect in bladder lining (epithelium)
  • Autoimmune reaction
  • Nerve dysfunction
  • Mast cell activation
  • Hereditary factors

Diagnosis

  • Based on symptoms after ruling out other conditions
  • Urinalysis and culture (to rule out infection)
  • Cystoscopy with hydrodistention (in some cases)
  • Potassium sensitivity test

Treatment

Lifestyle and Diet

  • IC diet - Avoid trigger foods (acidic, spicy, caffeine, alcohol)3
  • Bladder training
  • Stress management
  • Pelvic floor physical therapy

Oral Medications

  • Pentosan polysulfate (Elmiron) - FDA-approved for IC
  • Amitriptyline
  • Hydroxyzine
  • Cimetidine

Bladder Instillations

  • DMSO
  • Heparin/lidocaine cocktails

Procedures

  • Hydrodistention
  • Nerve stimulation
  • Botox injections
