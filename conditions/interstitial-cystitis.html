Interstitial Cystitis

Chronic bladder pain syndrome

What Is Interstitial Cystitis?

Interstitial cystitis (IC), also called painful bladder syndrome, is a chronic condition causing bladder pressure, bladder pain, and sometimes pelvic pain. It affects an estimated 1-4% of women and 0.5% of men.1

Symptoms

  • Bladder pressure and pain
  • Urgent need to urinate
  • Frequent urination (up to 60x/day in severe cases)
  • Pain during sex
  • Pelvic pain
  • Symptoms may flare and remit

Possible Causes2

  • Bladder lining defect
  • Mast cell activation
  • Nerve abnormalities
  • Autoimmune factors
  • Pelvic floor dysfunction
  • Often overlaps with fibromyalgia, IBS

Diagnosis

  • Symptoms for ≥6 weeks
  • Rule out infection and other causes
  • Cystoscopy with hydrodistension (optional)
  • Potassium sensitivity test (controversial)

Treatment3

Lifestyle Modifications

  • Identify and avoid trigger foods
  • Stress management
  • Bladder training

Oral Medications

  • Pentosan polysulfate (Elmiron)
  • Antihistamines
  • Tricyclic antidepressants

Bladder Treatments

  • Bladder instillations
  • Hydrodistension

Physical Therapy

  • Pelvic floor therapy
  • Myofascial release
