Interstitial Cystitis

Painful bladder syndrome

What Is Interstitial Cystitis?

Interstitial cystitis (IC), also called painful bladder syndrome (PBS or IC/BPS), is a chronic condition causing bladder pressure, bladder pain, and sometimes pelvic pain. It affects mostly women and can significantly impact quality of life.1

Symptoms2

  • Pelvic or bladder pain
  • Persistent urge to urinate
  • Frequent urination (often small amounts)
  • Pain during intercourse
  • Pain relieved by urination
  • Symptoms may fluctuate (flares and remissions)

Causes

  • Exact cause unknown
  • Possibly damaged bladder lining
  • Autoimmune component suspected
  • Nerve dysfunction
  • Often co-occurs with other chronic pain conditions

Diagnosis

  • Symptom history and examination
  • Urine tests to rule out infection
  • Cystoscopy (looking inside bladder)
  • Potassium sensitivity test
  • Diagnosis of exclusion

Treatment3

Lifestyle Modifications

  • Identify and avoid trigger foods
  • Bladder training
  • Stress management
  • Physical therapy for pelvic floor

Medications

  • Pentosan polysulfate (Elmiron)
  • Amitriptyline
  • Antihistamines
  • Pain medications

Bladder Treatments

  • Bladder instillations
  • Hydrodistension
  • Nerve stimulation

Dietary Triggers

Common trigger foods include:

  • Citrus fruits
  • Tomatoes
  • Coffee and tea
  • Alcohol
  • Artificial sweeteners
  • Spicy foods
