Long COVID

Persistent symptoms after COVID-19

What Is Long COVID?

Long COVID, also called Post-Acute Sequelae of SARS-CoV-2 infection (PASC), refers to new or ongoing symptoms that persist weeks to months after the initial COVID-19 infection. It can occur even after mild initial illness and significantly impacts quality of life.1

Common Symptoms2

Fatigue and Post-Exertional Malaise

  • Profound, persistent fatigue
  • Worsening symptoms after exertion
  • Similar to ME/CFS presentation

Pain Symptoms

  • Muscle and joint pain
  • Headaches
  • Chest pain
  • Nerve pain

Cognitive Symptoms

  • "Brain fog"
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Memory problems

Other Symptoms

  • Shortness of breath
  • Heart palpitations
  • Dizziness on standing
  • Sleep problems
  • Loss of smell/taste
  • Anxiety and depression

Who Develops Long COVID?

  • Can occur after any severity of illness
  • More common in women
  • May be more common in certain age groups
  • Prior conditions may increase risk
  • Vaccination may reduce risk

Diagnosis3

  • Based on history of COVID and persistent symptoms
  • No single diagnostic test
  • Rule out other conditions
  • May need various specialist evaluations

Management Approaches

Pacing

  • Critical for those with post-exertional malaise
  • Avoid pushing through symptoms
  • Balance activity and rest

Symptom Treatment

  • Address each symptom individually
  • Pain management as needed
  • Sleep support
  • Treatment for dysautonomia if present

Rehabilitation

  • Careful, paced physical therapy
  • Respiratory therapy if needed
  • Occupational therapy
  • Cognitive rehabilitation

Long COVID Clinics

  • Multidisciplinary approach
  • Growing number of specialized clinics
  • May need referral
  • Access varies by location

Research and Future

  • Active area of research
  • Multiple clinical trials ongoing
  • Mechanisms still being understood
  • Treatment options evolving
