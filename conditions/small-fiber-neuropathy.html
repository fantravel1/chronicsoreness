Small Fiber Neuropathy

When small nerve fibers are affected

What Is Small Fiber Neuropathy?

Small fiber neuropathy (SFN) affects the small nerve fibers in the skin and organs. These fibers carry pain and temperature sensations and control autonomic functions. Unlike large fiber neuropathy, SFN often doesn't show up on standard nerve conduction tests, making diagnosis challenging.1

Types of Symptoms2

Sensory Symptoms

  • Burning pain, often in feet
  • Tingling or pins and needles
  • Electric shock sensations
  • Extreme sensitivity to touch
  • Feeling of walking on pebbles
  • May spread to hands ("stocking-glove" pattern)

Autonomic Symptoms

  • Abnormal sweating
  • Dry eyes and mouth
  • Digestive problems
  • Lightheadedness on standing
  • Rapid heart rate
  • Skin color changes

Causes

  • Diabetes (most common identifiable cause)
  • Prediabetes/metabolic syndrome
  • Autoimmune conditions (SjÃ¶gren's, lupus)
  • Vitamin B12 deficiency
  • Celiac disease
  • Medications or toxins
  • Idiopathic (unknown) in about 50%

Diagnosis3

  • Clinical history and examination
  • Skin biopsy (gold standard)
  • Nerve conduction studies often normal
  • Autonomic testing
  • Workup for underlying causes

Treatment

Treating Underlying Cause

  • Blood sugar control for diabetes
  • Vitamin replacement if deficient
  • Treating autoimmune conditions
  • Eliminating toxins

Pain Management

  • Gabapentinoids (gabapentin, pregabalin)
  • SNRIs (duloxetine)
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • Topical treatments (capsaicin, lidocaine)
  • IVIG for certain causes

Managing Autonomic Symptoms

  • Compression stockings for blood pooling
  • Increased salt and fluids (if appropriate)
  • Rise slowly from sitting or lying
  • Cool environment for temperature issues

Living with SFN

  • Protect feet and hands from injury
  • Regular foot care if sensation reduced
  • Manage blood sugar carefully
  • Stay active within tolerance
  • Connect with others who understand
