Small Fiber Neuropathy

Damage to the small nerve fibers

What Is Small Fiber Neuropathy?

Small fiber neuropathy (SFN) affects the small unmyelinated and thinly myelinated nerve fibers that transmit pain, temperature, and autonomic signals. Unlike large fiber neuropathy, standard nerve tests are often normal.1

Symptoms2

Sensory

  • Burning, stinging, or prickling pain
  • Often starts in feet ("burning feet")
  • Allodynia (pain from light touch)
  • Electric shock sensations
  • Worse at night

Autonomic

  • Sweating abnormalities
  • Dizziness on standing
  • GI symptoms
  • Dry eyes and mouth
  • Urinary problems

Causes

  • Diabetes/prediabetes (most common)
  • Autoimmune diseases
  • SjÃ¶gren's syndrome
  • Vitamin B12 deficiency
  • Celiac disease
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Idiopathic (30-50%)

Diagnosis3

  • Standard EMG/nerve conduction often normal
  • Skin punch biopsy (gold standard)
  • Quantitative sensory testing
  • Autonomic testing
  • Search for underlying cause

Treatment

Treat Underlying Cause

  • Blood sugar control
  • Immunotherapy if autoimmune
  • Vitamin supplementation

Symptom Management

  • Neuropathic pain medications
  • Topical lidocaine or capsaicin
  • Low-dose naltrexone (emerging)
  • IVIG (if autoimmune)
