Scoliosis

Living with spinal curvature

What Is Scoliosis?

Scoliosis is an abnormal lateral curvature of the spine, typically forming a C or S shape. While mild scoliosis may cause no problems, more significant curves can lead to pain, breathing issues, and reduced quality of life.1

Types

  • Idiopathic - Most common, unknown cause (usually adolescent)
  • Congenital - Present at birth
  • Neuromuscular - Due to conditions like cerebral palsy
  • Degenerative - Adult-onset, from spine degeneration

Symptoms2

  • Uneven shoulders or hips
  • Visible spinal curve
  • Back pain (more common in adults)
  • Muscle fatigue
  • Breathing difficulty (severe cases)
  • Nerve symptoms (if compression)

Scoliosis and Pain

  • Mild curves often don't cause pain
  • Pain more common in adult scoliosis
  • May develop arthritis at curve
  • Muscle imbalances can cause discomfort
