Cervical Radiculopathy

When a pinched neck nerve affects your arm

What Is Cervical Radiculopathy?

Cervical radiculopathy occurs when a nerve root in the neck (cervical spine) becomes compressed or irritated. This causes pain, numbness, tingling, or weakness that radiates from the neck into the shoulder, arm, or hand. The pattern of symptoms depends on which nerve root is affected.1

Common Causes2

  • Herniated disc (younger patients)
  • Degenerative changes (older patients)
  • Bone spurs (osteophytes)
  • Foraminal stenosis
  • Trauma or injury
  • Tumors (rare)

Symptoms

  • Neck pain
  • Pain radiating to shoulder, arm, hand
  • Numbness or tingling
  • Weakness in arm or hand
  • Symptoms follow nerve distribution
  • May worsen with neck movements
  • Often one-sided

Nerve Root Patterns

  • C5: Shoulder pain, deltoid weakness
  • C6: Thumb/index finger, biceps weakness
  • C7: Middle finger, triceps weakness
  • C8: Ring/pinky finger, grip weakness

Diagnosis3

  • History and physical exam
  • Spurling test (neck compression)
  • Reflex and strength testing
  • MRI (best for soft tissue)
  • EMG/nerve conduction studies
  • CT scan if MRI not possible

Treatment Options

Conservative (First Line)

  • Activity modification
  • Physical therapy
  • NSAIDs for pain
  • Soft cervical collar (short-term)
  • Ice or heat
  • Nerve pain medications

Interventional

  • Epidural steroid injections
  • Selective nerve root blocks
  • Can provide significant relief

Surgical

  • When conservative treatment fails
  • Progressive weakness
  • Disc removal (discectomy)
  • Fusion or disc replacement

Prognosis

  • Many improve with conservative care
  • 75-90% improve without surgery
  • Recovery may take weeks to months
  • Some develop chronic symptoms

Red Flags

  • Progressive weakness
  • Bowel or bladder changes
  • Balance problems (myelopathy signs)
  • Seek urgent evaluation
