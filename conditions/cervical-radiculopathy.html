Cervical Radiculopathy

Pinched nerve in the neck

What Is Cervical Radiculopathy?

Cervical radiculopathy occurs when a nerve root in the neck becomes compressed or irritated. This causes pain, numbness, or weakness that radiates from the neck into the shoulder, arm, or hand. It's commonly called a "pinched nerve."1

Causes2

  • Herniated cervical disc
  • Cervical spondylosis (arthritis)
  • Bone spurs (osteophytes)
  • Spinal stenosis
  • Less commonly: tumors, infections

Symptoms

  • Neck pain
  • Pain radiating to shoulder, arm, or hand
  • Sharp or burning pain
  • Numbness or tingling in arm or fingers
  • Weakness in arm or hand
  • Symptoms often follow specific nerve patterns

Nerve Root Patterns

  • C5: Shoulder and upper arm pain/weakness
  • C6: Pain to thumb, weak biceps
  • C7: Pain to middle finger, weak triceps
  • C8: Pain to pinky, weak hand grip

Diagnosis3

  • Physical examination
  • Spurling's test
  • MRI (best for soft tissue)
  • CT scan
  • EMG/nerve conduction studies

Treatment

Conservative (Most Improve)

  • Activity modification
  • NSAIDs or other pain relievers
  • Physical therapy
  • Cervical collar (short-term if needed)
  • Epidural steroid injections

Surgery

  • Considered if conservative treatment fails
  • Progressive weakness or myelopathy
  • Options include discectomy, fusion

Prognosis

  • Most improve without surgery
  • Natural history generally favorable
  • Recovery may take weeks to months

When to Seek Urgent Care

  • Progressive weakness
  • Difficulty with walking or balance
  • Bladder or bowel changes
  • Signs of spinal cord compression
