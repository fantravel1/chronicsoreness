Plantar Fasciitis

Common cause of heel and foot pain

What Is Plantar Fasciitis?

Plantar fasciitis is inflammation and degeneration of the plantar fascia, a thick band of tissue that runs along the bottom of the foot connecting the heel bone to the toes. It's one of the most common causes of heel pain.1

Symptoms

  • Sharp pain in heel, especially first steps in morning
  • Pain after (not during) exercise
  • Pain after prolonged standing
  • Improves with some walking, returns after rest
  • Tenderness at heel

Risk Factors2

  • Age 40-60 most common
  • Obesity/overweight
  • Activities with repetitive impact (running)
  • Prolonged standing occupations
  • Flat feet or high arches
  • Tight calf muscles
  • Improper footwear

Diagnosis

  • Clinical history and examination
  • Tenderness at medial heel
  • Pain with dorsiflexion of toes
  • Imaging usually not needed (but can show thickening)

Treatment3

First Line

  • Stretching - Calf and plantar fascia stretches
  • Supportive footwear - Good arch support
  • Orthotics - Over-the-counter or custom
  • Ice - Rolling frozen bottle under foot
  • NSAIDs - For pain and inflammation
  • Night splints - Keep foot flexed overnight

Second Line

  • Physical therapy
  • Corticosteroid injections
  • Extracorporeal shockwave therapy
  • Platelet-rich plasma (PRP)

Surgery

  • Rarely needed (<5% of cases)
  • For severe, refractory cases

Prognosis

Most cases (80-90%) resolve within 12 months with conservative treatment. Consistency with stretching and footwear modifications is key.
