Plantar Fasciitis

The most common cause of heel pain

What Is Plantar Fasciitis?

Plantar fasciitis is inflammation of the plantar fascia, the thick band of tissue connecting your heel to your toes. It's one of the most common causes of heel pain, affecting about 10% of people at some point in their lives.1

Classic Symptoms2

  • Pain on bottom of heel
  • Worst with first steps in morning
  • Pain after prolonged sitting
  • Pain may improve with walking, then worsen
  • Tender to press on heel

Risk Factors

  • Prolonged standing
  • Obesity
  • Tight calf muscles
  • High arches or flat feet
  • Age 40-60
  • Running
  • Poor footwear

Treatment3

First-Line

  • Stretching (calf and plantar fascia)
  • Supportive footwear
  • Orthotics or heel cups
  • Ice
  • NSAIDs
  • Night splints

If Persistent

  • Physical therapy
  • Corticosteroid injection
  • Shockwave therapy
  • PRP injection

Surgery (Rarely Needed)

  • Plantar fascia release
  • Only after 6-12 months of conservative care

Key Stretches

  • Calf stretches (wall stretch)
  • Plantar fascia stretch (pull toes back)
  • Towel stretch in morning
  • Rolling foot on frozen water bottle

Prognosis

Most cases improve within 10-12 months. About 90% resolve with conservative treatment.
