Thoracic Outlet Syndrome

Compression at the neck-shoulder junction

What Is Thoracic Outlet Syndrome?

Thoracic outlet syndrome (TOS) occurs when nerves, arteries, or veins are compressed in the thoracic outletâ€”the space between your collarbone and first rib. This compression causes shoulder, arm, and hand symptoms.1

Types2

  • Neurogenic TOS - Nerve compression (most common, ~95%)
  • Venous TOS - Vein compression causing blood clots
  • Arterial TOS - Artery compression (rare but serious)

Symptoms

Neurogenic TOS

  • Pain in shoulder, arm, hand
  • Numbness and tingling in fingers
  • Weakness in hand grip
  • Aching in neck and shoulder
  • Symptoms often worse with arms raised

Vascular TOS

  • Arm swelling or discoloration
  • Blood clot symptoms
  • Cold fingers or hands
  • Weak pulse in arm

Causes

  • Anatomical abnormalities (extra rib)
  • Poor posture
  • Repetitive arm movements
  • Trauma (whiplash, fractures)
  • Pregnancy

Treatment3

Conservative Treatment

  • Physical therapy (mainstay of treatment)
  • Posture correction
  • Stretching exercises
  • Avoiding aggravating activities
  • Pain medications

Surgery

  • First rib resection
  • Scalenectomy
  • Reserved for severe or vascular cases
