Thoracic Outlet Syndrome

Compression in the neck and shoulder region

What Is Thoracic Outlet Syndrome?

Thoracic outlet syndrome (TOS) refers to a group of conditions caused by compression of nerves or blood vessels in the thoracic outletâ€”the space between the collarbone and first rib. This compression can cause pain, numbness, and weakness in the neck, shoulder, arm, and hand.1

Types of TOS2

Neurogenic TOS (Most Common - 95%)

  • Compression of brachial plexus nerves
  • Pain, numbness, tingling in arm/hand
  • Weakness in hand muscles

Venous TOS

  • Compression of subclavian vein
  • Arm swelling, discoloration
  • Blood clot risk

Arterial TOS (Rare)

  • Compression of subclavian artery
  • Cold, pale fingers
  • Weak pulse in affected arm

Causes

  • Anatomical defects (cervical rib, abnormal muscle)
  • Trauma (car accident, repetitive injury)
  • Repetitive arm movements
  • Poor posture
  • Pregnancy
  • Obesity

Symptoms3

Neurogenic Symptoms

  • Pain in neck, shoulder, arm
  • Numbness/tingling in fingers
  • Weakness in arm or hand
  • Symptoms often worse with arm raised
  • May drop things frequently

Vascular Symptoms

  • Arm swelling or discoloration
  • Cold or pale arm/hand
  • Weak arm pulse
  • Blood clot symptoms

Diagnosis

  • Physical examination
  • Provocative tests (Adson's, ROOS)
  • Nerve conduction studies
  • Imaging (X-ray, MRI, ultrasound)
  • Angiography for vascular TOS

Treatment

Conservative (First-Line)

  • Physical therapy (cornerstone)
  • Posture correction
  • Stretching exercises
  • Pain medications
  • Muscle relaxants
  • Ergonomic modifications

Surgery

  • Considered when conservative fails
  • Removal of cervical rib if present
  • First rib resection
  • Scalene muscle surgery

Self-Care

  • Maintain good posture
  • Take breaks from repetitive activities
  • Avoid carrying heavy bags on shoulder
  • Sleep positions that reduce compression
  • Weight management
