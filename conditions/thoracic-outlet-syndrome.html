Thoracic Outlet Syndrome

Compression of nerves or blood vessels at the thoracic outlet

What Is Thoracic Outlet Syndrome?

Thoracic outlet syndrome (TOS) is a group of disorders caused by compression of nerves or blood vessels in the thoracic outletâ€”the space between the collarbone and first rib. Neurogenic TOS (nerve compression) accounts for 95% of cases.1

Types

  • Neurogenic TOS - Brachial plexus compression (most common)
  • Venous TOS - Subclavian vein compression
  • Arterial TOS - Subclavian artery compression (rare)

Symptoms2

Neurogenic

  • Pain in shoulder, arm, hand
  • Numbness or tingling in arm/fingers
  • Weakness in hand
  • Hand coldness
  • Worse with overhead activities

Vascular

  • Arm swelling (venous)
  • Arm discoloration
  • Cold, pale arm (arterial)
  • Weak pulse

Causes

  • Cervical rib (extra rib)
  • Trauma (whiplash, repetitive)
  • Poor posture
  • Anatomical variations
  • Pregnancy
  • Muscle abnormalities

Treatment3

Conservative (First Line)

  • Physical therapy
  • Posture correction
  • Stretching exercises
  • Pain medications
  • Nerve gliding exercises

Surgery

  • First rib resection
  • Scalenectomy
  • Cervical rib removal
