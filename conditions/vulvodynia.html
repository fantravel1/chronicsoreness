Vulvodynia

Chronic vulvar pain

What Is Vulvodynia?

Vulvodynia is chronic vulvar pain lasting at least 3 months without an identifiable cause. It affects an estimated 8-16% of women at some point in their lives. Despite being common, it's often underdiagnosed and undertreated.1

Types2

  • Localized - Pain in specific area (vestibulodynia most common)
  • Generalized - Pain throughout vulvar region
  • Provoked - Pain with touch or pressure
  • Unprovoked - Spontaneous pain
  • Mixed - Combination

Symptoms

  • Burning sensation
  • Stinging or rawness
  • Soreness or aching
  • Pain with intercourse (dyspareunia)
  • Pain with tampon use
  • Pain with sitting or wearing tight clothing

Causes

  • Exact cause unknown
  • Nerve damage or irritation
  • Hormonal factors
  • Pelvic floor muscle dysfunction
  • History of infections
  • Genetic factors

Diagnosis

  • Medical history
  • Cotton swab test
  • Ruling out other causes
  • No specific test confirms diagnosis

Treatment3

Medications

  • Topical lidocaine
  • Topical estrogen
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • Anticonvulsants

Physical Therapy

  • Pelvic floor physical therapy
  • Biofeedback
  • Relaxation techniques

Other Treatments

  • Nerve blocks
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy
  • Surgery (vestibulectomy for vestibulodynia)

Self-Care Tips

  • Wear 100% cotton underwear
  • Avoid scented products
  • Use lubricants for intimacy
  • Rinse vulva with cool water after urination
  • Avoid tight clothing
