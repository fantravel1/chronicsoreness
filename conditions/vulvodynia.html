Vulvodynia

Understanding chronic vulvar pain

What Is Vulvodynia?

Vulvodynia is chronic pain or discomfort of the vulva (the external female genitalia) lasting at least 3 months without an identifiable cause. It affects an estimated 8-16% of women at some point in their lives.1

Types

  • Localized - Pain in a specific area (often vestibulodynia)
  • Generalized - Pain throughout the vulva
  • Provoked - Pain with touch/pressure
  • Unprovoked - Spontaneous pain
  • Mixed - Both provoked and unprovoked

Symptoms

  • Burning, stinging, or rawness
  • Aching, soreness, throbbing
  • Itching
  • Pain with intercourse (dyspareunia)
  • Pain with tampon insertion
  • Pain with sitting or wearing tight clothing

Causes

The cause is not fully understood but may involve:2

  • Nerve injury or irritation
  • Increased nerve fiber density
  • Pelvic floor muscle dysfunction
  • Hormonal changes
  • History of recurrent yeast infections
  • Genetic factors

Diagnosis

  • Medical history and symptom description
  • Physical examination
  • Cotton swab test (Q-tip test)
  • Rule out infections, skin conditions, other causes

Treatment

Self-Care

  • Wear cotton underwear, loose clothing
  • Avoid irritants (scented products, douches)
  • Use unscented, gentle soaps
  • Cool compresses

Medical Treatment3

  • Topical - Lidocaine, estrogen cream
  • Oral medications - Tricyclic antidepressants, anticonvulsants
  • Pelvic floor physical therapy - Often very helpful
  • Nerve blocks
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy
  • Surgery - Vestibulectomy for localized vestibulodynia (last resort)
