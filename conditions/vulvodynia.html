Vulvodynia

Understanding chronic vulvar pain

What Is Vulvodynia?

Vulvodynia is chronic pain of the vulva lasting three months or more without an identifiable cause. It affects an estimated 8-16% of women at some point in their lives. Despite being common, it's often underdiagnosed and undertreated. The pain can significantly impact quality of life, relationships, and sexual health.1

Types2

Localized

  • Pain in specific area (often vestibule)
  • Vestibulodynia most common type
  • Often provoked by touch or pressure

Generalized

  • Pain throughout vulvar area
  • May be constant or intermittent
  • Provoked or unprovoked

Symptoms

  • Burning sensation
  • Stinging or rawness
  • Soreness or throbbing
  • Pain with intercourse
  • Pain with tampon use
  • Pain with sitting
  • Pain with tight clothing

Possible Contributing Factors

  • Nerve damage or irritation
  • Pelvic floor muscle dysfunction
  • Inflammation
  • Genetic factors
  • Previous infections
  • Hormonal changes
  • Central sensitization

Diagnosis3

  • Detailed history
  • Physical examination
  • Q-tip test (cotton swab testing)
  • Ruling out other causes
  • Often delayed diagnosis

Treatment Approaches

Self-Care

  • Avoid irritants (soaps, douches)
  • Wear cotton underwear
  • Use preservative-free lubricants
  • Cold compresses for relief

Physical Therapy

  • Pelvic floor physical therapy
  • Often essential component
  • Internal and external work

Medications

  • Topical lidocaine
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • Anticonvulsants
  • Topical estrogen (if appropriate)

Other Options

  • Nerve blocks
  • Biofeedback
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy
  • Surgery (vestibulectomy) - last resort

Finding Help

  • Seek specialist familiar with condition
  • Gynecologists, pain specialists
  • National Vulvodynia Association
  • Multidisciplinary care often best
