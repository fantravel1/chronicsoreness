Vulvodynia

Chronic vulvar pain without clear cause

What Is Vulvodynia?

Vulvodynia is chronic vulvar pain lasting three months or more without an identifiable cause. It affects approximately 8-16% of women at some point in their lives and is often underdiagnosed.1

Types

  • Localized (vestibulodynia) - Pain at vaginal opening
  • Generalized - Pain throughout vulva
  • Provoked - Pain with touch or pressure
  • Spontaneous - Pain without trigger

Symptoms2

  • Burning, stinging, rawness
  • Soreness or throbbing
  • Pain with intercourse (dyspareunia)
  • Pain with tampon use
  • Pain with sitting or tight clothing
  • Normal appearance on exam

Possible Contributing Factors

  • Nerve irritation or damage
  • History of yeast infections
  • Hormonal factors
  • Pelvic floor muscle dysfunction
  • Genetic predisposition
  • Central sensitization

Treatment3

First-Line

  • Vulvar care measures
  • Topical lidocaine
  • Pelvic floor physical therapy

Medications

  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • Anticonvulsants (gabapentin)
  • Topical estrogen (if indicated)

Other Options

  • Cognitive behavioral therapy
  • Nerve blocks
  • Vestibulectomy (surgery for refractory cases)

Self-Care Tips

  • Wear 100% cotton underwear
  • Avoid irritants (soaps, douches)
  • Use lubricants during intercourse
  • Cool compresses for relief
