Lupus (SLE)

Autoimmune disease with widespread effects

What Is Lupus?

Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) is an autoimmune disease where the immune system attacks healthy tissue. It can affect joints, skin, kidneys, brain, heart, and lungs. Lupus affects women 9 times more often than men.1

Common Symptoms2

  • Joint pain and swelling
  • Extreme fatigue
  • Butterfly-shaped facial rash
  • Skin rashes
  • Sensitivity to sunlight
  • Fever
  • Hair loss
  • Mouth sores

Pain in Lupus

  • Arthritis (usually non-erosive)
  • Muscle pain
  • Headaches
  • Fibromyalgia-like symptoms (common)
  • Flares and remissions

Diagnosis

  • Clinical symptoms
  • ANA (antinuclear antibody) test
  • Other autoantibodies (anti-dsDNA, anti-Smith)
  • Blood tests, urinalysis
  • Often delayed diagnosis

Treatment3

Medications

  • Hydroxychloroquine (cornerstone)
  • NSAIDs for joint pain
  • Corticosteroids (flares)
  • Immunosuppressants (severe disease)
  • Biologics (belimumab)

Lifestyle

  • Sun protection essential
  • Rest during flares
  • Regular exercise when able
  • Stress management

Living with Lupus

  • Disease course varies widely
  • Regular monitoring important
  • Manage flares proactively
  • Build support network
