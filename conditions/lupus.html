Lupus

Systemic autoimmune disease affecting multiple organs

What Is Lupus?

Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) is a chronic autoimmune disease where the immune system attacks healthy tissue. It can affect joints, skin, kidneys, heart, lungs, brain, and blood cells. Pain is a common symptom, affecting up to 90% of patients.1

Symptoms2

Common

  • Fatigue
  • Joint pain and swelling
  • Butterfly rash on face
  • Skin rashes
  • Fever
  • Photosensitivity

Other Manifestations

  • Kidney problems
  • Mouth ulcers
  • Hair loss
  • Raynaud's phenomenon
  • Chest pain (pleuritis)
  • Cognitive difficulties ("brain fog")

Pain in Lupus

  • Joint pain (arthralgia) - most common
  • Inflammatory arthritis
  • Muscle pain
  • Headaches
  • Fibromyalgia (common co-occurrence)

Diagnosis

  • Clinical criteria (symptoms)
  • ANA (antinuclear antibody)
  • More specific antibodies (anti-dsDNA, anti-Smith)
  • Blood counts, kidney function
  • Often requires specialist evaluation

Treatment3

Medications

  • Hydroxychloroquine - Cornerstone treatment
  • NSAIDs - For mild symptoms
  • Corticosteroids - For flares
  • Immunosuppressants - For organ involvement
  • Biologics - Belimumab for active disease

Lifestyle

  • Sun protection
  • Not smoking
  • Regular exercise
  • Stress management
  • Adequate rest
