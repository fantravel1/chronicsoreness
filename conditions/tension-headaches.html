Tension-Type Headaches

The most common headache type

What Are Tension-Type Headaches?

Tension-type headaches (TTH) are the most common primary headache disorder, affecting up to 80% of people at some point. They cause a pressing or tightening sensation, often described as a "band around the head."1

Types

  • Episodic - Less than 15 days per month
  • Chronic - 15 or more days per month for 3+ months

Symptoms2

  • Pressing or tightening quality (not throbbing)
  • Mild to moderate intensity
  • Both sides of head affected
  • Not worsened by physical activity
  • No nausea or vomiting
  • May have sensitivity to light OR sound (not both)
  • Can last 30 minutes to 7 days

Triggers and Contributing Factors

  • Stress and anxiety
  • Poor posture
  • Eye strain
  • Jaw clenching
  • Skipped meals
  • Dehydration
  • Poor sleep
  • Depression

Treatment3

Acute Treatment

  • Simple analgesics (acetaminophen, NSAIDs)
  • Limit to 2-3 days per week to avoid medication overuse
  • Caffeine combinations (use cautiously)

Preventive Treatment (for chronic)

  • Amitriptyline (first-line)
  • Other antidepressants
  • Physical therapy
  • Stress management

Non-Drug Approaches

  • Regular exercise
  • Stress management
  • Improve posture
  • Regular sleep schedule
  • Relaxation techniques
  • Massage
  • Acupuncture

When to See a Doctor

  • Sudden severe headache
  • Headache pattern changes
  • Frequent headaches
  • Headaches not responding to treatment
  • Need pain medication most days
