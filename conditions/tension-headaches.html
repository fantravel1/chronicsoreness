Tension-Type Headaches

Understanding the most common headache type

Key Takeaways

  • Tension-type headaches are the most common primary headache
  • Often described as a tight band around the head
  • Lifestyle factors and stress play major roles
  • Prevention strategies can significantly reduce frequency

What Are Tension-Type Headaches?

Tension-type headaches (TTH) are the most common form of primary headache, affecting up to 80% of people at some point. They typically cause mild to moderate pain and are often described as a pressing or tightening sensation on both sides of the head.1

38%
of the global population has tension-type headaches annually2

Types

  • Episodic infrequent: Less than 1 day per month
  • Episodic frequent: 1-14 days per month for 3+ months
  • Chronic: 15+ days per month for 3+ months

Symptoms

Typical characteristics:3

Pain Pattern

  • Pressing or tightening (non-pulsating) quality
  • Mild to moderate intensity
  • Both sides of head (bilateral)
  • "Band-like" sensation around head
  • Not worsened by routine physical activity

What's NOT Present (Unlike Migraine)

  • Usually no nausea or vomiting
  • Usually no sensitivity to light AND sound (may have one)
  • No aura
  • Not significantly worsened by activity

Duration

  • 30 minutes to 7 days
  • Often build gradually
  • May come and go throughout day

Associated Features

  • Neck and shoulder tension common
  • Scalp tenderness
  • May have mild light OR sound sensitivity (not both)

Causes and Triggers

Contributing Factors

  • Muscle tension: In head, neck, and shoulders
  • Stress: Emotional or physical
  • Poor posture: Especially forward head position
  • Eye strain: Computer work, reading
  • Jaw clenching: Often unconscious
  • Central sensitization: In chronic cases

Common Triggers

  • Stress and anxiety
  • Poor sleep or sleep deprivation
  • Skipping meals
  • Dehydration
  • Caffeine withdrawal
  • Prolonged screen time
  • Poor posture
  • Weather changes

Keeping a Headache Diary

Track your headaches along with potential triggers like sleep, meals, stress levels, and activities. This can help identify patterns and triggers specific to you, making prevention more effective.

Treatment

Treatment depends on frequency and severity:4,5

Acute Treatment (For Individual Headaches)

  • Over-the-counter pain relievers: Acetaminophen, ibuprofen, aspirin, naproxen
  • Caffeine combinations: May enhance pain relief
  • Take earlyâ€”more effective when taken at headache onset
  • Follow dosing instructions

Non-Drug Approaches

  • Rest in quiet, dark room
  • Cold or warm compress
  • Massage of temples, neck, shoulders
  • Relaxation techniques
  • Gentle stretching

Preventive Treatment (For Frequent Headaches)

  • Antidepressants: Amitriptyline most studied
  • Muscle relaxants: Sometimes helpful
  • Other preventives: Based on individual factors

Non-Drug Prevention

  • Stress management
  • Regular exercise
  • Biofeedback
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy
  • Physical therapy
  • Acupuncture

Medication Overuse Headache

Taking pain medications too frequently (10-15+ days/month) can actually cause more headaches. This is called medication overuse headache. If you need pain medication frequently, discuss preventive options with your healthcare provider.

Prevention Strategies

Lifestyle Modifications

  • Regular sleep schedule: Same bed/wake times
  • Stay hydrated: Drink water throughout day
  • Don't skip meals: Eat regular, balanced meals
  • Limit caffeine: And keep consistent intake
  • Regular exercise: 30 minutes most days
  • Manage stress: Daily relaxation practice

Workplace Ergonomics

  • Monitor at eye level
  • Proper chair height and support
  • Take breaks from screen every 20-30 minutes
  • Ensure adequate lighting
  • Avoid forward head posture

Stress Management

  • Progressive muscle relaxation
  • Deep breathing exercises
  • Meditation or mindfulness
  • Regular breaks during stressful work
  • Physical activity as stress relief

Self-Care Techniques

Relaxation Exercises

  • Progressive muscle relaxation: Tense and release muscle groups
  • Deep breathing: Slow, diaphragmatic breathing
  • Visualization: Imagine peaceful scenes

Stretches for Tension Relief

  • Neck rolls (gentle)
  • Shoulder shrugs and rolls
  • Chin tucks
  • Upper trapezius stretch
  • Levator scapulae stretch

Self-Massage Points

  • Temples
  • Base of skull
  • Upper shoulders/trapezius
  • Between eyebrows
  • Use gentle, circular pressure

When to See a Doctor

Seek Evaluation If

  • Headaches are new or different from usual
  • Headaches are becoming more frequent or severe
  • Needing pain medication more than 2 days per week
  • Headaches interfere with daily life
  • Preventive strategies aren't working

Seek Immediate Care If

  • Sudden, severe headache ("thunderclap")
  • Headache with fever, stiff neck, confusion
  • Headache after head injury
  • Headache with vision changes, weakness, or speech problems
  • Worst headache of your life
