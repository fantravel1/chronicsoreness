Tension Headaches

The most common type of headache disorder

What Are Tension Headaches?

Tension-type headaches (TTH) are the most common primary headache disorder, affecting up to 78% of the population at some point. They're characterized by bilateral, pressing pain of mild to moderate intensity.1

Symptoms

  • Pressing or tightening quality (non-pulsating)
  • Bilateral location
  • Mild to moderate intensity
  • Not aggravated by routine physical activity
  • Duration: 30 minutes to 7 days
  • No nausea or vomiting

Classification2

  • Episodic - Less than 15 days per month
  • Chronic - 15 or more days per month for 3+ months

Common Triggers

  • Stress and anxiety
  • Poor posture
  • Eye strain
  • Jaw clenching or TMJ issues
  • Sleep deprivation
  • Skipped meals
  • Dehydration

Treatment

Acute Treatment

  • Simple analgesics (acetaminophen, NSAIDs)
  • Avoid medication overuse (<10-15 days/month)

Prevention3

  • Stress management
  • Regular sleep schedule
  • Posture correction
  • Regular exercise
  • Amitriptyline (for chronic TTH)
