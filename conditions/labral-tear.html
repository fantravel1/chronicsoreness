Labral Tears

Injuries to the cartilage rim of hip or shoulder

What Is a Labral Tear?

The labrum is a ring of cartilage that surrounds and deepens the socket of ball-and-socket joints (hip and shoulder). A labral tear is damage to this cartilage, causing pain and instability.1

Hip Labral Tears

Symptoms

  • Deep groin or hip pain
  • Clicking, catching, or locking
  • Stiffness
  • Pain with sitting or activity
  • Limited range of motion

Causes2

  • Femoroacetabular impingement (FAI)
  • Hip dysplasia
  • Trauma
  • Repetitive motions (sports)
  • Degeneration with age

Shoulder Labral Tears

Types

  • SLAP tear - Superior labrum (where biceps attaches)
  • Bankart lesion - Anterior-inferior (with dislocations)
  • Posterior labral tear - Back of socket

Symptoms

  • Deep, aching shoulder pain
  • Pain with overhead activities
  • Popping or grinding
  • Decreased strength
  • Feeling of instability

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Activity modification
  • Physical therapy
  • NSAIDs
  • Corticosteroid injections

Surgical

  • Arthroscopic labral repair
  • Labral debridement
  • Address underlying cause (FAI, etc.)
