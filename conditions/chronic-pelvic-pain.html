Chronic Pelvic Pain

Persistent pain in the pelvic region

What Is Chronic Pelvic Pain?

Chronic pelvic pain (CPP) is persistent pain in the lower abdomen or pelvis lasting 6 months or longer. It affects both men and women and often involves multiple body systems. CPP can have significant impacts on quality of life, relationships, and work.1

Symptoms2

  • Constant or intermittent pelvic pain
  • Dull aching or sharp pain
  • Pain during intercourse
  • Pain with urination or bowel movements
  • Pain with sitting
  • Low back pain

Possible Causes

In Women

  • Endometriosis
  • Adenomyosis
  • Interstitial cystitis
  • Vulvodynia
  • Pelvic inflammatory disease
  • Ovarian cysts

In Men

  • Chronic prostatitis
  • Interstitial cystitis

Both Sexes

  • Pelvic floor dysfunction
  • Irritable bowel syndrome
  • Nerve entrapment
  • Musculoskeletal causes
  • Sometimes no clear cause found

Diagnosis

  • Detailed history
  • Physical examination
  • Pelvic ultrasound
  • Laparoscopy (if indicated)
  • Often multidisciplinary evaluation

Treatment3

Multidisciplinary Approach

  • Gynecology or urology
  • Physical therapy
  • Pain management
  • Psychology

Physical Therapy

  • Pelvic floor therapy
  • Manual therapy
  • Relaxation training

Medications

  • Pain medications
  • Hormonal treatments (for women)
  • Antidepressants
  • Anticonvulsants

Other Options

  • Nerve blocks
  • Trigger point injections
  • Surgery (for specific causes)
