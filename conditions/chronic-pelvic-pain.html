Chronic Pelvic Pain

Persistent pain in the lower abdomen and pelvis

What Is Chronic Pelvic Pain?

Chronic pelvic pain (CPP) is persistent pain in the lower abdomen and pelvis lasting 6 months or more. It affects both women and men, with various underlying causes. It's estimated to affect 15% of women.1

Causes in Women2

  • Endometriosis
  • Adenomyosis
  • Pelvic inflammatory disease
  • Ovarian cysts/remnants
  • Uterine fibroids
  • Vulvodynia
  • Pelvic floor dysfunction

Causes in Men

  • Chronic prostatitis/CPPS
  • Pelvic floor dysfunction
  • Pudendal neuralgia

Causes in Both Sexes

  • Interstitial cystitis/bladder pain syndrome
  • Irritable bowel syndrome
  • Musculoskeletal sources
  • Myofascial pain
  • Nerve entrapment
  • Central sensitization

Symptoms

  • Dull aching or sharp pain
  • Pain with intercourse
  • Urinary symptoms
  • Bowel symptoms
  • Pain with sitting
  • Menstrual-related symptoms

Diagnosis

  • Thorough history and exam
  • Pelvic exam
  • Ultrasound, MRI
  • Laparoscopy (sometimes)
  • Evaluation for overlapping conditions

Treatment3

Multidisciplinary Approach

  • Pelvic floor physical therapy
  • Medications (hormonal, pain medications, nerve medications)
  • Psychological support
  • Treating specific conditions
  • Trigger point injections
  • Nerve blocks
  • Surgery (when indicated)
