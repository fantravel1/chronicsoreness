Endometriosis

Understanding this common cause of chronic pelvic pain

Key Takeaways

  • Endometriosis affects ~10% of reproductive-age women
  • Average delay in diagnosis is 7-10 years
  • Symptoms range from mild to severely debilitating
  • Treatment is availableâ€”you don't have to suffer in silence

What Is Endometriosis?

Endometriosis is a chronic condition where tissue similar to the lining of the uterus (endometrium) grows outside the uterus. This tissue responds to hormonal changes during the menstrual cycle, causing inflammation, pain, and sometimes scarring (adhesions).1

190 Million
women worldwide are affected by endometriosis2

Where It Occurs

  • Ovaries (most common)
  • Fallopian tubes
  • Pelvic lining (peritoneum)
  • Bowel and bladder
  • Rarely: lungs, diaphragm, other locations

Symptoms

Symptoms vary widely in type and severity:3

Pain Symptoms

  • Painful periods (dysmenorrhea): Often severe, may be debilitating
  • Chronic pelvic pain: May occur throughout cycle
  • Pain with intercourse (dyspareunia): Especially deep penetration
  • Pain with bowel movements or urination: Often during period
  • Ovulation pain: Mid-cycle pain
  • Lower back pain

Other Symptoms

  • Heavy or irregular periods
  • Bloating ("endo belly")
  • Fatigue
  • Nausea
  • Diarrhea or constipation (especially during period)
  • Infertility (30-50% of those with infertility have endo)

Symptom Severity Doesn't Equal Disease Severity

The amount of pain does not correlate with the extent of disease. Some people with minimal endometriosis have severe symptoms, while others with extensive disease may have few symptoms. Pain is real regardless of what imaging shows.

Causes

The exact cause is unknown, but several theories exist:4

Proposed Mechanisms

  • Retrograde menstruation: Menstrual tissue flows backward through tubes
  • Cellular transformation: Cells transform into endometrial-like tissue
  • Immune dysfunction: Body doesn't clear misplaced tissue
  • Stem cells: May spread through blood or lymph
  • Genetic factors: Runs in families

Risk Factors

  • Family history (especially mother or sister)
  • Early menstruation
  • Short menstrual cycles
  • Heavy periods
  • Never having given birth
  • Low body mass index

Diagnosis

Diagnosis can be challenging:5

Clinical Evaluation

  • Detailed symptom history
  • Pelvic examination
  • Assessment of pain patterns

Imaging

  • Ultrasound: Can detect endometriomas (ovarian cysts)
  • MRI: More detailed view, may detect deep disease
  • Normal imaging does NOT rule out endometriosis

Definitive Diagnosis

  • Laparoscopy: Surgical visualization is the gold standard
  • Allows both diagnosis and treatment
  • Not always required to start treatment

Diagnostic Delay

Average time from symptom onset to diagnosis is 7-10 years. If your symptoms are dismissed as "normal periods," seek another opinion. Severe menstrual pain that interferes with life is not normal.

Treatment

Treatment depends on symptoms, goals, and preferences:6

Pain Management

  • NSAIDs: First-line for pain
  • Take before period starts for best effect
  • Other pain medications as needed

Hormonal Treatment

  • Birth control pills: Continuous use to skip periods
  • Progestins: Pills, IUD (Mirena), injection, implant
  • GnRH agonists/antagonists: Create temporary menopause-like state
  • Aromatase inhibitors: For resistant cases

Surgery

  • Excision surgery: Removes endometriosis tissue
  • Ablation: Burns tissue (less effective than excision)
  • Performed laparoscopically
  • May provide long-lasting relief
  • Specialist surgeons have better outcomes

For Infertility

  • Surgery may improve natural conception
  • Assisted reproduction (IVF)
  • Fertility specialist consultation

Complementary Approaches

  • Pelvic floor physical therapy
  • Acupuncture
  • Anti-inflammatory diet
  • Stress management
  • Heat therapy for pain

Living with Endometriosis

Self-Care Strategies

  • Track symptoms and menstrual cycle
  • Use heat for pain relief
  • Gentle exercise when tolerable
  • Anti-inflammatory foods
  • Prioritize sleep and stress management
  • Connect with support community

Workplace Accommodations

  • Flexible scheduling around flares
  • Work from home options
  • Access to bathroom and break room
  • Consider FMLA for severe cases

Mental Health

  • Chronic pain affects mental health
  • Seek support if struggling
  • Support groups can help
  • Therapy can help with coping

Finding Care

When to Seek Help

  • Severe period pain affecting daily life
  • Pain between periods
  • Pain with intercourse
  • Difficulty getting pregnant
  • Bowel or bladder symptoms with cycle

Finding a Specialist

  • Gynecologist with endometriosis expertise
  • Reproductive endocrinologist for fertility
  • Endometriosis excision specialist for surgery
  • Ask about their experience and approach
