Endometriosis

When tissue similar to uterine lining grows outside the uterus

What Is Endometriosis?

Endometriosis is a chronic condition where tissue similar to the uterine lining (endometrium) grows outside the uterus. This tissue responds to hormonal cycles, causing inflammation, pain, and sometimes scar tissue. It affects about 10% of reproductive-age women.1

Common Locations

  • Ovaries
  • Fallopian tubes
  • Pelvic peritoneum
  • Bladder, bowel surface
  • Rectovaginal septum

Symptoms2

  • Painful periods (dysmenorrhea)
  • Chronic pelvic pain
  • Pain with intercourse (dyspareunia)
  • Pain with bowel movements or urination
  • Heavy periods
  • Infertility
  • Fatigue
  • Bloating

Diagnosis

  • Clinical suspicion from symptoms
  • Pelvic exam
  • Ultrasound (can detect endometriomas)
  • MRI
  • Laparoscopy - Gold standard for definitive diagnosis
  • Average 7-10 years to diagnosis

Treatment3

Pain Management

  • NSAIDs
  • Hormonal treatments (suppress ovulation)
  • Oral contraceptives
  • Progestins
  • GnRH agonists/antagonists

Surgery

  • Laparoscopic excision of lesions
  • Can relieve pain and improve fertility
  • Recurrence common

Other Approaches

  • Pelvic floor physical therapy
  • Pain psychology
  • Dietary modifications (anti-inflammatory)
  • Multidisciplinary care
