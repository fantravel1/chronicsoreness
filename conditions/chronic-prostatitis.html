Chronic Prostatitis

Chronic pelvic pain syndrome in men

What Is Chronic Prostatitis?

Chronic prostatitis/chronic pelvic pain syndrome (CP/CPPS) is a condition causing pelvic pain and urinary symptoms in men for at least 3 months. Despite its name, it's now understood to be more than just a prostate problem and often involves the pelvic floor muscles and nervous system.1

Types

  • Category III (CPPS) - No infection, most common
  • IIIa - Inflammatory
  • IIIb - Non-inflammatory
  • Category II - Chronic bacterial (rare)

Symptoms2

  • Pain in perineum (between scrotum and rectum)
  • Pain in groin, lower abdomen, or genitals
  • Pain during or after ejaculation
  • Urinary frequency or urgency
  • Weak urine stream
  • Pain with urination
  • Sexual dysfunction

Causes

  • Often unknown
  • Pelvic floor muscle dysfunction
  • Nerve sensitization
  • Previous infection
  • Stress and tension
  • Possibly autoimmune

Treatment3

Multimodal Approach

Multiple treatments together work better than one alone.

Physical Therapy

  • Pelvic floor physical therapy
  • Trigger point release
  • Stretching exercises
  • Relaxation training

Medications

  • Alpha-blockers
  • Anti-inflammatory medications
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • Sometimes antibiotics (if indicated)

Other Approaches

  • Stress management
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy
  • Heat therapy
  • Dietary modifications

Self-Care

  • Avoid prolonged sitting
  • Use cushion with cutout
  • Warm baths
  • Avoid caffeine and alcohol
  • Regular exercise
  • Stress reduction
