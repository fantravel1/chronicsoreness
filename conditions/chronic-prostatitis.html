Chronic Prostatitis/CPPS

Persistent pelvic pain in men

What Is CP/CPPS?

Chronic prostatitis/chronic pelvic pain syndrome (CP/CPPS) is characterized by pelvic pain in men lasting 3+ months without evidence of bacterial infection. It's the most common form of prostatitis and affects 10-15% of men at some point.1

Symptoms

  • Pain in perineum, penis, testicles
  • Lower abdominal or low back pain
  • Pain with urination
  • Urinary frequency or urgency
  • Pain with ejaculation
  • Sexual dysfunction

Possible Contributing Factors2

  • Pelvic floor muscle dysfunction
  • Nerve sensitization
  • Previous infection
  • Autoimmune factors
  • Psychological stress
  • Myofascial trigger points

Diagnosis

  • Symptoms for ≥3 months
  • Rule out bacterial infection
  • Rule out other causes (cancer, stricture)
  • NIH-CPSI symptom questionnaire

Treatment3

Multimodal Approach (UPOINT)

  • U - Urinary symptoms (alpha-blockers)
  • P - Psychosocial (CBT, stress management)
  • O - Organ-specific (prostate therapy)
  • I - Infection (if present)
  • N - Neurologic/systemic (neuropathic meds)
  • T - Tenderness (pelvic floor PT)

Physical Therapy

  • Pelvic floor physical therapy
  • Myofascial release
  • Stretching
