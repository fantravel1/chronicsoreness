Chronic Prostatitis/CPPS

Understanding chronic pelvic pain in men

What Is CP/CPPS?

Chronic prostatitis/chronic pelvic pain syndrome (CP/CPPS) is a condition causing pain in the pelvic region in men without evidence of bacterial infection. It's the most common form of prostatitis and can significantly impact quality of life.1

Symptoms

  • Pain in perineum, genitals, lower abdomen, lower back
  • Pain with urination
  • Urinary frequency and urgency
  • Pain with or after ejaculation
  • Erectile dysfunction
  • Sitting pain
  • Symptoms waxing and waning

Causes

Often no single cause; likely multifactorial:2

  • Pelvic floor muscle dysfunction
  • Nerve sensitization
  • Previous infections
  • Autoimmune factors
  • Psychological factors (stress, anxiety)

Diagnosis

  • Medical history and physical exam
  • Urinalysis and cultures to rule out infection
  • Prostate exam
  • NIH Chronic Prostatitis Symptom Index

Treatment

Multimodal Approach3

  • Pelvic floor physical therapy - Often most effective
  • Alpha-blockers - For urinary symptoms
  • Medications - Amitriptyline, NSAIDs
  • Stress management
  • Heat therapy
  • Trigger point release
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy

Self-Care

  • Warm baths
  • Cushion for sitting
  • Avoid prolonged sitting
  • Regular exercise
  • Stress reduction
  • Avoid caffeine, alcohol, spicy foods
