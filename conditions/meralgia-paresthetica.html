Meralgia Paresthetica

Numbness and burning in the outer thigh

What Is Meralgia Paresthetica?

Meralgia paresthetica is a condition caused by compression of the lateral femoral cutaneous nerve, which provides sensation to the outer thigh. This compression causes numbness, tingling, and burning pain in the outer thigh. The condition is sometimes called "tight pants syndrome" because constrictive clothing can trigger it.1

Symptoms2

  • Numbness on outer thigh
  • Tingling or "pins and needles"
  • Burning sensation
  • Increased sensitivity to light touch
  • Usually one side only
  • Symptoms often worse standing or walking
  • May improve when sitting

Causes and Risk Factors

  • Tight clothing (belts, pants)
  • Obesity or weight gain
  • Pregnancy
  • Diabetes
  • Prolonged standing
  • Hip surgery or injury
  • Seat belt injuries
  • Tool belts (occupational)

Diagnosis3

  • Based on symptoms and examination
  • Specific area of numbness is characteristic
  • No weakness (only sensory nerve)
  • Nerve conduction studies (sometimes)
  • Rule out lumbar spine problems

Treatment

Conservative (Often Effective)

  • Wear loose clothing
  • Weight loss if overweight
  • Avoid prolonged standing
  • NSAIDs for discomfort

Medical Treatment

  • Medications for nerve pain
  • Corticosteroid injection
  • Physical therapy

Surgery (Rare)

  • Nerve decompression
  • Only if conservative fails

Self-Care

  • Loosen belt
  • Wear comfortable, loose pants
  • Take sitting breaks
  • Stretching exercises
  • Apply ice if inflamed

Prognosis

  • Often resolves with lifestyle changes
  • May take weeks to months
  • Excellent outlook with treatment
  • Can recur if triggers return
