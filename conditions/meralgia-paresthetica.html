Meralgia Paresthetica

Burning and numbness in the outer thigh

What Is Meralgia Paresthetica?

Meralgia paresthetica is a condition caused by compression of the lateral femoral cutaneous nerve, resulting in tingling, numbness, and burning pain in the outer thigh. It's a purely sensory condition with no muscle weakness.1

Symptoms

  • Burning or aching in outer thigh
  • Tingling or numbness
  • Sensitivity to light touch
  • One side typically affected
  • No weakness (sensory nerve only)
  • Symptoms may worsen with standing or walking

Risk Factors2

  • Obesity
  • Pregnancy
  • Tight clothing or belts
  • Diabetes
  • Leg length discrepancy
  • Prolonged standing
  • Recent weight gain

Diagnosis

  • Clinical examination
  • Sensory testing of outer thigh
  • Nerve conduction studies (may be normal)
  • Pelvic compression test

Treatment3

Conservative (Usually Effective)

  • Wear loose clothing
  • Weight loss if overweight
  • Avoid prolonged standing
  • NSAIDs

Medical Treatment

  • Neuropathic medications
  • Local nerve block
  • Corticosteroid injection

Surgery (Rare)

  • Nerve decompression
  • Neurectomy
