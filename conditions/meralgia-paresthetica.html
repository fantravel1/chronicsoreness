Meralgia Paresthetica

Burning numbness in the outer thigh

What Is Meralgia Paresthetica?

Meralgia paresthetica is a condition causing numbness, tingling, and burning pain in the outer thigh. It results from compression of the lateral femoral cutaneous nerve as it passes through the groin. It's relatively common and usually resolves with conservative treatment.1

Symptoms2

  • Burning or aching pain in outer thigh
  • Numbness on outer thigh
  • Tingling or "pins and needles"
  • Hypersensitivity to light touch
  • Symptoms usually one-sided
  • No muscle weakness
  • May worsen with standing or walking

Causes

  • Tight clothing (belts, jeans, corsets)
  • Obesity
  • Pregnancy
  • Diabetes
  • Prolonged standing or walking
  • Scar tissue after surgery
  • Seat belt injury

Diagnosis

  • Clinical history and examination
  • Characteristic symptom distribution
  • Nerve conduction studies (if needed)
  • Rule out other causes of thigh symptoms

Treatment3

Conservative (Usually Effective)

  • Wear loose clothing
  • Weight loss if appropriate
  • Avoid prolonged standing
  • NSAIDs
  • Ice or heat

Medications

  • Gabapentin or pregabalin
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • Topical lidocaine

Procedures

  • Local nerve block
  • Surgery (rare, if conservative fails)

Prognosis

Most cases resolve within months with conservative treatment. Removing the source of compression is usually curative.
