Meralgia Paresthetica

Nerve compression causing thigh symptoms

What Is Meralgia Paresthetica?

Meralgia paresthetica occurs when the lateral femoral cutaneous nerveâ€”a sensory nerve in the thighâ€”becomes compressed or trapped. This causes burning pain, numbness, and tingling on the outer thigh. It's a purely sensory nerve, so there's no muscle weakness involved.1

Symptoms2

  • Burning or aching pain in outer thigh
  • Numbness and tingling
  • Increased sensitivity to light touch
  • Pain with prolonged standing
  • Usually one side only
  • No weakness present
  • Symptoms may vary in intensity

Common Causes

  • Tight clothing (belts, jeans)
  • Weight gain or obesity
  • Pregnancy
  • Prolonged standing or walking
  • Diabetes
  • Seatbelt injuries
  • Hip surgery complications

Risk Factors

  • Obesity
  • Diabetes
  • Occupations with tool belts
  • Tight clothing worn frequently
  • Pregnancy

Diagnosis3

  • Clinical history and exam
  • Reproduction of symptoms with palpation
  • Sensory testing of outer thigh
  • Usually clinical diagnosis
  • Nerve conduction studies sometimes
  • Imaging to rule out other causes

Treatment

Conservative Measures

  • Wear loose clothing
  • Weight loss if applicable
  • Avoid prolonged standing
  • Ice to the area
  • Physical therapy

Medications

  • NSAIDs for inflammation
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • Gabapentin or pregabalin
  • Lidocaine patches

Interventional

  • Nerve block with local anesthetic
  • Corticosteroid injection
  • Surgery (rarely needed)

Prognosis

  • Often resolves with conservative care
  • Addressing cause is key
  • Some cases become chronic
  • Most improve within months

Prevention

  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Avoid tight belts and clothing
  • Take breaks from prolonged standing
