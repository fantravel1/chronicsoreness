Meniscus Tears

Injury to the knee's shock absorbers

What Is a Meniscus Tear?

The menisci are C-shaped pieces of cartilage that act as shock absorbers between the thighbone and shinbone. A meniscus tear is one of the most common knee injuries, occurring from twisting or degenerative wear.1

Types of Tears

  • Acute/traumatic - From sudden twist or injury
  • Degenerative - Gradual wear, often in older adults
  • Tear patterns: horizontal, radial, bucket handle, complex

Symptoms2

  • Pain, especially with twisting
  • Swelling
  • Catching or locking
  • Feeling knee may give way
  • Stiffness
  • Difficulty fully extending knee

Diagnosis

  • Clinical examination
  • McMurray's test
  • Joint line tenderness
  • MRI (gold standard)

Treatment3

Conservative (Many Cases)

  • Rest, ice, compression, elevation
  • Physical therapy
  • NSAIDs
  • Especially for degenerative tears

Surgery

  • Meniscectomy - Remove damaged portion
  • Meniscus repair - Suture tear (preferred when possible)
  • Better outcomes preserving meniscus

Important Note

Many degenerative tears are found incidentally on MRI and don't require surgery. Treatment should match symptoms, not just MRI findings.
