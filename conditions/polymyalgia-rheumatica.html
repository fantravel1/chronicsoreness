Polymyalgia Rheumatica

Inflammatory muscle pain and stiffness

What Is Polymyalgia Rheumatica?

Polymyalgia rheumatica (PMR) is an inflammatory disorder causing muscle pain and stiffness, especially in the shoulders and hips. It almost exclusively affects people over 50 and is more common in women. The cause is unknown but involves inflammation of the lining around joints and tendons.1

Symptoms2

Primary Symptoms

  • Aching and stiffness in shoulders, upper arms
  • Pain and stiffness in hips, thighs
  • Stiffness worst in morning
  • Stiffness after inactivity
  • Symptoms typically bilateral (both sides)

Other Symptoms

  • Fatigue
  • General feeling of being unwell
  • Low-grade fever
  • Loss of appetite
  • Unintended weight loss
  • Depression

Diagnosis

  • Clinical symptoms in person over 50
  • Elevated inflammatory markers (ESR, CRP)
  • Rapid response to corticosteroids
  • Exclusion of other conditions
  • Ultrasound may show bursitis

Connection to Giant Cell Arteritis

  • About 15-30% develop giant cell arteritis (GCA)
  • GCA involves blood vessel inflammation
  • Watch for: new headache, jaw pain, vision changes
  • Report these symptoms immediately
  • GCA is a medical emergency (can cause blindness)

Treatment3

Corticosteroids

  • Mainstay of treatment
  • Usually low doses (10-20mg prednisone)
  • Dramatic improvement typically within days
  • Gradual taper over months to years
  • Some people need long-term low doses

Managing Side Effects

  • Calcium and vitamin D for bone health
  • Bone density monitoring
  • Blood sugar monitoring
  • Weight management

Prognosis

  • Most people respond well to treatment
  • Duration typically 1-5 years
  • Many eventually achieve remission
  • Relapses can occur during tapering

Living with PMR

  • Take medications as prescribed
  • Stay physically active within comfort
  • Report new symptoms promptly
  • Regular monitoring appointments
  • Focus on bone and overall health
