Polymyalgia Rheumatica

Inflammatory muscle pain in older adults

What Is Polymyalgia Rheumatica?

Polymyalgia rheumatica (PMR) is an inflammatory condition causing muscle pain and stiffness, primarily in the shoulders and hips. It almost exclusively affects people over 50, with average onset around age 70. It responds dramatically to corticosteroids.1

Symptoms2

  • Aching in shoulders and upper arms
  • Aching in hips and thighs
  • Morning stiffness (>45 minutes)
  • Difficulty raising arms
  • Trouble getting up from chair
  • Fatigue
  • Low-grade fever
  • Weight loss

Diagnosis

  • Elevated ESR and/or CRP
  • Clinical presentation
  • Rapid response to prednisone
  • No specific test - diagnosis of exclusion
  • Rule out other conditions

Association with Giant Cell Arteritis

  • 15-20% of PMR patients develop GCA
  • Watch for headache, vision changes, jaw pain
  • GCA is a medical emergency

Treatment3

Corticosteroids

  • Prednisone is first-line treatment
  • Dramatic response within days
  • Slow taper over 1-2 years
  • Many need treatment longer

Managing Side Effects

  • Calcium and vitamin D
  • Bone density monitoring
  • Blood sugar monitoring

Prognosis

PMR is self-limiting but may last 1-5 years. Most people eventually taper off medication completely.
