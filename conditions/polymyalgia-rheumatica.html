Polymyalgia Rheumatica

Inflammatory condition affecting shoulders and hips

What Is Polymyalgia Rheumatica?

Polymyalgia rheumatica (PMR) is an inflammatory disorder causing muscle pain and stiffness, especially in the shoulders and hips. It almost exclusively affects people over 50 and responds dramatically to low-dose corticosteroids.1

Symptoms2

  • Pain and stiffness in shoulders, upper arms
  • Pain and stiffness in hips, thighs
  • Stiffness worse in morning (>45 minutes)
  • Difficulty raising arms above shoulders
  • Difficulty getting out of chair
  • Fatigue
  • General malaise
  • Sometimes low-grade fever

Risk Factors

  • Age >50 (average 70)
  • Women more than men
  • Northern European ancestry

Diagnosis

  • Elevated inflammatory markers (ESR, CRP)
  • Typical clinical presentation
  • Dramatic response to steroids
  • Exclude other conditions
  • Ultrasound may show bursitis

Giant Cell Arteritis Association

  • 15-20% of PMR patients develop GCA
  • Watch for: new headache, jaw pain, vision changes
  • GCA is medical emergency (risk of blindness)

Treatment3

Corticosteroids (Mainstay)

  • Low-dose prednisone (15-20mg initially)
  • Rapid improvement (often within days)
  • Gradual taper over 1-2 years
  • Some need longer treatment

Managing Steroid Side Effects

  • Calcium and vitamin D
  • Bone density monitoring
  • Blood sugar monitoring
  • Weight management

Prognosis

  • Usually self-limited (1-5 years)
  • Excellent response to treatment
  • Some have relapses
