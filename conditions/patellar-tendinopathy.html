Patellar Tendinopathy

Also known as jumper's knee

What Is Patellar Tendinopathy?

Patellar tendinopathy, commonly called jumper's knee, is a condition involving pain and dysfunction of the patellar tendon. It's an overuse injury common in athletes who jump, though it can affect anyone.1

Symptoms

  • Pain at bottom of kneecap
  • Pain with jumping, running, stairs
  • Pain when sitting with knee bent
  • Stiffness after rest
  • Tenderness at tendon
  • May worsen gradually

Risk Factors2

  • Jumping sports (volleyball, basketball)
  • Running
  • Sudden increase in training
  • Hard playing surfaces
  • Poor landing mechanics
  • Quadriceps weakness or tightness
  • Poor flexibility

Staging

  • Stage 1 - Pain only after activity
  • Stage 2 - Pain during and after activity
  • Stage 3 - Pain that limits performance
  • Stage 4 - Complete tendon rupture

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Load management (relative rest)
  • Eccentric strengthening exercises
  • Heavy slow resistance training
  • Ice after activity
  • Patellar tendon strap

Additional Options

  • Shockwave therapy
  • PRP injections
  • Physical therapy

Surgery

  • Rare, for refractory cases
  • Debridement of degenerative tissue
