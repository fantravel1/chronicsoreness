Bursitis

Inflammation of the fluid-filled sacs that cushion joints

What Is Bursitis?

Bursitis is inflammation of a bursa - a small, fluid-filled sac that acts as a cushion between bones and soft tissues. When inflamed, bursae cause pain, swelling, and tenderness near joints.1

Common Locations

  • Shoulder (subacromial) - Most common
  • Hip (trochanteric) - Pain on outer hip
  • Knee (prepatellar, pes anserine)
  • Elbow (olecranon)
  • Heel (retrocalcaneal)

Causes2

  • Repetitive motion or overuse
  • Direct trauma or pressure
  • Prolonged kneeling or leaning
  • Infection (septic bursitis)
  • Inflammatory conditions (RA, gout)
  • Age-related changes

Symptoms

  • Localized pain and tenderness
  • Swelling (sometimes visible)
  • Pain with movement or pressure
  • Stiffness
  • Warmth/redness (if infected)

Diagnosis

  • Physical examination
  • X-ray (rule out other causes)
  • Ultrasound or MRI (confirm bursa)
  • Aspiration (if infection suspected)

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Rest and activity modification
  • Ice application
  • NSAIDs
  • Padding/cushioning
  • Physical therapy

Medical

  • Corticosteroid injection
  • Aspiration of fluid
  • Antibiotics (if infected)
  • Surgery (rarely needed)

Prevention

  • Use padding when kneeling
  • Take breaks from repetitive activities
  • Strengthen surrounding muscles
  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Proper technique in sports/work
