Bursitis

Inflammation of the joint cushions

What Is Bursitis?

Bursitis is inflammation of a bursa, a small fluid-filled sac that cushions bones, tendons, and muscles near joints. When irritated, bursae become painful and swollen. Common locations include the shoulder, hip, elbow, and knee.1

Common Types

  • Trochanteric bursitis - Outer hip
  • Subacromial bursitis - Shoulder
  • Olecranon bursitis - Elbow ("student's elbow")
  • Prepatellar bursitis - Front of knee ("housemaid's knee")
  • Pes anserine bursitis - Inner knee
  • Retrocalcaneal bursitis - Heel

Symptoms2

  • Localized pain
  • Tenderness to touch
  • Swelling (visible in superficial bursae)
  • Stiffness
  • Pain with movement
  • Redness, warmth (if infected)

Causes

  • Repetitive motion or overuse
  • Prolonged pressure (kneeling, leaning on elbows)
  • Direct trauma
  • Infection (septic bursitis)
  • Arthritis conditions
  • Gout

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Rest and activity modification
  • Ice application
  • NSAIDs
  • Padding/protection
  • Physical therapy

Medical

  • Corticosteroid injection
  • Aspiration (if swollen)
  • Antibiotics (if infected)
  • Surgery (rare, for chronic cases)

Prevention

  • Use cushioning when kneeling
  • Take breaks from repetitive activities
  • Strengthen supporting muscles
  • Maintain healthy weight
