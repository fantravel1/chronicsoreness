Tarlov Cysts

Fluid-filled cysts on spinal nerve roots

What Are Tarlov Cysts?

Tarlov cysts (also called perineural cysts or sacral nerve root cysts) are fluid-filled sacs that form on the nerve roots of the spine, most commonly in the sacral (lower back) area. They contain cerebrospinal fluid. Many are asymptomatic, but some cause significant pain and dysfunction.1

Prevalence

  • Found in 1-5% of spine MRIs
  • Most are incidental findings
  • Only a fraction cause symptoms
  • More common in women

Symptoms2

When Symptomatic

  • Sacral/low back pain
  • Sciatica-like leg pain
  • Buttock pain
  • Pain sitting (worse than standing)
  • Numbness or tingling

Possible Other Symptoms

  • Bladder dysfunction
  • Bowel changes
  • Sexual dysfunction
  • Weakness in legs

Diagnosis

  • MRI is primary imaging
  • Often found incidentally
  • Challenge: determining if symptoms are from cyst
  • Location and size matter
  • Other causes must be ruled out

Treatment Challenges3

  • Treatment is controversial
  • Many doctors unfamiliar with symptomatic Tarlov cysts
  • Determining causation is difficult
  • Limited treatment options

Treatment Options

Conservative

  • Pain medications
  • Physical therapy
  • Activity modification
  • Pain management procedures

Procedures

  • CT-guided aspiration (often temporary)
  • Fibrin glue injection
  • Surgical options (specialized centers)
  • Recurrence is common with some treatments

Finding Care

  • Few specialists experienced with symptomatic cysts
  • May need to travel for expert care
  • Patient advocacy groups can help find doctors
  • Get opinions from specialists familiar with condition

Living with Tarlov Cysts

  • Cushions may help with sitting
  • Avoid activities that worsen symptoms
  • Connect with support communities
  • Advocate for yourself with healthcare providers
  • Keep records of symptoms and imaging
