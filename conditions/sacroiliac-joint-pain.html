Sacroiliac Joint Pain

When the SI joint causes lower back and hip pain

What Is the Sacroiliac Joint?

The sacroiliac (SI) joints connect the sacrum (base of spine) to the iliac bones of the pelvis. There are two SI joints, one on each side. These joints have limited movement but play an important role in transferring weight and forces between the upper body and legs. SI joint dysfunction is estimated to cause 15-30% of lower back pain cases.1

Causes2

  • Too much movement (hypermobility)
  • Too little movement (hypomobility)
  • Pregnancy and childbirth
  • Leg length discrepancy
  • Arthritis
  • Trauma or injury
  • Spine surgery (especially fusion)
  • Infection (rare)

Symptoms

  • Pain in lower back on one side
  • Pain in buttock or hip
  • Pain may radiate to groin or thigh
  • Usually doesn't go below the knee
  • Worse with sitting, standing from sitting
  • Pain climbing stairs
  • Difficulty lying on affected side

Diagnosis3

  • Physical examination
  • Provocative tests (FABER, Gaenslen's, thigh thrust)
  • Multiple positive tests increase likelihood
  • Diagnostic injection (most definitive)
  • Imaging may rule out other conditions

Treatment

Conservative

  • Physical therapy
  • SI joint stabilization exercises
  • SI joint belt
  • NSAIDs
  • Ice or heat
  • Activity modification

Injections

  • Corticosteroid injections
  • May provide temporary relief
  • Can be repeated if helpful

Procedures

  • Radiofrequency ablation
  • Prolotherapy
  • SI joint fusion (severe cases)

Physical Therapy Exercises

  • Core stabilization
  • Gluteal strengthening
  • Gentle stretching
  • Pelvic floor exercises
  • Movement retraining

Self-Care

  • Avoid prolonged sitting
  • Change positions frequently
  • Use supportive seating
  • Sleep with pillow between knees
  • Avoid asymmetric activities
