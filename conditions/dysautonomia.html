Dysautonomia and POTS

When the autonomic nervous system misfires

What Is Dysautonomia?

Dysautonomia is an umbrella term for disorders affecting the autonomic nervous systemâ€”the system that controls automatic functions like heart rate, blood pressure, digestion, and temperature regulation. POTS (Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome) is a common form.1

Types of Dysautonomia2

POTS (Most Common)

  • Heart rate increases excessively on standing
  • 30+ bpm increase or over 120 bpm within 10 minutes
  • No significant drop in blood pressure
  • Symptoms on standing that improve lying down

Other Types

  • Orthostatic hypotension (blood pressure drop)
  • Neurocardiogenic syncope
  • Multiple system atrophy
  • Autonomic neuropathies

Common Symptoms

  • Dizziness/lightheadedness on standing
  • Racing heart
  • Fatigue
  • Brain fog
  • Exercise intolerance
  • Nausea
  • Headaches
  • Temperature dysregulation
  • Sweating abnormalities
  • Digestive problems

Associated Conditions

  • Ehlers-Danlos syndrome (common overlap)
  • Mast cell disorders
  • ME/CFS
  • Long COVID
  • Autoimmune conditions
  • Small fiber neuropathy

Diagnosis3

  • Tilt table test
  • Standing test (active stand)
  • Heart rate and blood pressure monitoring
  • Autonomic function tests
  • Rule out other conditions

Treatment Approaches

Non-Medication

  • Increase salt and fluids (2-3L water, 3-10g salt)
  • Compression garments
  • Counter-maneuvers (leg crossing, squatting)
  • Careful, reclined exercise (recumbent bike, swimming)
  • Avoid triggers (heat, prolonged standing)
  • Rise slowly from lying/sitting

Medications

  • Fludrocortisone (increases blood volume)
  • Midodrine (raises blood pressure)
  • Beta blockers (control heart rate)
  • Ivabradine
  • Others based on specific symptoms

Living with POTS

  • Pace activities
  • Stay hydrated throughout day
  • Avoid standing still for long periods
  • Plan for bad days
  • Many improve over time with treatment
