Hip Arthritis

Managing degenerative hip pain

What Is Hip Arthritis?

Hip osteoarthritis occurs when the cartilage covering the hip joint wears down, leading to bone-on-bone contact. It's one of the most common causes of hip pain and disability, especially in older adults.1

Risk Factors

  • Age (>50)
  • Previous hip injury
  • Hip abnormalities (dysplasia, impingement)
  • Obesity
  • Family history
  • Repetitive stress

Symptoms2

  • Groin pain (classic location)
  • Thigh or buttock pain
  • Morning stiffness (<30 minutes)
  • Difficulty with stairs
  • Limping
  • Reduced range of motion
  • Pain with prolonged sitting

Diagnosis

  • Clinical examination
  • X-rays show joint space narrowing
  • MRI usually not needed

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Exercise (strengthening, flexibility)
  • Weight management
  • Physical therapy
  • NSAIDs or acetaminophen
  • Walking aids if needed
  • Corticosteroid injections

Hip Replacement

  • When conservative treatment fails
  • Significant impact on quality of life
  • Very successful surgery
  • Prosthetics last 15-25 years

Exercise Recommendations

  • Low-impact activities (swimming, cycling)
  • Avoid high-impact sports
  • Hip strengthening exercises
  • Gentle stretching
