Iliotibial Band Syndrome

Outer knee pain from IT band friction

What Is IT Band Syndrome?

Iliotibial band syndrome (ITBS) is an overuse injury causing pain on the outer side of the knee. The iliotibial band is a thick band of tissue running from the hip to below the knee. ITBS is one of the most common causes of lateral knee pain in runners and cyclists.1

Anatomy

  • IT band runs from hip to tibia
  • Crosses the lateral femoral epicondyle at the knee
  • Repetitive friction causes irritation
  • Pain typically at ~30 degrees knee flexion

Symptoms2

  • Sharp or burning pain on outer knee
  • Pain worse during activity (running, cycling)
  • Pain going downhill or downstairs
  • Clicking or popping sensation
  • May start mild, worsens with continued activity
  • Tenderness over lateral knee

Risk Factors

  • Rapid increase in training
  • Running on banked surfaces
  • Weak hip abductors
  • Leg length discrepancy
  • High weekly mileage
  • Worn-out shoes

Treatment3

Initial Management

  • Rest from aggravating activities
  • Ice application
  • NSAIDs
  • Activity modification

Rehabilitation

  • Hip strengthening (especially abductors)
  • IT band stretching
  • Foam rolling
  • Gait analysis and correction
  • Gradual return to activity

Other Options

  • Corticosteroid injection
  • Physical therapy
  • Surgery (rarely needed)

Prevention

  • Gradual training increases
  • Hip strengthening exercises
  • Proper footwear
  • Vary running surfaces
  • Adequate warm-up and cool-down
