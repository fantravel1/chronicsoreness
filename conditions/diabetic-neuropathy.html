Diabetic Neuropathy

Nerve damage caused by diabetes

What Is Diabetic Neuropathy?

Diabetic neuropathy is nerve damage that occurs as a complication of diabetes. High blood sugar over time can injure nerve fibers throughout the body, but most commonly affects the legs and feet. It affects up to 50% of people with diabetes.1

Types

  • Peripheral neuropathy - Most common; affects feet and legs first, then hands and arms
  • Autonomic neuropathy - Affects involuntary functions (digestion, bladder, heart rate)
  • Proximal neuropathy - Affects hips, thighs, buttocks
  • Focal neuropathy - Sudden weakness of one nerve or group

Peripheral Neuropathy Symptoms2

  • Numbness or reduced sensation
  • Tingling or burning sensations
  • Sharp, shooting pain
  • Increased sensitivity to touch
  • Muscle weakness
  • Loss of balance
  • Foot ulcers and infections

Risk Factors

  • Poor blood sugar control
  • Long duration of diabetes
  • Kidney disease
  • Overweight/obesity
  • Smoking

Treatment3

Blood Sugar Control (Most Important)

  • Tight glucose control slows progression
  • May not reverse damage already done

Pain Management

  • Duloxetine (Cymbalta) - FDA approved
  • Pregabalin (Lyrica) - FDA approved
  • Gabapentin
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • Topical treatments (capsaicin, lidocaine)

Foot Care

  • Daily foot inspections
  • Proper footwear
  • Regular podiatry visits
  • Prompt treatment of injuries

Prevention

  • Maintain good blood sugar control
  • Regular exercise
  • Healthy diet
  • Don't smoke
  • Limit alcohol
