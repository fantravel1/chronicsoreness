Morton's Neuroma

Ball-of-foot nerve pain

What Is Morton's Neuroma?

Morton's neuroma is a painful condition affecting the ball of the foot, typically between the third and fourth toes. It involves thickening of tissue around one of the nerves leading to the toes. It can feel like standing on a pebble or having a fold in your sock.1

Causes and Risk Factors2

  • High heels (compress forefoot)
  • Tight, narrow shoes
  • High-impact activities
  • Foot deformities (bunions, flat feet)
  • Repetitive stress
  • More common in women

Symptoms

  • Sharp, burning pain in ball of foot
  • Feels like standing on a pebble
  • Numbness or tingling in toes
  • Pain worse with walking, especially in shoes
  • Relief when removing shoes
  • No visible lump

Diagnosis3

  • Physical examination
  • Mulder's click (clicking sensation)
  • Ultrasound often diagnostic
  • MRI in some cases
  • X-rays to rule out other conditions

Treatment

Self-Care

  • Wider shoes with low heels
  • Metatarsal pads
  • Rest from aggravating activities
  • Ice for pain

Medical Treatment

  • Custom orthotics
  • Anti-inflammatory medications
  • Corticosteroid injections
  • Alcohol sclerosing injections
  • Physical therapy

Surgical

  • Neurectomy (nerve removal)
  • Decompression surgery
  • When conservative treatment fails
  • May cause permanent numbness

Prevention

  • Wear shoes with wide toe box
  • Avoid high heels
  • Use padding when needed
  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Address foot mechanics issues

Prognosis

  • Many improve with conservative care
  • Surgery usually effective
  • May recur if footwear issues not addressed
  • Early treatment typically better outcomes
