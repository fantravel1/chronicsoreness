Morton's Neuroma

A nerve condition causing ball-of-foot pain

What Is Morton's Neuroma?

Morton's neuroma is a painful condition affecting the ball of the foot, most commonly between the third and fourth toes. It involves thickening of tissue around a nerve leading to the toes.1

Symptoms

  • Pain in ball of foot when walking
  • Feeling like standing on a pebble
  • Burning, tingling, or numbness
  • Pain radiating to toes
  • Symptoms worse with tight shoes or heels
  • Relief when removing shoe

Causes and Risk Factors2

  • High heels or tight, narrow shoes
  • High-impact activities (running, court sports)
  • Foot deformities (bunions, hammertoes, flat feet)
  • Repetitive irritation
  • More common in women

Diagnosis

  • Physical examination
  • Mulder's click test
  • Ultrasound
  • MRI if needed

Treatment3

Conservative (First Line)

  • Wide, comfortable shoes
  • Avoid high heels
  • Metatarsal pads
  • Custom orthotics
  • Ice and NSAIDs
  • Rest from aggravating activities

Interventional

  • Corticosteroid injections
  • Alcohol sclerosing injections

Surgical

  • Neurectomy (nerve removal)
  • Decompression surgery
