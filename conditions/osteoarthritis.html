Osteoarthritis

The most common form of arthritis

What Is Osteoarthritis?

Osteoarthritis (OA) is a degenerative joint disease characterized by breakdown of cartilage, the protective tissue covering bone ends in joints. It affects over 32 million adults in the US and is a leading cause of disability.1

Commonly Affected Joints

  • Knees (most common)
  • Hips
  • Hands (fingers, base of thumb)
  • Spine (facet joints)
  • Big toe

Symptoms2

  • Joint pain with activity
  • Stiffness, especially in morning (<30 minutes)
  • Reduced range of motion
  • Crepitus (grinding, crackling sounds)
  • Swelling (sometimes)
  • Joint instability
  • Bony enlargement

Risk Factors

  • Age (most important)
  • Obesity (especially for knee OA)
  • Previous joint injury
  • Repetitive joint stress
  • Genetics
  • Female sex
  • Muscle weakness

Treatment3

Non-Pharmacological (First Line)

  • Exercise - Strengthening, aerobic, flexibility
  • Weight loss - If overweight
  • Physical therapy
  • Assistive devices - Canes, braces
  • Heat/cold

Pharmacological

  • Topical NSAIDs (first-line for knee/hand)
  • Oral NSAIDs, acetaminophen
  • Duloxetine (for knee OA)
  • Intra-articular injections (corticosteroids, hyaluronic acid)

Surgical

  • Joint replacement (when conservative fails)
  • Very effective for severe knee/hip OA

Supplements

  • Glucosamine/chondroitin - evidence mixed
  • May help some people
  • Generally safe to try
