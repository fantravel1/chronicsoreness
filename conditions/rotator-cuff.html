Rotator Cuff Problems

Understanding shoulder pain from rotator cuff issues

What Is the Rotator Cuff?

The rotator cuff is a group of four muscles and their tendons that stabilize the shoulder joint and allow arm movement. Problems range from inflammation (tendinitis) to partial or complete tears, and are a common cause of shoulder pain.1

Types of Problems

  • Tendinitis/Tendinopathy - Inflammation or degeneration
  • Bursitis - Inflammation of bursa
  • Impingement - Tendons pinched during movement
  • Partial tear - Some tendon fibers torn
  • Full-thickness tear - Complete tear through tendon

Symptoms2

  • Pain with overhead activities
  • Pain at night, especially lying on affected side
  • Weakness with lifting or rotating arm
  • Pain reaching behind back
  • Catching or crackling with movement
  • Limited range of motion

Risk Factors

  • Age (degeneration over 40)
  • Repetitive overhead activities
  • Sports (baseball, tennis, swimming)
  • Occupations with overhead work
  • Acute injury or trauma

Diagnosis

  • Physical examination (special tests)
  • X-rays (rule out other causes)
  • Ultrasound (can see tears)
  • MRI (gold standard for tears)

Treatment3

Conservative (First Line)

  • Rest and activity modification
  • Physical therapy - strengthening, stretching
  • NSAIDs
  • Ice
  • Corticosteroid injections (limited use)

Surgery

  • Considered for large tears, failed conservative treatment
  • Arthroscopic repair common
  • Rehabilitation crucial after surgery

Prevention

  • Shoulder strengthening exercises
  • Proper technique in sports/work
  • Avoid repetitive overhead strain
  • Maintain flexibility
