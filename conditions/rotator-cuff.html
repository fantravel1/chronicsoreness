Rotator Cuff Injuries

Understanding shoulder tendon problems

What Is the Rotator Cuff?

The rotator cuff is a group of four muscles and their tendons that surround the shoulder joint, keeping the head of the upper arm bone (humerus) firmly within the shallow socket of the shoulder. These muscles allow you to lift and rotate your arm. Rotator cuff injuries are among the most common causes of shoulder pain.1

Types of Rotator Cuff Problems2

Tendinitis

  • Inflammation of the tendons
  • Often from repetitive overhead activities
  • Usually responds to conservative treatment

Bursitis

  • Inflammation of the bursa (fluid-filled sac)
  • Often occurs with tendinitis
  • Causes pain with movement

Impingement

  • Tendons get pinched between bones
  • Pain with overhead movements
  • Can lead to tears if untreated

Tears

  • Partial or complete tears
  • Can be acute (injury) or degenerative (wear)
  • May or may not need surgery

Symptoms

  • Pain at rest and at night
  • Pain when lifting or lowering arm
  • Weakness when lifting or rotating arm
  • Crackling sensation with movement
  • Pain reaching behind back
  • Pain lying on affected shoulder

Risk Factors

  • Age (more common after 40)
  • Repetitive overhead work or sports
  • Construction, painting, carpentry
  • Baseball, tennis, swimming
  • Family history

Treatment3

Conservative (Often First-Line)

  • Rest and activity modification
  • Ice and heat
  • Physical therapy
  • NSAIDs
  • Corticosteroid injections

Physical Therapy

  • Strengthening exercises
  • Stretching
  • Posture correction
  • Scapular stabilization

Surgery

  • May be needed for complete tears
  • Often arthroscopic (minimally invasive)
  • Requires extensive rehabilitation

When to See a Doctor

  • Sudden severe pain after injury
  • Inability to raise arm
  • Significant weakness
  • Pain that doesn't improve with rest
  • Night pain that disrupts sleep
