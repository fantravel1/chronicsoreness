Rotator Cuff Problems

Common source of shoulder pain

What Is the Rotator Cuff?

The rotator cuff is a group of four muscles and tendons that stabilize the shoulder joint and allow arm movement. Problems range from inflammation (tendinopathy) to partial or complete tears, and are a leading cause of shoulder pain.1

The Four Muscles

  • Supraspinatus (most commonly injured)
  • Infraspinatus
  • Teres minor
  • Subscapularis

Types of Problems2

  • Tendinopathy - Degeneration without tear
  • Partial tear - Incomplete tear of tendon
  • Full-thickness tear - Complete tear
  • Impingement - Tendons pinched during movement

Symptoms

  • Pain at front/side of shoulder
  • Pain at night (lying on shoulder)
  • Weakness raising arm
  • Pain reaching behind back
  • Painful arc during arm movement

Treatment3

Conservative (First Line)

  • Physical therapy (most important)
  • Activity modification
  • NSAIDs
  • Corticosteroid injections (limited use)

Surgery

  • For large/complete tears
  • When conservative treatment fails
  • Arthroscopic repair common
  • Recovery takes months

Prognosis

Many rotator cuff problems improve with physical therapy. Even some tears can be managed without surgery.
