Spondylolisthesis

When vertebrae slip out of alignment

What Is Spondylolisthesis?

Spondylolisthesis occurs when one vertebra slips forward over the vertebra below it. This misalignment can compress nerves and cause pain. It most commonly affects the lower lumbar spine.1

Types

  • Isthmic - Stress fracture in pars interarticularis
  • Degenerative - Age-related disc and joint degeneration
  • Congenital - Birth defect in spine formation
  • Traumatic - From injury or fracture
  • Pathologic - Disease weakening bone

Grading System2

  • Grade I - Less than 25% slippage
  • Grade II - 25-50% slippage
  • Grade III - 50-75% slippage
  • Grade IV - 75-100% slippage
  • Grade V - Complete slip (spondyloptosis)

Symptoms

  • Lower back pain
  • Muscle tightness (hamstrings)
  • Pain, numbness in legs
  • Difficulty walking
  • Worsened by standing/walking
  • Some people have no symptoms

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Physical therapy (core strengthening)
  • Activity modification
  • Anti-inflammatory medications
  • Epidural steroid injections
  • Bracing (sometimes)

Surgical

  • Spinal fusion (for severe cases)
  • Decompression surgery
  • Reserved for failed conservative treatment
