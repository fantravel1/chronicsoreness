Spondylolisthesis

When one vertebra slips forward over another

What Is Spondylolisthesis?

Spondylolisthesis occurs when one vertebra slips forward (anterolisthesis) or backward (retrolisthesis) relative to the vertebra below it. This can cause back pain and nerve compression. Most commonly occurs at L4-L5 or L5-S1.1

Types

  • Isthmic - Stress fracture of pars interarticularis
  • Degenerative - Age-related arthritis/instability
  • Congenital - Born with abnormal spine formation
  • Traumatic - From injury
  • Pathologic - From disease (tumor, infection)

Grading (Meyerding)2

  • Grade I - 0-25% slip
  • Grade II - 25-50% slip
  • Grade III - 50-75% slip
  • Grade IV - 75-100% slip
  • Spondyloptosis - >100% slip

Symptoms

  • Low back pain
  • Pain worse with extension/standing
  • Hamstring tightness
  • Leg pain if nerves compressed
  • Numbness, weakness in legs
  • Some people have no symptoms

Diagnosis

  • X-rays (standing, flexion/extension)
  • MRI for soft tissue/nerve assessment
  • CT for bony detail

Treatment3

Conservative (First Line)

  • Physical therapy - core strengthening
  • NSAIDs
  • Activity modification
  • Bracing (in some cases)
  • Epidural injections for leg pain

Surgery

  • For persistent symptoms, high-grade slips
  • Decompression with or without fusion
