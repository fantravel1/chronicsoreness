Spondylolisthesis

Understanding vertebral slippage

What Is Spondylolisthesis?

Spondylolisthesis occurs when one vertebra slips forward over the one below it. This can occur anywhere in the spine but is most common in the lower back (lumbar spine). It may cause back pain, leg pain, and stiffness, though some people have no symptoms.1

Types2

Isthmic

  • Defect in part of vertebra (pars)
  • Often from repetitive stress
  • Common in young athletes

Degenerative

  • From wear and tear
  • More common over age 50
  • Arthritis weakens joints

Congenital

  • Born with spine abnormality
  • Present from birth

Other Types

  • Traumatic (from injury)
  • Pathological (disease weakens bone)
  • Post-surgical

Grading3

  • Grade I: 0-25% slippage
  • Grade II: 25-50% slippage
  • Grade III: 50-75% slippage
  • Grade IV: 75-100% slippage
  • Grade V: Vertebra completely off (spondyloptosis)

Symptoms

  • Low back pain
  • Stiffness
  • Leg pain (if nerves affected)
  • Numbness or tingling in legs
  • Tight hamstrings
  • Waddling gait (severe cases)
  • Some have no symptoms

Diagnosis

  • Physical examination
  • X-rays (standing/flexion-extension)
  • CT scan
  • MRI (assess nerves)

Treatment

Conservative

  • Activity modification
  • Physical therapy
  • Core strengthening
  • NSAIDs
  • Epidural injections
  • Bracing (sometimes)

Surgical

  • When conservative treatment fails
  • Progressive slippage
  • Severe neurological symptoms
  • Decompression and/or fusion

Living with Spondylolisthesis

  • Core strengthening important
  • Avoid extreme extension
  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Good posture
  • May need activity modifications
