Tension-Type Headaches

The most common type of headache

What Are Tension Headaches?

Tension-type headaches (TTH) are the most common type of headache, affecting up to 80% of people at some point. They cause a dull, aching pain often described as a band tightening around the head. While usually mild to moderate, they can become chronic and significantly impact quality of life.1

Types2

Episodic

  • Less than 15 days per month
  • Can be infrequent or frequent
  • More easily treated

Chronic

  • 15+ days per month for 3+ months
  • More difficult to treat
  • May evolve from episodic

Symptoms3

  • Dull, aching head pain
  • Sensation of tightness or pressure
  • Both sides of head affected
  • Mild to moderate intensity
  • Not worsened by routine activity
  • May have mild light or sound sensitivity
  • No nausea or vomiting (unlike migraine)

Triggers

  • Stress and anxiety
  • Poor posture
  • Eye strain
  • Jaw clenching
  • Lack of sleep
  • Skipped meals
  • Dehydration
  • Muscle tension

Treatment

Acute

  • OTC pain relievers (acetaminophen, ibuprofen)
  • Limit to 2 days per week (prevent medication overuse)
  • Rest in quiet, dark room
  • Hot or cold compress

Preventive

  • Tricyclic antidepressants (amitriptyline)
  • Other antidepressants
  • Muscle relaxants (sometimes)

Non-Drug Approaches

  • Stress management
  • Physical therapy
  • Biofeedback
  • Relaxation techniques
  • Acupuncture
  • Massage

Prevention

  • Manage stress
  • Regular sleep schedule
  • Good posture
  • Regular meals
  • Stay hydrated
  • Regular exercise
  • Take breaks from screens

Medication Overuse Warning

  • Frequent pain reliever use can worsen headaches
  • Can lead to "rebound" or medication overuse headache
  • Limit acute medications to 2 days/week
  • Preventive treatment better for frequent headaches
