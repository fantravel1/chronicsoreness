Piriformis Syndrome

When a deep hip muscle causes sciatic-type pain

What Is Piriformis Syndrome?

Piriformis syndrome is a neuromuscular condition where the piriformis muscleâ€”a small muscle deep in the buttockâ€”irritates or compresses the sciatic nerve. This causes pain, numbness, and tingling along the back of the leg, similar to sciatica from spinal causes.1

Anatomy

  • Piriformis muscle runs from sacrum to hip
  • Sciatic nerve typically passes beneath it
  • In some people, nerve passes through the muscle
  • Muscle tightness or spasm can irritate nerve

Symptoms

  • Deep buttock pain
  • Pain radiating down back of leg
  • Pain worse with sitting
  • Pain with climbing stairs or inclines
  • Tenderness in buttock
  • Difficulty sitting for prolonged periods
  • Pain with hip movements

Causes

  • Muscle tightness - From overuse or prolonged sitting
  • Trauma - Fall on buttock, car accident
  • Anatomical variation - Nerve passing through muscle
  • Repetitive activities - Running, cycling
  • Sitting on wallet - Classic trigger

Diagnosis

Diagnosis is clinical; no definitive test exists:2

  • Physical examination with provocative maneuvers
  • FAIR test (Flexion, Adduction, Internal Rotation)
  • MRI to rule out spinal causes
  • Diagnostic injection may confirm

Treatment

Conservative Treatment

  • Stretching - Piriformis stretches multiple times daily3
  • Physical therapy - Stretching, strengthening, posture
  • Heat or ice - For symptom relief
  • NSAIDs - For pain and inflammation
  • Activity modification - Avoid prolonged sitting
  • Foam rolling - Self-massage of piriformis

Injections

  • Corticosteroid injection into piriformis
  • Botulinum toxin injection (for muscle relaxation)

Surgery

Rarely needed; piriformis release surgery for refractory cases

Self-Care

  • Regular piriformis stretching
  • Avoid sitting on hard surfaces
  • Remove wallet from back pocket
  • Take breaks from sitting
  • Hip strengthening exercises
