Piriformis Syndrome

When the piriformis muscle irritates the sciatic nerve

What Is Piriformis Syndrome?

Piriformis syndrome is a neuromuscular disorder occurring when the piriformis muscle, located deep in the buttock, irritates or compresses the sciatic nerve. It can mimic sciatica from lumbar disc problems.1

Symptoms

  • Deep buttock pain
  • Pain radiating down back of leg
  • Worse with sitting
  • Pain with climbing stairs
  • Tenderness in buttock
  • Pain with hip movements
  • Tingling or numbness in leg

Causes2

  • Piriformis muscle spasm
  • Prolonged sitting
  • Direct trauma to buttock
  • Anatomical variation (sciatic nerve through muscle)
  • Overuse from running or cycling
  • Leg length discrepancy

Diagnosis

  • Clinical examination
  • FAIR test (positive)
  • Pace sign
  • Freiberg sign
  • MRI to rule out other causes
  • Diagnostic injection

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Piriformis stretching
  • Physical therapy
  • Avoid prolonged sitting
  • Ice and heat
  • NSAIDs
  • Muscle relaxants

Interventional

  • Piriformis injection (steroid/local)
  • Botox injection
  • Dry needling

Surgery (Rare)

  • Piriformis release
