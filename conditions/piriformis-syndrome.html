Piriformis Syndrome

Deep buttock pain with sciatica

What Is Piriformis Syndrome?

Piriformis syndrome occurs when the piriformis muscle in the buttock irritates or compresses the sciatic nerve. This causes buttock pain and sciatica-like symptoms radiating down the leg. It accounts for about 6-8% of sciatica cases.1

Anatomy

  • Piriformis is a small muscle deep in the buttock
  • Runs from sacrum to top of femur
  • Sciatic nerve runs beneath (or through) this muscle
  • Muscle spasm or tightness can irritate the nerve

Symptoms2

  • Deep aching in buttock
  • Pain radiating down back of leg
  • Worse with sitting (especially on hard surfaces)
  • Pain climbing stairs or walking uphill
  • Tingling or numbness in buttock or leg
  • Difficulty sitting for long periods

Causes

  • Prolonged sitting
  • Trauma to buttock
  • Overuse (running, cycling)
  • Anatomical variations
  • Hip surgery complications

Diagnosis

  • Clinical examination
  • Reproduction of symptoms with specific tests
  • MRI to rule out other causes
  • Often a diagnosis of exclusion

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Piriformis stretching exercises
  • Physical therapy
  • Avoiding prolonged sitting
  • NSAIDs
  • Ice or heat
  • Massage therapy

Injections

  • Piriformis muscle injection
  • Botox injection (for refractory cases)

Self-Care Tips

  • Stretch piriformis regularly
  • Use cushion when sitting
  • Avoid sitting on wallet
  • Regular movement breaks
