Ankylosing Spondylitis

Inflammatory arthritis primarily affecting the spine

What Is Ankylosing Spondylitis?

Ankylosing spondylitis (AS) is a type of inflammatory arthritis that primarily affects the spine and sacroiliac joints. Over time, it can cause vertebrae to fuse, leading to reduced flexibility. It typically starts before age 45.1

Symptoms

  • Chronic back pain and stiffness
  • Worse in morning and with rest
  • Better with movement and exercise
  • SI joint pain (buttock pain)
  • Fatigue
  • Can affect other joints (hips, shoulders)
  • Eye inflammation (uveitis)
  • Enthesitis (inflammation where tendons attach)

Diagnosis2

  • Clinical history and examination
  • Inflammatory markers (ESR, CRP)
  • HLA-B27 genetic marker (in ~90%)
  • X-ray or MRI of SI joints
  • Often delayed diagnosis (average 8-10 years)

Treatment

Medications

  • NSAIDs - First-line; continuous use may slow progression
  • Biologics - TNF inhibitors, IL-17 inhibitors
  • DMARDs - For peripheral joint involvement

Non-Drug Treatment

  • Exercise - Essential; maintains flexibility and posture3
  • Physical therapy
  • Swimming and water exercise
  • Good posture awareness
  • Not smoking - Worsens outcomes

Prognosis

  • Variable course
  • Modern treatments have improved outcomes
  • Early treatment may prevent fusion
  • Exercise is protective
