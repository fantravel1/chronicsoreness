Adhesive Capsulitis

Also known as frozen shoulder

What Is Adhesive Capsulitis?

Adhesive capsulitis, commonly known as frozen shoulder, is a condition characterized by stiffness and pain in the shoulder joint. It occurs when the shoulder capsule thickens and tightens, limiting movement.1

Three Stages2

Stage 1: Freezing (2-9 months)

  • Gradual onset of pain
  • Pain often worse at night
  • Motion begins to decrease

Stage 2: Frozen (4-12 months)

  • Pain may decrease somewhat
  • Significant stiffness
  • Difficulty with daily activities

Stage 3: Thawing (5-24 months)

  • Gradual return of motion
  • Pain continues to decrease

Risk Factors

  • Diabetes (2-4x higher risk)
  • Thyroid disorders
  • Age 40-60
  • Women more affected
  • Shoulder injury or surgery
  • Immobilization

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Physical therapy (gentle stretching)
  • Heat before stretching
  • NSAIDs for pain
  • Corticosteroid injections

Invasive Options

  • Hydrodilatation (joint distension)
  • Manipulation under anesthesia
  • Arthroscopic capsular release

Prognosis

Most cases resolve within 1-3 years, though some residual stiffness may persist.
