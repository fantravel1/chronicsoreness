Adhesive Capsulitis

Frozen shoulder - stiffness and pain

What Is Frozen Shoulder?

Adhesive capsulitis, commonly known as frozen shoulder, is a condition where the shoulder joint becomes stiff and painful, severely limiting movement. It typically develops gradually, worsens, then eventually resolvesâ€”often over 1-3 years.1

Stages2

Freezing Stage (2-9 months)

  • Pain develops gradually
  • Pain often worse at night
  • Movement becomes limited

Frozen Stage (4-12 months)

  • Pain may improve
  • Stiffness is worst
  • Daily activities very difficult

Thawing Stage (5-24 months)

  • Movement gradually improves
  • Pain decreases
  • Return to near-normal function

Symptoms

  • Shoulder pain (dull or aching)
  • Pain worse at night
  • Progressive stiffness
  • Limited range of motion
  • Difficulty with daily tasks

Risk Factors

  • Age 40-60
  • Female sex
  • Diabetes (higher risk)
  • Thyroid disorders
  • Prolonged immobility
  • Previous shoulder injury or surgery

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Physical therapy (gentle stretching)
  • Heat before stretching
  • NSAIDs
  • Corticosteroid injections
  • Home exercises

Procedures

  • Hydrodilatation (injection to stretch capsule)
  • Manipulation under anesthesia
  • Arthroscopic capsular release

Self-Care Tips

  • Do prescribed exercises consistently
  • Apply heat before stretching
  • Ice after exercises if needed
  • Be patientâ€”recovery takes time
  • Sleep on unaffected side with pillow

Prognosis

Most cases resolve within 1-3 years. Some residual stiffness may remain. Treatment may speed recovery.
