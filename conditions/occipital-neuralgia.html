Occipital Neuralgia

Stabbing pain at the back of head

What Is Occipital Neuralgia?

Occipital neuralgia is a type of headache caused by irritation or injury to the occipital nerves, which run from the upper neck to the back of the head. It causes distinctive shooting, electrical-type pain in the back of the head and scalp.1

Symptoms2

  • Shooting, stabbing, or electric shock-like pain
  • Pain starts at base of skull, radiates up
  • Usually one-sided (can be both)
  • Scalp tenderness
  • Pain behind eye
  • Light sensitivity
  • Pain with neck movement

Causes

  • Neck muscle tightness
  • Whiplash or trauma
  • Arthritis of upper cervical spine
  • Compression of nerve
  • Diabetes
  • Gout
  • Infection
  • Often no clear cause found

Diagnosis

  • Clinical examination
  • Tenderness over occipital nerves
  • Response to nerve block (diagnostic)
  • MRI to rule out other causes

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Heat or ice
  • Rest
  • Massage
  • Physical therapy
  • NSAIDs
  • Muscle relaxants

Medications

  • Anticonvulsants (gabapentin, pregabalin)
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • Muscle relaxants

Procedures

  • Occipital nerve block (often diagnostic and therapeutic)
  • Botox injections
  • Radiofrequency ablation
  • Occipital nerve stimulation (severe cases)

Self-Care

  • Gentle neck stretches
  • Good posture
  • Avoid triggers
  • Stress management
  • Regular sleep schedule
