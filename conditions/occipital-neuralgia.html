Occipital Neuralgia

Nerve pain at the back of the head

What Is Occipital Neuralgia?

Occipital neuralgia is a condition caused by irritation or injury to the occipital nerves, which run from the top of the spinal cord through the scalp. It causes severe, sharp, shooting, or electric shock-like pain in the back of the head, upper neck, and behind the ears.1

Symptoms2

  • Sharp, shooting, or electric pain
  • Usually one-sided
  • Pain in back of head, upper neck
  • Pain behind eyes
  • Scalp tenderness
  • Light sensitivity
  • Pain triggered by neck movement or touch

Causes

  • Tight neck muscles
  • Neck injury (whiplash)
  • Cervical disc disease
  • Arthritis
  • Tumors (rare)
  • Inflammation
  • Often cause unknown

Diagnosis3

  • Physical examination
  • Reproduction of pain with pressure
  • Response to diagnostic nerve block
  • MRI may rule out other causes
  • Often diagnosed clinically

Treatment

Conservative

  • Heat therapy
  • Massage
  • Rest
  • Physical therapy
  • NSAIDs

Medications

  • Muscle relaxants
  • Anticonvulsants (gabapentin, pregabalin)
  • Tricyclic antidepressants

Procedures

  • Occipital nerve blocks
  • Botox injections
  • Radiofrequency ablation
  • Occipital nerve stimulation
  • Surgery (rare)

Self-Care

  • Apply heat to neck and back of head
  • Gentle neck stretches
  • Good posture
  • Avoid triggers (if known)
  • Rest in quiet, dark room during attacks

Differentiating from Migraine

  • Occipital neuralgia: sharp, shooting pain
  • Migraine: throbbing, pulsating pain
  • Can coexist
  • Treatment may overlap
