Occipital Neuralgia

Sharp pain in the back of the head

What Is Occipital Neuralgia?

Occipital neuralgia is a condition characterized by chronic pain in the upper neck, back of the head, and behind the eyes. It occurs when the occipital nervesâ€”which run from the top of the spinal cord through the scalpâ€”become irritated or injured. The pain is often described as sharp, shooting, or electric shock-like.1

Symptoms2

  • Intense pain starting at skull base
  • Sharp, shooting, electric-like sensations
  • Pain radiating to top of head
  • Pain behind the eyes
  • Scalp tenderness
  • Sensitivity to light
  • Pain with neck movement
  • Usually one-sided (can be both)

Causes and Triggers

  • Muscle tightness in neck
  • Neck injury or whiplash
  • Compression of nerves
  • Arthritis in cervical spine
  • Tumors (rare)
  • Infection or inflammation
  • Diabetes-related nerve damage
  • Sometimes no clear cause

Diagnosis3

  • Clinical history and examination
  • Tenderness over occipital nerves
  • Diagnostic nerve block
  • MRI to rule out other causes
  • Often confused with migraines

Treatment Options

Conservative

  • Heat/ice application
  • Massage therapy
  • Physical therapy
  • Rest and posture correction

Medications

  • Anti-inflammatory drugs
  • Muscle relaxants
  • Anticonvulsants
  • Tricyclic antidepressants

Interventional

  • Occipital nerve blocks
  • Botox injections
  • Radiofrequency ablation
  • Occipital nerve stimulation
  • Surgery (rare, severe cases)

Differentiating from Migraines

  • Location tends to be more posterior
  • Pain is more shooting/electric
  • Less nausea than migraines
  • Responds to nerve blocks
  • Can co-occur with migraines
