Occipital Neuralgia

Understanding this distinct cause of severe headaches

What Is Occipital Neuralgia?

Occipital neuralgia is a condition characterized by chronic pain in the upper neck, back of the head, and behind the eyes. It results from irritation or injury to the occipital nerves, which run from the top of the spinal cord through the scalp.1

Symptoms

  • Intense, sharp, jabbing pain starting at base of skull
  • Pain radiating to back of head, often one-sided
  • Electric shock-like sensations
  • Aching, burning, throbbing pain between episodes
  • Pain behind the eye on affected side
  • Sensitivity to light
  • Tender scalp
  • Pain with neck movement

Causes

  • Muscle tension - Tight neck muscles entrapping nerves
  • Trauma - Whiplash, head injury
  • Cervical disc disease
  • Arthritis - Upper cervical spine
  • Tumors - Rarely
  • Gout
  • Diabetes
  • Blood vessel inflammation

Diagnosis

Diagnosis is primarily clinical:2

  • Characteristic symptom pattern
  • Tenderness over occipital nerves
  • Diagnostic nerve block (if pain relieved, confirms diagnosis)
  • MRI to rule out structural causes

Treatment

Conservative

  • Heat - Applied to neck
  • Physical therapy - Neck stretching and strengthening
  • Massage - Neck and suboccipital muscles
  • NSAIDs
  • Muscle relaxants

Medications

  • Anticonvulsants (gabapentin, carbamazepine)
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • Muscle relaxants

Interventional

  • Occipital nerve blocks - Local anesthetic Â± steroid3
  • Botox injections
  • Pulsed radiofrequency
  • Occipital nerve stimulation - For refractory cases

Self-Care

  • Apply heat to back of neck
  • Gentle neck stretches
  • Rest in quiet, dark room during flares
  • Massage base of skull
  • Maintain good posture
