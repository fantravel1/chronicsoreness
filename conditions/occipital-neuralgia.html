Occipital Neuralgia

Sharp, shooting pain from irritated occipital nerves

What Is Occipital Neuralgia?

Occipital neuralgia is a condition characterized by chronic pain in the upper neck, back of the head, and behind the eyes. It occurs when the occipital nerves are irritated or damaged.1

Symptoms

  • Sharp, shooting, electric-like pain
  • Pain from base of skull to top of head
  • Pain behind eyes
  • Sensitivity of scalp
  • Pain on one or both sides
  • Triggered by neck movement or pressure

Causes2

  • Tight neck muscles
  • Neck injury or trauma
  • Cervical disc disease
  • Arthritis in cervical spine
  • Tumors or infections (rare)
  • Diabetes
  • Blood vessel inflammation

Diagnosis

  • Clinical examination
  • Tenderness over occipital nerves
  • Diagnostic nerve block
  • MRI to rule out structural causes

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Heat and massage
  • Physical therapy
  • NSAIDs and muscle relaxants
  • Rest

Interventional

  • Occipital nerve blocks
  • Botox injections
  • Radiofrequency ablation
  • Occipital nerve stimulation
