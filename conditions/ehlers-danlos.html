Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome

Understanding connective tissue disorders

What Is EDS?

Ehlers-Danlos syndrome (EDS) is a group of inherited connective tissue disorders affecting collagen. This leads to joint hypermobility, stretchy skin, and tissue fragility. Chronic pain is one of the most common and challenging aspects of living with EDS, affecting quality of life significantly.1

Types of EDS2

Hypermobile EDS (hEDS)

  • Most common type
  • Joint hypermobility predominates
  • Chronic pain often severe
  • No known genetic test yet

Classical EDS

  • Very stretchy, fragile skin
  • Easy bruising
  • Poor wound healing

Vascular EDS

  • Most serious type
  • Risk of organ/vessel rupture
  • Requires monitoring

Other Types

  • Kyphoscoliotic
  • Arthrochalasia
  • Dermatosparaxis
  • Several rarer forms

Common Symptoms3

Joint/Musculoskeletal

  • Joint hypermobility
  • Frequent dislocations/subluxations
  • Chronic joint and muscle pain
  • Early osteoarthritis
  • Muscle weakness

Skin

  • Soft, velvety skin
  • Stretchy skin (varies by type)
  • Easy bruising
  • Poor wound healing

Other Features

  • Fatigue
  • Dysautonomia (POTS)
  • GI issues
  • Mast cell activation
  • TMJ dysfunction

Pain in EDS

  • Often severe and widespread
  • Multiple types (joint, muscle, nerve)
  • Frequently undertreated
  • Can be disabling
  • Often dismissed by providers

Diagnosis

  • Clinical criteria for hEDS
  • Genetic testing for some types
  • Beighton score for hypermobility
  • Often missed or delayed
  • Specialist evaluation helpful

Management

Physical Therapy

  • Essential for management
  • Strengthening to protect joints
  • Proprioception training
  • Avoid overstretching

Pain Management

  • Often requires multimodal approach
  • Medications (cautiously)
  • Bracing and taping
  • Pacing activities

Lifestyle

  • Joint protection strategies
  • Activity modification
  • Assistive devices if needed

Finding Knowledgeable Care

  • Geneticists for diagnosis
  • Physical therapists familiar with EDS
  • Pain specialists
  • Patient advocacy groups
