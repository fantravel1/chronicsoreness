Ehlers-Danlos Syndromes

A group of inherited connective tissue disorders

What Are Ehlers-Danlos Syndromes?

Ehlers-Danlos syndromes (EDS) are a group of inherited connective tissue disorders affecting the body's collagen. The 13 types share features like joint hypermobility, skin hyperextensibility, and tissue fragility.1

Types of EDS

  • Hypermobile EDS (hEDS) - Most common; primarily joint hypermobility
  • Classical EDS (cEDS) - Skin hyperextensibility, fragility, scarring
  • Vascular EDS (vEDS) - Thin skin, arterial/organ fragility (serious)
  • Kyphoscoliotic EDS - Severe muscle weakness, curvature
  • Plus 9 other rare types

Common Symptoms

  • Joint hypermobility
  • Chronic pain (joints and muscles)
  • Frequent dislocations/subluxations
  • Soft, stretchy skin
  • Easy bruising
  • Poor wound healing
  • Chronic fatigue
  • Dysautonomia (often POTS)
  • GI problems

Diagnosis2

  • Clinical evaluation (2017 criteria for hEDS)
  • Genetic testing (for most types except hEDS)
  • Family history
  • Often delayed diagnosis

Management

  • Physical therapy - Strengthening, stability (crucial)
  • Pain management - Multimodal approach
  • Joint protection - Bracing when needed
  • Treat comorbidities - POTS, GI issues, etc.
  • Pacing activities
  • Psychological support

Important Notes

  • No cure; focus is management
  • Avoid overstretching hypermobile joints
  • vEDS requires special precautions
  • Multidisciplinary care is best
