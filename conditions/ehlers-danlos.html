Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome

Living with connective tissue disorders

Key Takeaways

  • EDS is a group of inherited connective tissue disorders
  • Hypermobile EDS (hEDS) is the most common type and causes chronic pain
  • Joint protection and strengthening are essential for management
  • Multidisciplinary care provides the best outcomes

What Is Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome?

Ehlers-Danlos Syndromes (EDS) are a group of inherited disorders affecting connective tissueâ€”the "glue" that provides structure and support throughout the body. This affects skin, joints, blood vessels, and other organs. The most common form, hypermobile EDS (hEDS), causes joint hypermobility, chronic pain, and fatigue.1

Types of EDS

  • Hypermobile EDS (hEDS): Most common; joint hypermobility, pain
  • Classical EDS (cEDS): Very stretchy skin, severe scarring
  • Vascular EDS (vEDS): Rare, serious; affects blood vessels
  • 10+ other rare types with specific features

Symptoms

Symptoms vary by type but often include:3

Joint Symptoms

  • Joint hypermobility ("double-jointed")
  • Chronic joint pain
  • Frequent dislocations and subluxations
  • Joint instability
  • Early-onset arthritis

Skin Features

  • Soft, velvety skin
  • Stretchy skin (varies by type)
  • Easy bruising
  • Poor wound healing
  • Atrophic (thin, papery) scars

Systemic Features

  • Chronic fatigue
  • Chronic pain (often widespread)
  • Dysautonomia/POTS (blood pressure/heart rate issues)
  • GI problems (gastroparesis, IBS-like symptoms)
  • Headaches
  • TMJ dysfunction
  • Pelvic floor dysfunction

Common Comorbidities

  • Postural orthostatic tachycardia syndrome (POTS)
  • Mast cell activation syndrome (MCAS)
  • Chronic fatigue syndrome
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Anxiety and depression

Diagnosis

Clinical Evaluation

  • Detailed medical and family history
  • Physical examination
  • Beighton Score: Measures joint hypermobility
  • Skin assessment
  • Assessment for systemic features

hEDS Diagnostic Criteria (2017)

Requires all three:4

  1. Generalized joint hypermobility (Beighton score)
  2. Two or more features from: systemic manifestations, positive family history, musculoskeletal complications
  3. Exclusion of other conditions

Genetic Testing

  • Available for most EDS types
  • No genetic test yet for hEDS (clinical diagnosis)
  • Important for ruling out vascular EDS

Diagnostic Delay

Many people with EDS wait years for diagnosis because symptoms are attributed to other conditions. If you have unexplained joint hypermobility with pain and other symptoms, seek evaluation from a provider familiar with EDS.

Management

No cure exists, but symptoms can be managed:5

Physical Therapy (Essential)

  • Strengthening to stabilize joints
  • Focus on muscle control, not just flexibility
  • PT experienced with hypermobility is crucial
  • Low-impact exercises
  • Proprioceptive training
  • Avoid overstretching

Joint Protection

  • Learn to avoid hyperextending joints
  • Bracing or taping for unstable joints
  • Ergonomic modifications
  • Activity pacing

Pain Management

  • Multimodal approach recommended
  • NSAIDs for acute pain (caution with frequent use)
  • Pain psychology and coping strategies
  • Heat or cold therapy
  • TENS
  • Caution with opioids (limited evidence, risks)

Managing Comorbidities

  • POTS: Salt, fluids, compression, medications
  • GI issues: Dietary modifications, motility agents
  • Fatigue: Pacing, sleep optimization

Surgery Considerations

  • Higher complication rates with EDS
  • Delayed healing
  • Conservative approaches preferred when possible
  • Experienced surgical team essential if needed

Living with EDS

Daily Strategies

  • Pace activities to avoid boom-bust cycles
  • Use assistive devices without shame
  • Protect joints during daily tasks
  • Plan for rest periods
  • Adapt activities rather than avoiding them

Exercise Guidelines

  • Low-impact exercises: swimming, cycling, Pilates
  • Strength training (controlled, not to fatigue)
  • Avoid high-impact activities and contact sports
  • Don't push into hypermobile range
  • Consistency over intensity

Finding Care

  • Seek providers familiar with EDS
  • Geneticist for diagnosis/type confirmation
  • PT experienced with hypermobility
  • May need multiple specialists
  • Connect with EDS community for provider recommendations

Support and Resources

  • Ehlers-Danlos Society (ehlers-danlos.com)
  • Support groups (in-person and online)
  • Patient education resources
  • Mental health support important
