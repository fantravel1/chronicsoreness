Hand Osteoarthritis

Arthritis affecting the fingers and thumb

What Is Hand Osteoarthritis?

Hand osteoarthritis (OA) is a degenerative joint condition affecting the small joints of the fingers and thumb. It's very common, especially in women over 50. While it can cause pain and stiffness, many people maintain good hand function with proper management.1

Symptoms

  • Pain with grip and pinch
  • Stiffness, especially in morning
  • Swelling around joints
  • Bony bumps on finger joints
  • Reduced grip strength
  • Difficulty with fine motor tasks
  • Joint deformity over time

Risk Factors

  • Age (increases over 40)
  • Female sex
  • Family history
  • Previous hand injury
  • Repetitive hand use
  • Obesity

Diagnosis3

  • Physical examination
  • X-rays (show joint space narrowing, spurs)
  • Usually no blood tests needed
  • Distinguish from rheumatoid arthritis

Treatment

Non-Drug

  • Hand exercises
  • Splints (especially for thumb OA)
  • Heat or cold
  • Joint protection techniques
  • Occupational therapy

Medications

  • Topical NSAIDs (first-line)
  • Oral NSAIDs
  • Acetaminophen
  • Capsaicin cream

Surgery

  • Joint fusion (DIP/PIP)
  • Joint replacement (some joints)
  • Thumb CMC surgery
  • Usually when severe

Self-Management

  • Use larger handles on tools
  • Avoid tight gripping
  • Use two hands when possible
  • Daily hand exercises
  • Warm hands before activity

Hand Exercises

  • Finger bends
  • Finger spreads
  • Thumb circles
  • Make a fist and release
  • Theraputty exercises
