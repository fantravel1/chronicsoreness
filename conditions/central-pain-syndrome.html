Central Pain Syndrome

Pain from central nervous system damage

What Is Central Pain Syndrome?

Central pain syndrome (CPS) is a neurological condition caused by damage to or dysfunction of the central nervous system (brain or spinal cord). The damage disrupts pain processing, causing persistent pain.1

Causes2

  • Stroke - Post-stroke pain (thalamic pain syndrome)
  • Multiple sclerosis
  • Spinal cord injury
  • Brain tumors
  • Parkinson's disease
  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Epilepsy

Symptoms

  • Constant pain, often burning quality
  • Intermittent sharp, shooting pain
  • Allodynia (pain from light touch)
  • Increased sensitivity to temperature
  • Pain in area of sensory loss
  • May affect large body areas

Characteristics

  • Often delayed onset after injury
  • Worsened by touch, movement, emotions, weather
  • May be accompanied by numbness
  • Usually difficult to treat

Treatment3

Medications

  • Tricyclic antidepressants (amitriptyline)
  • Anticonvulsants (gabapentin, pregabalin)
  • Lamotrigine (especially post-stroke)
  • Opioids (limited effectiveness)

Other Treatments

  • Transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS)
  • Deep brain stimulation
  • Motor cortex stimulation
  • Physical therapy
  • Psychological support
