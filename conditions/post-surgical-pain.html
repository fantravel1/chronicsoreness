Chronic Post-Surgical Pain

When pain persists after surgery should have healed

What Is Chronic Post-Surgical Pain?

Chronic post-surgical pain (CPSP) is pain that persists for at least 3 months after surgery, beyond normal healing time. It affects 10-50% of patients after common surgeries and can significantly impact quality of life.1

High-Risk Surgeries2

  • Mastectomy (30-50%)
  • Thoracotomy (30-50%)
  • Amputation (50-85% phantom pain)
  • Hernia repair (10-15%)
  • Cesarean section (6-18%)
  • Total knee replacement (10-34%)
  • Cardiac surgery (30-40%)

Risk Factors

Patient Factors

  • Pre-operative pain (strongest predictor)
  • Younger age
  • Female sex
  • Anxiety and depression
  • Pain catastrophizing
  • Previous chronic pain

Surgical Factors

  • Nerve damage during surgery
  • Longer operation duration
  • Open vs. minimally invasive
  • Severe acute post-operative pain

Types of Pain

  • Neuropathic - From nerve injury (burning, shooting)
  • Nociceptive - From tissue injury
  • Mixed - Both components

Treatment3

Medications

  • Anticonvulsants (gabapentin, pregabalin)
  • Antidepressants (SNRIs, TCAs)
  • Topical treatments (lidocaine, capsaicin)
  • Careful use of opioids if needed

Non-Pharmacological

  • Physical therapy
  • Desensitization techniques
  • TENS
  • Psychological support
  • Nerve blocks (sometimes)

Prevention

  • Optimize pre-operative pain
  • Multimodal analgesia perioperatively
  • Nerve-sparing surgical techniques
  • Address anxiety/depression pre-op
