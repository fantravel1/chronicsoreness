Complex Regional Pain Syndrome

CRPS: A challenging chronic pain condition

What Is CRPS?

Complex Regional Pain Syndrome (CRPS) is a chronic pain condition that usually affects a limb after an injury, surgery, stroke, or heart attack. The pain is disproportionate to the triggering event and is accompanied by changes in skin color, temperature, and swelling. It was formerly called Reflex Sympathetic Dystrophy (RSD).1

Types2

CRPS-I (Type 1)

  • No confirmed nerve injury
  • Most common (90% of cases)
  • Formerly called RSD

CRPS-II (Type 2)

  • Associated with confirmed nerve injury
  • Formerly called causalgia

Symptoms3

Pain

  • Burning, intense pain
  • Disproportionate to injury
  • Allodynia (pain from light touch)
  • Hyperalgesia (increased pain sensitivity)

Sensory/Motor

  • Skin color changes
  • Temperature changes (hot or cold)
  • Swelling
  • Sweating abnormalities
  • Nail and hair changes
  • Stiffness and tremor
  • Weakness

Triggers

  • Fractures
  • Sprains/strains
  • Surgery
  • Immobilization (cast)
  • Minor injuries
  • Sometimes no identifiable cause

Diagnosis

  • Clinical criteria (Budapest criteria)
  • No single diagnostic test
  • Based on signs and symptoms
  • Rule out other conditions
  • Early diagnosis important

Treatment

Physical/Occupational Therapy

  • Essential component
  • Gradual, progressive exercise
  • Desensitization techniques
  • Mirror therapy

Medications

  • Nerve pain medications
  • Corticosteroids (early)
  • Bisphosphonates
  • Topical treatments

Procedures

  • Sympathetic nerve blocks
  • Spinal cord stimulation
  • Intrathecal drug delivery

Multidisciplinary Approach

  • Pain specialists
  • Physical therapists
  • Psychologists
  • Occupational therapists

Prognosis

  • Early treatment improves outcomes
  • Many improve significantly
  • Some develop chronic condition
  • Can spread to other limbs
