Coccydynia

Understanding persistent tailbone pain

What Is Coccydynia?

Coccydynia is pain in or around the coccyx (tailbone), the small triangular bone at the base of the spine. It's more common in women and can significantly impact daily activities, especially sitting.1

Causes

  • Direct trauma (fall onto buttocks)
  • Childbirth
  • Prolonged sitting on hard surfaces
  • Repetitive strain (cycling, rowing)
  • Hypermobility or instability
  • Idiopathic (no clear cause)

Symptoms2

  • Pain at base of spine
  • Pain worse with sitting
  • Pain when rising from seated
  • Pain with bowel movements
  • Pain during intercourse (women)
  • Tenderness to touch

Diagnosis

  • Clinical examination
  • X-rays (static and dynamic)
  • MRI if needed
  • Rule out other causes

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Coccyx cushion (donut or wedge pillow)
  • Avoid prolonged sitting
  • NSAIDs
  • Physical therapy
  • Manual manipulation

Interventional

  • Corticosteroid injections
  • Ganglion impar block
  • Radiofrequency treatment

Surgery

  • Coccygectomy (removal) - last resort
  • Reserved for severe, refractory cases

Self-Management Tips

  • Use cushion with coccyx cutout
  • Lean forward when sitting
  • Stand when possible
  • Ice for acute pain
