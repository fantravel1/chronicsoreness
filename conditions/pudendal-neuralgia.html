Pudendal Neuralgia

Understanding chronic pelvic pain from pudendal nerve irritation

What Is Pudendal Neuralgia?

Pudendal neuralgia is chronic pain in the areas supplied by the pudendal nerveâ€”the perineum (area between genitals and anus), genitals, and rectum. The pain typically worsens with sitting and improves with standing or lying down.1

This condition is often underdiagnosed and can significantly impact quality of life.

Symptoms

  • Burning, stabbing, or aching pain in perineum
  • Pain in genitals, rectum, or lower buttocks
  • Pain worse with sitting
  • Pain better when standing or lying down
  • Pain relief when sitting on toilet seat (removes pressure)
  • Sensation of foreign body in rectum or vagina
  • Sexual dysfunction
  • Urinary symptoms (frequency, urgency)

Nantes Criteria for Diagnosis2

  • Pain in pudendal nerve territory
  • Pain worse with sitting
  • No awakening at night from pain
  • No sensory loss on examination
  • Positive response to pudendal nerve block

Causes

  • Nerve entrapment - Compression by ligaments or muscles
  • Prolonged sitting - Especially cycling
  • Childbirth trauma
  • Pelvic surgery
  • Pelvic floor dysfunction
  • Falls or trauma

Treatment

Conservative

  • Avoid prolonged sitting - Standing desk, cushions
  • Pelvic floor physical therapy - Often essential3
  • Cushions - Donut or coccyx cushions
  • Medications - Gabapentin, amitriptyline, duloxetine

Injections

  • Pudendal nerve block (diagnostic and therapeutic)
  • Botox injections

Surgery

Pudendal nerve decompression for refractory cases

Self-Care

  • Minimize sitting time
  • Use appropriate cushion when sitting
  • Stand or lie down frequently
  • Avoid activities that compress nerve (cycling)
  • Pelvic floor relaxation exercises
