Pudendal Neuralgia

Chronic pelvic nerve pain

What Is Pudendal Neuralgia?

Pudendal neuralgia is chronic pain in the area innervated by the pudendal nerveâ€”the perineum, genitals, and around the anus. The pain is often described as burning, aching, or stabbing and is typically worse with sitting. It's an underdiagnosed condition that can significantly impact quality of life.1

Symptoms2

  • Pain in perineum, genitals, anus area
  • Burning, stabbing, or aching quality
  • Worse with sitting
  • Better when standing or lying down
  • Usually one-sided (can be both)
  • Foreign body sensation
  • Urinary symptoms sometimes
  • Sexual dysfunction

Nantes Criteria (Diagnostic)

  • Pain in pudendal nerve territory
  • Worsened by sitting
  • Not waking from sleep due to pain
  • No objective sensory loss
  • Relieved by diagnostic pudendal block

Causes and Risk Factors3

  • Cycling (prolonged)
  • Childbirth trauma
  • Pelvic surgery
  • Falls on tailbone
  • Prolonged sitting
  • Nerve entrapment
  • Sometimes no clear cause

Diagnosis

  • Clinical history is key
  • Physical examination
  • Diagnostic pudendal nerve block
  • MRI may show nerve issues
  • EMG sometimes helpful
  • Exclusion of other causes

Treatment

Conservative

  • Avoid sitting triggers
  • Use cushion with cutout
  • Pelvic floor physical therapy
  • Lifestyle modifications

Medications

  • Neuropathic pain medications
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • Gabapentin/pregabalin
  • Muscle relaxants

Interventional

  • Pudendal nerve blocks
  • Pulsed radiofrequency
  • Nerve stimulation

Surgery

  • Decompression surgery
  • For clear entrapment
  • Specialized surgeons needed

Living with Pudendal Neuralgia

  • Standing desk options
  • Special cushions
  • Limit sitting duration
  • Regular position changes
  • Pelvic floor work important
