Pudendal Neuralgia

Chronic pelvic and perineal pain

What Is Pudendal Neuralgia?

Pudendal neuralgia is chronic pain in the areas supplied by the pudendal nerveâ€”the perineum, genitals, and anus. It's caused by irritation, compression, or damage to this nerve. It's often underdiagnosed and can significantly impact quality of life.1

Symptoms2

  • Burning, stabbing, or aching pain
  • Pain in perineum (area between genitals and anus)
  • Pain in vulva, vagina, scrotum, or penis
  • Pain around anus
  • Pain worse when sitting
  • Relief when standing or lying down
  • Bladder or bowel symptoms
  • Sexual dysfunction

Causes

  • Prolonged sitting (especially cycling)
  • Childbirth trauma
  • Pelvic surgery
  • Stretching or compression of nerve
  • Pelvic floor dysfunction
  • Falls onto buttocks
  • Sometimes no clear cause

Diagnosis

  • Clinical history and examination
  • Nantes criteria commonly used
  • Diagnostic nerve block
  • MRI to rule out other causes
  • Neurophysiology testing (sometimes)

Nantes Criteria

  • Pain in pudendal nerve territory
  • Pain predominantly when sitting
  • Pain doesn't wake you at night
  • No sensory loss on examination
  • Relief with diagnostic nerve block

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Avoid prolonged sitting
  • Use cushion with cutout
  • Pelvic floor physical therapy
  • Activity modification

Medications

  • Anticonvulsants
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • SNRIs
  • Muscle relaxants

Procedures

  • Pudendal nerve blocks
  • Pulsed radiofrequency
  • Surgical decompression (in select cases)
