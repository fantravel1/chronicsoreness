Pudendal Neuralgia

Chronic pelvic pain from pudendal nerve irritation

What Is Pudendal Neuralgia?

Pudendal neuralgia is chronic pain in the areas innervated by the pudendal nerve: the genitals, perineum, and rectum. Pain typically worsens with sitting and improves when standing or lying down.1

Symptoms

  • Burning, stabbing, or shooting pain
  • Pain in perineum, genitals, or rectum
  • Worsens with sitting
  • Improves when standing or lying
  • Sensation of foreign body
  • Urinary or bowel dysfunction
  • Sexual dysfunction

Nantes Criteria for Diagnosis2

  • Pain in pudendal nerve territory
  • Pain worsened by sitting
  • Patient is not awakened by pain at night
  • No sensory loss on examination
  • Positive response to pudendal nerve block

Causes

  • Prolonged sitting (especially cycling)
  • Childbirth trauma
  • Pelvic surgery
  • Entrapment by ligaments
  • Trauma or falls

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Avoid prolonged sitting
  • Cushions that relieve perineal pressure
  • Physical therapy (pelvic floor)
  • Neuropathic pain medications

Interventional

  • Pudendal nerve blocks
  • Pulsed radiofrequency
  • Surgical decompression
