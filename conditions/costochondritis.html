Costochondritis

Understanding chest wall pain from rib cartilage inflammation

What Is Costochondritis?

Costochondritis is inflammation of the cartilage connecting the ribs to the breastbone (sternum). It causes chest pain that can be alarming but is not dangerousâ€”unlike cardiac chest pain, which it can mimic.1

Symptoms

  • Sharp, aching, or pressure-like chest pain
  • Pain usually on left side of breastbone
  • Pain affecting multiple ribs
  • Pain worse with deep breathing, coughing, movement
  • Tenderness when pressing on rib joints
  • Pain may radiate to back or abdomen

Causes

  • Unknown - Often no clear cause
  • Physical strain - Heavy lifting, exercise
  • Injury - Trauma to chest
  • Respiratory infections
  • Arthritis
  • Repetitive coughing

Diagnosis

  • Physical examination - tenderness on palpation
  • Ruling out cardiac and other serious causes
  • ECG, chest X-ray if needed to exclude other conditions
  • No specific test confirms costochondritis

Treatment2

  • NSAIDs - Ibuprofen, naproxen
  • Heat - Applied to affected area
  • Rest - Avoid aggravating activities
  • Stretching - Gentle chest stretches
  • Physical therapy - For persistent cases
  • Steroid injections - For refractory cases

Prognosis

Usually resolves within weeks to months, though some cases become chronic. Reassurance that it's not cardiac is important.
