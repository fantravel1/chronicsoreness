Costochondritis

Chest wall pain at the breastbone

What Is Costochondritis?

Costochondritis is inflammation of the cartilage that connects ribs to the breastbone (sternum). It causes chest pain that can be alarming but is not heart-related. It's a common cause of chest pain, especially in women and people over 40.1

Symptoms2

  • Sharp or aching chest pain
  • Pain usually on left side (can be either)
  • Pain at rib-sternum junction
  • Tenderness when pressing the area
  • Pain worse with movement or breathing
  • Pain may radiate to back or abdomen

Causes

  • Often unknown
  • Physical strain
  • Injury to chest
  • Repeated coughing
  • Heavy lifting
  • Viral infections
  • Arthritis conditions

Diagnosis

  • Physical examination
  • Reproduction of pain with palpation
  • Tests to rule out heart problems
  • Usually no imaging needed

Important Note

Always seek immediate medical attention for chest pain if you have:

  • Pain spreading to arm or jaw
  • Shortness of breath
  • Sweating, nausea
  • History of heart disease
  • Risk factors for heart attack

Treatment3

Conservative

  • Rest
  • Ice or heat
  • NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen)
  • Avoid aggravating activities
  • Gentle stretching

Other Options

  • Topical anti-inflammatories
  • Physical therapy
  • Local anesthetic injection (rarely needed)

Self-Care Tips

  • Apply heat to the area
  • Gentle stretching exercises
  • Avoid activities that worsen pain
  • Use proper technique when lifting
  • Stress management

Prognosis

Costochondritis usually resolves on its own within weeks to months. Some people have recurring episodes. It's not dangerous but can be uncomfortable.
