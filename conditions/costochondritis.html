Costochondritis

Chest wall pain from cartilage inflammation

What Is Costochondritis?

Costochondritis is inflammation of the cartilage that connects the ribs to the breastbone (sternum). It causes localized chest pain that can be quite alarming but is not related to the heart. The condition is usually self-limiting but can become chronic in some people.1

Symptoms2

  • Sharp, aching chest pain
  • Usually on left side of sternum
  • May affect multiple ribs
  • Tender to touch
  • Worse with deep breathing
  • Worse with coughing
  • Pain with arm movements
  • May radiate to back or abdomen

Not Heart-Related

  • Reproducible with palpation
  • Not associated with shortness of breath
  • No sweating or nausea
  • Doesn't worsen with exertion
  • However, always rule out cardiac causes

Causes

  • Often unknown
  • Physical strain (heavy lifting)
  • Repetitive activities
  • Severe coughing
  • Respiratory infections
  • Injury to chest
  • Associated with fibromyalgia
  • Inflammatory conditions

Diagnosis3

  • Clinical examination
  • Tenderness on palpation
  • No specific test
  • May do tests to rule out other causes
  • EKG and cardiac enzymes if needed
  • Chest X-ray

Treatment

Self-Care

  • Rest and activity modification
  • Local heat or ice
  • Gentle stretching
  • Avoid activities that worsen pain

Medications

  • NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen)
  • Acetaminophen
  • Topical anti-inflammatories
  • Muscle relaxants if needed

Other Treatments

  • Physical therapy
  • TENS unit
  • Corticosteroid injections (rarely)
  • Intercostal nerve block (severe cases)

Chronic Costochondritis

  • Some cases persist months
  • May flare and remit
  • Associated conditions should be treated
  • Focus on pain management

When to Seek Care

  • Chest pain with shortness of breath
  • Pain radiating to arm or jaw
  • Fever with chest pain
  • Severe or worsening symptoms
