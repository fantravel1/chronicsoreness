Costochondritis

Inflammation of rib cartilage causing chest pain

What Is Costochondritis?

Costochondritis is inflammation of the cartilage connecting the ribs to the breastbone (sternum). It causes localized chest wall pain that can mimic heart problems but is benign. It's also called costosternal syndrome.1

Symptoms

  • Sharp, aching, or pressure-like chest pain
  • Usually on left side of breastbone
  • Affects multiple ribs (2nd-5th most common)
  • Pain with deep breathing or coughing
  • Tenderness when pressing on rib joints
  • Pain may radiate to back or abdomen

Causes2

  • Often unknown (idiopathic)
  • Physical strain or injury
  • Severe coughing
  • Respiratory infections
  • Arthritis
  • Tumors (rare)
  • Repetitive movements

Diagnosis

  • Physical examination
  • Reproducible tenderness
  • Rule out cardiac causes
  • EKG and chest X-ray (if needed)
  • No specific testâ€”diagnosis of exclusion

Treatment3

Self-Care

  • Rest from aggravating activities
  • Heat or ice
  • Gentle stretching
  • Avoiding sudden movements

Medications

  • NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen)
  • Topical NSAIDs
  • Acetaminophen

Other Options

  • Corticosteroid injection
  • Physical therapy
  • Transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS)

Prognosis

Costochondritis usually resolves on its own within weeks to months, though some cases become chronic.
