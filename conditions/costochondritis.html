Costochondritis

Chest wall pain from cartilage inflammation

What Is Costochondritis?

Costochondritis is inflammation of the cartilage that connects the ribs to the breastbone (sternum). This causes chest pain that can be alarming because it may feel like a heart attack, but it's generally harmless and resolves on its own. It's one of the most common causes of chest pain in people visiting emergency rooms.1

Symptoms2

  • Sharp, aching, or pressure-like chest pain
  • Usually left side of breastbone
  • May affect multiple ribs
  • Tenderness when pressing on rib joints
  • Pain worsens with deep breathing, coughing
  • Pain with movement or stretching

Costochondritis vs. Tietze Syndrome

  • Both cause similar pain
  • Tietze syndrome has visible swelling
  • Tietze usually affects one rib joint
  • Tietze is less common
  • Treatment is similar

Causes

  • Often unknown
  • Repeated minor trauma
  • Physical strain or overuse
  • Respiratory infections
  • Arthritis conditions
  • Severe coughing

Diagnosis3

  • Physical examination
  • Reproduction of pain with palpation
  • No specific test confirms it
  • May need tests to rule out heart problems
  • Diagnosis of exclusion

When It's NOT Costochondritis

Seek emergency care if chest pain includes:

  • Pain spreading to arm, jaw, or back
  • Shortness of breath
  • Sweating
  • Nausea
  • Feeling of impending doom
  • History of heart disease

Treatment

Self-Care

  • Rest and avoid aggravating activities
  • Heat application
  • Gentle stretching
  • Over-the-counter pain relievers

Medical Treatment

  • NSAIDs
  • Topical pain relievers
  • Physical therapy
  • Corticosteroid injection (if severe)

Prognosis

  • Usually resolves in weeks to months
  • Some cases become chronic
  • May recur
  • Not dangerous, just painful

Living with Costochondritis

  • Avoid activities that worsen pain
  • Sleep in comfortable position
  • Manage stress (can worsen symptoms)
  • Gentle mobility exercises
