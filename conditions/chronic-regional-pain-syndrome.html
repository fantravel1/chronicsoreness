Complex Regional Pain Syndrome

A debilitating condition of persistent pain and dysfunction

What Is CRPS?

Complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS) is a chronic pain condition characterized by persistent, severe pain usually affecting a limb. The pain is disproportionate to the initial injury. CRPS was previously called reflex sympathetic dystrophy (RSD).1

Types

  • CRPS-I - No confirmed nerve injury (90% of cases)
  • CRPS-II - Confirmed nerve injury (causalgia)

Symptoms2

Sensory

  • Intense burning or throbbing pain
  • Allodynia (pain from light touch)
  • Hyperalgesia (increased pain response)

Vasomotor

  • Temperature changes (hot or cold)
  • Color changes (red, blue, mottled)

Sudomotor/Edema

  • Swelling
  • Sweating changes

Motor/Trophic

  • Decreased range of motion
  • Weakness
  • Tremor
  • Changes to skin, hair, nails

Treatment3

Physical/Occupational Therapy

  • Gentle, graded exercise
  • Mirror therapy
  • Desensitization
  • Graded motor imagery

Medications

  • Neuropathic pain medications
  • Bisphosphonates (early CRPS)
  • Corticosteroids (early, short course)
  • Topical treatments

Interventional

  • Sympathetic nerve blocks
  • Spinal cord stimulation
  • Intrathecal drug delivery
