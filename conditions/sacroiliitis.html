Sacroiliitis

Inflammation of the sacroiliac joint

What Is Sacroiliitis?

Sacroiliitis is inflammation of one or both sacroiliac (SI) joints, which connect the lower spine to the pelvis. It can cause pain in the lower back, buttocks, and sometimes legs. Sacroiliitis can be part of inflammatory arthritis conditions or occur due to mechanical causes.1

Anatomy

  • SI joints connect sacrum to iliac bones
  • Located in lower back/upper buttock
  • Allow small amount of movement
  • Transmit forces between spine and legs

Symptoms2

  • Pain in lower back, buttock, hip
  • May radiate down leg
  • Usually one-sided (can be both)
  • Worse with prolonged sitting or standing
  • Pain climbing stairs
  • Stiffness in hips and lower back
  • Pain turning in bed

Causes

Inflammatory

  • Ankylosing spondylitis
  • Psoriatic arthritis
  • Reactive arthritis
  • Inflammatory bowel disease-related

Mechanical

  • Trauma or injury
  • Pregnancy
  • Altered gait
  • Leg length discrepancy
  • Osteoarthritis

Diagnosis3

  • Physical examination maneuvers
  • Multiple provocation tests
  • MRI shows inflammation early
  • X-rays show structural changes
  • Diagnostic injection (most specific)
  • Blood tests for inflammation

Treatment

Conservative

  • Rest during flares
  • Ice or heat
  • Physical therapy
  • SI joint belt

Medications

  • NSAIDs (often first line)
  • DMARDs for inflammatory causes
  • Biologics for spondyloarthritis

Interventional

  • SI joint injection
  • Radiofrequency ablation
  • SI joint fusion (refractory cases)

Physical Therapy Focus

  • Core stabilization
  • Pelvic stabilization exercises
  • Hip strengthening
  • Stretching tight muscles
  • Posture correction
