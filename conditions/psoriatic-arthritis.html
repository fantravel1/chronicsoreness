Psoriatic Arthritis

Inflammatory arthritis linked to psoriasis

What Is Psoriatic Arthritis?

Psoriatic arthritis (PsA) is an inflammatory arthritis that occurs in some people with psoriasis (skin condition). It can affect any joint and may cause permanent damage if not treated. About 30% of people with psoriasis develop PsA.1

Types/Patterns

  • Asymmetric oligoarthritis - Few joints, not symmetric
  • Symmetric polyarthritis - Similar to RA
  • Distal - Finger/toe joints near nails
  • Spondylitis - Spine involvement
  • Arthritis mutilans - Severe, deforming (rare)

Symptoms2

  • Joint pain, swelling, stiffness
  • Morning stiffness >30 minutes
  • Dactylitis (sausage fingers/toes)
  • Enthesitis (Achilles, plantar fascia)
  • Nail changes (pitting, separation)
  • Psoriasis (skin plaques)
  • Back pain/stiffness
  • Fatigue

Diagnosis

  • Clinical examination
  • Personal or family history of psoriasis
  • X-rays, MRI, ultrasound
  • Blood tests (usually RF negative)
  • CASPAR criteria

Treatment3

Medications

  • NSAIDs - Mild disease
  • DMARDs - Methotrexate, sulfasalazine
  • Biologics - TNF inhibitors, IL-17 inhibitors, IL-12/23 inhibitors
  • JAK inhibitors - Tofacitinib, upadacitinib
  • PDE4 inhibitor - Apremilast

Lifestyle

  • Regular exercise
  • Weight management
  • Smoking cessation
  • Protect joints

Prognosis

  • Variable course
  • Early treatment prevents damage
  • Modern biologics very effective
