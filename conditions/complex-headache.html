New Daily Persistent Headache

A headache that starts suddenly and never goes away

What Is NDPH?

New daily persistent headache (NDPH) is a primary headache disorder characterized by sudden onset of a persistent headache that becomes daily and unremitting within 24 hours. Patients can often name the exact day their headache began.1

Characteristics2

  • Daily headache from onset
  • Persistent and unremitting
  • No prior history of similar headaches
  • Can remember exact onset day
  • Lasts more than 3 months
  • Often triggered by infection or stressful event

Subtypes

  • Migraine-like - Features resembling migraine
  • Tension-type-like - Features resembling tension headache

Possible Triggers

  • Viral illness (often reported)
  • Stressful life event
  • Surgery
  • Flulike illness
  • Sometimes no identifiable trigger

Challenge of NDPH3

  • Often resistant to treatment
  • Two courses: self-limiting or refractory
  • Self-limiting may resolve in months
  • Refractory can persist for years

Treatment Approaches

  • Migraine preventives (if migraine-like)
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • Muscle relaxants
  • Nerve blocks
  • Botox
  • Often trial and error
