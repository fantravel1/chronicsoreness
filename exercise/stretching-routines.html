Stretching Routines

Improving flexibility and reducing tension

Why Stretching Helps

Regular stretching can help manage chronic pain by improving flexibility, reducing muscle tension, increasing blood flow, and promoting relaxation. It's a gentle, accessible form of movement suitable for most people with pain conditions.1

Types of Stretching2

Static Stretching

  • Hold position for 15-60 seconds
  • Gentle, sustained stretch
  • Good for improving flexibility
  • Best after warm-up or exercise

Dynamic Stretching

  • Controlled movement through range of motion
  • Good for warm-up
  • Prepares muscles for activity

General Guidelines3

  • Warm up first (5 minutes of light movement)
  • Hold each stretch 15-30 seconds
  • Don't bounce
  • Stretch to mild tension, not pain
  • Breathe deeply and slowly
  • Both sides equally
  • Repeat 2-4 times each

Sample Full-Body Routine

Neck

  • Chin tucks
  • Neck tilts (ear to shoulder)
  • Gentle neck rotations

Shoulders

  • Shoulder rolls
  • Cross-body arm stretch
  • Doorway chest stretch

Back

  • Cat-cow stretches
  • Child's pose
  • Knee-to-chest stretch
  • Trunk rotation (lying down)

Hips and Legs

  • Hip flexor stretch
  • Piriformis stretch
  • Hamstring stretch
  • Quadriceps stretch
  • Calf stretch

Tips for Success

  • Start gently
  • Consistency is key
  • Morning and evening work well
  • Modify positions as needed
  • Use props (pillows, straps) if helpful
  • Stop if pain increases significantly

Cautions

  • Avoid stretching acutely inflamed joints
  • Don't stretch hypermobile joints forcefully
  • Check with provider if unsure
  • Modify for your condition
