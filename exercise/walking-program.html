Walking Program

One step at a time

Why Walking?

Walking is one of the best exercises for people with chronic pain. It's low-impact, free, requires no equipment, and can be done almost anywhere. Research shows walking can reduce pain, improve mood, and increase function.1

Benefits2

  • Reduces chronic pain
  • Improves cardiovascular health
  • Builds endurance gradually
  • Strengthens muscles
  • Improves sleep
  • Reduces depression and anxiety
  • Low risk of injury
  • Can be social

Getting Started3

Start Where You Are

  • If you can walk 5 minutes, start there
  • Even 1-2 minutes counts
  • No "right" starting point
  • Consistency matters more than duration

Week 1-2

  • Walk 5-10 minutes
  • 3 days per week
  • Slow, comfortable pace
  • Focus on forming the habit

Weeks 3-4

  • Add 2-5 minutes per session
  • 4 days per week
  • Maintain comfortable pace

Progression

  • Increase duration before speed
  • Add 10% per week maximum
  • Eventually work toward 30 minutes
  • 5-7 days per week goal

Tips for Success

  • Wear supportive, comfortable shoes
  • Start with flat terrain
  • Warm up with slow walking
  • Cool down the same way
  • Track your walks
  • Find a walking buddy

Managing Pain While Walking

  • Some discomfort is normal
  • Stop if pain is severe or worsening
  • Short, frequent walks may be easier
  • Walk during lower-pain times
  • Use walking aid if needed

Making It Enjoyable

  • Listen to music or podcasts
  • Walk in pleasant locations
  • Vary your route
  • Walk with friends or family
  • Use as mindfulness practice
