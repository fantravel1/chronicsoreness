Core Strengthening

Building stability to support your spine

Why Core Strength Matters

The core muscles are essential for spinal stability and proper movement. Weakness in these muscles is associated with low back pain and can contribute to poor posture and movement patterns. Strengthening the core helps support the spine, reduce strain, and may decrease painâ€”though it should be done gradually and with proper form.1

What Is the Core?2

  • Not just "abs" or six-pack muscles
  • Includes multiple muscle groups
  • Deep stabilizing muscles important
  • Works as integrated system

Core Muscles Include

  • Transverse abdominis (deep)
  • Multifidus (along spine)
  • Internal and external obliques
  • Rectus abdominis
  • Pelvic floor muscles
  • Diaphragm
  • Erector spinae

Benefits for Pain

  • Improved spinal stability
  • Better posture
  • Reduced strain on back
  • Improved movement patterns
  • May decrease pain intensity
  • Better function in daily activities

Important Principles3

  • Start gentlyâ€”don't overdo it
  • Focus on quality over quantity
  • Learn proper form first
  • Breathing matters
  • Progress slowly
  • Avoid exercises that worsen pain

Beginner Exercises

Diaphragmatic Breathing

  • Foundation for core work
  • Activates deep stabilizers
  • Start here first

Pelvic Tilts

  • Lying on back, knees bent
  • Gently flatten lower back
  • Hold briefly, release

Dead Bug

  • On back, arms up, knees bent 90Â°
  • Slowly lower opposite arm and leg
  • Keep lower back flat

Bird Dog

  • On hands and knees
  • Extend opposite arm and leg
  • Keep spine neutral

Exercises to Approach Carefully

  • Traditional sit-ups (spine flexion)
  • Double leg raises
  • Full planks (if very deconditioned)
  • Any exercise causing pain

Working with Professionals

  • Physical therapist evaluation helpful
  • Ensure proper form
  • Get personalized program
  • Address specific weaknesses

Tips for Success

  • Consistency beats intensity
  • A few minutes daily is beneficial
  • Don't hold breath during exercises
  • Stop if pain increases
  • Patienceâ€”takes weeks to build strength
