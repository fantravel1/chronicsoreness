Core Strengthening

Building stability to support the spine

Why Core Strength Matters

The core muscles support the spine and help distribute loads during movement. Weakness or poor coordination of these muscles has been linked to back pain. Core stabilization exercises are a key part of back pain rehabilitation.1

Core Muscles Include

  • Transverse abdominis (deep abdominal)
  • Multifidus (deep back muscles)
  • Internal and external obliques
  • Rectus abdominis
  • Pelvic floor muscles
  • Diaphragm

Beginner Exercises2

Abdominal Bracing

  • Lie on back, knees bent
  • Tighten abdominals as if preparing for punch
  • Hold while breathing normally
  • Progress by adding arm/leg movements

Dead Bug

  • Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90 degrees
  • Lower opposite arm and leg slowly
  • Keep back flat on floor
  • Return and repeat other side

Bird Dog

  • On hands and knees
  • Extend opposite arm and leg
  • Keep spine neutral
  • Hold briefly, alternate

Progression3

  • Start with endurance (longer holds, more reps)
  • Progress to stability challenges
  • Add resistance as tolerated
  • Include functional movements

Key Principles

  • Quality over quantity
  • Avoid breath-holding
  • Maintain neutral spine
  • Stop if painful
  • Consistency is key
