Core Strengthening

Building stability for spine health and pain management

Why Core Strength Matters

The core muscles support and stabilize the spine. Weakness or poor coordination of these muscles has been linked to low back pain. Core strengthening is a key component of rehabilitation for many pain conditions.1

Core Muscles Include

  • Transversus abdominis - Deep abdominal stabilizer
  • Multifidus - Deep spine muscles
  • Rectus abdominis - "Six-pack" muscles
  • Internal/external obliques - Side muscles
  • Pelvic floor muscles
  • Diaphragm
  • Erector spinae - Back extensors

Benefits for Pain2

  • Better spine stability
  • Improved posture
  • Reduced strain on spine structures
  • Better load distribution
  • Improved function
  • May reduce pain recurrence

Safe Exercises for Beginners3

Abdominal Bracing

  • Contract abdominals without holding breath
  • Imagine bracing for a punch
  • Hold 5-10 seconds, breathe normally

Bird Dog

  • On hands and knees
  • Extend opposite arm and leg
  • Keep spine neutral
  • Hold 5-10 seconds, alternate

Dead Bug

  • Lie on back, arms up, knees bent 90Â°
  • Lower opposite arm and leg
  • Keep low back pressed to floor

Modified Plank

  • On forearms and knees (progress to toes)
  • Keep body in line
  • Don't let hips sag or pike
  • Build duration gradually

Cautions

  • Avoid sit-ups/crunches if back pain
  • Progress gradually
  • Quality over quantity
  • Stop if pain increases
  • Breathe normally (don't hold breath)

Progression

  • Master basic exercises first
  • Increase hold time before reps
  • Add unstable surfaces later
  • Progress to functional movements
