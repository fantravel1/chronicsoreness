Core Strengthening

Building trunk stability for pain relief

What Is the Core?

The "core" includes all the muscles that stabilize your trunkâ€”not just the abs. These muscles work together to support your spine and allow controlled movement. A strong, stable core is essential for reducing and preventing back pain.1

Core Muscles Include

  • Rectus abdominis (front abs)
  • Transverse abdominis (deep stabilizer)
  • Obliques (sides)
  • Erector spinae (back extensors)
  • Multifidus (deep back stabilizers)
  • Pelvic floor muscles
  • Diaphragm

Why Core Strength Matters2

  • Supports the spine
  • Reduces load on spinal structures
  • Improves posture
  • Allows safer movement
  • May prevent back pain episodes

Safe Starting Exercises3

Abdominal Bracing

  • Gently tighten abdominal muscles
  • Like preparing to be poked in stomach
  • Hold while breathing normally
  • Foundation for other exercises

Pelvic Tilts

  • Lie on back, knees bent
  • Flatten lower back to floor
  • Hold 5 seconds, release
  • Very gentle starting exercise

Dead Bug

  • Lie on back, arms and legs raised
  • Lower opposite arm and leg slowly
  • Keep lower back pressed to floor
  • Alternate sides

Bird Dog

  • On hands and knees
  • Extend opposite arm and leg
  • Keep spine neutral
  • Hold, return, switch sides

Modified Plank

  • On forearms and knees (modified) or toes (full)
  • Keep body straight
  • Don't let hips sag or pike up
  • Hold as long as good form allows

Progression Principles

  • Start with easier variations
  • Master form before adding challenge
  • Increase hold time before adding difficulty
  • Quality over quantity
  • No pain should occur during exercises

Common Mistakes

  • Holding breath
  • Arching lower back
  • Moving too quickly
  • Progressing too fast
  • Focusing only on abs (ignoring back)

How Often?

  • Daily practice is ideal
  • Can be done in short sessions
  • Even 5-10 minutes helps
  • Consistency matters more than duration

Working with Professionals

  • Physical therapists can assess your needs
  • Customize program for your condition
  • Ensure proper form
  • Progress safely
