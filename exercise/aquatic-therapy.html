Aquatic Therapy

Using water's properties for pain relief and rehabilitation

What Is Aquatic Therapy?

Aquatic therapy (hydrotherapy) uses the properties of waterâ€”buoyancy, resistance, and warmthâ€”for exercise and rehabilitation. Water supports the body while allowing movement that may be difficult on land.1

Benefits of Water Exercise

  • Buoyancy - Reduces weight on joints by up to 90%
  • Resistance - Water provides natural resistance for strengthening
  • Warmth - Warm water relaxes muscles, increases blood flow
  • Hydrostatic pressure - May reduce swelling
  • Pain relief - Many find immediate relief in water

Conditions That Benefit2

  • Osteoarthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic low back pain
  • Chronic hip/knee pain
  • Post-surgical rehabilitation
  • Obesity with joint pain

Types of Aquatic Exercise

  • Water walking/jogging
  • Swimming (if able)
  • Water aerobics classes
  • Specific rehabilitation exercises
  • Ai Chi (water tai chi)

Finding Programs

  • Hospital-based aquatic therapy
  • Physical therapy clinics with pools
  • Arthritis Foundation water exercise programs
  • YMCA water fitness classes
  • Community recreation centers

Tips

  • Start in warm water (83-88Â°F ideal)
  • Begin with short sessions
  • Use flotation devices if needed
  • Stay hydrated despite being in water
  • Consider water shoes for grip
