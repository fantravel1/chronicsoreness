Aquatic Therapy

Healing through water-based exercise

What Is Aquatic Therapy?

Aquatic therapy uses the properties of waterâ€”buoyancy, resistance, and warmthâ€”to facilitate exercise and rehabilitation. Water reduces stress on joints while providing resistance for strengthening. It's particularly valuable for people with chronic pain who find land-based exercise difficult.1

Benefits of Water Exercise2

Physical Benefits

  • Reduces joint stress (buoyancy supports body weight)
  • Provides gentle resistance for strengthening
  • Warm water relaxes muscles
  • Improves flexibility and range of motion
  • Enhances circulation
  • Reduces swelling (hydrostatic pressure)

Pain-Specific Benefits

  • Allows movement that's difficult on land
  • Hydrostatic pressure may reduce pain perception
  • Warmth soothes sore muscles
  • Less fear of falling
  • Can exercise longer than on land

Conditions That Benefit3

  • Osteoarthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic back pain
  • Joint replacements (recovery)
  • Limited mobility
  • Balance problems

Types of Aquatic Programs

Individual Aquatic Physical Therapy

  • One-on-one with therapist
  • Customized to your condition
  • May be covered by insurance

Group Water Exercise Classes

  • Arthritis Foundation classes
  • Water aerobics
  • Ai Chi (water-based tai chi)
  • Social support benefits

Common Techniques

  • Walking in water (forward, backward, sideways)
  • Leg kicks and lifts
  • Arm movements against water resistance
  • Stretching exercises
  • Balance activities
  • Using foam noodles or paddles for resistance

Getting Started

  • Talk to your doctor first
  • Find a warm water pool (83-92Â°F ideal)
  • Start slowly (15-20 minutes)
  • Increase gradually
  • Don't swimâ€”focus on upright exercises

What You'll Need

  • Swimsuit
  • Water shoes (helpful for grip)
  • Towel and robe for after
  • Goggles optional

Finding Programs

  • Hospital rehabilitation departments
  • Physical therapy centers with pools
  • YMCA/community centers
  • Arthritis Foundation AFYAP classes
  • Ask your healthcare provider

Cautions

  • Open wounds or infections
  • Uncontrolled seizures
  • Some heart conditions
  • Incontinence issues (discuss solutions)
  • Fear of water (may need gradual exposure)
