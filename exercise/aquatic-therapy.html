Aquatic Therapy

Exercise in water for reduced joint stress

Why Water Works

Water provides a unique environment for exercise. Buoyancy reduces joint stress by up to 90%, while water resistance provides strengthening without weights. Warm water also relaxes muscles and increases circulation.1

Benefits

  • Reduced weight-bearing on joints
  • Decreased pain during exercise
  • Improved range of motion
  • Muscle strengthening
  • Better balance (water support)
  • Cardiovascular conditioning
  • Relaxation from warm water

Who Benefits Most2

  • Osteoarthritis
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Low back pain
  • Post-surgical rehabilitation
  • Obesity with joint pain
  • Those unable to exercise on land

Types of Aquatic Exercise

Water Walking

  • Walk forward, backward, sideways
  • Chest to waist-deep water
  • Great starting point

Water Aerobics

  • Group classes common
  • Uses water resistance
  • May use equipment (noodles, weights)

Swimming

  • Full-body workout
  • Modify strokes as needed

Ai Chi/Water Yoga

  • Slow, flowing movements
  • Focus on relaxation
