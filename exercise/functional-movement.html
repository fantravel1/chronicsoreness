Functional Movement Training

Exercise for real-world activities

What Is Functional Movement?

Functional movement training focuses on exercises that improve your ability to perform everyday activitiesâ€”like bending, lifting, reaching, and climbing stairs. Rather than isolating single muscles, it trains movement patterns that translate to real life.1

Why It Matters for Chronic Pain2

  • Improves ability to do daily activities
  • Addresses movement patterns, not just muscles
  • Builds confidence in movement
  • Can reduce fear of movement
  • Practical improvements in quality of life

Basic Movement Patterns

Squat/Sit-to-Stand

  • Getting up from chairs
  • Picking things up
  • Using toilet

Hinge

  • Bending forward safely
  • Lifting objects from ground

Push/Pull

  • Opening doors
  • Reaching for objects
  • Pushing carts

Carry

  • Carrying groceries
  • Moving objects

Lunge/Step

  • Climbing stairs
  • Getting in/out of bathtub

Example Exercises3

Chair Squats

  • Stand up from chair without using arms
  • Control the descent back down
  • Progress to lower surfaces

Step-Ups

  • Step onto low step
  • Control step down
  • Progress step height as able

Farmer's Carry

  • Walk while holding weights at sides
  • Start light
  • Mimics carrying bags

Wall Push-Ups

  • Pushing against wall
  • Progress to lower surfaces as able

Principles for Success

  • Start with your current ability
  • Progress gradually
  • Focus on quality over quantity
  • Train movements you need for daily life
  • Modify as needed for pain

Working with a Professional

  • Physical therapists specialize in functional training
  • Can assess your movement patterns
  • Identify compensations or problems
  • Create customized program
  • Especially important with chronic pain

Benefits

  • Improved independence
  • Better quality of life
  • Reduced disability
  • More confidence with movement
  • May reduce fall risk
