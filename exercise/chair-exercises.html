Chair Exercises

Exercise options when standing is difficult

Why Chair Exercises?

Chair exercises provide a safe, accessible way to exercise when standing is painful, balance is a concern, or fatigue limits activity. They can maintain strength and flexibility even during pain flares.1

Who Benefits

  • Severe pain flares
  • Limited mobility
  • Balance problems
  • Wheelchair users
  • Recovering from surgery
  • Starting exercise after being sedentary

Getting Started2

  • Use sturdy chair without wheels
  • Sit toward front of chair
  • Keep feet flat on floor
  • Maintain good posture
  • Start with 5-10 minutes

Upper Body Exercises

  • Arm circles
  • Overhead reach
  • Shoulder rolls
  • Bicep curls (with or without weights)
  • Chest stretch
  • Neck stretches

Lower Body Exercises3

  • Marching in place
  • Knee extensions
  • Ankle circles and pumps
  • Heel raises
  • Seated leg lifts
  • Hip abduction

Core Exercises

  • Seated twists
  • Abdominal bracing
  • Pelvic tilts

Cardio Options

  • Seated marching (faster)
  • Arm movements with marching
  • Chair aerobics videos

Tips

  • Breathe normally throughout
  • Do what you can, skip what you can't
  • Any movement counts
  • Progress gradually
