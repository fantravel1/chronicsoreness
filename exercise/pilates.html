Pilates

Core-focused exercise for strength and stability

What Is Pilates?

Pilates is a low-impact exercise method focusing on core strength, flexibility, and body awareness. Developed by Joseph Pilates, it emphasizes controlled, precise movements and proper breathing.1

Benefits for Pain2

  • Strengthens core (spine support)
  • Improves posture
  • Increases flexibility
  • Builds balanced strength
  • Low impact on joints
  • Enhances body awareness
  • Can be modified extensively

Conditions Helped

  • Low back pain
  • Neck pain
  • Chronic pain conditions
  • Post-surgical rehabilitation
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Osteoporosis

Key Principles

  • Centering - Focus on core/powerhouse
  • Concentration - Mindful movement
  • Control - Quality over quantity
  • Precision - Proper alignment
  • Breath - Coordinated breathing
  • Flow - Smooth transitions

Types of Pilates

Mat Pilates

  • Floor exercises
  • Uses body weight
  • May use props (bands, balls)
  • More accessible

Reformer/Equipment Pilates

  • Uses specialized machines
  • Spring resistance
  • More variety and support
  • Requires studio access

Getting Started3

  • Find a certified instructor
  • Look for clinical/rehabilitation Pilates
  • Inform instructor of pain conditions
  • Start with fundamentals
  • Modify as needed
