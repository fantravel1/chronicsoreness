Pilates

Core-focused movement for strength and flexibility

What Is Pilates?

Pilates is an exercise method developed by Joseph Pilates in the early 20th century. It emphasizes core strength, controlled movement, proper alignment, and breathing. It's widely used in rehabilitation and for chronic pain management.1

Core Principles

  • Centering - Focus on core muscles (powerhouse)
  • Concentration - Mindful attention to movement
  • Control - Precise, controlled movements
  • Precision - Quality over quantity
  • Breath - Coordinated breathing patterns
  • Flow - Smooth, continuous movements

Benefits for Pain2

  • Strengthens core muscles
  • Improves posture
  • Increases flexibility
  • Builds body awareness
  • Low impact on joints
  • Can be modified for limitations
  • Reduces muscle imbalances

Conditions That May Benefit3

  • Chronic low back pain (strongest evidence)
  • Neck pain
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Post-surgical rehabilitation
  • Scoliosis
  • General deconditioning

Types of Pilates

Mat Pilates

  • Exercises on floor mat
  • Uses body weight as resistance
  • May use small props (balls, bands)
  • Most accessible for beginners

Equipment-Based

  • Reformer (most common)
  • Cadillac/Trapeze Table
  • Chair
  • Provides resistance and support

Getting Started

  • Start with beginner classes
  • Inform instructor of pain conditions
  • Consider private sessions initially
  • Look for clinical Pilates for rehabilitation
  • Wear comfortable, fitted clothing

Finding an Instructor

  • Look for certified Pilates instructors
  • Ask about experience with your condition
  • Physical therapists trained in Pilates
  • Studios, gyms, and private practices

Precautions

  • Inform instructor of all conditions
  • Avoid exercises that increase pain
  • Start slowly and progress gradually
  • Proper form is essential
