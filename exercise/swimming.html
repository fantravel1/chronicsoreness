Swimming

Low-impact full-body exercise

Why Swimming for Chronic Pain?

Swimming and water exercise are often recommended for chronic pain because water supports the body, reducing stress on joints. The buoyancy reduces effective body weight by up to 90%, allowing movement that might be painful on land.1

Benefits2

  • Low impact on joints
  • Full-body workout
  • Builds strength without weights
  • Improves cardiovascular fitness
  • Increases flexibility
  • Water resistance builds muscle
  • Relaxation and stress relief

Conditions That Benefit

  • Arthritis (OA and RA)
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Back pain
  • Chronic widespread pain
  • Joint problems
  • Limited mobility

Getting Started3

  • Start with 10-15 minutes
  • Warm pool (83-88Â°F) often best for pain
  • Consider water walking first
  • Use flotation devices if needed
  • Try different strokes
  • Build up gradually

Strokes and Modifications

Best for Beginners

  • Backstroke (easiest on neck/back)
  • Sidestroke (gentle)
  • Water walking

May Need Modification

  • Breaststroke (can stress knees/neck)
  • Butterfly (demanding)

Tips

  • Warm up before swimming
  • Stay hydrated (even in water)
  • Listen to your body
  • Consider water aerobics classes
  • Many pools have accessible entry
