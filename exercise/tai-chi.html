Tai Chi

Ancient movement practice for modern pain relief

What Is Tai Chi?

Tai chi is a gentle form of exercise that originated in China as a martial art. It involves slow, flowing movements combined with deep breathing and mental focus. Often called "meditation in motion," tai chi is now recognized as a beneficial practice for various chronic pain conditions.1

Key Elements

  • Slow, continuous movements
  • Deep, relaxed breathing
  • Mental concentration
  • Weight shifting
  • Balance and posture
  • Relaxation of muscles

Benefits for Chronic Pain2

  • Reduces pain intensity
  • Improves flexibility and balance
  • Increases strength gently
  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Improves sleep
  • Enhances mood
  • Promotes body awareness

Research Evidence3

Strong Evidence For

  • Knee osteoarthritis
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Falls prevention
  • Balance improvement

Promising Evidence For

  • Low back pain
  • Neck pain
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • General chronic pain

Why It Helps Pain

  • Low-impact, joint-friendly movement
  • Improves muscle strength around joints
  • Reduces stress (which amplifies pain)
  • Mind-body connection
  • Improves sleep quality
  • Provides social interaction in classes

Getting Started

  • Find beginner or "modified" classes
  • Look for instructors experienced with chronic pain
  • Community centers often offer affordable classes
  • Online videos for home practice
  • Start slowly, listen to your body

Adaptations for Pain

  • Can be done seated in chair
  • Movements can be modified
  • Range of motion can be reduced
  • Rest when needed
  • Shorter sessions to start

What to Expect

  • May feel awkward at first (normal)
  • Benefits develop over weeks to months
  • Consistency more important than perfection
  • 2-3 sessions per week often recommended
  • Each session typically 30-60 minutes

Styles of Tai Chi

  • Yang style (most common, gentle)
  • Sun style (often used for arthritis)
  • Wu style
  • Chen style (more vigorous)
