Tai Chi

Gentle movement meditation

What Is Tai Chi?

Tai Chi (also Tai Ji or Taiji) is an ancient Chinese practice combining slow, flowing movements with deep breathing and meditation. Originally a martial art, it's now practiced worldwide for health benefits, particularly for chronic pain, balance, and stress reduction.1

How It Works

  • Slow, continuous movements
  • Deep diaphragmatic breathing
  • Mental focus and mindfulness
  • Weight shifting and balance
  • Low impact on joints
  • Can be done seated if needed

Benefits for Pain2

  • Improves balance and reduces falls
  • Increases flexibility
  • Strengthens muscles gently
  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • May decrease pain intensity
  • Improves sleep quality
  • Enhances body awareness

Conditions with Evidence3

  • Knee osteoarthritis (strongest evidence)
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic low back pain
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Chronic neck pain
  • Parkinson's disease
  • Balance disorders

Styles of Tai Chi

  • Yang style - Most popular, gentle, flowing
  • Sun style - Gentle, good for arthritis
  • Chen style - More vigorous, original style
  • Wu style - Smaller movements

Getting Started

  • Take a class with qualified instructor
  • Look for classes for beginners
  • Wear comfortable, loose clothing
  • Flat, flexible shoes or barefoot
  • Start with shorter sessions
  • Modify movements as needed

Finding Classes

  • Community centers
  • Senior centers
  • Hospitals and clinics
  • Martial arts schools
  • Arthritis Foundation programs
  • Online videos for home practice
