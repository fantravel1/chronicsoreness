Tai Chi

Ancient moving meditation for modern pain relief

What Is Tai Chi?

Tai chi is a gentle Chinese mind-body practice involving slow, flowing movements combined with deep breathing and meditation. Originally a martial art, it's now widely practiced for health benefits, including pain management.1

Benefits for Chronic Pain2

  • Reduces pain intensity
  • Improves balance and reduces falls
  • Increases flexibility
  • Builds strength (especially legs)
  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Improves sleep
  • Enhances body awareness

Conditions Helped

  • Osteoarthritis (especially knee)
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Low back pain
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Neck pain
  • Chronic headaches

Key Principles

  • Slow movements - Deliberate, continuous flow
  • Deep breathing - Coordinates with movement
  • Mindfulness - Present-moment awareness
  • Relaxation - Release unnecessary tension
  • Weight shifting - Builds balance and strength

Getting Started3

  • Find a beginner class or instructor
  • Look for adapted tai chi for pain/arthritis
  • Can be done standing or seated
  • Start with short sessions (15-20 min)
  • No special equipment needed
  • Videos available for home practice

Popular Styles

  • Yang style (most common, gentle)
  • Sun style (higher stance, good for arthritis)
  • Tai Chi for Arthritis program
