Walking with Chronic Pain

A fundamental exercise for pain management

Why Walking Helps

Walking is one of the most accessible and beneficial exercises for people with chronic pain. It's low-impact, requires no special equipment, can be done almost anywhere, and can be easily adapted to different fitness levels. Regular walking can reduce pain, improve mood, increase energy, and maintain function.1

Benefits2

  • Releases natural painkillers (endorphins)
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Improves sleep
  • Maintains joint mobility
  • Strengthens muscles that support joints
  • Reduces depression and anxiety
  • Helps with weight management
  • Improves cardiovascular health

Getting Started

Starting Small

  • Begin with what you can do today
  • Even 5 minutes counts
  • Walk at a comfortable pace
  • Use time, not distance, as your measure

Building Up

  • Increase by small amounts (1-2 minutes)
  • Add time before adding intensity
  • Progress slowly over weeks
  • Listen to your body

Pacing Strategies3

  • Use baseline: distance you can walk on bad days
  • Stop before pain forces you to stop
  • Consistent daily walks better than occasional long ones
  • Take rest breaks if needed
  • Walk multiple short times rather than one long one

Practical Tips

  • Wear supportive, comfortable shoes
  • Warm up with gentle movements
  • Walk on even surfaces when possible
  • Use walking poles for stability if needed
  • Walk during times of day when pain is lower

When Walking Hurts

  • Some discomfort during activity can be normal
  • Pain should settle within 24-48 hours
  • If pain flares last longer, reduce duration
  • Sharp or sudden pain: stop and reassess

Adaptations

  • Walking in water (pool)
  • Using a walker or cane if needed
  • Treadmill for controlled environment
  • Walking indoors (malls) in bad weather
  • Seated marching if standing is too difficult

Staying Motivated

  • Track your progress
  • Walk with a friend or pet
  • Listen to music or podcasts
  • Vary your routes
  • Focus on how you feel after, not during

Sample Starting Program

  • Week 1-2: 5-10 minutes daily
  • Week 3-4: 10-15 minutes daily
  • Week 5-6: 15-20 minutes daily
  • Adjust based on your response
