Pilates for Pain

Core strength and controlled movement

What Is Pilates?

Pilates is a form of low-impact exercise that emphasizes core strength, flexibility, and body awareness. Developed in the early 20th century, it focuses on controlled, precise movements and breath work. Modified Pilates can be an effective and safe option for many people with chronic pain.1

Key Principles2

  • Centering (core engagement)
  • Concentration (mindful movement)
  • Control (quality over quantity)
  • Precision (exact movements)
  • Breath (coordinated breathing)
  • Flow (smooth transitions)

Benefits for Pain

  • Strengthens core muscles
  • Improves posture
  • Increases flexibility
  • Enhances body awareness
  • Low impact on joints
  • Promotes relaxation
  • Can be modified for limitations

Research Support3

  • Evidence strongest for low back pain
  • May help neck pain
  • Shows promise for fibromyalgia
  • Improves function and disability
  • Comparable to other exercise approaches

Mat vs. Equipment Pilates

Mat Pilates

  • Uses body weight
  • Can do at home
  • More accessible
  • May need modifications

Equipment/Reformer

  • Uses spring resistance
  • Can be easier for some exercises
  • Provides support
  • Requires studio access

Getting Started Safely

  • Clear with healthcare provider first
  • Find instructor experienced with pain
  • Consider private sessions initially
  • Communicate your limitations
  • Start slowly
  • Avoid pushing through pain

Modifications to Consider

  • Smaller range of motion
  • Props for support (blocks, pillows)
  • Avoid problematic positions
  • More rest between exercises
  • Focus on breath if flaring

Conditions That May Benefit

  • Low back pain
  • Neck pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Postural issues
  • Post-surgical recovery

Cautions

  • Unstable spine conditions
  • Acute injury
  • Severe osteoporosis (modify flexion)
  • Always work within your limits
