Resistance Band Exercises

Strength training with elastic bands

Why Resistance Bands?

Resistance bands are elastic bands used for strength training. They're ideal for people with chronic pain because they provide variable resistance, are easy on joints, and allow you to control the intensity. They're portable, affordable, and versatile.1

Benefits for Pain2

  • Build strength with less joint stress
  • Control resistance level easily
  • Can exercise in any position
  • Improve muscle tone and stability
  • Work in any range comfortable for you
  • Easy to progress or regress

Types of Bands

  • Loop bands - Continuous loops, good for lower body
  • Tube bands - With handles, good for upper body
  • Therapy bands - Flat, wide bands for rehabilitation
  • Color coding - Usually indicates resistance level

Sample Exercises3

Upper Body

  • Bicep curls
  • Tricep extensions
  • Chest press
  • Rows
  • Shoulder external rotation

Lower Body

  • Squats with band
  • Side-lying leg raises
  • Clamshells
  • Standing hip abduction
  • Leg press (seated)

Core

  • Pallof press
  • Rotations
  • Dead bugs with band

Getting Started

  • Start with light resistance
  • Focus on form over resistance
  • 8-12 repetitions per exercise
  • 1-2 sets to start
  • 2-3 sessions per week
  • Progress gradually

Tips for Success

  • Move slowly and controlled
  • Maintain tension throughout movement
  • Don't let band snap back
  • Breatheâ€”exhale on effort
  • Stop if pain increases
  • Check band for damage before use

Precautions

  • Inspect bands for tears
  • Don't overstretch bands
  • Secure anchors properly
  • Avoid snapping bands
  • Consult PT if unsure about exercises
