Graded Motor Imagery

Retraining the brain to reduce pain

What Is Graded Motor Imagery?

Graded Motor Imagery (GMI) is a sequence of brain exercises designed to normalize brain activity and reduce pain. It's based on the understanding that chronic pain involves changes in how the brain represents the painful body part. GMI gradually retrains these brain pathways.1

The Science Behind GMI2

  • Chronic pain changes brain maps of body parts
  • Mental practice activates similar brain areas as actual movement
  • Gradual exposure reduces threat response
  • Mirror therapy creates visual illusion of normal movement

The Three Stages

Stage 1: Laterality Recognition

  • Looking at pictures of body parts
  • Identifying if it's left or right
  • Trains the brain to recognize body parts
  • Usually done with apps or flashcards
  • Progress to faster, more accurate responses

Stage 2: Imagined Movements

  • Imagine moving the painful body part
  • Don't actually moveâ€”just visualize
  • Start with simple movements
  • Progress to more complex movements
  • Should not increase pain

Stage 3: Mirror Therapy

  • Mirror placed between body parts
  • Watch reflection of unaffected side move
  • Brain "sees" affected side moving normally
  • Gradually introduces actual movement

Conditions That May Benefit3

  • Complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS)
  • Phantom limb pain
  • Stroke recovery
  • Some other chronic pain conditions

How to Get Started

  • Work with trained physical or occupational therapist
  • Apps available for laterality training (Recognise)
  • Requires consistent daily practice
  • Each stage takes 2-4 weeks typically
  • Progress when stage is comfortable

Tips for Success

  • Practice regularly (multiple times daily)
  • Keep sessions short
  • Don't push through pain
  • Progress gradually
  • Track your progress

Evidence

  • Best evidence for CRPS and phantom limb pain
  • Some evidence for other conditions
  • Works best with proper guidance
  • Part of comprehensive treatment approach
