Yoga for Chronic Pain

Mind-body practice for pain management

Why Yoga for Pain?

Yoga combines physical postures, breathing exercises, and meditation in a way that can benefit people with chronic pain. Research supports yoga for conditions like low back pain, arthritis, and fibromyalgia. It improves flexibility, strength, and body awareness while reducing stressâ€”all helpful for pain management.1

Benefits2

  • Improves flexibility and range of motion
  • Builds strength gently
  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Improves body awareness
  • Encourages relaxation response
  • May reduce pain intensity
  • Improves sleep quality
  • Builds mind-body connection

Conditions That May Benefit

  • Chronic low back pain
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic neck pain
  • Headaches/migraines
  • Carpal tunnel syndrome

Best Styles for Chronic Pain3

Hatha Yoga

  • Gentle, slower-paced
  • Good for beginners
  • Focus on basic postures

Restorative Yoga

  • Very gentle, uses props
  • Held positions with support
  • Deep relaxation focus

Yin Yoga

  • Long-held passive poses
  • Targets connective tissue
  • Meditative quality

Chair Yoga

  • Seated or using chair for support
  • Good for limited mobility
  • Accessible for most

Styles to Approach Carefully

  • Hot yoga (Bikram)
  • Power yoga
  • Ashtanga (athletic)
  • Any very vigorous style

Getting Started Safely

  • Talk to your doctor first
  • Find an experienced, certified teacher
  • Look for therapeutic or adaptive classes
  • Inform instructor of your condition
  • Start very slowly
  • Use props liberally
  • Never push through pain

Important Principles

  • Listen to your body
  • Pain is a signal to back off
  • Modifications are always okay
  • Progress slowly
  • Consistency matters more than intensity
  • Breath is as important as movement

At-Home Practice

  • Online classes available
  • Start with 10-15 minutes
  • Learn basics in person first if possible
  • Use props (blocks, straps, blankets)
