Seated Exercises

Chair-based movement for pain management

Why Seated Exercise?

Seated exercises allow you to stay active even when standing or traditional exercise is too difficult. Whether due to pain, fatigue, balance issues, or flares, chair exercises provide a way to maintain strength, flexibility, and cardiovascular fitness. Any movement is better than no movement.1

Benefits

  • Maintain muscle strength
  • Preserve flexibility
  • Improve circulation
  • Reduce stiffness
  • Can be done during flares
  • Good for bad days
  • Adaptable to many conditions

Upper Body Exercises2

Arms and Shoulders

  • Arm circles
  • Shoulder shrugs
  • Overhead reach
  • Arm raises (front and side)
  • Bicep curls (with or without weights)
  • Tricep extensions

Chest and Back

  • Chest stretch
  • Seated rows (with band)
  • Shoulder blade squeezes

Lower Body Exercises

  • Seated marching
  • Leg extensions
  • Ankle circles
  • Heel raises
  • Toe raises
  • Knee lifts
  • Leg slides

Core Exercises3

  • Seated abdominal bracing
  • Gentle trunk rotation
  • Seated side bends
  • Pelvic tilts

Stretches

  • Neck stretches (all directions)
  • Shoulder stretches
  • Seated hamstring stretch
  • Seated spinal twist
  • Ankle stretches

Getting Started

  • Use a sturdy chair without wheels
  • Sit forward in the chair
  • Feet flat on floor if possible
  • Start with 5-10 minutes
  • Do what feels manageable

Cardio Options

  • Seated marching (faster pace)
  • Arm punches with leg lifts
  • Chair dancing
  • Upper body cycling motion

Tips for Success

  • Consistency over intensity
  • Listen to your body
  • Modify as needed
  • Use videos for guidance
  • Progress gradually
