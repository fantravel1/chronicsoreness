Seated Exercises

Effective workouts from a chair

Why Seated Exercises?

Seated exercises allow you to stay active when standing or floor exercises aren't possible. They're great for flare-ups, during recovery, or for those with significant mobility limitations. You can still build strength, improve circulation, and maintain flexibility while seated.1

Benefits2

  • Accessible for various ability levels
  • Reduce fall risk
  • Still provides cardiovascular benefits
  • Maintains muscle strength
  • Improves flexibility
  • Can be done anywhere

Upper Body Exercises

  • Arm circles - Forward and backward
  • Overhead press - With or without weights
  • Bicep curls - Using weights or resistance bands
  • Tricep extensions - Behind the head
  • Chest press - Push arms forward
  • Rowing motion - Pull elbows back

Lower Body Exercises

  • Leg extensions - Straighten knee, hold, lower
  • Marching in place - Lift knees alternately
  • Ankle circles - Rotate feet
  • Heel raises - Lift heels off floor
  • Toe taps - Lift toes, keeping heels down
  • Seated leg lifts - Lift straight leg

Core Exercises

  • Seated crunches - Lean forward slightly
  • Oblique twists - Rotate upper body
  • Knee lifts - Engage core to lift
  • Seated cat-cow - Arch and round spine

Cardio Options3

  • Seated marching (fast)
  • Arm swings with marching
  • Seated jumping jacks (arms and legs)
  • Boxing punches
  • Pedal exercisers

Tips for Success

  • Use sturdy chair without wheels
  • Sit toward front of chair
  • Keep feet flat on floor
  • Maintain good posture
  • Start slowly and build up
  • Add weights or bands as you progress
