Balance Training

Improving stability and preventing falls

Why Balance Matters

Many chronic pain conditions affect balance through deconditioning, medication side effects, or the condition itself. Poor balance increases fall risk, which can cause injury and worsen pain. Balance training can improve stability and confidence.1

Conditions Affecting Balance

  • Peripheral neuropathy
  • Arthritis (hip, knee, ankle)
  • Chronic pain (general)
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Vestibular problems
  • Medication side effects

Components of Balance2

  • Vision - Seeing where you are in space
  • Vestibular - Inner ear balance sensors
  • Proprioception - Body position sense
  • Strength - Muscles to correct balance

Basic Exercises (With Support)

Standing Near Counter/Chair

  • Feet together stance
  • Tandem stance (heel-toe)
  • Single leg stance
  • Weight shifts side to side

Progression

  • Reduce hand support
  • Close eyes (harder)
  • Add head movements
  • Softer surface

Dynamic Balance

  • Heel-toe walking
  • Side stepping
  • Walking with head turns
  • Stepping over obstacles

Safety Tips3

  • Start with support available
  • Clear area of tripping hazards
  • Wear supportive footwear
  • Progress slowly
  • Consider supervision initially

Home Safety

  • Remove loose rugs
  • Good lighting
  • Handrails on stairs
  • Grab bars in bathroom
  • Non-slip mats
