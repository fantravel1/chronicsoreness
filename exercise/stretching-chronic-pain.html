Stretching for Pain

Gentle flexibility work for pain management

Why Stretching Helps

Regular, gentle stretching can help manage chronic pain by maintaining flexibility, reducing muscle tension, improving circulation, and promoting relaxation. Tight muscles can contribute to pain and limit movement, creating a cycle that stretching can help break.1

Benefits2

  • Maintains or improves flexibility
  • Reduces muscle tension
  • Increases blood flow to muscles
  • May reduce pain intensity
  • Improves range of motion
  • Can be calming and relaxing
  • Prepares body for other activities

Types of Stretching

Static Stretching

  • Hold stretch for 15-60 seconds
  • No bouncing
  • Gentle, sustained stretch
  • Most commonly recommended for chronic pain

Dynamic Stretching

  • Controlled movements through range
  • Good for warm-up
  • Arm circles, leg swings

Safe Stretching Principles3

  • Warm up first (5 minutes of light movement)
  • Move into stretches slowly
  • Stop at mild tension, not pain
  • Don't bounce or force
  • Breathe normally throughout
  • Hold for 15-30 seconds
  • Repeat 2-4 times each side

When to Stretch

  • After warming up muscles
  • After a hot shower or bath
  • When muscles feel tight
  • Throughout the day (brief stretches)
  • After prolonged sitting

Common Problem Areas

  • Neck and upper back
  • Shoulders
  • Lower back
  • Hips and hip flexors
  • Hamstrings
  • Calves

Stretching Cautions

  • Don't stretch into sharp pain
  • Avoid overstretching
  • Be careful with hypermobile joints
  • Modify for your condition
  • Get guidance if unsure

Building a Routine

  • Start with 5-10 minutes
  • Focus on problem areas
  • Consistency over intensity
  • Daily stretching is ideal
  • Progress gradually

Getting Guidance

  • Physical therapist can teach proper technique
  • Customize stretches for your condition
  • Online videos (verify source quality)
  • Group classes (yoga, gentle stretching)
