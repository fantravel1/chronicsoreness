Yoga for Chronic Pain

Mind-body practice for pain management

Why Yoga for Pain?

Yoga combines physical postures, breathing techniques, and meditation/relaxation. Research shows it can help reduce chronic pain intensity, improve function, and enhance quality of life for various pain conditions.1

Benefits

  • Improved flexibility and strength
  • Better body awareness
  • Reduced muscle tension
  • Stress and anxiety reduction
  • Improved sleep
  • Enhanced coping skills
  • Mind-body connection

Evidence for Pain Conditions2

  • Low back pain - Strong evidence
  • Neck pain - Moderate evidence
  • Fibromyalgia - Moderate evidence
  • Osteoarthritis - Moderate evidence
  • Headaches - Some evidence

Types of Yoga

  • Hatha - Gentle, good for beginners
  • Iyengar - Uses props, precise alignment
  • Restorative - Very gentle, uses props for support
  • Viniyoga - Therapeutic, individualized
  • Chair yoga - Adapted for mobility issues

Getting Started3

  • Start with gentle/beginner classes
  • Inform instructor of your condition
  • Listen to your body - modify as needed
  • Avoid poses that increase pain
  • Consider yoga therapy or adaptive classes
  • Consistency more important than intensity

Safety Tips

  • Avoid extreme poses
  • Don't push through pain
  • Use props for support
  • Avoid hot yoga if heat-sensitive
  • Consult provider if spine issues
