Graded Exercise Therapy

Gradually increasing activity in a structured way

What Is Graded Exercise?

Graded exercise therapy (GET) is an approach that involves gradually and progressively increasing activity levels. Starting from a sustainable baseline, activity is systematically increased over time to improve fitness, function, and confidence.1

Why Gradual Increase?

  • Avoids boom-bust cycles
  • Builds confidence
  • Allows body to adapt
  • Reduces fear of movement
  • Sustainable long-term

Key Principles2

  • Find baseline - Activity level you can do consistently
  • Time-based, not symptom-based - Don't stop based on pain
  • Small increments - Increase by 10-20% at a time
  • Planned progression - Scheduled increases regardless of how you feel
  • Consistency - Same amount on good and bad days

Getting Started

Step 1: Establish Baseline

  • Test what you can do (e.g., walking time)
  • Take average over several days
  • Start at 80% of this level

Step 2: Maintain Baseline

  • Do same amount for 1-2 weeks
  • Build consistency

Step 3: Gradual Increases

  • Increase by small amount (e.g., 1-2 minutes)
  • Maintain new level for several days
  • Increase again

Example Progression

  • Week 1-2: 5 minutes walking
  • Week 3-4: 6 minutes walking
  • Week 5-6: 7 minutes walking
  • Continue gradual increase

Managing Setbacks3

  • Drop back to previous level
  • Don't stop completely
  • Resume progression when stable
  • Setbacks are normal, not failure

Important Notes

  • Work with healthcare provider
  • Not appropriate for all conditions
  • May need to modify for ME/CFS
  • Listen to your body within the plan
