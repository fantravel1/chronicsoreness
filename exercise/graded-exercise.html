Graded Exercise

Building activity tolerance gradually

What Is Graded Exercise?

Graded exercise therapy (GET) involves starting with very gentle activity and progressively increasing intensity over time. The goal is to improve function and fitness while avoiding significant pain flares. It's often used in rehabilitation for chronic pain conditions where fear of movement or deconditioning is a factor.1

The Concept2

  • Start below current capability
  • Increase gradually and systematically
  • Based on time, not symptoms
  • Builds confidence and capacity
  • Avoids boom-bust cycle

Why It Helps

  • Addresses deconditioning
  • Reduces fear of movement
  • Improves cardiovascular fitness
  • Builds strength gradually
  • Teaches body exercise is safe
  • Improves confidence

Key Principles3

  • Start very low (below what you think you can do)
  • Progress in small increments (5-10%)
  • Increase only when current level is comfortable
  • Use time-based goals, not distance/reps
  • Stay consistent even on bad days
  • Don't skip ahead on good days

Example Progression

Walking Program

  • Week 1: 5 minutes, 3x/week
  • Week 2: 7 minutes, 3x/week
  • Week 3: 9 minutes, 3x/week
  • And so on...
  • Progress may be slower for some

Important Distinctions

  • Different from "push through pain"
  • Not appropriate for all conditions
  • ME/CFS requires different approach (pacing)
  • Should be individualized
  • Guided by physical therapist ideally

When Pain Increases

  • Don't stop completely
  • Maintain current level (don't increase)
  • May need to step back temporarily
  • Then resume progression
  • Some discomfort expected, severe pain not

Conditions It May Help

  • Chronic low back pain
  • Fibromyalgia (with care)
  • Chronic neck pain
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Post-surgical deconditioning

Working with Professionals

  • Physical therapist can create plan
  • Address fears about movement
  • Monitor progress
  • Adjust as needed
