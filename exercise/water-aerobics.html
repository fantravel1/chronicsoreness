Water Aerobics

Low-impact exercise in the pool

Why Water Exercise?

Water aerobics uses the unique properties of water to provide exercise with minimal joint stress. Buoyancy supports your weight, reducing impact by up to 90%, while water resistance provides strength training. It's ideal for people with chronic pain conditions.1

Benefits2

  • Reduced joint stress and impact
  • Improved strength and endurance
  • Better balance (water supports you)
  • Increased flexibility
  • Cardiovascular conditioning
  • Warm water can soothe pain
  • Social interaction in classes

Conditions That Benefit

  • Arthritis (all types)
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Back pain
  • Joint replacements
  • Obesity
  • Balance disorders
  • Most chronic pain conditions

Types of Water Exercise

  • Water walking - Simple and effective
  • Aqua aerobics classes - Structured group exercise
  • Deep water running - Using flotation belt
  • Aqua yoga/stretching - Gentle flexibility work
  • Lap swimming - Cardio workout

Sample Exercises

  • Water walking (forward, backward, sideways)
  • Leg lifts
  • Arm circles
  • Knee lifts
  • Standing hip movements
  • Flutter kicks at wall

Finding Classes

  • Community recreation centers
  • Hospital rehabilitation pools
  • Arthritis Foundation classes
  • Physical therapy pools
