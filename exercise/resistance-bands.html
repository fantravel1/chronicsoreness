Resistance Band Exercises

Versatile, low-impact strength training

Why Resistance Bands?

Resistance bands offer a gentle, effective way to build strength. They're portable, inexpensive, and allow for controlled resistance that can be easily adjustedâ€”ideal for people with chronic pain.1

Benefits for Chronic Pain

  • Variable resistance throughout movement
  • Low impact on joints
  • Easy to modify intensity
  • Can strengthen muscles without heavy loads
  • Portableâ€”exercise anywhere
  • Affordable

Types of Bands2

  • Flat bands - Basic, versatile
  • Tube bands with handles - Good grip
  • Loop bands - For lower body
  • Therapy bands - Often used in rehab
  • Different colors indicate different resistances

Getting Started

  • Start with lighter resistance
  • Focus on controlled movements
  • Don't snap or jerk the band
  • Progress gradually
  • Aim for 10-15 repetitions

Sample Exercises3

Upper Body

  • Rows (seated or standing)
  • Chest press
  • Shoulder external rotation
  • Bicep curls

Lower Body

  • Clamshells
  • Side steps (monster walks)
  • Squats with band
  • Leg extensions

Core

  • Pallof press
  • Wood chops

Safety Tips

  • Inspect bands for tears before use
  • Secure anchor points properly
  • Avoid releasing bands suddenly
  • Stop if pain increases
