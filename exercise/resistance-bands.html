Resistance Band Exercises

Gentle strength training anywhere

Why Resistance Bands?

Resistance bands are elastic bands or tubes that provide resistance for strengthening exercises. They're ideal for people with chronic pain because you can easily adjust the resistance, work at your own pace, and exercise at home. They're portable, affordable, and can strengthen muscles without heavy weights.1

Benefits for Pain2

  • Builds strength gradually
  • Supports joints
  • Improves stability
  • Can work around painful areas
  • Easy to modify resistance
  • Joint-friendly
  • Home-based convenience

Types of Bands

  • Flat bands (therapy bands)
  • Tube bands with handles
  • Loop bands (mini bands)
  • Figure-8 bands
  • Color-coded by resistance level

Resistance Levels

  • Extra light (yellow/tan)
  • Light (red/green)
  • Medium (green/blue)
  • Heavy (blue/black)
  • Extra heavy (black/silver)
  • Start with lighter resistance

Basic Exercises3

Upper Body

  • Rows (seated or standing)
  • Chest press
  • Shoulder external rotation
  • Bicep curls
  • Tricep extensions

Lower Body

  • Side steps (with loop)
  • Leg press (lying down)
  • Hip abduction
  • Clamshells
  • Glute bridges with band

Core

  • Pallof press
  • Woodchops
  • Seated rotations

Getting Started

  • Clear with healthcare provider
  • Start with lightest band
  • Learn proper form
  • Work with PT initially if needed
  • Start with few repetitions
  • Progress slowly

Safety Tips

  • Check bands for tears before use
  • Secure anchor points properly
  • Control the movement
  • Don't let band snap back
  • Stop if sharp pain occurs
  • Replace worn bands

Exercise Principles

  • Slow, controlled movements
  • Focus on form over reps
  • Breathe (don't hold breath)
  • Work both sides equally
  • Rest between sets
  • 2-3 times per week

Progressing Safely

  • Increase reps before resistance
  • Move to next band color gradually
  • Add exercises one at a time
  • Listen to your body
  • Some soreness OK, pain not
