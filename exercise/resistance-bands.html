Resistance Band Exercises

Gentle strength training for chronic pain

Why Resistance Bands?

Resistance bands offer a gentle, joint-friendly way to build strength. They provide variable resistance throughout the movement, can be adjusted to any fitness level, and are commonly used in physical therapy. For people with chronic pain, they allow strengthening without the joint stress of heavy weights.1

Benefits for Chronic Pain

  • Low impact on joints
  • Easily adjustable resistance
  • Can be used lying down, sitting, or standing
  • Portable and affordable
  • Allows smooth, controlled movements
  • Reduces risk of injury from dropped weights

Types of Bands2

  • Flat therapy bands (no handles)
  • Tube bands with handles
  • Loop bands (closed circle)
  • Mini bands (small loops for legs)
  • Colors typically indicate resistance level

Upper Body Exercises

Shoulder

  • External rotation
  • Internal rotation
  • Shoulder press
  • Rows
  • Lateral raises

Arms

  • Bicep curls
  • Tricep extensions
  • Chest press

Lower Body Exercises3

  • Leg press (seated)
  • Clamshells
  • Side-lying leg lifts
  • Standing hip abduction
  • Seated knee extension
  • Monster walks

Core Exercises

  • Pallof press (anti-rotation)
  • Seated trunk rotation
  • Dead bugs with band

Getting Started

  • Start with lightest resistance
  • Learn proper form first
  • Progress slowly
  • 10-15 repetitions is typical
  • 2-3 sets per exercise
  • Rest between sets

Safety Tips

  • Check bands for tears before use
  • Secure anchors properly
  • Avoid letting band snap back
  • Move slowly and controlled
  • Stop if pain increases
  • Don't hold breath

When to Get Guidance

  • Starting a new exercise program
  • After injury or surgery
  • If unsure about proper form
  • Pain condition is complex
