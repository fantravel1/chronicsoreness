Cycling for Chronic Pain

Low-impact cardiovascular exercise

Why Cycling?

Cycling is an excellent low-impact exercise option for people with chronic pain. It provides cardiovascular benefits while minimizing stress on joints, making it suitable for many pain conditions.1

Benefits

  • Low impact on joints
  • Adjustable intensity
  • Builds leg strength
  • Cardiovascular fitness
  • Weight management
  • Mood improvement
  • Can be done indoors or outdoors

Good For2

  • Knee osteoarthritis
  • Hip osteoarthritis
  • Low back pain (often)
  • Fibromyalgia
  • General deconditioning

Types of Cycling

  • Stationary bike - Controlled, indoor, safe
  • Recumbent bike - Supports back, easier on spine
  • Outdoor cycling - More engaging, balance required
  • Spin classes - Group motivation (modify intensity)

Proper Setup3

  • Seat height - Slight knee bend at bottom of pedal stroke
  • Handlebar height - Higher for back issues
  • Seat position - Knee over ball of foot at 3 o'clock
  • Consider bike fit - Professional fitting if issues

Getting Started

  • Start with 5-10 minutes
  • Low resistance initially
  • Gradually increase duration
  • Add intensity later
  • Listen to your body

Considerations

May Need Modification

  • Neck pain - Adjust posture, handlebar height
  • Low back pain - Recumbent may be better
  • Hand/wrist pain - Padded gloves, grips

Use Caution

  • Balance issues (use stationary)
  • Severe hip flexion limitations
  • Significant spinal stenosis
