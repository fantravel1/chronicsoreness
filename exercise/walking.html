Walking

The simplest and most accessible exercise

Why Walk?

Walking is one of the most accessible and beneficial forms of exercise for people with chronic pain. It requires no equipment, can be done anywhere, and is easily modified to your current ability level.1

Benefits for Chronic Pain

  • Low impact on joints
  • Improves circulation
  • Reduces stiffness
  • Releases endorphins
  • Improves mood and sleep
  • Helps with weight management
  • Builds endurance gradually

Conditions Helped2

  • Low back pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Chronic fatigue
  • Depression associated with pain

Getting Started

  • Start with what you can do (even 5 minutes)
  • Walk at comfortable pace
  • Use supportive shoes
  • Choose flat, even surfaces initially
  • Walk at your best time of day

Progression3

  • Increase duration before intensity
  • Add 1-2 minutes per week
  • Work toward 30 minutes most days
  • Listen to your body
  • Some discomfort normal; adjust if pain increases significantly

Tips for Success

  • Make it routine (same time daily)
  • Walk with a friend or pet
  • Use music or podcasts
  • Track progress for motivation
  • Split into shorter walks if needed
  • Use walking aids if helpful
