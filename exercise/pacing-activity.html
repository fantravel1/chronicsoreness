Activity Pacing

Finding sustainable activity levels

What Is Activity Pacing?

Activity pacing is a self-management strategy that involves breaking activities into manageable segments with rest periods in between. The goal is to maintain consistent activity levels without triggering significant pain flares. It's one of the most important skills for living well with chronic pain.1

The Boom-Bust Cycle2

  • "Boom": Overdo on good days
  • "Bust": Crash and can't do anything
  • Recovery takes days
  • Then feel better, overdo again
  • Cycle repeats
  • Net loss in function over time

Why Pacing Works

  • Prevents flare-ups
  • Maintains consistent baseline
  • Allows gradual activity increase
  • Conserves energy
  • Reduces frustration
  • More accomplished over time

Key Principles3

  • Set time-based (not task-based) limits
  • Take breaks before pain forces you to stop
  • Alternate activities
  • Balance rest and activity
  • Plan ahead
  • Be consistent

How to Start

Step 1: Establish Baseline

  • Track current activity levels
  • Note how long until pain increases
  • Find your sustainable time for each activity

Step 2: Set Initial Limits

  • Start below what triggers pain
  • Use time limits, not task completion
  • Schedule regular breaks

Step 3: Gradually Progress

  • Increase by small amounts (10%)
  • Only when current level is manageable
  • Expect some setbacks

Practical Examples

  • Stand 10 minutes, sit 5 minutes
  • Garden 15 minutes, then inside break
  • Computer work in 30-minute blocks
  • Alternate sitting and standing tasks
  • Schedule rest periods into day

Common Challenges

  • Feeling you "should" do more
  • Guilt about resting
  • Pressure from others
  • Good days tempt overdoing
  • Feeling the approach is too slow

Tips for Success

  • Use timers
  • Prioritize activities
  • Communicate with family
  • Be patient with yourself
  • Celebrate consistency
