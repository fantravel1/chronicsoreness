Walking for Pain

Simple, accessible movement

Why Walking Helps

Walking is often recommended as a starting point for exercise with chronic pain. It's low-impact, requires no equipment, can be done almost anywhere, and is easily modified. Research shows regular walking can help reduce pain intensity, improve function, and enhance mood in many chronic pain conditions.1

Benefits2

  • Low impact on joints
  • Improves cardiovascular health
  • Releases endorphins
  • Reduces stiffness
  • Improves sleep
  • Boosts mood
  • Maintains mobility
  • Manageable intensity

Tips for Walking with Pain

Preparation

  • Supportive, comfortable shoes
  • Warm up gently
  • Choose flat, even surfaces
  • Walk when pain is typically lower

During

  • Start slowly
  • Maintain comfortable pace
  • Use walking aids if needed
  • Rest if necessary

After

  • Gentle stretching
  • Rest appropriately
  • Track how you feel

Modifications

  • Use walking stick or cane
  • Indoor walking (mall, treadmill)
  • Water walking (pool)
  • Shorter, more frequent walks
  • Sit and rest breaks

When to Be Cautious

  • During severe flares
  • If pain significantly increases
  • Balance or fall concerns
  • Certain cardiac conditions
  • Always check with provider

Making It Enjoyable

  • Music or podcasts
  • Walk with others
  • Vary your routes
  • Nature walks when possible
  • Focus on the positives

Building the Habit

  • Same time each day
  • Link to existing routine
  • Don't skip bad days entirely
  • Even 5 minutes counts
  • Celebrate consistency
