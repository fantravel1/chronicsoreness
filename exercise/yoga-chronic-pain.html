Yoga for Chronic Pain

Mindful movement for pain management

Why Yoga for Chronic Pain?

Yoga combines physical postures, breathing exercises, and meditation. For chronic pain, it can improve flexibility, strength, and body awareness while reducing stress. Research supports its use for various pain conditions.1

Evidence-Based Benefits2

  • Reduced pain intensity
  • Improved function and mobility
  • Better sleep quality
  • Decreased stress and anxiety
  • Improved body awareness
  • Enhanced quality of life

Conditions That May Benefit

  • Low back pain
  • Neck pain
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Headaches

Styles for Chronic Pain

  • Gentle/restorative yoga - Slow, supported poses
  • Yin yoga - Long-held, gentle stretches
  • Chair yoga - For limited mobility
  • Therapeutic yoga - Tailored for conditions

Getting Started3

  • Find an experienced instructor
  • Inform teacher of your condition
  • Start with gentle classes
  • Use props (blocks, straps, blankets)
  • Modify or skip poses as needed
  • Never push through sharp pain

Key Principles

  • Listen to your body
  • Breathe through discomfort, not pain
  • Progress slowly
  • Consistency matters more than intensity
  • Focus on how you feel, not how you look

Cautions

  • Avoid hot yoga if heat-sensitive
  • Be careful with hypermobility
  • Modify inversions if needed
  • Consult doctor for specific conditions
