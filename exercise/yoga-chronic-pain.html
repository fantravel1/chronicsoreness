Yoga for Chronic Pain

Gentle movement for body and mind

Why Yoga for Pain?

Yoga combines gentle movement, breathing, and mindfulnessâ€”all elements that can help with chronic pain. Research shows yoga can reduce pain intensity, improve function, and decrease disability in various chronic pain conditions. It addresses both the physical and psychological aspects of pain.1

Evidence for Chronic Pain2

  • Chronic low back pain: Strong evidence
  • Neck pain: Moderate evidence
  • Fibromyalgia: Moderate evidence
  • Arthritis: Moderate evidence
  • Headaches: Some evidence

Benefits Beyond Pain

  • Improved flexibility and strength
  • Better balance
  • Reduced stress and anxiety
  • Improved sleep
  • Greater body awareness
  • Enhanced mood

Types of Yoga for Pain3

Gentle/Hatha Yoga

  • Slow-paced
  • Focus on basic poses
  • Good for beginners

Restorative Yoga

  • Uses props extensively
  • Poses held longer (5-20 minutes)
  • Very gentle
  • Promotes deep relaxation

Yin Yoga

  • Long-held, passive poses
  • Targets connective tissue
  • Meditative quality

Chair Yoga

  • Modified poses using chair
  • Good for limited mobility
  • Accessible for most people

Getting Started Safely

  • Start with gentle or beginner classes
  • Look for teachers experienced with pain/injuries
  • Inform teacher about your condition
  • Use props (blocks, straps, blankets)
  • Listen to your body
  • Avoid "pushing through" pain

Key Principles

  • Pain should not increase during practice
  • Modify poses as needed
  • Rest when necessary
  • Focus on breath
  • Progress gradually
  • Consistency over intensity

What to Avoid

  • Hot yoga (can increase inflammation)
  • Extreme stretches
  • Competitive attitudes
  • Pushing into pain
  • Classes that don't allow modifications

Finding Classes

  • Local yoga studios (ask about gentle options)
  • Physical therapy offices
  • Online classes (many free options)
  • Yoga therapists for individualized instruction
