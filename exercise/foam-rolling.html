Foam Rolling

Self-myofascial release for muscle pain

What Is Foam Rolling?

Foam rolling is a form of self-myofascial release (SMR) that uses body weight pressure on a foam cylinder to massage muscles and soft tissues. It can help reduce muscle tightness, increase flexibility, and may provide temporary pain relief. While not a cure for chronic pain, it can be a useful tool in a comprehensive management plan.1

How It May Help2

  • Reduces muscle tension
  • Increases blood flow to tissues
  • May improve flexibility
  • Helps with muscle recovery
  • Provides self-massage benefits
  • May reduce delayed onset muscle soreness

Types of Foam Rollers

Soft/Low Density

  • Good for beginners
  • Gentler pressure
  • Less intense sensation

Firm/High Density

  • More pressure
  • More durable
  • For experienced users

Textured

  • Ridges and bumps
  • More targeted pressure
  • More intense

Basic Technique3

  • Position body over roller
  • Use body weight for pressure
  • Roll slowly over muscle
  • Pause on tender spots (30-60 seconds)
  • Avoid rolling directly on bones/joints
  • Breathe deeply and try to relax

Common Areas

  • Upper back (thoracic spine)
  • Quadriceps (front of thigh)
  • IT band (side of thigh)
  • Hamstrings (back of thigh)
  • Calves
  • Glutes
  • Lats (side of back)

Areas to Avoid

  • Lower back (use ball instead)
  • Directly on spine
  • Neck
  • Joints (knees, elbows)
  • Injured or inflamed areas
  • Bony prominences

Tips for Chronic Pain

  • Start with softer roller
  • Use lighter pressure initially
  • Shorter sessions at first
  • Don't push through severe pain
  • Discomfort okay, pain not okay
  • Stay hydrated

Precautions

  • Avoid if taking blood thinners (check with doctor)
  • Don't use over broken skin
  • Caution with osteoporosis
  • Avoid with acute injuries
  • Not appropriate for all conditions

When to Use

  • Before exercise (warm-up)
  • After exercise (recovery)
  • When muscles feel tight
  • Part of regular routine
  • Not during acute pain flares
