Foam Rolling

Self-myofascial release at home

What Is Foam Rolling?

Foam rolling is a form of self-myofascial release using a cylindrical foam roller to apply pressure to muscles and fascia. It's used to reduce muscle tightness, improve flexibility, and aid recovery.1

Proposed Benefits2

  • Reduced muscle soreness
  • Improved range of motion
  • Enhanced blood flow
  • Decreased muscle tension
  • May help with recovery

Types of Rollers

  • Soft/low density - For beginners, sensitive areas
  • Medium density - Most common, general use
  • Firm/high density - More intense pressure
  • Textured - Extra trigger point work
  • Vibrating - Added stimulation

Common Areas

  • Upper back (thoracic spine)
  • Quadriceps
  • Hamstrings
  • IT band/outer thigh
  • Calves
  • Glutes

How to Foam Roll3

  • Roll slowly (about 1 inch per second)
  • Pause on tender spots for 30-60 seconds
  • Apply moderate pressure
  • Breathe and relax
  • Discomfort okay, sharp pain not

When to Use

  • Before exercise (brief, to warm up)
  • After exercise (recovery)
  • As standalone mobility work
  • When muscles feel tight

Cautions

  • Avoid directly on spine
  • Skip if acute injury or inflammation
  • Not over varicose veins
  • Avoid bony prominences
  • Use caution if on blood thinners
