Stretching for Chronic Pain

Improving flexibility and reducing muscle tension

Why Stretch?

Regular stretching can help manage chronic pain by improving flexibility, reducing muscle tension, increasing blood flow, and maintaining range of motion. For many pain conditions, gentle stretching is a key component of management.1

Benefits

  • Reduces muscle tension and stiffness
  • Improves range of motion
  • Increases blood flow to muscles
  • May reduce pain intensity
  • Promotes relaxation
  • Prepares body for activity

Types of Stretching

Static Stretching

  • Hold a position for 15-60 seconds
  • Most common and safest type
  • Best done when muscles are warm

Dynamic Stretching

  • Controlled movements through range of motion
  • Good as warm-up before activity
  • Examples: leg swings, arm circles

Stretching Guidelines2

  • Warm up first (light walking, warm shower)
  • Hold stretches 15-60 seconds
  • Don't bounce
  • Breathe normally
  • Feel stretch, not pain
  • Stretch both sides equally
  • Consistency matters - daily is best

Common Stretches

  • Neck: Gentle tilts and turns
  • Shoulders: Cross-body stretch, doorway stretch
  • Back: Cat-cow, child's pose, knee-to-chest
  • Hips: Figure-four, piriformis stretch
  • Legs: Hamstring, calf, quad stretches

Cautions

  • Avoid stretching acutely inflamed areas
  • Don't push into pain
  • Hypermobile individuals: focus on stability, not more flexibility
  • Ask provider about specific restrictions
