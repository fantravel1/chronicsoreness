Stretching

Improving flexibility and reducing tension

Benefits of Stretching

Regular stretching can improve flexibility, reduce muscle tension, increase range of motion, and help manage chronic pain. It's an accessible form of exercise that can be done almost anywhere.1

Types of Stretching2

Static Stretching

  • Hold stretch for 15-60 seconds
  • No bouncing
  • Best after warming up
  • Good for improving flexibility

Dynamic Stretching

  • Controlled movements through range
  • No holding positions
  • Good warm-up before activity

PNF Stretching

  • Proprioceptive neuromuscular facilitation
  • Contract-relax technique
  • Often done with partner/therapist
  • Very effective for gaining flexibility

Safe Stretching Guidelines3

  • Warm up first (5 minutes light activity)
  • Stretch to mild tension, not pain
  • Don't bounce (static stretches)
  • Breathe normally, don't hold breath
  • Hold static stretches 15-60 seconds
  • Stretch both sides equally
  • Be consistent (daily if possible)

Key Areas to Stretch

  • Neck and shoulders
  • Chest (often tight from posture)
  • Hip flexors (tight from sitting)
  • Hamstrings
  • Lower back
  • Calves

When to Avoid Stretching

  • Acute injury or inflammation
  • Unstable joints
  • Sharp pain during stretch
  • Recent fracture
