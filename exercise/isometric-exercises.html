Isometric Exercises

Building strength without joint movement

What Are Isometric Exercises?

Isometric exercises involve contracting muscles without moving the joints. You hold a position and create tension in the muscle. These exercises are particularly useful when joint movement causes pain, making them ideal for many chronic pain conditions.1

Benefits for Chronic Pain2

  • No joint movement required
  • Can work around painful ranges
  • Build strength at specific angles
  • Lower injury risk than dynamic exercise
  • Can be done anywhere
  • Research shows pain-relieving effects

Upper Body Isometrics

Wall Push

  • Stand facing wall, arms extended
  • Push into wall, hold 10-30 seconds
  • Works chest and shoulders

Isometric Rows

  • Sit in doorway, grab frame at chest height
  • Pull without moving, hold
  • Works back muscles

Neck Isometrics

  • Press hand against forehead, resist movement
  • Repeat on sides and back of head
  • Good for neck pain

Lower Body Isometrics

Wall Sit

  • Back against wall, slide down to sitting position
  • Thighs parallel to floor (or higher if painful)
  • Hold 10-60 seconds

Isometric Quad Set

  • Sit with leg extended
  • Press knee down into floor/mat
  • Hold 5-10 seconds
  • Excellent for knee pain

Glute Squeeze

  • Lying or standing, squeeze buttocks together
  • Hold 5-10 seconds
  • Works gluteal muscles

Core Isometrics3

Plank

  • On forearms and toes (or knees)
  • Keep body straight
  • Hold as long as possible with good form

Dead Bug Hold

  • Lie on back, arms and legs raised
  • Press lower back into floor
  • Hold position

How to Use Isometrics

  • Hold each position 5-30 seconds
  • Don't hold breath (breathe normally)
  • Start with low intensity
  • Repeat 3-5 times per exercise
  • Work at pain-free angles
  • Progress by increasing hold time or intensity

Cautions

  • Avoid breath-holding (can raise blood pressure)
  • Consult doctor if you have heart or blood pressure issues
  • Start gently and progress gradually
  • Stop if sharp pain occurs
