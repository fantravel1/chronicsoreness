Isometric Exercises

Strengthening without movement

What Are Isometric Exercises?

Isometric exercises involve contracting muscles without moving the joint. You push or pull against an immovable object or hold a position. They're excellent for building strength when movement is painful or restricted.1

Benefits for Chronic Pain2

  • Strengthen without stressing joints
  • Can be done at any angle
  • Maintain strength during flares
  • Good for acute injuries
  • May reduce pain (tendinopathy)
  • Safe starting point

Examples

Upper Body

  • Wall push - Push into wall with hands
  • Doorway press - Press out against doorframe
  • Prayer press - Press palms together
  • Bicep hold - Hold weight at 90 degrees

Lower Body

  • Wall sit - Hold squat against wall
  • Quad sets - Tighten thigh, leg straight
  • Glute squeeze - Tighten buttocks
  • Inner thigh squeeze - Pillow between knees

Core

  • Plank - Hold push-up position
  • Side plank - Side position hold
  • Dead bug hold - Hold position

How to Perform3

  • Contract muscle at 50-75% effort
  • Hold for 5-10 seconds
  • Breathe normally (don't hold breath)
  • Rest, repeat 5-10 times
  • Can do multiple angles

Cautions

  • Avoid breath-holding (raises BP)
  • Use caution with high blood pressure
  • Stop if sharp pain
  • Progress gradually
