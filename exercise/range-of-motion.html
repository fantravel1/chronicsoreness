Range of Motion Exercises

Maintaining joint mobility

Why ROM Exercises Matter

Range of motion (ROM) exercises help maintain or improve the ability of joints to move through their normal range. For people with chronic pain, maintaining flexibility is important to prevent further stiffness, reduce pain, and preserve function. ROM exercises are gentle and can be done even during pain flares.1

Benefits2

  • Maintains joint flexibility
  • Prevents stiffness
  • Preserves function
  • Can reduce pain
  • Improves circulation
  • Gentle starting point for exercise
  • Can be done daily

Types of ROM Exercises

Passive ROM

  • Someone else moves the joint
  • No muscle effort from patient
  • For very weak or painful conditions
  • Physical therapist may perform

Active-Assisted ROM

  • Patient moves with help
  • Uses other hand, strap, or therapist
  • Bridge between passive and active

Active ROM

  • Patient moves joint independently
  • Uses own muscles
  • Most common type

General Principles3

  • Move joints through full available range
  • Move slowly and smoothly
  • Don't push through sharp pain
  • Some discomfort is okay, pain is not
  • Breathe normally
  • Repeat each movement 5-10 times
  • Daily is ideal

Examples by Body Area

Neck

  • Chin tucks
  • Rotation (look left/right)
  • Side bending
  • Gentle nods

Shoulders

  • Shoulder shrugs
  • Arm circles
  • Pendulum swings
  • Wall slides

Hips and Knees

  • Knee bends (lying down)
  • Hip circles
  • Knee to chest
  • Leg slides

Ankles and Feet

  • Ankle circles
  • Point and flex
  • Toe spreads

When to Do ROM Exercises

  • Morning stiffness
  • After prolonged sitting
  • Before more vigorous exercise
  • During pain flares (gently)
  • As part of daily routine

Tips for Success

  • Warm up with heat if helpful
  • Consistency matters
  • Even small movements help
  • Work with PT for personalized program
  • Track your progress
